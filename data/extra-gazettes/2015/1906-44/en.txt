Dissanayake Mudiyanselage Kulamina Chandana Dissanayake, Commissioner of Co-operative Development and Registrar
of Co-operative Societies, of the North Western Province, do by this Order hereby make the following Rules for the election
of a Branch Committee .of a Co-operative Society.

1. Short Title :
These Rules may be cited as Rules for the election of a Branch Committee of Multi-Purpose Co-operative Societies
in the North Western Province.
2. Voters' List :
2.1  For the purpose of electing the required number of Branch Committee members
under By-law 22(1) of a Multi-Purpose Co-operative Society, a list of members who are eligible to vote shall be
prepared in triplicate for each year ending 31st December of every year by the Board of Directors. Such list shall be prepared
prior to 15th January of the ensuing year.
One of these lists shall be exhibited in a prominent place in the office of the Branch and another copy of it in a
prominent place of business in the Branch for a period from 16th January to 31st January each year.
However, a list of members of any Co-operative Society for the year ending on the last day of a month which
presides three months of the date fixed for the election of the Society shall be prepared prior to the 15th day of the ensuing

General
APPOINTMENT OF CHIEF ELECTIONS OFFICER, ELECTIONS OFFICERS IN-CHARGE OF ZONES AND ASSISTANT
ELECTIONS OFFICERS
3.1  The Commissioner shall appoint for every Multi-Purpose Co-operative Society a  Chief Elections Officer, Elections
Officers In-Charge of Zones and Assistant Elections Officers as may be necessary under these Rules. Such appointment
shall be in the form Specified in Schedule “A”.
3.2  The Elections Officers so appointed by the Commissioner shall exercise all such powers referred to in these
Rules except those reserved for the Commissioner.
3.3  The Secretary of the Society shall furnish to the Commissioner the particulars of the Branches where the
elections are to be held three months prior to the expiry of their terms and the Commissioner may fix in advance the dates
before which nominations shall be called and the elections shall be held. The Commissioner shall have the power to very such
order.

Part II
DISQUALIFICATIONS
4.1  No member shall be nominated for election or be elected to a Branch Committee of  a Society if he : -
(a) is a person whose name does not appear in the V oters' List of the Branch ;
(b) is below the age of 18 years ;
(c) has within 3 immediately proceeding years been convicted of a criminal offence or has been sentenced to a
term of imprisonment exceeding three months or such sentence has been suspended ;
(d) has filed a declaration of insolvency or is adjudicated an insolvent ;
(e) is in default for three months or above in respect of any loan or any surcharge payable under Section 44(6)
of the Co-operative Law or any other dues payable in terms of any regulation made under Section 66 to this
society or to any either registered co-operative society;
(f) is registered as a trader with this Society or is carrying on business which conflicts with the interests of the
society;
(g) holds any office or place of profit under the society or receives any honorarium without the prior approval
of the Commissioner;
(h) is a paid employee of the Society or any other registered society ;
(i)  is directly or indirectly interested, save in his capacity as a member, in any contract made with the society or
in any sale or purchase made by the society, or in any contract or transaction of the society which concerns
the finances of the society and which is either subsisting or in the course of negotiation;
(j) is a person who, within the last 5 years has had, to vacate his office or employment in terms of Rules made
under the Co-operative Law;

NOMINATIONS
5.1  Nomination Paper Every candidate who stands for election to the Branch Committee shall be proposed by a
member of the Society and shall be seconded by another member of the Society. Each candidate, his proposer and seconder
shall be members of the Society whose names appear in the V oters’ List of the Branch to which elections are to be held. The
nomination of each candidate shall be in writing on the form specified in Schedule “B” of these Rules.
DECLARATION  BY  A  CANDIDATE, PROPOSER  AND  SECONDER
5.2  Each candidate shall attach to his nomination paper a declaration in the form  specified in Schedule “c” of these
Rules.
5.3  Each such Proposer and Seconder shall likewise make a declaration in the form specified in Schedule “C 1 ” of
these Rules.
RECEIVING    NOMINATION    PAPERS
6.1 On the day fixed for notifications for elections to a Branch Committee of a Society  the Chief Elections Officer/
Elections Officer; In-Charge of Zones/Assistant Elections Officer shall be present at the place fixed for receipt of such
nominations and shall receive such nomination papers as may be handed in.
6.2  Each nomination paper shall he prepared in quadruplicate in the form set out in  Schedule “B” and signed by the
Proposer and Seconder and the candidate and handed in by the candidate himself or his Proposer or Seconder together with
the declarations made under Schedule “C” and “Cl” between the hours 9.00 am. and 10.00 pm on the day fixed for such
nomination to . the Chief Elections Officer/Elections Officers In-Charge of Zones/Assistant Elections Officers.
6.3  On receipt of such nomination paper, the Chief Elections Officer/Elections Officer In-Charge of Zones/Assistant
Elections Officer shall verify that the candidate, the Proposer and Seconder are not disqualified under Rule 4. 1 of these Rules
and shall reject the nomination of the candidate who is so disqualified or whose nomination is not in conformity with Rule 5.1
or 5.2 or 5.3 of these Rules.
6.4  The Elections Chief Elections Officer/Elections Officers In-Charge of  Zones/Assistant Elections Officers shall
thereafter cause two copies of each Nomination Paper as is not rejected by him under Rule 6. 3 above, to be conspicuously
displayed at the place where those nominations are received.
RECEIVING  OF  OBJECTIONS
7.1  Any person whose name appears in the V oters’ list of the Branch in which elections are to be held may file
objections to any nomination. Such objections shall be in writing and shall be handed into the Chief Elections Officer/

ELECTIONS   PROCEDURE
8.1  Uncontested Elections
Where the number of candidates duly nominated to nine or less and more than 5, each candidate shall be deemed to
be elected and the Chief Elections Officer/Elections Officer In-Charge of the zone/Assistant Elections Officer shall issue a
statement in writing to the Secretary of the Society to that effect.
8.2  Contested Elections
Where the number of candidates duly nominated is more than nine, the Elections Chief Elections Officer/Elections
Officer In-Charge of zone/Assistant Elections Officer shall intimate to the Society in writing the names of each of the

ALLOCATION   OF   SYMBOLS   TO   CANDIDATES
9.1  The Chief Elections Officer/Elections Officer In-Charge of zone/Assistant Elections Officer shall use such
number of symbols as there are candidates and the symbols so selected shall be the symbols in serial order commencing from
1 as printed on the ballot paper.
For example:- if there are fifteen candidates, the symbols 1 to 15 shall be used. Each candidate shall be assigned a
symbol from among these symbols by the draw of lots. The Chief Elections Officer/Elections Officer In-Charge of zone/
Assistant Elections Officer shall maintain a record of the symbols assigned to each candidate.
BALLOT   PAPERS
10. 1. Each Ballot Paper shall be printed as specified in Schedule ‘D’ of these rules.
11.1 The Chief Elections Officer/Elections Officer In-Charge of zone/Assistant Elections Officer shall preside at the
elections.
11.2 Prior to the issue of Ballot Papers, the Chief Elections Officer/Elections Officer In-Charge of zone/Assistant
Elections Officer shall strike off such the symbols that have not been allocated to any of the candidates and shall affix the
Branch Seal on each Ballot Paper used and sign thereon.
11.3 Any person whose name appears in the V oters’ List of the Branch shall be entitled so a Ballot Paper on proof of
his identity by the production of his National Identity Card or valid Motor Driving License or valid Passport or valid Identity
card with the photograph issued by the employer or the Pension Identity Card or other similar document acceptable to Chief
Elections Officer/Elections Officer In-Charge of zone/Assistant Elections Officer.
11.4 Before a Ballot Paper is issued to a voter, the person issuing the Ballot Paper shall first satisfy himself that the
voter’s name appears in the V oters’ List.
11.5 As and when each voter is given a Ballot Paper his name in the V oters’ List shall be marked.
11.6 No voter shall be issued more than ballot paper
11.7 A voter shall be entitled to cast only one vote to each candidate of his choice and the maximum number of
candidates for whom he may vote shall be nine. If a voter votes for more than nine candidates, his Ballot Paper shall be
considered spoilt.
11.8 On receipt of a Ballot Paper each voter shall indicate his preference on the given ballot paper by placing a mark
in the cage against each of the symbols and/or numbers of his choice and shall deposit the ballot paper in a receptacle placed
within the view of the  Chief Elections Officer/Elections Officer In-Charge of zone/Assistant Elections Officer.
11.9. A voter, who is not able to mark a Ballot Paper due to any physical disability, may request the Chief Elections
Officer/Elections Officer In-Charge of zone! Assistant Elections Officer to mark the ballot paper on his behalf.

11.10. The Chief Elections Officer/Elections Officer In-Charge of zone/Assistant Elections Officer shall declare the

COUNTING
12.1. Immediately after the closure of the poll the Chief Elections Officer/Elections Officer In-Charge of zone/
Assistant Elections Officer with such assistance as is available will proceed to count the votes that are in the receptacle. For
this purpose all votes which are not rejected by him under Rule 12.2 and 12.3 below shall be counted with the aid of  score
sheets.
12.2. He shall reject any ballot paper which is marked for more than nine candidates.
12.3 Where in a ballot paper a mark is placed and it is not clear for which of two candidates the voter has voted, the
Chief Elections Officer/Elections Officer In-Charge of zone/Assistant Elections Officer shall have the power to reject such
vote and to accept the balance votes.
12.4 Where in a ballot paper a voter has made more than one mark in favour of a candidate, all such markings shall
constitute only one vote.
12.5. Where the Chief Elections Officer/Elections Officer In-Charge of zone/Assistant Elections Officer rejects a
ballot paper under Rule 12.2 and 12.5 such rejected ballot papers shall be bound together, counted and reconciled with his
ballot paper account.
ELECTION  RESULTS
13.1 On the completion of the count, each of the nine candidates who receive more votes than each of the remaining
candidate shall be declared elected as members of the branch committee.
In the event of an equality of votes among two or more candidates, the Chief Elections Officer/Elections Officer In-
Charge of zone/Assistant Elections Officer shall, where necessary, decide, by the drawing of Jots, the candidate or candidates
among them to be elected to the committee. However, such drawing of lots shall not be necessary where, even with an
equality of votes amongst some candidates, the required number of candidates can be declared elected as members to the
Committee.
13.2 The Elections Officer/Assistant Elections Officer shall communicate the results of the election in writing to the
Society specifying the names of candidates declared elected to the Committee. A copy of this communication shall be sent by
the Chief Elections Officer/Elections Officer In-Charge of zone/Assistant Elections Officer to the Assistant Commissioner of
Co-operative Development of the area.
13.3. The Secretary of the Society shall display conspicuously for a period not less than 10 days the names of the
elected members at the place of business and at the office of the particular branch.
14.1 The decision of the Chief Elections Officer/Elections Officer In-Charge of zone/Assistant Elections Officer on
any question arising in respect of the conduct of the poll,  counting of votes, or communicating the results of the poll shall
be final and conclusive.

INTERPRETATIONS
In these Rules :
19.1. ‘By-laws’ mean the registered by-laws of the society for the time being in force and include registered amendments
of the by-laws;
19.2 Except in Rules 7 (2) (a), 17 and 18 ‘Commissioner’ shall mean and shall include the Commissioner of Co-
operative Development or Deputy Commissioner of Co-operative Development or Senior Assistant Commissioner of Co-
operative Development or Assistant Commissioner of Co-operative Development or any other person duly authorized by
any of those above named,
19.3 In Rules 7 (2) (a), 17 and 18 the ‘Commissioner’ shall mean the North Western Provincial Commissioner /
Registrar of Co-operative Development;
19.4 ‘The Chief Elections Officer/Elections Officer In-Charge of zone/Assistant Elections Officer’ shall mean the
persons appointed by the Assistant Commissioner in charge of the division for the particular branch of society under these
Rules.
19.5 ‘V oter’ shall mean the person admitted to membership in ‘the Society from the area of operations of the branch
whose name is included in the voters’ list duly certified for-the purpose of elections to the said branch committee of the
Society.
19.6 ‘Co-operative Act’ shall mean the Co-operative Society Act or the Co-operative Society Statute for the time
being in force in the North Western Province.
20.  In the event of any inconsistence between the Sinhala and Tamil texts of these  Rules, the Sinhala text shall
prevail.
