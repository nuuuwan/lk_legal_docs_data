PUBLIC  SERVICE  COMMISSION
Delegation of powers by the Public Service Commission  in terms of Article 57(1) of the Constitution
BY virtue of the powers vested in terms of paragraph (1) of Article 57 of the Constitution of the Democratic Socialist
Republic of Sri Lanka, the Public Service Commission hereby delegates to the Public Officers set out in Column II of the
schedule hereto, the powers set out in the corresponding entry in Column III of the schedule hereto, in respect of the
category of officers set out in the corresponding entry in column I of the schedule hereto.
The delegation herein made is subject to the following conditions-
a) all such powers as are delegated herein shall be exercised and discharged in full compliance with such procedures,
rules and regulations as are applicable to the exercise and discharge of such powers.
b) in the event that a public officer referred to in column II of the schedule hereto, is either the complainant, or a
witness or a potential witness at any disciplinary inquiry in respect of a officer belonging to a category set out
in the corresponding item in column I of the schedule hereto, the powers delegated in respect of such officer,
shall ( be deemed to have been rescinded) and the Public Service Commission shall exercise the powers so
delegated in respect of such officer, as if no such delegation has been made in respect of such officer; and
The delegation of powers as herein contained shall be effective from the Third (3
rd) day of November, Two
Thousand and Fifteen (2015) and the delegation of powers published in Gazette Extraordinary No. 1733/52 dated 25
November, 2011 shall stand rescinded with effect from that date.
                                                          By order of the Public Service Commission,
