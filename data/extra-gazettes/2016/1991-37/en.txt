Notice under Section 84
NOTICE is hereby given in terms of Section 84 of the National Water Supply and Drainage Board Law No. 02 of 1974 that
the following tariffs will be charged with effect from the 01st day of December 2016, from all the consumers supplied

2012 in respect of the water tariffs with effect from the 01st day of October 2012 and applicable to all consumers who
are supplied with water by the National Water Supply and Drainage Board.

MISCELLANEOUS   CHARGES   AND   CONDITIONS
(1) New Service Connections:
The cost of providing a new service connection will be levied from the consumer.
(2) Customers who do not use water at all during a particular month are charged at minimum monthly service
charge.
(3) If a meter is found to be out of order or if it is removed for repairs or calibration, the consumption, of water
during the time that the meter is not available to record consumption, shall be calculated according to the
average rate of daily consumption obtained during any two successive readings immediately preceding the
removal of meter or the meter becoming defective.
(4) Testing of water meters at the request of consumers:
The fee for testing of water meters at the request of the consumers, shall be determined by the General Manager
of the National Water Supply and Drainage Board, based on costs incurred for the testing of such meter. If on
testing such meter it is proved that the meter had been over/under registering by more than 2% of the correct
consumption, this fee will be refunded.
 (5) Incentive for prompt Payment:
Consumers paying their water bills by cash within 14 working days from the date of issue of the bill will be
given a rebate of 1.5% on the value of the bill. The customers who pay water bills using credit cards / Debit
cards will not be eligible for this rebate. Arrangements are available for consumers to deposit money with the
Board in advance to meet the cost of water bills.
(6) Surcharge for delay in Payment:
Consumers are expected to pay bills, within a period of 14 days. If consumers fails to settle the water bill within
a period of 30 days from the date of issuing a bill, an additional charge of 2.5% per month on the balance
outstanding shall be made from the date the bill was issued.

(7) Disconnection of Service connections:
The General Manager of the National Water Supply and Drainage Board shall have the power to disconnect the
service connection of consumers, whose bills are in arrears for a period of more than 30 days.
(8) Re-Connection Fee:
The fee for re-connecting the supply, after the service has been disconnected, shall be determined by the General
Manager of the National Water Supply and Drainage Board, based on the costs incurred for such re-connection.
(9) Violation of Regulations:
If any regulation, under which the water supply has been provided, is violated by any consumer, action will be
taken under the relevant provisions of the National Water Supply and Drainage Board Law No. 02 of 1974, the
National Water Supply and Drainage Board (Amendment) Act, No. 13 of 1992 or any other subsequent
amendment to the legislation.
