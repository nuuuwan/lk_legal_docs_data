Regulations
1. These  regulations may be cited as the charges applicable for the usage of all aerodromes in Sri Lanka except
Bandaranaike International Airport (BIA), Mattala Rajapaksa International Airport (MRIA) and Ratmalana Air-
port (RMA) and shall come into effect on 01st of July.
2. These Regulations shall amend the Regulations published in the Extraordinary Gazette Notification
No. 1262/5 dated 12th November 2002.
3. Every aircraft except those operated by the Armed Forces of Sri Lanka shall pay charges mentioned in para-
graph 6 here-under whenever applicable.
4. Notwithstanding the provisions of paragraph 3 above, aircraft operated by the Armed Forces and aircraft in the
use of the Government of Sri Lanka, where such aircraft are engaged in the carriage of passengers and cargo
for hire or for reward shall pay charges mentioned in paragraph 6 whenever applicable.

6. Charges applicable
6.1 Landing Charges :
Weight of A/C New Landing Charges
Upto 10,000 Kg Rs. 200.00 per 1,000 kg
10,000 Kg to 20,000 Kg Rs. 2,000.00 plus Rs. 300.00 per 1,000 Kg
in excess of 10,000 Kg
Over 20,000 Kg Rs. 5,000.00 plus 500.00 per 1,000 Kg
in excess of 20,000 Kg
* All training flights shall be charged fifty per centum (50%) of above rates
6.2 Housing Charges :
Fifty per centum (50%) of the landing charge for each complete period of twenty four (24) hours and
part thereof.
6.3 Parking Charges :
Ten per centum (10%) of the Landing Charges for each period of three (03) hours or part thereof, in
excess of the first three (03) hours of parking.
Note :
i) For calculting free parking period, irrespective of actual time taken, a standard time of 15 minues
of taxing from parking shed to take-off point shall be added.
ii) For calculating chargeable parking time, part of an hour should be rounded off to the nearest hour.
Charges of each period of parking are also rounded off to nearest Rupee.
iii) Landing Charges for Cargo Operations are same as for Passenger Operation.
iv) In all cases, total weight of the aircraft is calculated to the nearest thousand Kg and charges to be
rounded off to the nearest Rupee.
