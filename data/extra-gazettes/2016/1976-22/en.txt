Co-operative Employees Commission Statute No. 1 of 2016 (Amendment) of Uva Provincial
Council
This Statute is imposed to amend the Uva Provincial Cooperative Employees Commission Statute
No. 04 of 2010, and,
Incorporated by the Uva Provincial Council, by virtue of the powers vested in the Provincial
Councils by the 13th Amendment of the Constitution of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri
Lanka.
01. This Statute may be cited as the “Uva Provincial Council Co-operative Employees
Commission Statute No. 1 of 2016  (amended) and shall come into operation on such
date as the Governor of the province may approve or appoint, unless the context requires
otherwise.

02. Uva Provincial Cooperative Employees Commission Statute No. 04 of 2010
(hereinafter referred to as the “main statute”) is hereby amended by the following
substitution for sub-sections (1) & (3) of the Section 5 of the said statute.
(1) The Commission shall be consisting of five (05) members appointed by the
Minister-in-charge of Co-operative Affairs of Uva Provincial Council, referred
to as the “Minister” hereinafter.
(3) Three (03) of the members of the Co-operative Employees Commission shall
be appointed from the retired government officials who were in a staff grade
position, and one of the three should be a person with a sound knowledge in the
Co-operative field.
and
03. Sub-sections (2), (3) and (4) of Section 7 of the Uva Provincial Cooperative Employees
Commission Statute No. 04 of 2010 is hereby replaced by the following substitutions.
(2) Three (3) members shall constitute the quorum for a meeting of the commission.
(3) If at any meeting only four (04) members of the commission are present and
such members are divided in opinion as to the decision to be taken on any question,
such question shall be referred to a meeting at which the all five (05) members
of the commission are present.
(4) If at any meeting, at which the five (05) members of the commission are present,
and the members of the commission are divided in opinion as the decision to be
given on any question, such question shall be decided according to the opinion
of the majority of the members, but if the members are equally divided, then, the
Chairman shall have a casting vote.
In the event of any inconsistency between the Sinhala and Tamil text of this statute, the Sinhala text
shall prevail.
