CONSTITUTION   OF  THE  DEMOCRATIC   SOCIALIST  REPUBLIC  OF  SRI   LANKA
RULES made by the National Police Commission by virtue of the powers vested in it under Paragraph (4) of Article 155K. of
the Constitution.
S. T. HETTIGE,
Chairman,
National Police Commission.
Colombo 01,
27th October 2016.
Rules
01. These Rules may be cited as National Police Commission Rules (Appeal Procedure) ; 2016.
02. Any Police Officer who is aggrieved by an order relating to promotion, transfer or any order on a disciplinary
matter or a dismissal made by a Committee appointed by the Commission or the Inspector General of Police
or Police Officer appointed respectively under Articles 155H. and 155J. in respect of himself/herself may appeal
in writing to the National Police Commission against such order.
03. There shall be only one appeal to the Commission.
04. Every appeal shall be addressed to the Secretary to the Commission and sent by registered post to him or
handed over to the Office of the National Police Commission and acknowledgement shall be obtained.
05.
(a) Every appeal shall be made within Six Months from the date of the receipt by the aggrieved party of such
an Order made by a Committee, appointed by the Commissioner or the Inspector General of Police or
the Police Officer.

(b) If there is a delay beyond Six Months the National Police Commission may consider such cases purely
on the merits of the facts and circumstances of the cases. In such instance, National Police Commission
shall specifically state the fact that the appeal has not been made within the stipulated Six Months time
and it was considered only on exceptional circumstances of the case.
06. (a) In hearing the appeal, the National Police Commission may-
I. Call for reports, files or any other relevant documents from the Inspector General of Police, as the case
may be and
II. Summon the appellant
It shall be the duty of the Inspector General of Police or the Police Officer to comply with such requests
of calling for files and any other relevant documents not later than one month from the date of such
request as the case may be under this paragraph.
07. An appeal which is not filed in the manner specified under obove rules shall not be entertained by the National
Police Commission.
08. The decision of the Commission in respect of any appeal shall be conveyed in writing to the aggrieved officer
within thirty days from the date of the decision of the Commission.
09. The “Commission” shall mean the “National Police Commission” established under Article 155 of the
Constitution.
10. The National Police Commission Rules (Appeals Procedure), 2007 published in the Gazette Extraordinary
No. 1495/4 of Monday April 30, 2007 are hereby rescinded.
