THE   CONSTITUTION  OF  THE  DEMOCRATIC  SOCIALIST  REPUBLIC  OF  SRI  LANKA
Delegation of Powers by the National Police Commission in terms of Article 155J
BY virtue of the powers vested in the National Police Commission in terms of Article 155J of the Constitution of the
Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka, the said National Police Commission does hereby -
1. Delegate to Inspector General of Police and other Police Officers in terms of Article 155J specified in Column III of
the Schedule hereto, its power of appointment, promotion, transfer, disciplinary control and dismissal as are specified in
Column II of the Schedule hereto, in respect of the categories of Police Officers as are specified in Column I of the
Schedule hereto.
Provided that in the event of any question arising in respect of any delegation or as to the implementation of delegation
made by the National Police Commission so made, the decision of the National Police Commission shall be final and
conclusive.
Provided further that, if any of the Police Officer specified in Column III is the complainant or a witness or a potential
witness at any disciplinary inquiry against a police officer specified in Column I, the National Police Commission shall
notwithstanding any delegation which may have been made by it, continue to exercise its power of appointment, promotion,
transfer, disciplinary control and dismissal as are specified in Column II of the Schedule hereto as if no delegation in
respect thereof had been made.
2. Specify that in the exercise of the powers herein delegated, Inspector General of Police and the other police officers
to whom such powers are delegated shall at all times comply with the Procedural Rules, in respect of appointment,
promotion and transfer of police officers, made by the National Police Commission in terms of Article 155G of the
Constitution and also with such conditions and any Rules, Regulations and other Procedures which the said Commission
may from time to time determine :
