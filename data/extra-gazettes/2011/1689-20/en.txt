EXCISE  ORDINACE  NOTICE
Excise Notification No. 935
BY virtue of the powers vested in me by section 25 (Chapter 52) of the Excise Ordinance, I Mahinda Rajapaksa, Minister of
Finance and Planning, do by this notification declare that a special approval for serving Wine, Beer and all liquids containing
alcohol which have been produced according to acceptable standards, shall be granted by the Commissioner General of
Excise, under the authority vested in him by section 7(a) of the Excise Ordinance, (chapter 52), to Hotels approved by the Sri
Lanka Tourism Development Authority and which are under continuous supervision of Sri Lanka Tourism Development
Authority in order to maintain the international standards and compliance by such Hotels.
1. On a certificate issued by the Director General of Tourism Development Authority confirming the followings,
(i) In respect of Hotels established within Municipal area limits, the investment should not be less than US
Dollars 4 million or equivalent amount of Sri Lankan Rupees, and in respect of Hotels established outside
Municipal area limits, the investment should not be less than US Dollars 2 million or equivalent amount of
Sri Lankan Rupees;
(ii) The Hotel should have been classified by the Sri Lanka Tourism Development Authority as a three star or
above class Hotel or a Boutique Villa Hotel or Boutique Villa Hotel or a Heritage Hotel;
(iii) In respect of a Hotel established as a Boutique Hotel or a Boutique Villa Hotel or a Heritage Hotel or a Hotel
established under same standards within Municipal area limits, the number of rooms should not be less than
15, and in respect of Hotels established outside Municipal area limits, the number of rooms should be
equivalent to or more than 07.
and
2. (i) On a payment of prescribed Authorization fees described In Part II of this notification.
(ii) the approval is valid for a period of three calendar years.
(iii) The approval is subject to conditions and restrictions set out in Part 1 of this notification.

PART I
Rules
1. These rules shall apply to serve Wine, Beer and all liquids containing alcohol which have been produced
according to acceptable standards, under section 18 of the Excise Ordinance (chapter 52).
2. Hotel - shall mean all accommodation approved by Sri Lanka Tourism Development Authority where a sum of over
USD 4 million have being invested in respect of Municipal areas and over US Dollars 2 million have being invested in other
areas, and classified as a three star or above class Hotel, Boutique Hotel, Boutique villa or Heritage Hotel.
3. (i) All alcoholic beverages should be purchased from an authorized dealer. The Hotel shall keep an account of
alcohol brought and issued in the form prescribed by the Commissioner General of Excise.
(ii) The approval to serve alcoholic beverages within the premises will be given  for the Hotel. Any transfer of
ownership of the Hotel, or substantial change of shareholding shall not be affected without the sanction of
Sri Lanka Tourism Development Authority and Commissioner General of Excise. Such transfers should be
affected if approved by the Sri Lanka Tourism Development Authority and Commissioner General of Excise.
Cost of such a transfer shall be Rs. One million.
(iii) The approval is valid for alcoholic beverages issued for the consumption on the premises.
(iv) Approval will be issued for a period of three consecutive financial years. In the case of fresh approval being
issued during a financial year, such approval will be issued for the remaining period of that year and for two
further consecutive financial years. If the approval is to be renewed after three calendar years, a request
should be forwarded to Commissioner General of Excise, with the recommendation of Sri Lanka Tourism
Development Authority at least 45 days prior to the expiry of such approval.
(v) Alechclic beverages shoud not be served on all full moon poya days.
(vi) Approval is similar to FL 7 license in the Excise Notification 902. No additional fees will be levied for serve
liquor in the Hotel premises, other than fees specified in Part II.
(vii) Kitchen, Toilets, fittings and fixtures and surrounding environment should be maintained at high level.
(viii) Sri Lanka Tourism Development Authority should submit a certificate to the Commissioner General of Excise
at the end of each year, that the relevant Hotel was being maintained to the standards prescribed by them.
(ix) Cancellation of the approval of Sri Lanka Tourism Development Authority due to any violation of conditions
will result in automatic cancellation of the approval for alcoholic beverages.
(x) Failure to comply with any of conditions impose by Sri Lanka Tourism Development Authority and  ‘or
Excise Commissioner General may result in the authorization being cancelled and’or subject to Technical
Crime Reports being submitted in accordance with the provisions of the Excise Ordinance. Alternation to the
Authorization can be made only by the Commissioner General of Excise.
