Statute of Preventing Public Health Nuisances, No. 03 of  2012
 I, Angage Jagath Thilakasiri Perera, Hon. Minister of Health, Indigenous Medicine, Social Welfare & Women Affairs,
Probation & Childcare and Council Affairs of Western Province Provincial Council do hereby impose following regulations
which should be read along with Clause 21 of the Statute of Preventing Public Health Nuisances, No. 03 of 2012. These
regulations shall come into effect from the date on which they are published on the Gazette.

RULES
1. The rules shall be cited as the Rules of Preventing Public Health Nuisances, No. 03 of  2012
2. The officers mentioned below who are nominated under Clause 26 of this Statute, shall be appointed as the
authorized officers under this statute and they shall perform the duties and tasks subject to the supervision of
the competent authority :
(a) Colombo, Gampaha, and  Kalutara Regional Directors of Health Service of the Provincial Department of
Health Services in the Western Province.
(b) Medical Officers of Health.
(c) Chief Medical Officers of Health, Additional Medical officers of Health and all other Medical Officers of
Health serving in Municipal Councils.

3. (1) A card should be introduced to every premises with the name of "Premises Card for Preventing Public
Health Nuisances" in accordance with the format indicated in Schedule 1, to be used by the —competent
authority or a representative of the competent authority who enters any premises as per Clause 15 of this
Statute, to inspect any nuisance or nuisances mentioning his entering into the premises or the inspection
he conducts.
(2) The said card shall be numbered in accordance with the number of the household given by the voters’
register. If any household has not received such a number, the card shall be issued under a temporary
number.
(3) The owner/ occupant or caretaker of the premises shall be bound to ensure the safe custody of the card, to
submit it when requested by the competent authority or a representative of the competent authority or any
authorized officer and to respect the orders imposed by the card.
(4) The card shall not be misplaced, disfigured or destroyed and legal actions shall be taken against any
owner/ occupant or caretakers who do so.
4. The format of the paper containing the amount of the spot fine which is imposed by the authorized officer to the
owner/ occupant or caretaker of the premises with his/her consent,  in any case where the competent authority
or authorized officer identifies that the orders of the Sub Clause (1) of Clause 5 of this statute are violated by
keeping open tins, bottles, boxes, coconut shells, coconut husks, tire or any other material or vessel containing
water with mosquito larvae, shall be in line with the format indicated in Schedule 2. The said format shall be
perfected in three copies and the original and second copies shall be handed over to the person who is responsible
for the offence.  The third copy shall be kept under the custody of the authorized officer and the person who is
responsible for the offence shall submit the second copy to the relevant Divisional Secretariat to pay the relevant
fine.
5. The notice to be issued to the owner/occupant or caretaker by the competent authority or authorized officer
under Clause 4(1) of this Statute where an offence is revealed violating Clause 2 of this statute, shall be in line
with the format indicated in Schedule 3. This format shall be completed in two copies and the first copy shall be
handed over to the person who is responsible for the offence. The second copy shall be kept in the custody of
the authorized officer.
6. The notice to be issued to the owner/occupant or caretaker by the competent authority or authorized officer
under Clause 7 of this Statute where an offence is revealed violating Clause 5 of this statute, shall be in line with
the format indicated in Schedule 4. This format shall be completed in two copies and the first copy shall be given
to the person who is responsible for the offence. The second copy shall be kept in the custody of the authorized
officer.
7. The spot fine mentioned in Sub Clause 6(1) of this Statute shall be Rs.2000/-. This amount can be revised on
timely requirements.

8. (1) An amount which does not exceed 25% of the fine recovered from a person after making guilty by a court
on an offence fallen under this statute can be awarded to the authorized officer who takes action to prove
the offence committed by the person.
(2) An amount equivalent to 10% of the spot fine indicated in Sub Clause 1, Clause 6 of this Statute can be
awarded to the officer who takes action to prove the offence committed by the person.
(3) The incentive from the spot fine shall be paid to the relevant officer by the Divisional Secretariat of the
Divisional Secretary’s Division where the relevant premises are situated.
9. (1) At the instances where an owner, occupant, caretaker of a certain premises or any authority of an institution
indicated under Sub Clause 14 (1), to whom a written notice has been issued under Clauses 4 and 7 of this
statute, neglects to follow the orders issued, an institution or company shall be entrusted to take actions
or make orders to remove the nuisance after determining either by the competent authority or authorized
officer that the public health nuisance at a certain premises shall be removed as per Sub Clause 1 of
Section 9.
(2) The fee for the removal of public health nuisance to be paid to a person institution or company to whom the
tasks to be performed at a certain premises are entrusted, shall be in accordance with the fee indicated in
Schedule 5. Such amount of fees shall be revised from time to time by the competent authority in accordance
with the market value.
(3) Any expense borne by a person institution or company for the removal of public health nuisance, to whom
such tasks or steps are entrusted with regard to a certain premises shall be paid by the competent authority
to such person institution or company after making proper estimate on the task or the step performed by
the above mentioned parties.
(4) The amount borne by the competent authority for the removal of public health nuisance revealed at the
premises shall be paid to the competent authority by the owner/occupant or caretaker of the premises. The
specimen of the certificate containing the fee to be paid, which is prepared either by the competent
authority or officer authorized by the competent authority shall be in accordance with the specimen
attached in Schedule 6. The said specimen shall be prepared by the officer who is concerned to remove
public health nuisance.
10. The specimen of the certificate prepared by the competent authority for the submission of particulars of the fine
ordered under sub clause 9 (2) of the Statute to the courts under Sub Clause 9 (3) shall be in accordance with the
specimen attached in Schedule 7

UBLIC HEALTH NUISANCES
1. Discharging waste water into a public waterway.
2. Throwing garbage into drains or road or keeping garbage bins which are not well covered at public places.
3. Washing vehicles in such a manner that it makes a road, street, foot path untidy.
4. Causing animals to pass faeces on any entry road or public places.
5. Keeping any premises unclean, allowing waste water or toilet waste to overflow or leak and allowing stagnated
water to stagnate further.
6. Keeping loathsome drains that emits bad smell.
7. Rearing cattle, goats and swine in such a manner that is a nuisance to public health.
8. Leaving a building parapet wall in a dilapidated state.
9. Keeping factory in such a manner that it is detrimental to public health
10. Keeping any article, material or liquid in such a manner that it is detrimental to health
