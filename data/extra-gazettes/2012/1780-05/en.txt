Rules made and published by the North Western Province Passenger Transport Servicers Authority in terms of
Section 17(f) of North Western Province Passenger Transport Services Statute No. 04 of 1995.
01. These Rules shall be known as the rules to ensure the safety and comfort of the passengers, and shall come into
effect from 01.09.2012.
PART I
02. The drivers and conductors who serve in every bus operated on Passenger Transport Service under a permit or
any other such authority issued by the Road Passenger Transport Services Authority of the North Western Province or shall,
whilst on duty in the bus be always attired in a uniform stipulated by that Authority.
03. The uniform worn by the dirvers and conductors shall be the uniform indicated in the First Schedule below and
the official badge stipulated by the Authority shall always be worn with that uniform.
04. It is mandatory that the badge to be worn by the drivers and conductors on their uniform should be prepared as
setout in the Schedule 2 hereunder.

05. Every time the driver or the conductor wears the uniform (shirt) stipulated in these Rules to be worn for the upper
part of the body, he should wear a pair of trousers or a sarong extending down to the ankle.
06. The period said to be on duty includes the entire period of time commencing from the time of the first trip of the
relevant bus on the particular day, up to the time of the conclusion of trips.
07. In case the uniform and the official badge worn are different from or contrary to those stipulated by the
Authority, the person concerned shall be considered as not wearing the uniform and the badge.
08. Where at the time of obtaining trips for  a particular bus the dirver and the conductor of that bus are not wearing
the prescribed uniform and the badge, the time keeper shall not allocate turns or the bus concerned, and the matter should be
brought to the notice of the relevant officer in charge.
09. The responsibility of ascertaining whether drivers and conductors are wearing the uniform and the badge
stipulated by the Authority rests with the permit holder of the bus.
10. The permit holders of passenger transport services who employ drivers and conductors who do not wear the
uniform and the badge stipulated by the Authority, shall be deemed to contravene these Rules.
11. The conductors and drivers shall keep their uniform in a clean condition and also follow the instructions given
by the Authority from time to time in relation to the uniform and the badge.
12. (i) The drivers and conductors who act in breach of the Rules made in relation to the uniforms and the badge,
commit an offence, and are liable to a service charge of —
(1) Rs. 250 on the first instance ;
(2) Rs. 500 on the second instance ; and
(3) Rs. 1,000 on the third instance.
(ii) The registration of the conductors and drivers who act in contravention of the Rules after the third instance,
stand cancelled and such cancellation shall be intimated to the driver and the conductor and driver’s identity card or the
conductors license shall be returned to the Authority by the driver or the conductor concerned, within 07 days.
13. (i) The buses in which the drivers or conductors who contravene the Rules relating to uniforms and badges after
the third instance serve, shall be blacklisted and such black listing on more than two instances will result in suspension or
cancellation of the route permit of such bus.
(ii) No Officer of the Authority shall, without seeking instructions from the Chairman of the Authority shall, assign
turns or issue monthly running charts (log sheets) to any bus blacklisted for contravening the Rules relating to uniforms and
badges.
