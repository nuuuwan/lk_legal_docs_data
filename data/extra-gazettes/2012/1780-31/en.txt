DR, RAJITHA SENARATNA,
Minister of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources Development.
Colombo,
08th  October  2012.
Regulations
1. These regulations may be cited as the  Fisheries Management (Collection of dead shells)  Regulations, 2012.
2. No Person shall take, remove, collect, harvest or transport dead shells Sri Lanka Waters or from the area adjacent
to the sea for commercial purposes except under the authority of a valid licence issued in that  behalf by the Director General.
3.  No person shall engage in mechanized dredging for the purpose of taking, removing, collecting or harvesting of
dead shells.
4. Every application for a licence referred to in Regulation 2 shall be made to the Director General or to a  Licensing
Officer appointed by the Director General substantially in the Form specified in Schedule I hereto.
5. Every licence issued under Regulation 2 shall be -
(a) in the Form specified in Schedule II hereto and unless it is cancelled earlier, shall be in force for a period of
six months from the date of issue : and ;
(b) Subject to such terms and conditions as may be specified in such licence.
6. No person shall possess or transport any dead shells except under the authority of a valid licence issued by the
Director- General.

7. Every application for a licence referred to in Regulation 6 shall be-
(a) made to the Director - General or to the Licencing Officer appointed by the Director - General, substantially
in the Form specified in Schedule III hereto ; and
(b) Accompanied by a  fee calculated at the reate of five hundred rupees (Rs. 500.00) per thousand kilogram of
dead shells.
8. Every licence issued under regulation 6 shall be-
(a) in the Form specified in Schedule IV hereto  and  unless it is cancelled earlier shall be in force for  a period
of six months from the date  of issue ; and
(b) subject to such terms and conditions as may be specified insuch licenc ;
9. No. Person shall possess, sell, exhibit for sale or transport any dead shells except under the authority of a valid
licence for ornamental industry issued by the Director - General.
10. Every application for a licence Referred to in Regulation 9 shall be -
(a) made to the Director - General or to the Licencing Officer appointed by the Director - General, substantially
in the Form specified in Schedule V hereto ; and
(b) be accompanied by a fee of rupees two hundred and fifty (Rs. 250.00).
11. Every licence issued under Regulation 9 shall be -
(a) in the Form specified in Schedule VI  hereto and unless it is cancelled earlier, shall be in force for a period
of six months from the date of issue : and ;
(b) subject to such terms and conditions as may be specified in such licence.
12. The Director - General may determine the number of licences that may be issued having regard to the sustainability
of the resources and the recommendations made by the Committee.
13. A Licence may be cancelled by the Director - General or the Licencing Officer appointed by the Director - General,
where the Licence Holder has contravened -
(a) the provisions of the Fisheries and Aquatic Resources Act, No. 2 of 1996 or any regulation made
thereunder ; or
(b) any term or condition contained in such licence .
Provided however, prior  to the cancellation of such licence the Director - General or the Licencing Officer appointed
by the Director -General, shall afford an opportunity to the Licence Holder to be heard.
14. In these regulations -
“Committee” means the committee appointed by the Director - General to review the matters relating to the
availability of the resources ;
“dead shells” means the dead shells of phylum molluscam (excluding Turbinella phyrum) ;
