CENTRAL  PROVINCE  PROVINCIAL  COUNCIL
By Laws of Ududumbara Pradeshiya Sabha
IT, is hereby notified that I, Sarath Ekanayake, as the Minister in charge of the subject of Local Government in the Central
Province, by virute of powers vested in me under Sub-Section (1) of the Section 123 of Pradeshiya Sabha Act, No. 15 of 1987
to be read with Sub Section (1) of Sub-Section 2 of the Provincial Council Act, No. 12 of 1989 (Consequential Provisions) has
approved the under mentioned By-Laws framed by Ududumbara Pradeshiya Sabha, according to power vested in the said
Pradeshiya Sabha under paragraph (4) Sub-Section VII of Section 126 of the said Pradeshiya Sabha Act, No. 15 of 1987 to be
read with Subsection 1 of Section 122 of the said Act, and the Ududumbara Pradeshiya Sabha authority area from the date of
publication of this notificationi in the Government Gazette.
Further, the approved By-Law No. 06 framed for the purpose of parking motor vehicles as set out in the approved
By-Laws of the Government Gazette Extraordinary, No. 520/7 of 23.08.1988 complied by the Minister of Local Government,
Housing and Constructions is rescinded from the date of coming into operation of the aforesaid By-Laws within the
Ududumbara Pradeshiya Sabha authority area from the date of publication of this notice in Government Gazette.

BY-LAWS  REGARDING  TO  PARKING  OF  HIRING  MOTOR  VEHICLES
-UDUDUMBARA  PRADESHIYA  SABHA
1. These By-Laws are framed in terms of the powers vested by Section 122 and 126 (vii) (hi) of the Pradeshiya Sabha
Act, No. 15 of 1987.
2. These By-Laws are known as By-Laws to control, regulate supervise, and charge of hiring motor vehicles, within
the administrative limits of Ududumbara Pradeshiya Sabha.

3. These By-Laws approved by the Minister, will be effective from the date of publication in the Gazette of Democratic
Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka.
4. No Hiring vehicle shall be parked in any place, other than the places denoted in the first Schedule and approved
by the Council and fixed by the Chairman such places may be dicided by means of a resolution of the Council.
5. For parking hiring vehicles in the places reserved in terms of By-Laws III within the administrative limits of
Ududumbara Pradeshiya Sabha, a fee in terms of Schedule Two, Shall be paid monthly to the Pradeshiya Sabha. Such fees
should be fixed from time to time, by resolution of the Council.
6. No person shall park a hiring vehicle, in any place which is reserved as a parking place, in the administrative area
of the Pradeshiya Sabha (with amendments) without a permit issued by the Chairman of the Pradeshiya Sabha of any other
officer duly authorized by him and it is effective from 5.00 a.m. to 7.00 p.m.
7. A permit issued under these By-Laws for any month will lapse on last day of the month, in which it is issued unless
it is earlier cancelled by the Chairman or any other officer who has been authorized by him.
8. An application form as per third Schedule for a permit shall be submitted to the Chairman. Application form can be
obtained from the office of the Ududumbara Pradeshiya Sabha, and all the such applications.
9. The owner or the driver of any hiring vehicles parked at a common parking place, shall forward the permit when
called upon to do so for inspection by a police officer or traffic warden or any person duly authorized by the chairman.
10. Before first week of every month, a permit to park hiring vehicles, shall be obtained at a time only one permit
which is valid for a month will be issued.
11. Except the Motor Vehicles that are presently registered with the Council any other Motor Vehicle is to be parked
in a common parking place. Must be made an application to the Council and a permit must be obtained before parking.
12. Any Motor Vehicle ceased to park in a parking place or temporary ceased to park for more than two weeks due
to repairs the office must be informed if such information is not given the relevant monthly fees must be paid.
13. No Motor V ehicle shall be repaired at the hiring car stand and vehicles shall be parked without obstructing the
other vehicles.
14. No person shall wash or caused to be wasted a vehicle parked in any common parking place.
15. No person shall behave while being drunk in a common parking place or in a hiring vehicle parked in a common
parking place or behave in a disorderly manner to be a hindrance to others.
16. Any person who contravene any of these By-Laws from 1 to 15 if convicted by a competent court of jurisdiction
is liable to a fine not exceeding Rs. 750 and in the event of continued contravention the Chairman or any other officer duly
authorized by him by giving a written notice regarding the continued contravention, he is liable to a further commission or the
offence, fine should be credited to the Pradeshiya Sabha Council fund.
17. In addition to the fine, ordered by a competent court of jurisdiction, any person who is convicted for more than
twice for contravention of these By-Laws, the permit issued to him is liable to be cancelled and no compensation can be
claimed by the permit holder.
