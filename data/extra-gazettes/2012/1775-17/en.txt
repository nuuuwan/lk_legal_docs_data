PART  II
The minimum monthly rate of wages for time work applicable to workers of the below mentioned classes of the
mechanized and non-mechanized factories of the Biscuit and Confectionary  (Including Chocolate Manufacturing)
Manufacturing Trade shall be the rates set out in the table I and II respectively.
Classes of workers
Class ‘A’ workers (Skilled)
(a) Biscuit Manufacturing :
Workers  engaged in the following.–  Operations connected with the mixing of dough including the cleaning of
mixing tubs and containers, weighing and measuring of ingredients, adding of  chemicals, essences and colouring and
feeding the mixers ; Work connected with the cutting machine ; Operations connected with cutting and embossing the baking
of biscuit including the work of oven loaders and oven helpers, Operation connected with the stacking machine ;
(b) Confectionery Manufacturing :
Workers engaged in the following.–  Boiling of ingredients : Work connected with the transferring of “batch” to and
from “hot tables” ; Work connected with the transferring of “batch” to and from “cool-tables” ; Operations connected with
the “batch feeder”, Work connected with the emptying of “batch” to trays ; Operations connected with “tableting machine”,
Operations connected with the “centre manufacturing machines” ; Work connected with the oscillating pans including the
adding of essences, sugar and colouring materials ; Operations with the sifting machine ; cleaning and roasting  of
cocoa beans ; Emptying roasted cocoa beans into “refitting hull” ; Work connected with the “tempering machine” and the
“depositer” ; Work connected with the “vibrating” and the “cooling tunnel”.
Class ‘B’ workers (semi-Skilled)
(a) Biscuit Manufacturing :
Workers engaged in the following.–   Work connected with the receipt and storage of flour and sugar margarine,
vegetable fat, glucose, lecine, essences, cocoa powder, cheese, malt, milk powder, chemicals, butter, ammonia, baking
powder, eggs and other ingredients ; Work connected with loading and unloading, carrying and conveying of ingredients ;
Work connected with flour sifting spreading rolling and flattening of mixed dough on cutting table work connected
with carrying stacking loading and unloading of buscuit tins and packets ; pocketing heat sealing, belling packing and
pasting of cartoons ;
(b) Confectionary Manufacturing :
Workers engaged in the following. –  Work connected with the receipt and storage of ingredients, including
glucose, sugar essences, coco beans, milk powder, vegetable butter, gelatin, chemicals and flavors ; Weighing, measuring
and mixing of ingredients ; weighing and packing of toffees, chocolates and sweets, collection of sweets in trays and drying
sweets in the heating room, the adding of gelatin and colouring material, Collection of jujubes in trays, weighing and packing
of jujubes rapping, I belling and packing of sweets and chocolates ; and any other operations or activities connected with or
incidental to the confectionery manufacturing.
Class ‘C’ workers (unskilled).
(a) Biscuit Manufacturing :
Workers engaged in the following  - Operations connected with the breaking of dough ; Carrying of baked biscuit
to and from the cooling rack ; washing and cleaning of trays and containers ; Collecting and arranging cut biscuit ; and any
other operations connected with or incidental to Biscuit Manufacturing.
