Regulations
Tea Small Holdings Development Authority (Planting of Tea) Subsidy Regulations 1989, published in Gazette Ex-
traordinary No. 576/18 of September 22, 1989, and as last amended by Regulation published in Gazette Extraordinary No.
1209/9 of November 5, 2001 and by Regulation published in Gazette Extraordinary No. 1701/1 of April 11, 2011 are hereby
further amended as follows:
1. by the substitution for regulation 3 thereof, of the following regulations ;
“3(1) the amount of subsidy payable for the entirety or any specified part of a tea small holding or other
tea holding suitable for planting vegetatively propagated tea plants on a permit issued with effect from January 1,
2012 shall be Rs. 1,50,000 in respect of each hectare in the area to be planted and may be paid in the following
manner :-
(a) the first instalment of Rs. 50,000 per hectare may be paid for the eradication of the existing tea plants
and other vegetation and adoption of necessary soil conservation measures in the area to be planted
with tea ;
(b) the second instalment of Rs. 15,000 per hectare may be paid after six (6) months for growing grass for the
rehabilitation of the soil in the area to be planted with tea ;
(c) the third instalment of Rs. 15,000 per hectare may be paid after eighteen (18) months for growing grass
for the rehabilitation of the soil in the area to be planted with tea ;
(d) the fourth instalment of Rs. 70,000 per hectare may be paid for the completion of the planting of  tea.

(2) The amount of subsidy payable in respect of a permit issued for planting vegetatively propagated tea

2. by the substitution for regulation 4 thereof, of the following regulation :
“4 The amount of subsidy payable for replanting, the entirety or any specified part of a tea small holding or
other tea holding with vegetatively propagated tea plants, where the land is prepared in accordance with the
requirements laid down by the Authority, shall be Rs. 3,00,000 in respect of each hectare of the area to be replanted
and may be paid in five instalments in the following manner :-
(a) the first instalment of Rs. 80,000 per hectare may be paid for eradication of the existing tea plants and
other vegetation and adoption of necessary soil conservation measures in the area to be replanted with
tea ;
(b) the second instalment of Rs. 30,000 per hectare may be paid after six (6) months of growing grass for the
rehabilitation of the soil in the area to be replanted with tea ;
(c) the third instalment of Rs. 45,000 per hectare may be paid after eighteen (18) months of growing grass
for the rehabilitation of the soil in the area to be replanted with tea ;
(d) the fourth instalment of Rs. 1,15,000 per hectare may be paid on the completion of the replanting ;
(e) the fifth instalment of Rs. 30,000 per hectare for the satisfactory maintenance of the replanted tea plants
for a period of one year.”
3. by the substitution for regulation 5 thereof, of the following regulation :
“5 (1) The instalment of subsidy payable in respect of the permits to be issued for replanting of tea small
holdings with effect from January 1, 2012 may be paid at the rates specified in regulation 4.
(2) The amount of subsidy payable in respect of permits issued for replanting of tea small holdings with
vegetatively propagated tea plants prior to January 1, 2012 and the validity of which has not expired shall be the
same as specified at the time of issue of such permit and may be paid in the manner specified therein.”
4. by the substitution for regulation 6 thereof, of the following regulation :
“6. In computing the amount of subsidy payable where the area to be planted or replanted is less than one
hectare in extent or where such area includes a part which is less than one hectare, the rates specified in paragraph
(1) of regulation  3 and regulation 4, as the case may be, as are applicable to the extent or part of the area, shall apply
in the proportion that such extent or part of the area bears to one hectare.”
5. by the substitution for regulation 8 thereof, of the following regulation :
“8. The instalment of subsidy payable under regulation 3 or regulation 4 or regulation 5 thereof may be fully
or partly paid as an advance or as material assistance.”
