RULES made under Article 30 of the Road Passenger Transport Services Ordinance, No. 01 of 1992 of the Western 
Provincial Council as amended from time to time, read in  conjunction with Article 1 54A of the Constitution of the 
Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka. 
 
Authorized powers vested in me by the Governor of the Western Province under Article 30 of the Road Passenger 
Transport Service Ordinance No. 01 of 1992 of the Western Provincial Council, which is amended from time to time to 
be read in conjunction with Article 154A of the Constitution of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka I hereby 
announce that the following rules regarding the operation of a passenger transport service will be published herewith.

RULES 
 
1. These rules shall be enacted to prov ide provisions for registration of the th ree wheelers used for passenger transport 
service in Western Province with the anticipation of a fare or a reward for using them for such purpose and to issue a 
permit for using for such purpose and matters incidental to that. 
2. These Rules shall be cited as the “Rules with regard  to operate a Road Passenger Transport Service by Three 
Wheelers in the Western Province”. 
3. Each Three Wheeler used in passenger  transport service in Western Province with an anticipation of a fare or a 
reward shall be registered under The Three Wheel Bur eau hereafter and be a three wheeler which obtained a 
passenger transport service permit in conformity with the format included in the First Schedule under these Rules. 
4. Each person applied for a Passenger service permit for registration under Road Passenger Transport Service Three 
Wheelers Statute No. 06 of 2002 shall have fulfilled the conditions mentioned below : 
(1) Each request made for registration shall be applied under an application in conformity with the format 
included in the Second enacted under these Rules.

3A IV(A) ෙකොටස - ශී ලංකා පජාතාන්තික සමාජවාදී ජනරජෙය් අති විෙශෂ ගැසට් පතය - 2021.12.15 
PART  IV(A) – GAZETTE EXTRAORDINARY OF THE DEMOCRATIC SOCIALIST REPUBLIC OF SRI LANKA – 15.12.2021 
(2) The fitness of the Three Wheelers used in the pa ssenger transport service shall have been certified by 
a fitness certificate obtained form an institution re gistered in the Office of the Commissioner Motor 
Traffic of Western Province within a period of three months immediately prior to the date of 
application for registration on fitness to be used in such service. 
(3) The Three Wheeler used for the relevant purpose sh all be insured by a fully comprehensive insurance. 
(4) The revenue license of the Three Wheeler fo r the current year shall have been obtained. 
(5) If the applicant for the relevant Three Wheeler is not a driver registered in the Bureau as a three 
wheeler driver for the purpose of passenger transport, the driver deployed for the relevant purpose 
shall be confirmed by the relevant documents to prove that he is the registered driver of the Bureau. 
(6) In the case the applicant is a company registered under the Compan ies Act, No. 07 of 2007, the  
certificate of registration as a company shall be provided. 
5. For every three wheeler registered under the Bureau, a Passenger Service Permit applicable for the next 12 
months valid from the date of registration shall be issued by the Head of the Department of the Bureau. 
6. A person who is dissatisfied by the rejection of an application for registration may submit an appeal relevant 
for it to the General Manager of the Western Provincial  Road Passenger Transport Authority within 30 days 
form the date of rejection. 
7. Decisions relating to appeals under Section 6 above shall be notified to the appellants within 30 days of the 
submission of the appeals and the decision of the Gene ral Manager of the Road Passenger Transport Authority 
regarding the relevant request shall be considered as the final decision. 
8. Each Three Wheeler provided with a permit under these Rules shall provide the relevant service in compliance 
with the following conditions when using the Three Wheeler for passenger transport service in the Western 
Province: 
 (1) The maximum number of passeng ers that can be carried by a Th ree Wheeler in passenger transport 
shall not exceed the number of passengers approved by the Commissioner General of Motor Traffic or 
the number of passengers specified under any written law from time to time. 
 (2) The service shall be provided  under a driver who has a valid driving license for the passenger 
transport service and registered in the Bureau for the relevant purpose. 
 (3) The relevant Three Wheeler shall have been co vered by a comprehensive insurance at all times of 
service. 
 (4) The rear right door of eac h Three Wheeler used for passenger transport shall be covered. 
 (5) At every instance of passenger transportation, the driver on duty should w ear the identity card issued 
by the Bureau. 
 (6) Following information should be displayed as to  clearly seen by the passenger on every three wheeler 
engaged in passenger transportation — 
 (a) Name, address and the telephone num ber of the owner of the three wheeler. 
 (b) Name, address and the telephon e number of the driver on duty. 
 (c) Registration number of the Three wheeler in  Bureau and Driver’s registration number. 
 (d) Driver’s photograph. 
 (7) A taxi meter should be affixed to be seen by the passengers and to charge fares accordingly with  
prescribed fares. 
 (8) List of fares applicable to that day for passenge r transportation should  be displayed on the front left 
corner of the three wheeler.

 (9) At every instance of passenger transport, following  documents should be kept with the driver. 
(a) Passenger Transport Service Permit 
(b) Full and unlimited insurance cover for all passengers 
(c) Valid Revenue License 
(d) Driving License and Identity Card issued by the Bureau 
 (10) The section of the permit which states that it should be displayed, has to be clearly displayed in the 
front side of the three-wheeler. 
 (11) In accordance with the provisi ons of the Motor Traffic Act, ever y three wheeler engaged in passenger 
transportation should maintain all other facilities including seats for the comfort of the passenger. 
 (12) In a passenger transport three wheeler should not be in a way of an obstacle to the well-being of  
service expected passengers and general public in a manner contrary to morality or with improper  
intentions or in a way that causes physical or mental distress by generating sounds or displaying any 
picture or statement. 
9. When an individual person applies for passenger transportation service for several three wheelers, he should 
separately apply for each three wheeler and each registra tion and issuance of a passenger service permit should 
be considered as separate service and acted accordingly. 
10. A passenger service permit obtained for a certain three wheeler should be applicable only for the use of the 
three-wheeler registered with the permit and should not be used for any other purpose under any circumstances. 
11. Within the duration applicable to the issued passenger transport service permit, it cannot be alienated by any 
means without the full permission of the Bureau. 
12. In the following instances; the permit issued in respect of a three wheeler and its registration with the Bureau 
should be considered as invalid. 
(1) Having convicted three or more times for violation of the Road Passenger Transport Service 
Statute No. 6 of 2002 or the rules made therein by the registered owner or the driver. 
(2) Change of ownership of the three wheeler. 
(3) Whenever it is confirmed in a court that the rele vant registered three wheeler has been used by its 
owner or the driver for any criminal activity. 
13. Under Section 12 above, it is the duty of the Head of the Department of the Bureau to cancel the registration 
and the permit at any instance when they are to be ca ncelled and should  act accordingly to inform the  
registered individual regarding such decision by Registered Post. 
14. A person who is dissatisfied with the decision taken by the Head of the Department under Article 13 above can 
appeal to the General Manager of the Authority within 30 days from the date of the relevant notice and the  
decision of the General Manager with regard to that appeal will be the final. 
15. The Registered person of the three wheeler subjected to the decision of cancelling the registration and the  
permit under Section 13 of the above shall be ineligible to apply for a registration or a permit to operate  
passenger transport service by three wheeler for 03 cons ecutive years form the date of cancellation of that  
permit under the Road Passenger Three wheeler Transport Service Statute No. 06 of 2002 or any other statute 
subsequent to it. 
16. All requests for extension of validity of a permit for a registered three wheeler for passenger transport should be 
submitted within a period of 02 months prior to the expiry of the permit.

17. All  requests made under Section 16 above sha ll be applicable to Section 04 under these Rules. 
18. These rules shall not be an obstruc tion to accept any delayed application made for extending the validity period 
of a permit levying the due charge along with a surcharge for each delayed request. 
19. Each three-wheel driver employed in operating a passenge r transport service with the expectation of obtaining a 
fare or a reward from the three-wheeler shall be registered driver in the bureau. 
20. Every person applying for the registration under the above Section 19 shall apply it through an application  
prescribed by the Bureau. 
21. All persons applying for the functions under Section 19 above should not be eligible unless the following  
conditions are fulfilled: 
(1) Shall be a person who fulfills the requirements  complying the conditions which are made under the 
Motor Traffic Act and the orders made thereunder. 
(2) Shall have a valid driving licen se to drive three-wheelers in accordance with the Motor Traffic Act and 
the conditions thereof. 
(3) Shall be certified by a recognized person within a period of preceding one month prior to the date of 
application for having of excellent character. 
(4) Shall be a person who has duly completed the requirements prescribed by the Bureau under the 
application to apply for registration. 
(5) A certificate issued by the Grama Niladhari confirming the residence should be submitted. 
22. In the event the Head of the Department of the Bureau accepts an applicatio n submitted under the above 
Section 21, a certificate completed acco rding to the Schedule III under th ese rules shall be issued to the 
applicant. 
23. Any person unsatisfied with the decision of rejecting the application under Section 21 by the due authority can 
submit an appeal to the General Manager within 30 days from the date of informing the rejection and the 
decision taken by the General Manager in this regard shall be the final. 
24. It is legal to cancel the registration made under Section 22 by the Head of the Department of the Bureau under 
the following circumstances: 
(1) At any instance the Head of th e Department deems the registered person constantly breaches the Motor 
Traffic Act or a the directives made thereof. 
(2) At any instance the registered person has been convicted by a court of law for a criminal offense. 
(3) At any instance where the Head of the Department deems the registered person constantly breaches the 
directives prescribed by the Bureau or the provi sions of Road Passenger Three-wheeler Services  
Statute No. 06 of 2002 or the provisions under these rules. 
25. Any person unsatisfied with the decision regarding th e cancellation of an application under Section 24, can 
submit an appeal within 30 days of informing the decisions to the General Manager and the decision taken by 
the General Manager in this regard shall be the final. 
26. It is legal to inspect or to request any document relate d for the Sectional Head or any other authorized officer of 
the Authority in determining whether a three-wheeler en gaged in passenger transport is in compliance with  
requirements of the Road Passenger Th ree-wheeler Service Statute No. 06 of 2002 or any of the provisions of 
these Rules whenever it is parked or ready to run.
