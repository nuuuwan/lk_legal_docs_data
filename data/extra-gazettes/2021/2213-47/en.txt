And whereas, in pursuance of the provisions of Section 5 of the Special Presidential Commission of Inquiry Act No.
7 of 1978 of the National State Assembly,  I do hereby amend as mentioned below, the first paragraph of the  warrant issued
in relation to the Special Presidential Commission of Inquiry established on 28
th January 2021 by the notification published in
the Extraordinary Gazette No. 2212/53 of 29thJanuary 2021 in accordance with Section 2 of the Special Presidential Inquiry Act
No. 7 of 1978 of the National State Assembly as amended by the Special Presidential Commission of Inquiry (Special
Provisions) Act No. 4 of 1978.
“Whereas, taking into consideration that subsequent to the submission of  the final report, comprising 3 volumes and an
erratum, of the Presidential Commission of  Inquiry appointed in terms of the powers vested in me by provisions of Section
2 of  the Commission of  Inquiry Act (Chapter 393) to obtain information, inquire into and investigate alleged political
victimizations said to have been committed directly or otherwise, during the period commencing  08
th January 2015 and ending
16th November 2019, on Public Officers, Employees of Public Corporations, members of the Armed Forces as well as Police
Service, or any other person or persons  who resigned from their posts in public service or otherwise ceased to hold office
with the change of the government, or continued to hold such office subsequent  to the change of such government, and/or
were holding posts prior to the Presidential Election and/or General Election held in January and August in the year 2015
respectively, Cabinet approval has been received for the implementation of decisions and recommendations contained in
items 08, 09 and 10 of the said final report”
Given at Colombo under the Seal of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka on this 05 day of February, Two
thousand and Twenty one.
                                     By His Excellency’s Command,
P. B. J
AYASUNDERA,
Secretary to the President.
