THE    LICENCING   OF   PRODUCE   BROKERS   ACT,   No.   9   OF   1979
REGULATIONS made by the Minister of  Plantation under Section 9 read with Sub-sections (4) and (6) of Section 2 of the

Dr. RAMESH PATHIRANA (M.P.),
Minister of  Plantation.
Colombo,
02nd March, 2021.
Regulations
The Licensing of Produce Brokers (Tea) Regulations, 1979 published in the Gazette Extraordinary No. 51/3 of August 27,
1979, as amended from time to time and lastly amended by regulations published in the Gazette Extraordinary No. 1055/1 of
November 23, 1998 are hereby further amended as follows :-
(1) by the repeal of regulation 9 thereof, and substitution therefor of the following new regulation :-
“ 9. (1) The license fee payable for a license to carry on the business of a produce broker shall be rupees one
million.
(2) All fees levied under Sub regulation (1) above, shall be credited to the Capital Fund established under
Section 14 of the Sri Lanka Tea Board Law, No. 14 of 1975.” ;

(2) by the repeal of regulation 10 thereof, and substitution therefor of the following new regulation :-
“10. (1) Every license shall, unless earlier cancelled or suspended expire on the thirty first day of December

- Two Hundred and Fifty Thousand rupees ;
(3) A renewable fee shall be as follows :-
(a) large scale produce broker (tea)
(b) medium and small scale produce broker (tea) - One Hundred and Twenty Five Thousand

(3) by the insertion immediately after the definition of expression “approved lending institution” in regulation 13, of the
following definitions of expressions :-
“large scale produce broker (tea)” means a produce broker who has over ten per centun (10%) market share in
terms of volume based on sales in the preceding financial year ended by March 31 ;
“medium and small scale produce broker (tea)” means a produce broker who has ten per centum (10%) or less
market share in terms of volume based on sales in the preceding financial year ended by March 31.”.
