INLAND  REVENUE  ACT,  NO. 24 OF 2017
BY virtue of the powers vested in me under Section 194 of the Inland Revenue Act, No. 24 of 2017, I, Mahinda Rajapaksa,
Minister of Finance do by this Order prescribe the information technology and enabled services referred to in Part I of the
SCHEDULE hereto, but subject to the conditions of Part II of the SCHEDULE for the purposes of the Subparagraph (ii)
of Paragraph (u) of Third Schedule of the Inland Revenue Act, No. 24 of 2017 as amended by the Inland Revenue
(Amendment) Act, No. 10 of 2021, effective from the date of  January 1, 2020.
M
AHINDA R AJAPAKSA,
Minister of Finance,
Ministry of Finance,
Colombo,
June 29, 2021.
SCHEDULE
P
ART  I
1. Software development services in Sri Lanka by any person.
2. Adaptation, upgrading, enhancement, implementation and similar services, related to any type of software developed
in Sri Lanka.

3. Following services provided by any local institution to any person :-
(a) services of computer programming;
(b) deployment of business Information Technology applications;
(c) system integration;

c) clinical database management services excluding clinical trials; and

requiring application of knowledge and advanced analytical and technical skills in relation to -
a) geographic information system;
b) engineering and design services; and
c) animation or content development and management.
P
ART II
1. With effect from  April 1, 2020, number of local technical employees related to information technology should
not be less than seventy percent (70%) of the total number of such type of employees deployed in the software
development referred to in item 1 or providing services referred to in item 2; as  the case may be; of Part I.
2. With effect form  April 1, 2020, in the case of services referred to in Part I other than the services referred to in
item 1 or 2 of such Part, minimum “local value addition” should be not less than fifity percent (50%) of the gross
income of such services.
For the purpose of calculating the “local value addition”, the cost of any goods imported by such person or locally
purchased but imported goods used by such person or any cost of service for which the payments are made to a
person outside Sri Lanka shall be deducted from the gross income of such services.
3. Any business set up or formed by splitting-up an existing business as at the effective date of this notification shall
not be qualified.
