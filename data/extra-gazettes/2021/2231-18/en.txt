iN terms of the powers vested in me by Section 20 read together with Sub-Section 4(1), Section 14 of the imports and exports 
(Control) act, No. 1 of 1969, as amended by act No. 48 of 1985 and act No. 28 of 1987, i, Mahinda rajapaksa, the Minister

 Mahinda Rajapaksa,
 Minister of finance.
Ministry of finance,
Colombo 01,

Regulations
1. These regulations may be cited as “Imports and Exports (Control) Regulations No. 10 of 2021”.  
2. The imports and exports (Control) regulations No. 04 of 2020 published in the Gazette Extraordinary No. 2184/21 
dated 16 July 2020, including its Schedules, as amended subsequently, are hereby repealed effective from 11 June 2021 
without prejudice to the importation of goods, which have the date of Bill of Lading/ airway Bill on or before 10 June 
2021. 
3. (a)  Goods specified in the Schedule I, except vehicles, auto parts, plastic goods, and agricultural products, are allowed 
to be imported for the purpose of processing and re-exporting or the purpose of processing and supply to another

 (b)  Goods specified in the Schedule I, except motor vehicles, auto parts, plastic goods, and agricultural products, 
and imported by an importer for the purpose of processing for export or the purpose of processing and supply to 
another exporter for export, but with local value addition below 20%, may be allowed on the recommendation of 
the Chairman / director General of Board of investment of Sri Lanka or director General of export development 
Board on a case by case basis.
4. (a)  Importation of goods specified in the Schedule I, except vehicles, auto parts, plastic goods, and agricultural products, 
by an importer for the purpose of process and re-export will be allowed if such importation of goods will be made;  
 (i) On the “No foreign exchange (Nfe)” Basis; 
 (ii) By using funds of the foreign buyer, without obtaining funds from local banks; 
 (iii) By using the deposits made out from the earnings of their exports in the foreign Currency Bank accounts 
(fCBu and Business foreign Currency accounts, BfCa), subject to not exceeding the positive balance. 
  in the above cases, the importer shall be required to declare his source of foreign currency earnings to the 
local bank(s) when importing under provisions of this regulation.
 (b)  When any importation will be made as per regulation Nos. 4(a)(i) and 4(a)(ii) above, goods can be cleared from 
the Sri Lanka Customs after submission of a declaration, which confirms that the said importation is made as per 
the said regulations and approvals of other institutions are not required.
5. The importer referred to in the regulations Nos. 3 and 4 shall under all circumstances bring foreign exchange earnings 
to a commercial bank in Sri Lanka within a period not more than 120 days reckoned from the date of the export of 
processed goods using the imported goods referred to in regulations Nos. 3 and 4 above.
6. in any conditions, the director General of Customs shall not entertain any letters of requests of any Ministry, Government 
department or Statutory Body, etc. other than the Board of investment or the export development Board (as provided in 
the Regulations Nos 3 and 10 in this Gazette), issued under any legal provisions to allow the importation of goods, which 
are listed out in the Schedule I (Temporary Suspension)  unless such letters of requests are channeled to the director 
General, Sri Lanka Customs through the Controller General of imports and exports in order to prevent misuses.
7. The goods specified in the Schedule I have been identified on the recommendation of the Tariff Determination Committee, 
appointed by the His excellency President with a view to strengthen and revive the national economy, and the importation 
of those goods shall be subject to the restrictions prescribed by the regulations below.
8. (a)  Importation of any goods, specified in the Schedule I under any Bill of Lading or airway Bill dated on or after 
11 June 2021, targeting supply to the local market under the payment terms 3(1)(a), 3(1)(b), and 3(1)(c), of the 
imports and exports (Control) regulations No. 01 of 2011 published in the Gazette Extraordinary No. 1739/3 
dated 2 January 2012, shall be temporarily suspended.  
 (b)  raw materials for any products which are locally manufactured with minimum local value addition of 35% or more 
shall be allowed to import required raw materials, which are not available locally but required for manufacturing 
such products and specified in the Schedule I subject to a minimum 30 days supplier’s credit facility.

 (c)  Notwithstanding the regulation No.8 (a) above, 
 i. Building Materials Cooperation (BMC) is hereby allowed to import goods, classified under the HS Heading

 ii. director General of Customs may clear Plastic Covers, Parts, and Connectors accompanied with the

9. Notwithstanding regulation No. 8 above, importation of Motor Vehicles (HS Codes categorized under HS Headings 
87.02 and 87.03 of this Gazette only) is allowed through a valid Letters of Credit (LC) for the use of the diplomatic 
officers, who are serving in the Diplomatic Missions in Sri Lanka, subject to the prior approval of the Foreign Ministry. 
The importer shall submit the approval of the foreign Ministry to any commercial banks to establish Letters of Credit 
and to the director General of Customs to clearance of the approved motor vehicles from the Sri Lanka Customs under 
this regulation.
10. (a)  importation of project-related capital Goods, listed out in the Schedule I (including international brands being 
imported by registered agents) for the purpose of any Strategic development Projects or other projects, which 
funded by foreign direct investors or local investors and approved under Section 17 of the Board of investment 
(BOi) of Sri Lanka act, No. 4 of 1978, is hereby permitted to be imported on the recommendation of the 
Chairman/ director General of BOi. furthermore, loans of any local banks shall not be used for such importation 
of project-related capital goods under this regulation.
 (b)  in such instances referred (a) above, the importer shall submit a recommendation from the Chairman / director 
General of Board of investment of Sri Lanka to commercial banks and the director General of Customs for 
effect the payment and clearing purpose from the Sri Lanka Customs.
11. Any person, who submits an application to a bank to make payment for importation of any goods, specified in the Schedule 
I shall provide the correct Harmonized Description System and Commodity Classification (HS) Code as specified in 
Schedule ‘a’ of the Customs Ordinance, description and relevant Schedule of the goods of this Gazette in his application.
12. In terms of Section 21 of the Imports and Exports Control Act No. 1 of 1969, restrictions specified in the Regulations 
issued under that act are deemed as the restrictions imposed under the Customs Ordinance. Therefore, the director 
General of Customs shall act as per the objectives of these regulations.
13. The Head of the department of foreign exchange and the Head of the department of Bank Supervisions of the Central 
Bank of Sri Lanka shall ensure that the commercial banks are carrying out related activities in compliance with the 
objectives of these regulations.
14. Where any commercial bank acts in violation of the provisions of these regulations, the director General of Customs 
shall notify such violations in detail together with relevant documentary evidence to the Head of the department of 
foreign exchange and the Controller General of imports and exports. The Head of the department of foreign exchange 
and the Controller General of imports and exports shall take action as provided for in the law or inform the relevant 
authority to take appropriate action, as the case may be.
15. Where any person, who is the declarant or the importer acts in violation of the provisions of these regulations, the 
director General of Customs and the Controller General of imports and exports shall take action as provided for in the 
law on the recommendations of the Tariff Determination Committee appointed by His Excellency President.

19. in these regulations – 
 i. ‘The temporary suspension of imports’ shall mean that the importation of goods which does not comply with 
the relevant regulations stated above is prohibited.
 ii. ‘Exportation’ shall mean the carrying and taking out of Sri Lanka or causing to be carried or taken out of Sri 
Lanka, when used in relation to any goods whether by sea or by air of such goods as mentioned in section 22 
of the imports and exports (Control) act, 1 of 1969. 
 iii. ‘Importation/Import’ shall mean the importing and bringing into Sri Lanka, or causing to be imported or 
brought into Sri Lanka, when used in relation to any goods whether by sea or by air of such goods as mentioned 
in section 22 of the imports and exports (Control) act, 1 of 1969. 
 iv. ‘Local Value Addition’ shall mean the enhancement of the value of the unit price of the imported goods made 
at the factory and it is calculated by using the following equation. 
   ex-factory Cost – Cost of imported raw Materials

a. Cost, insurance, freight (Cif) value of imported raw materials

for ex-factory cost, the followings shall only be included 
a. Cost of locally manufactured inputs (Materials and parts manufactured locally)
b. direct labor cost (Salaries and Wages)
c. depreciation cost
 v. ‘No foreign Exchange Basis’ shall mean the importation of goods without payment in foreign exchange out 
of Sri Lanka.
 vi. ‘Project-related capital goods’ shall mean construction Goods, machinery, plant & equipment, and/or any 
other capital item/material required by a Strategic development Project or a Section 17 project, except vehicles, 
auto parts, plastic goods, and agricultural products. 
 vii. ‘Strategic Development Project’ shall mean a project identified under the Strategic Development Projects 
act, No. 14 of 2008 and in terms of regulations published thereunder.
