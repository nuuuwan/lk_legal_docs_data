Orders
1. These orders are published to authorize Pradeshiya Sabhas in the North Western Province to spend
further time to take action pursuant to section 24 of Pradeshiya Sabha Act and to prescribe the procedure
to publish notifications stating the name list of roads and paths in terms of section 24 of Pradeshiya
Sabha Act and to make provisions on the matters related or incidental thereto considering the special
circumstances that roads and paths which are already vested in and shall be deemed to be vested in
Pradeshiya Sabhas in terms of section 23 of Pradeshiya Sabha Act should be permanently marked and
demarcated and designs of such roads and paths should have been prepared in terms of  section 24 of the
said Act within three years of coming into effect of Pradeshiya Sabha Act No. 15 of 1987, that is before
11th May 1990, and even though it has been ordered to publish notices on them as prescribed in the
orders, and since necessary orders have not been formulated yet and the time that the notices should be
made is already over.
2. These orders are cited as the orders with regard to planning roads, paths and publishing
notifications specifying the names list of roads and paths maintained by the Pradeshiya Sabhas in the
North Western Province.
3. (1) By virtue of powers vested under Subsection(l) of Section 24 of Pradeshiya Sabha Act
No. 15 of 1987, hereinafter referred to as “ Act” to be read with Section 2 of Provincial Councils
(Incidental Provisions) Act No. 12 of 1989, permission is hereby granted to each Pradeshiya Sabha in
the North Western Province hereinafter referred to as “Sabha” to extend the required time period up to
an additional period of twelve months from effective date of these orders are coming in to force, under
Section 24 of the Act.
(2) If any Pradeshiya Sabha in the North Western Province hereinafter referred to as “Sabha”
fails to complete the work within the period of twelve months permitted additionally under Subsection(l),
and requires additional time to complete the work; Pradeshiya Sabhas can submit special matters that
have caused to it and ask for permission for a longer period.
4. (1) Roads and paths hereinafter referred to as “Road”, that should be deemed as vested in the
Pradeshiya Sabha under section 23 of the Act, should be identified and a list of names of the roads and
paths should be prepared in accordance with the format prescribed by the Commissioner of Local Government
and the said list of names should be forwarded to the Regional Assistant Commissioner of Local Government
hereinafter referred to as “Assistant Commissioner” for his/her approval.
(2) The Chairman shall take action to include all the roads vested in the Sabha in terms of
Section 16 of the Act or included to immovable property vested by the Development Council of Kurunegala
District or Puttlam District in terms of Section 17 of the Act as the case may be, in that schedule.
(3) The procedure given in the ensuing Orders should be followed in respect of publishing
notices of roads and incidental matters thereto for which approval of the Assistant Commissioner has been
given to be deemed as the roads vested in the Sabha from among the roads included in the schedule given
in the Sub Section (1).
5. (1) An identification code number should be given for each road approved by the Assistant
Commissioner as per the format given in the First Schedule.
(2) It shall be the duty of the Chairman to make provisions to collect following details and
relevant geographical Coordinate data of each road for which an Identification Code Number has been
given.

relating to each road deemed as vested in the Sabha, and an electronic soft copy of the inventory of the
said roads and to update it as and when necessary.
15. In these Orders unless the context requires otherwise -
The term “road” means a road, a path, a lane and stairway which are to be deemed to
vested in the Sabha under Section 23 of the Act and any reservation of such a road,
a path, a lane and stairway and any bridge, culvert, box culvert, a causeway, an
abutment wall, a drain constructed or dug or a footed bridge connected to it shall also
be deemed as included to it.
“ Road surface” means a developmental medium of a Mattel and tar mixture, inter locked
blocks, concrete, asphalt concrete or gravel.
“ Chairman” means the Chairman of any Pradeshiya Sabha in the North Western Province
who acts under these Orders.

FIRST SCHEDULE
Order 5 (I)
Allocation of Code Numbers for roads
The following matters should be included into the Code assigned for a certain road deemed as to be vested in the Sabha.
Code : Province/ Pradeshiya Sabha/ Base line Road / road owned by the Local Government Institute/ By road
Example :  NWP / KUP / B247 / 04 / 00 or 08
o “NWP” Should be used for the code assigned in respect of the province.
o The code prescribed for the Pradeshiya Sabha should be assigned under the directions of the Commissioner of
Local Government.
o Base line road means a road owned by the Road Development Authority or Provincial Road Development Authority.
The number assigned to that route in relation to that particular road in a notification published in the Gazette by
that particular institute should be used for this purpose.
o the next number of the Road Code should be a number given according to the sequence or roads extending from
the Base Line Road. This number should consist of two digits.
o “ By Road” means a by road extending from a certain road owned by the Local Government Institute. In Case such
a by road, a number should be given for the last two digits of the code. The last two digits should be “00” in case
the road is owned by a Local Government Institute that is not a by road.
