CONSTITUTION OF THE DEMOCRATIC SOCIALIST REPUBLIC OF SRI LANKA
Notification
IT is hereby notified that the notification issued by me in the exercise of powers vested in me under Articles 44 (1), 45 (1) and
47 (1) (a) (b) respectively of the Constitution of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka and published in the Gazette
Extraordinary No. 2187/27 of 09th August, 2020 and amended by time to time, is further amended with effect from 03rd May,
2021 in the manner set out below.

Colombo 01,
May 02, 2021.

14.2 S tate Minister of Production, Supply and Regulation of Organic and  Natural Fertilizer

Assisting in the formulation of policies in relation to the subject of Production, Supply and Regulation of

plants and eco-friendly organic fertilizer of standardized quality for major crops including paddy and
grain varieties, pepper, cloves and cocoa as well as vegetable, fruit and home garden crops
2. Encouraging local production of eco-friendly organic fertilizer using local materials to international
standards and production of other high quality organic fertilizers within the country

(4) “By insertion of the following new Heading and “01. Subjects and Functions, 02. Special  Priorities” under thereof 
after the Heading No. “30.0 Minister of Public  Security” in that notification;
30.1   Sta te Minister of Community Police Services
1. Subjects and Functions
Assisting in the formulation of policies in relation to the subject of community police services for “ensuring 
the public security” under the direction and guidance of the Minister of Public Security in conformity with 
the prescribed Laws, Acts and Ordinances and implementing, monitoring and evaluating programmes and 
projects under the National Budget, State Investment and National Development Programme.
2. Special Priorities
1. Formulating and implementing appropriate programmes in  co-ordination with the Minister of Public 
Security for ensuring a public interest focused people-friendly police service.
2. Planning and implementing strategic activities with community participation at divisional and district  
level in order to develop a close relationship between the police and the public.
3. Formulating a methodology of assisting in consolidating security as rural units in emergency situations 
in co-ordination with the Community Police Units and the community leaders.
4. Implementing training programmes to broaden the knowledge of the r elevant officers on Community 
Police Service and for their attitudinal development.
5. To increase the number of Police divisions appropriately and establish Police Stations, so as to ensure 
public security and deploy the required number of Police personnel.
6. Speedily implementing such service reforms as are needed in order for the Police to develop close  
rapport with the community.
