reGULATiONS
1. (1)  These regulations may be cited as medical (Procedure for election of members) regulations No 1 of 2021.
 
(2)  The provisions of these regulations shall come into operation on the date on which the notification of approval 
of Parliament is published in the Gazette.

2. These regulations shall apply to –
(a) elect eight members under section 12(1)(c) of the medical Ordinance by medical practitioners registered 
under section 29 of the medical Ordinance;
(b)  elect three members under section 12(1)(cc) of the medical Ordinance by the medical specialists referred to in 
section 39A and registered under section 39b of the medical Ordinance;
(c) elect one member under section 12(1)(ccc) of the medical Ordinance by the dental specialists referred to in 
section 39A and registered under section 39b of the medical Ordinance;
 
(d) elect one member under section 12(1)(d) of the medical Ordinance  by persons entitled to practice medicine and 
surgery registered under section 41 of the medical Ordinance; and
(e)  elect one member under section 12(1)(e) of the medical  Ordinance  by dentists registered under section 43 of 
the medical Ordinance.
3. (1)  The Sri Lanka medical council (hereinafter referred to as the “medical council”) for the purpose of electing 
members under regulation 2 shall, by a resolution, declare the President of the medical council or registrar 
of the Medical Council as the Returning Officer.
(2) The Returning Officer shall conduct separate elections to elect members under paragraph (a), (b), (c), (d) or 
(e) of regulation 2.
4. The medical council may, where it deems necessary and for the purpose of conducting free and fair elections, obtain 
assistance of the election commission established under Article 103 of the constitution with the consent of such 
election commission for the entirety or part of such election.
5. The medical council shall, prior to the election, update the electronic register of the members as at the time of notice 
of the election.
6. (1)  Where a seat of the member specified in regulation 2 becomes vacant in terms of section 15 of the Medical 
Ordinance, the Medical Council shall, at the next meeting of the Council,  sanction the filling of such vacancy.
(2) The Returning Officer, upon sanction of the Medical Council, shall advertise the vacancy and publish the notice 
calling for nominations in respect of such vacancy.
(3) every notice of nomination for a vacancy under sections 12(1)(c), 12(1)(cc), 12(1)(ccc), 12(1)(d) and 12(1)
(e) shall be substantially in Form A1, A2, A3, A4 or A5, respectively as set out in the Schedule hereto.
 (4) (a)  A notice of nomination in respect of each vacancy occurred shall be published in local daily newspaper in 
Sinhala, Tamil, and English languages and in the official website of the Medical Council.
(b) every notice of nomination shall specify-
(i) the nomination period which shall commence on the seventh day  after the date of the publication  
of  notice of nomination and expire at 3.00 p.m. on the twenty-first day after the date of publication  
of notice of nomination.
(ii) type of vacancy referred to in regulation 2; and 
(iii) the place of receiving nomination.

7. (1)  Every nomination for election shall be handedover in duplicate to the Returning Officer by the candidate himself 
in person or to an officer of the Medical Council authorized by the Returning Officer, between 9 a.m. to 3 p.m. 
on week days.
(2) The candidates who contest in the election and the persons who proposes the nomination shall have valid 
registration as at the time of closing nominations.
 
(3) every nomination shall include-
(a)  full name of the candidate as it appears in the relevant register of the medical council;
(b) the registration number of the candidate, which shall be valid at the time of closure of nominations;
(c)  the consent of the candidate expressed under his signature, to contest in the election;
(d) a declaration by the candidate and a declaration by the persons nominating such candidate; and
(e) a professional biographical sketch of the candidate not exceeding two hundred and fifty words in Sinhala, 
Tamil or english languages.
(4) every nomination paper shall be substantially in the relevant Form b1, b2, b3, b4 or b5 as set out in the Schedule 
hereto.
(5) every candidate shall be nominated as follows: -
(a)  in the case of each member to be elected under section 12(1)(c) of the medical Ordinance, by ten or more 
medical practitioners possessing valid registration under section 29 of the medical Ordinance;
(b)  in the case of each member to be elected under section 12(1)(cc) of the Medical Ordinance, by five or more 
persons possessing valid registration from among the medical specialists processing valid registration 
under to in section 39b of the medical Ordinance;
(c) in the case of each member to be elected under section 12(l)(ccc) the Medical Ordinance, by five or more 
persons possessing valid registration from among the dental specialists processing valid registration 
under section 39 b;
(d)  in the case of each member to be elected under section 12(l)(d) of the Medical Ordinance, by five or more 
persons possessing valid registration as persons entitled to practice medicine and surgery under section 
41 of the medical Ordinance; and
(e)  in the case of each member to be elected under section 12(1)(e) of the Medical Ordinance, by five or more 
dentists possessing valid registration under section 43 of the medical Ordinance.
8. The Returning Officer shall display a copy of each nomination paper received by him on the notice board of the Council 
and in the official website of the Medical  Council after 3.00 p.m. on the date of the closure of nominations.
9. Any objections against nominations and withdrawals of nominations shall be submitted in writing to the returning 
Officer from 3.00 p.m. on the twenty-first day after the date of publication of notice of nominations to 3.00 p.m. of 
the twenty-fourth day after the date of publication of notice of nominations.
10. The Returning Officer shall reject any nomination which does not meet the requirements specified in the notice of 
nomination, any requirement referred to in paragraph (3) of regulation 7 or due to one or more of the following reasons:-
 (a)  where the candidate has been found guilty and punished by the Professional conduct committee of 
the Medical Council under its disciplinary procedure regulations, within the preceding five years of the notic e 
of nomination;

(c)  where the candidate has been dismissed from the public service by the Public Service commission established

(e)  where the candidate has been charged by a court of law for a criminal offence and the determination is pending;

(f)  where in any other instance not complying with any requirement stated in the notice of nomination.
11. The valid nominations accepted by the Returning Officer, nominations rejected by the Returning Officer and nominations 
withdrawn by candidate shall be declared and displayed on the notice board of the medical council and in the 
official website of the Medical Council.
12. Where any nomination papers have been rejected by the Returning Officer, the Returning Officer shall forthwith 
inform the candidate the reasons thereof. The decision of the Returning Officer to reject such nomination paper shall 
be final and conclusive.
13. Where the number of accepted nominations and the number of vacancies specified in the notice of nomination are 
equal, the Returning Officer shall renounce the election and declare such candidates as duly elected for each vacancy 
and shall inform the medical council accordingly at its next meeting.
14. (1)  Where the number of valid nominations is less than the number of vacancies specified in the notice of 
nomination, the Returning Officer shall declare such candidates as duly elected and inform to the Medical 
council accordingly at its next meeting.
 (2)  The Medical Council shall take steps to fill the remaining vacancies in terms of these regulations.
15. Where the number of valid nominations exceeds the number of vacancies specified in the notice of nomination, the 
Returning Officer shall hold an election in terms of these regulations.

16. The election to fill the vacancies shall be concluded within a period of three months from the date of the election 
notice.
 
17. The Returning Officer shall publish the Notice of Election in Form C as set out in the Schedule hereto indicating the 
names of the candidates contesting the election, the dates  of holding election and the closing date for application to 
be treated as a postal voter in a local newspaper in Sinhala, Tamil and English and the official website of the Medical 
council and display on the notice board of the medical council.
18. (1)  Any person who is entitled to cast a postal vote may send the application to be treated as a postal voter as 
substantially in Form d set out in the Schedule hereto, which shall be published along with the Notice of election.
(2) The duly completed application to be treated as a postal vote containing the full name of the applicant, 
registration number, the National identity card number and  the address of the applicant shall be sent by 
registered post addressed to the Returning Officer before the closing date specified in the notice of election.

 (3) every such application to be treated as a postal vote, shall be made individually.
19. (1) Upon the receipt of application to be treated as a postal vote, it shall be the duty of the Returning Officer to 
place the date stamp and, verify and enter the details in a separate register which shall be referred to as the 
“register of Applicants to be treated as Postal V otes.”
 (2)  The particulars of the applicant shall be checked with the information contained in the relevant registers at 
the medical council. An entry shall be made in the “register of Applicants to be treated as Postal V otes” 
in respect of each application indicating whether such application has been accepted or rejected, reasons for 
rejection and the date of dispatch of the postal ballot to the relevant applicant.
20. (1)  A person whose name has been erased for the relevant register shall not be entitled to vote and shall be 
informed immediately of such disqualification:
  Provided that such person shall be entitled to vote in person if the name of such person is restored in the 
relevant register in terms of the provisions of the medical Ordinance, prior to the commencement of voting in 
person.
(2) in all other instances, where the application to be treated as a postal vote of an applicant who is eligible to 
vote is rejected, such applicant shall  be informed immediately with reasons for such rejection. Such applicant 
shall be entitled to vote in person.
(3) The list of the applicants to be treated as the postal voters and status of each application indicating whether 
accepted or rejected, shall be published in the official website of the Medical Council prior to the sending of 
postal ballot papers to eligible postal voters.
21. (1)  Every ballot paper under paragraph (3) of regulation 20 shall be signed by the Returning Officer and place the 
official rubber stamp, on the reverse of such ballot paper.
 (2) every postal ballot paper shall be dispatched by registered post to the applicant to the relevant address registered 
with the medical council.
(3) The following documents shall be sent to the applicant to be treated as a postal voter along with the ballot 
paper.
(a) instructions for voting;
(b) a small envelope marked as 'A' with the following printed on it –
(i) full name of postal voter according to the registration made under the medical Ordinance;
(ii) registration number of the postal voter;
(iii) National identity card number;
(iv) address registered with the medical council;
(v) signature of the voter; and
(vi) names of two witnesses, their registration numbers and signatures; and
(c) a large envelope marked as 'b' for placing in the small envelope marked as 'A' and posting the postal vote 
to the Returning Officer.
22. (1) The postal ballot paper shall be witnessed by two witnesses before casting the vote.
(2) The witnesses referred to in paragraph (1) shall be a medical practitioner, a dentist, a person entitled to 
practice medicine and surgery under section 41 of the medical Ordinance, an Attorney-at-law or a Justice of 
the Peace who shall place their signatures and the official stamp on the envelope marked as ‘A’.
(3) Upon casting the vote, ballot paper shall be placed in the small envelope marked as ‘A’ by the voter and sealed 
and, the signature of the voter should be placed over the seal of the small envelope marked as ‘A’.

(5) The voter shall send the large envelope marked as ‘B’ to the Returning Officer by registered post to be received

(6) If there are more than one ballot paper in the smaller envelope marked as ‘A’ or if there are more than one smaller

23. (1)  The postal votes received by registered post after the closing date mentioned in the Notice of election shall be 
rejected. The last day of receiving postal vote shall be a working day of the week.
(2) The larger envelope marked as ‘B’ containing the smaller envelope marked as ‘A’ shall be date stamped, serially 
numbered, and kept in the custody of the Returning Officer, who shall be responsible for its safety.
(3) The name of the voter, the registration number and the address of the voter registered under medical 
Ordinance on the large envelope marked as ‘B’ shall be verified with the ‘Register of Applicants to be treated 
as Postal V oters and certified by the Returning Officer.
(4) Where the particulars on the envelope are correct, the envelope shall be deposited in the postal ballot box. Any 
discrepancy shall be brought to the notice of the Returning Officer, whose decision shall be the final and 
conclusive on the acceptance or the rejection of the postal vote.
24. (1)  The voting in person shall be conducted in a venue determined by the medical council and shall be held on a 
consecutive Tuesday and Wednesday.
(2) The voting in person shall commence at 9.00 a.m. and conclude at 4.00 p.m. on each day.
(3) An experienced election staff shall be employed on the dates of voting in person. Such staff shall be subject to 
the supervision of the Returning Officer.
(4) A list of eligible to vote in person shall be prepared according to the alphabetical order referred to as the ‘Register 
of V oters in Person’ and be given to the election staff to ensure the casting of votes.
(5) An arrangement shall be made to mark the ballot paper without being seen by anyone else.
(6) Any candidate or one of his agents may be allowed to observe the voting throughout the period of voting.
(7) A person entitled to vote shall declare his full name, registration number and prove his identity with the National 
identity card or medical council identity card or Passport or driving License.
(8) The slit through which votes are inserted into the ballot box shall be sealed with a strip of paper containing the 
signatures of the Returning Officer and the candidates or their agents placed across upon conclusion of voting.
25. For the purpose of elections, the retuning officer, with the assistance and advice of the Election Commission, shall 
appoint such officers and servants as may be necessary in addition to the staff of the Medical Council.

26. A voter shall not be allowed to vote -
 (a) if such voter fails or refuses to provide proof of his identity;
(b) Where it has  been identified that such voter has already voted in person or under postal voting; and
(c) Where the name of the voter has been erased from the relevant register.
27. (1) The ballot papers used for voting in person and postal voting shall be printed in two different colours.
(2) There shall be two separate boxes for postal ballot papers and ballot papers voted in person.
(3) The returning officer shall inform the candidates a date and time at which the ballot boxes are sealed. The candidate  
or one of his authorized agents shall be allowed to inspect the empty ballot boxes before they are sealed and place 
their signatures.

28.      (1) The counting of votes in respect of an election held under Part ii shall commence after the conclusion of vot-
ing in person.
(2) The counting shall be in the presence of the President of the Medical Council, the Returning Officer, the staff 
appointed for counting of votes and two authorized agents per candidate may be present at the time of counting 
of the votes.
(3) The postal votes shall be counted first, followed  by votes in person. The number of votes inside the two boxes 
shall be counted  separately. The numbers deposited in the ballot boxes shall be equal to the number voted by 
postal voting and voted in person.
29. (1)  The Returning Officer shall, prior to commencing the counting of votes, ascertain that the seals of the postal  
 voters’ ballot boxes are intact and, open in the presence of the staff and authorized agents and take out the     
 ballot papers.
(2) Upon the postal voters’ ballot box is opened, the Returning Officer shall count and note the number of covering 
envelopes and shall then open each covering  envelope separately.
(3) After the Returning Officer opens a covering envelope marked as ‘B’ and the smaller envelope marked as ‘A’  
shall be taken out and the particulars contained therein shall be verified with the ‘Register of Applicants to be 
treated as a postal voter’.
(4) In case of discrepancy, the decision of the Returning Officer shall be final on the acceptance or the rejection 
of the postal vote.
(5) The Returning Officer, while counting and recording the number of ballot papers shall keep the ballot papers 
with their face upwards and take all proper precautions for preventing any person from seeing the numbers 
printed on the back of the papers.
30. The ballot papers cast in person shall be counted separately against each candidate in the same manner.
31. If the mark of a ballot paper is unclear, it shall be decided by the Returning Officer whether to reject or accept the 
vote. The Returning Officer shall mark ‘rejected’ on every vote rejected and the total rejected votes shall be recorded 
separately.
32. The total valid votes and the number of valid votes secured by each candidate shall be counted separately. The 
candidate who has secured highest number of votes shall be declared as elected, thereafter the next highest shall be

34. The Medical Council shall ensure the employment of an experienced election staff and impartial officers for the 
supervision and monitoring of the election.
35. It shall be the duty of the Returning Officer to maintain transparency in the election process.
36.      (1) Any complaints regarding the holding of the election, counting of votes or  announcement of the results shall 
be informed in writing to the Returning Officer on the same day and the Returning Officer shall settle the issues 
without delay.
(2) The Returning Officer shall bring any unresolved matter to the notice of the Medical Council.
37.     All relevant documents pertaining to the election shall be kept under the safe custody of the Returning Officer.
38. A person who contravenes the provisions of these regulations shall commits an offence and be liable to a penalty 
specified in section 68 of the Medical Ordinance.
