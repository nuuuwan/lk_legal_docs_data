BY LAW RELATING TO CREMATORIUMS
I. This By-Law is cited as the  By-law for controlling  and regulating  the activities of the  Crematoriums of the
Pradeshiya Sabha of Yatinuwara.
2. This By-Law is enforced  for the purpose of controlling the usage of Yatinuwara Pradeshiya Sabha crematorium
and for determination and levying  fees for usage of the said Crematorium.
3. No dead body or a part of a dead body or human embryo be cremated in a crematorium own by the Pradeshiya
Sabha without a permit issued by the Chairman or by an officer authorized by him.

4. Any person hereinafter referred to as the Applicant, expecting to obtain a permit under By-Law No. 03 shall
prepare an application, as shown in the 1st schedule herein and forward, to the  Chairman or to an Officer Authorized by him
along with follwing documents.
(i) A document to prove the relationship between the applicant and the deceased person  (For this purpose
a certificate issued by the Grama Niladhari where the deceased person was residing or certificate of  a from
the member of the Pradeshiya Sabha or birth certificate or marriage certificate of the applicant or any other
certificate of such nature may be produced) ;
(ii) Photo copy of applicant’s National Identity card or  a copy of  a valued passport or, any other document
to prove the identity of the applicant ;
(iii) In the Death  Certificate issued by a Registrar of Births and Deaths under the provisions of section  II of
chapter 110 of the Births and Deaths  Registration.  Act,  No.41 of 1975 (amended), the place of Cremation
shall be mentioned as the  Crematorium of Yatinuwara  Pradeshiya Sabha;
(iv) If a post-mortem has been held under the Criminal Procedure Code in respect of the deceased person, the
certificate issued by the Coroner or by the Magistrate who held the Post Mortem, under section 41 (d) of
the Births  and Deaths  Registration Act, allowing the cremation of such dead body  ;
(v) If the dead body is in respect of a still-birth, the certificate issued under section 41 (1) of the Births and
Deaths Registration Act .
5. The perfected application when handed over to the Chairman or to an Officer authorized by him shall by registered
according to the order of receipt  of applications and  permit shall be issued giving the time of cremation in such order of
receipt of applications.
6. For the cremation of a dead body a cremation fee of Rs. 7,000 shall be charged,  if it is within the Pradeshiya sabha
authority area and when it is outside the authority area fees to be shall be Rs. 7500.
7. If the dead body is not to be accepted for cremation the  applicant shall be informed of  such refusal immediately
giving  reasons.
8. The dead bodies shall be handed over for cremation only  during  6.00 a.m to 6.00 p.m. daily,  Cremation beyond
this time period shall not be permitted.
9.  A dead body shall be handed over for  Cremation  at the time mentioned in the application or before that time. The
Pradeshiya Sabha shall not be held responsible for any inconvenience or loss caused due to any delay in handing over a
dead body.
10. The Crematorium Keeper shall maintain in every crematorium a register containing particulars such as  applicants
have and address,  have of deceased person and his address where he was residing before his death, the relationship
between the applicant and the deceased person and the date and time of cremation.
11. If a cremation of dead body is distupted during the time of cremation due to any mechanical defect, the applicant
shall agree to take any other alternative action in respect of such dead body on the advice of the Chairman.
12. The applicant or all  Authorized Representative or agent of the applicant shall take over the ashes of a dead body
before expiry of  21 days from the date of cremation. The ashes not taken over within the specified period shall be buried in
a common pit and nobody has any right to take legal action against to Pradeshiya Sabha for such an action.
13. It is an offence to enter in to the premises of the crematorium without the permission of the Crematorium Keeper
of the Chairman. However, a person or persons who accompany a permit holder for a cremation of corpse and presumed to
have received the required permission to enter the crematorium premises.

14. No person shall behave in any way to cause bread, of law and order or peace within the promises, of the
crematorium or damage the property of the Pradeshiya Sabha found within such premises or shall not obstruct the crematorium-
Keeper or his assistant in carrying out their duties. The permit holder shall be held responsible if such a damage is caused and
whenever such a loss or damage is caused, the permit holder shall be liable to pay the value of such damage when he is duly
informed, after assessing such damages : and if he defaults payment of such damages, it is lawful to summon him before a
court of law having jurisdiction and recover the damages as a fine.
15. No decoration shall be carried out within the crematorium premises without the written permission of the
Pradeshiya Sabha.
16. If the crematorium is to be kept closed for certain period of time for attending to any maintenance work and
repairs, a notice in respect of such work shall be exhibited in a prominently visible place in the Crematorium.
17. The violation of any of the provisions of these By-laws is an offence and when convicted in a court of law having
jurisdiction, the maximum fine that may be imposed for such violation, or when such viloation or contravention is committed
continuously and on conviction, or after delivering a written notice by the Chairman or by an  Officer authorized by him
drawing attention for such continuous violation, the maximum additional fine imposable for each day of continuing such
violation respectively be as defined under Sub-section 122 (2) the Pradeshiya Sabha Act, No. 15 of 1987.
18. In these By-laws unless the context otherwise requires :
(i) “Sabhawa” means,  Pradeshiya Sabha of Yatinuwara ;
(ii) “Chairman” means, the person appointed  as Chairman for the Yatinuwara Pradeshiya Sabha, according to
the provisions of written Law prevailing at the time ;
(iii) “Dead body” means, a dead body of a human being, or part of dead body, or a human body of a still birth ;
(iv) “Crematorium Keeper” means, a person appointed to be in-charge of a crematorium.
In the event of any inconsistency in the Sinhala and English Texts of these By-laws, the Sinhala Text shall prevail.
