THE DRAFT  ON  BY-LAWS  ON  PARKING  OF  THREE-WHEELERS
1. These By-laws may be cited as By-laws of the Agalawatta Pradeshiya Sabha relating to Parking of Three-
wheelers.
2. These By-laws shall be enacted for regulating, controlling and levying charges for parking of Three-wheelers
within the area of authority of Pradeshiya Sabha.
3. (1) The specified places for parking of Three-wheelers and the Maximum Number of Three-wheelers to be parked
at each park shall be decided on a resolution passed by the Pradeshiya Sabha and such decisions shall come into operation
from the date of publication of them in the Gazette or from a date specified therein.
This Gazette Extraordinary can be downloaded from www.documents.gov.lk
N.B.—Gazette Extraordinary Series No. 1801/1 ends with No. 1801/43.

3. (2) If the Pradeshiya Sabha is of opinion that the places and the Number of Three-wheelers to be parked therein,
decided under Section 3(1) above shall be amended, it may be done by a resolution passed by the Pradeshiya Sabha and such
amendment shall come in to operation from the date of publication of them in the Gazette or from a date specified therein.
3. (3)  The Pradeshiya Sabha shall seek recommendations of the Committee comprising of the members referred to in
the Schedule one in order to decide places of the Three-wheeler parks and its rangers and the miximum number of Three-
wheelers that can be parked in each park or to amend such decisions.
3. (4) A board shall be fixed at each park in order to identify the places to park Three-wheelers in terms of the Section
3(1) above and the code number of the relevant park shall be displayed in the board.
The maximum number of Three-wheelers that can be parked shall be displayed in the board in terms of Section 3(2).
3. (5) The Three-wheelers shall be properly parked and the manner in which they are to be parked shall  be marked
by an authorized Officer of the Pradeshiya Sabha with a white or yellow line of 4 inches in width.
4. It shall be legal to issue a license in the manner indicated in the Schedule Two of this By-law for parking Three-
wheelers by the Chairman and to impose charges and collect charges for such purpose by the Pradeshiya Sabha.
5. When issuing licenses for Three-wheelers for the purpose of parking in a Three-wheeler park priority shall be
given to the Three-wheelers owned by the residents in the closest proximity.
6. (1) For the purpose of parking a Three-wheeler in the Three-wheeler park nearest to the permanent residence, a
license shall be obtained from the Pradeshiya Sabha making an application as per specimen application form mentioned in the
Schedule Three and the application form shall be obtained from on payment of Rs. 30. This fee for the application may from
time to time be amended on a resolution passed by the Pradeshiya Sabha.
6. (2) In case where the driver 1 the owner the photo copies of the following documents of such person and in case
where the driver is a hired person, photocopies of such documents of both hired driver and the owner shall be accompanied
with the application. Such application shall be in the manner mentioned in the Schedule Three.
(i) The National Identity Card and the Valid Driving License of the owner ;
(ii) Certificate of Registration of the Three-wheeler ;
(iii) The National Identity Card and the Valid License of the hired driver.
6. (3) A register shall be maintained in respect of Three-wheelers which have been obtained licenses issued by the
Pradeshiya Sabha, and when cancelled such license it should be recorded with red ink.
6. (4) The Pradeshiya Sabha may issue licenses on a monthly, quarterly or annual basis for the purpose of parking
Three-wheelers.
6. (5) A photo copy of the valid license shall  be displayed on the windscreen of the Three-wheeler which can be seen
clearly.
6. (6) The right side of the Three-wheeler shall be blocked to prevent the passengers from entering or alighting from
that side.
6. (7) The license shall be issued in the name of the registered owner of the Three-wheeler.
6. (8) The Three-wheeler owner shall renew the license two weeks before the expiry of such license.

6. (9) In terms of  Subsection 6(4) above license fee imposed on the monthly, quarterly or annual basis shall be
decided by the Pradeshiya Sabha and such fee shall be charged with effect from the date when such decision is published in
the Gazette or such a date specified therein.
6. (10) In terms of By-law 4 Three-wheelers shall not be parked or entered into a Three-wheeler park unless the
Three-wheeler possesses a valid license issued by the Pradeshiya Sabha.
6. (11) Notwithstanding the possession of a valid license issued, a Police Officer or the Chairman or an Officer who
has been authorized to by him may order that a Three-wheeler be removed temporarily from the park, and this license shall not
be an obstruction to such order.
7. No other vehicle shall enter or kept parked in a Three-wheeler park.
8. Neither Three-wheeler nor any other vehicle shall be parked in the manner of blocking the entrance and the exit of
the Three-wheeler Park.
9. No any other repairs shall be done in the Three-wheeler Park other than the repairs relating to removal of the
Three-wheeler.
10. No Three-wheeler shall be parked in any other place awaiting hires other than the relevant parking place for
which the license has been obtained.
11. At the time licensed Three-wheelers have been parked in an Three-wheeler park the drivers shall not call and
allow outsiders to come into the park and loiter in the park.
12. No person shall use drugs or conduct immorality or commit any oppressive act in a Three-wheeler park and it is
a responsibility of the persons authorized to use the park, not to allow any person to commit such acts.
13. If there are any complaints from the public regarding any misbehaving at Three-wheeler parks by any person
who possesses a license, the Pradeshiya Sabha reserves the authority to inquire into those complaints and to cancel his
license if found guilty for the charges.
14.  The driver or the owner of the Three-wheeler shall produce the original copy of the valid license on demand by
the Chairman or an Officer authorized for this purpose by him or a Police Officer.
15. Violation of any Provision stipulated in the By-laws in an offence and after being convicted by a competent court
shall be liable of a fine not exceeding a sum of Rupees Seven Hundred and Fifty (Rs. 750.) In an occassion of repeating such
violation after being convicted by a competent court or after being handed over a written notice drawing the attention of the
offence of such violation by the Chairman or an Officer authorized for this purpose by him, shall be liable of an additional fine
not exceeding a sum of Rupees Two Hundred and Fifty (Rs. 250) for each and every day of committing such offence
repeatedly.
16. Notwithstanding anything mentioned contrary in these By-laws by a resolution passed by the Pradeshiya
Sabha, these By-laws may be amended, repeal by enacting new By-laws or enact additional By-laws.
17. Interpretation.
“Pradeshiya Sabha” means, Agalawatta Pradeshiya Sabha.
“Chairman” means, the Chairman of the Agalawatta Pradeshiya Sabha.
“An Officer Authorized for the purpose” means, any Officer of the Pradeshiya Sabha who has been authorized
by the Chairman.

“An Authorized Officer who has been delegated by the Chairman for the purpose” means, any Officer hold a
permanent post of the Pradeshiya Sabha who has been authorized by the Chairman in writing.
“Area of Authority” means, limits of Agalawatta Pradeshiya Sabha constituted under the Pradeshiya Sabha
Act, No. 15 of 1987.
“Three-wheeler” means, the Three-wheeler parked with the expectation for a hire.
“Hired Driver” means, the owner who drives the Three-wheeler or the person employed for driving by the
owner of the Three-wheeler.
“Parking place” means, Three-wheeler Park determined on a resolution passed by the Agalawatta Pradeshiya
Sabha.
“Monthly” means each month starting from 1st consisting 28, 30 or 31 days as applicable.
“Quarterly” means, successive three months starting from 1st of January up to 31st of December consisting
of the number of days as defined in “Monthly”.
“Annually” means, a calender year starting from 1st of January and ending on 31st of December.
“Police Officer” means, the same interpretation as defined under Section 104 in chapter 53 of the Police
Ordinance No. 16 of 1865.
“Other Vehicles” means, the same interpretation as defined in the Motor Vehicle Ordinance.
“Registered Owner” means, the same interpretation as defined in the Motor Vehicle Ordinance.
“Act of Nuisance” means, the same interpretation as defined in the Nuisance Ordinance.
18. If there is an inconsistency between Sinhala and Tamil texts of these By-laws, the Sinhala text shall prevail.
THE  FIRST  SCHEDULE
By-law No. 3.3
The Road Committee to be appointed shall consist of the member mentioned below.
1. The Chairman of the Committee shall the Chairman of the Pradeshiya Sabha and the Secretary shall be the
Revenue Inspector of the Pradeshiya Sabha.
2. The Secretary and the Technical Officer of the Pradeshiya Sabha.
3. The Executive Engineer of the Road Development Authority or a representative nominated by him.
4. The Chairman of the Provincial Road Development Authority or a representative nominated by him.
5. The Chairman of the Road Passenger Transport Authority, Kalutara or a representative nominated by him.
6. The Chairman of the Sri Lanka Transport Board or a representative nominated by him.
7. The Officer in Charge of the Traffic in the Police Stations coming under the jurisdiction of the Pradeshiya
Sabha or a representative from each station nominated by them.
