RULE MADE UNDER SECTION 45 (CHAPTER 190) OF THE POST OFFICE ORDINANCE
Money Orders issued with effect from 01.01.2018 to 31.12.2018
 1. A money order is valid for a period of six months from the last day of the month of its issue. under exceptional 
circumstances, the payment of a Lapsed Money Order is considered up to two years from the last day of the month 
of its issue. however, a Money Order cannot be revalidated or refunded after lapsing of two years from the last day 
of the month of its issue and the value of such Money order is credited to Government revenue.
 
 2. however, under the following exceptional circumstances, the value of Money Orders lapsed two years could be 
considered for refund from Government revenue subject to the recovery of relevant charges.
 
 I. Money Order being produced in a court of law
 II. the Money Order being retained in a rent Control Board on legal grounds.
 III. Payee of the Money Order being out of the country (the passport should be produced)
	 IV.	 Payee	 being	in	a	very	bad	state	of	health	for	a	long	period	(should	be	supported	by	a	Medical		Certificate	from	
a	Government	Medical	Officer)	
 3. If the Money Orders related to 2 (I) and (II) above, cannot be produced within three months from the date of 
publishing of this gazette	notification,	the	details	of	such	Money	Orders	with	the	related	case	numbers	etc.	should	
be sent to the controller of Money Order services in writing. If it is unable to produce Money Orders, it shall also 
be informed in writing. 
 4. But the Money orders  retaining by Court orders for the cases pending in the Court now or other legal  requirements, 
Money Orders to be retained in relations to the cases of a rent Control Board and the Money Orders subjected to 
disciplinary inquires held at the department and Ministerial levels are not covered by this.
