“4. (1) Every person who enters a National Park, Marine National Park, Nature Reserve or Marine Reserve 
(hereinafter categorically referred to as a “National Reserve”) falling within Category I, Category II or Category 
III as set out in Schedule I hereto, shall pay an entry fee at such rates as specified in Column II or Column III as the 
case may be of Schedule II hereto in respect of the categories of tourists as specified in Column I of that Schedule.
	 	 (2)	 The Director-General or an officer authorized by him shall issue a permit to a person who pays the 
required fee to enter any such National Reserve as specified under Category I, Category II or Category III of 
Schedule I hereto and such permit shall contain the terms and conditions subject to which it is issued, which terms 
and conditions shall be as specified in the Schedule VIII hereto.
	 	 (3)	 A permit issued under preceding sub regulation (2) shall be valid only to enter the relevant National 
Reserve on a single occasion.
	 	 (4)	 The Director-General may from time to time designate any National Reserve specified in these 
regulations to carry out tourist activities during daytime or daytime and nighttime. The period of time for visiting 
a National Reserve during day time shall be from 06.00 a.m. to 6.00 p.m. and the period of time for visiting a 
National Reserve during day and night shall be from 6.00 a.m. to 11.00 p.m.
	 	 (5)	 Any tourist or a group of tourists who intends to obtain accommodation in a circuit bungalow or 
a campsite specified in Schedule III hereto, shall pay a fee for such accommodation and services as set out in 
Schedule IV hereto and if such circuit bungalow or a campsite is situated within a National Reserve, shall pay a 
fee for entering vehicles to the National Reserve as set out in Schedule V hereto.
	 	 (6)	 A circuit bungalow or a campsite shall be reserved for a tourist group only for a maximum of three 
consecutive days.
  (7)  The Director-General may from time to time vary the category of a circuit bungalow specified under 
Schedule III hereto and he may also include new circuit bungalows to the said Schedule and when doing so, he 
may determine the category to which such circuit bungalows belong.
	 	 (8)	 A tourist who intends to engage in any activity specified under Schedule VI hereto, shall pay the 
corresponding fee specified thereunder.
	 	 (9)	 For the purpose of charging fees under these regulations, tourists referred to in sub regulation (1) 
are categorized as specified under Schedule VII hereto.
	 	 (10)	 For the purpose of ensuring that the provision of visitor services to any National Reserve shall 
not damage the ecosystem thereof as specified in section 2C of the Fauna and Flora Protection Ordinance 
(Chapter 469) and also for the purpose of managing the negative environmental impacts that may be caused 
by tourist activities, the Director-General may from time to time identify any period of time of any year as a 
“tourist season” taking into consideration the following matters: -
	( a)	  statistics on visiting of National Reserves by tourists;
	( b)	 nature of environmental impacts that may be caused by tourist activities; and
	( c)	 any other matter the Director-General may deem necessary under the relevant 
circumstances.
	 	( 11)	 All the charges specified in regulation 4 shall be subject to the taxes and charges imposed by the 
Government from time to time.

SCHEDULE VIII
[regulation 4(2)]
Conditions Applicable to Permits Issued
1.    The permit shall not be transferable.
2.   Persons intending to enter any National Reserve shall prior to entering the same, provide all the details required including 
the name, number of identity documents (NIC/Passport/NDL), address and contact  number to the entry point of the 
relevant National Reserve.
3.  Any person not possessing any identity document shall not be permitted to enter any National Reserve. 
4. When there are more than ten persons, each person shall have to apply for a separate Permit. In calculating the 
ten persons, the driver of the vehicle shall be counted as one person.
5. The Permit shall be available for inspection by officers of the Department of Wildlife Conservation  at all times.
6.  The permit holders shall always remain with the officer assigned to them by the officer-in-charge of the relevant National 
Reserve.
7.    The speed limit of a vehicle within a National Reserve shall not exceed 20 kilometres per hour.
8.  Every person who has been granted permission to enter a National Reserve specified in these regulations shall ensure not 
to cause any nuisance or disturbance in any manner within such National Reserve.
9.  Every tourist service provider who provides tourist services including vehicles to tourists who intend to visit any National 
Reserve specified in these regulations, shall ensure that the visitors obtaining his  services are complying with the 
above mentioned conditions. If any visitor fails to comply with such  conditions and the relevant service provider is 
unable to provide a satisfactory explanation for such failure at any inquiry conducted by the Director-General relating 
to same, the Director-General may prohibit vehicles of such service provider from entering the National Reserve for a 
period of not less than six months from the date of such inquiry.
