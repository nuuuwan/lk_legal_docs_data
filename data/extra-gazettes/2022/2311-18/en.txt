ImPORTS  AND  ExPORTS  CONTROL  ACT, No. 1 Of 1969  
IN terms of the powers vested in me by section 20 read together with sub-section 4(1) and section 14 of the Imports and 
exports (control) Act, No. 1 of 1969, as amended by Act, No. 48 of 1985 and Act, No. 28 of 1987, I, ranil wickremesinghe, 
the minister of Finance, economic stabilization and National Policies, promulgate following regulations.

colombo 01,

 Regulations
1. These regulations may be cited as the “Imports Control Regulations on Payment Terms No. 25 of 2022”. 
2. These regulations shall enter into force with effect from december 21, 2022.
3.
 The special Import License and Payment regulations, No. 1 of 2011, published in the Gazette extraordinary No. 1739/3 
dated January 02, 2012, as amended by the Imports control regulations on Payment Terms No. 17 of 2022, published 
in the Gazette Extraordinary No. 2300/12 dated October 05, 2022 is hereby further amended.

4.2 Importation of maize (HS Code of 1005.90.00) under the Open Account / consignment Account Payment terms 
for the purpose of manufacturing of poultry feed;
i by registered poultry feed manufacturers on the recommendation of the secretary, ministry in charge 
of subject of Agriculture obtained prior to arrival of such goods at Port / Airport in sri Lanka, as 
authorized by the regulation No. 5 of the Imports control regulations on Payment Terms No. 15 
of 2022, published in the Gazette No. 2297/79 dated september 16, 2022, as amended is hereby 
extended until January 15, 2023.
ii by any other importer except those who have referred in regulation No. 4.2 (i) above, is hereby 
allowed subject to recommendation of the secretary, ministry in charge of subject of Agriculture, 
obtained prior to arrival of such goods at the Ports / Airports of sri Lanka during the period of 
december 21, 2022 to January 15, 2023.
 4.3  The director General, sri Lanka customs shall consult the secretary, ministry in charge of subject of Agriculture  
 and controller General of Imports and exports on any importation made in violation of the regulation Nos. 4.2 (i)  
 and (ii) above.
5. The controller General of Imports and exports shall issue appropriate “Operational Instructions” to the director General 
of customs and the licensed banks for effective implementation of these regulations.
