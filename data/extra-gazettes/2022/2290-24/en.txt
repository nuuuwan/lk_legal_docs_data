THE  EXCISE  ORDINANCE
Excise Notification No. 05/2022
soft  liquor  liceNses
By Virtue of the powers vested in me by section 32 read with section 25 of the excise ordinance (chapter 52), i, ranil 
Wickremesinghe, Minister of finance, economic stabilization and National Policies authorize the commissioner General 
of excise, under the authority vested in him by section 7. (a) of the excise ordinance, to authorize only for the consumption 
of fermented liquor including wine and beer (soft liquor) inside the hotels and restaurants registered under the sri lanka 
tourism development authority which provide food and beverages to facilities tourists and the tourism institutions which 
provide accommodation and services situated in tourism zones identified by the Sri Lanka Tourism Development Authority as a 
flexible strategic approach which is required to promote the tourism industry under the below mentioned rules and restrictions.
       these rules shall come into force on july 28, 2022.

Rules
 1. in similar to the rules stipulated under section 18 of the excise ordinance (chapter 52), this license is applicable and 
authorized only for the consumption of fermented liquor including wine and beer (soft liquor) within the relevant 
premises.
 2. this license shall be applicable for 
 (a) the institutions registered under the sri lanka tourism development authority and recommended by the authority 
as mentioned specifically in schedule I and:
 (b) the tourism institutions situated in the tourism zones identified by the Sri Lanka Tourism Development Authority 
which provide food and beverages and accommodation facilities recommended by the authority.
for the consumption of soft liquor in the relevant premises only.
 3. Permission shall only be granted to consume soft liquor inside the approved border limits of the premises which is 
approved at the time of applying.
 4. A valid Certificate of Business Registration as per the Business Names Act No. 7 of 1987 or in case of a Company, 
Company Registration Certificate certified by a notary public shall be provided for a given time period for the place 
of applying which provide food and beverages and services to facilitate tourists.
 5. An affidavit of the applicant stating that the applicant has not been convicted of any offense under the Penal Code or 
the Excise Ordinance or any other offense of character within the last five (5) years and a report obtained from the 
officer-in-charge of the police station in the area where the licensed premises is located stating that the applicant has 
not been convicted of any offense under the Penal Code or the Excise Ordinance within  the last five (5) years should 
be submitted.
 6. When applying for a new license or when a license holder applies for renewal of the license, a non-refundable application 
fee of rs. 5,000.00 should be paid and proof of payment should be submitted.
 7. the license fee for this shall be as per schedule ii and any license shall be valid for a maximum period of one calendar 
year. however, new licenses issued in the middle of the year shall be issued on payment of the full license fee for that 
year for the remaining period.
 8. Purchasing soft liquor from other than an authorized dealer is prohibited. accounts of soft liquor brought into and 
consumed in the premises concerned shall be maintained in such forms as may be approved by the commissioner 
General of excise.
 9. the ownership of the premises for which the license is granted shall not be changed or transferred without the consent 
of the sri lanka tourism development authority and the commissioner General of excise. such change will be 
effective after the approval of the sri lanka tourism development authority and commissioner General of excise 
and after paying an amount of ten lakh rupees.
 10. from the date of commencement of these rules, all persons who intend to obtain the relevant approval shall apply as 
per the application form specified in Schedule III, and the Commissioner General of Excise shall proceed to issue the 
special permit only through that form.
 11. if any complaint is received, the relevant authorization, refusal or cancellation shall be subject to the discretion of the 
commissioner  General of excise after an investigation.
