REGuLATIONS
1. These regulations may be cited as the “Imports Control Regulations on Payment Terms No. 10 of 2022”. 
2. The imports Control regulations on Payment Terms No. 07 of 2022, published in the Gazette Extraordinary No. 2278/21 
dated May 06, 2022 as amended by the Gazette Extraordinary No. 2282/22 dated May 31, 2022 is hereby further amended.
3. The regulation No. 4 of the Gazette Extraordinary No. 2278/21 dated May 06, 2022 shall be substituted and read as 
follows.
4. 4. regulation No. 3 (i) (b) of the Special import License and Payment regulations, No. 1 of 2011, published in the 
Gazette Extraordinary No. 1739/3 dated january 02, 2012, shall be amended that the Open account Payment Terms 
or Consignment account Terms shall only be allowed to;
(a) exporters, who are settling their payments for importation of goods, which are necessary for their export purposes 
from,
(i) The export proceeds in their Business foreign Currency accounts (BfCas) or accounts maintained 
in the Off-Shore Banking unit (OBu);
or
(ii) foreign currency borrowings from person resident outside Sri Lanka (i.e., Overseas lenders) held in 
their external Commercial Borrowing accounts;
 or
(iii) Proceeds of loans obtained for working capital requirement (i.e., short term borrowings) from Overseas 
lenders as permitted by the regulations issued under the foreign exchange act, No. 12 of 2017 (fea); 
or
(iv) Proceeds of foreign currency received from non-resident investors into the share capital held in their 
Special foreign Currency account (SfCa), investee opened and maintained in terms of the Directions 
issued under the fea.
     (b) Local suppliers (indirect exporters), who import and supply goods for those exporters, receiving payments 
in the foreign currency and making payment for importation of goods for such supply using;
(i) The foreign currency so received to their Business foreign Currency accounts (BfCas) or 
accounts maintained in the Off-Shore Banking unit (OBu); 
or
(ii) Proceeds of foreign currency received in terms of provisions in the Paragraph (a) (ii), (iii) and 
(iv) referred above.
(c) Local manufacturers, who import goods as raw materials which are not available locally but required for 
manufacturing of their products with minimum local value addition of 30% or more, subject to a letter of

Terms No. 07 of 2022 to the Director General, Sri Lanka Customs.
6. The Controller General, Department of imports and exports Control shall issue required “Operating instructions” 
to the Director General, Sri Lanka Customs and licensed banks for effective implementation of these regulations.
7. The Director General of Customs shall act in accordance with the provisions of the Customs Ordinance on any 
importation made in violation of the above regulations. However, The Director General, Sri Lanka Customs shall 
consult the Controller General, Department of imports and exports Control on any importation made in violation 
of the regulation No. 4(c) of the Gazette Extraordinary No. 2278/21 dated May 06, 2022 as amended above.
