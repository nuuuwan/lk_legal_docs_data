SChedule
The decisions made by the Wages Board for the Motor Transport Trade, and set out in the Schedule to the notification published 
in Gazette No. 9667 of 14th February 1947 as varied from time to time and last varied by notification in Gazette Extraordinary 
No. 2108/35 of 30th January 2019, shall be further varied by the substitution of the following New part II of that schedule.
Part II
Minimum wage
CLASS Of  wORkERS
Class ‘A’ workers - 
drivers of omnibuses licensed to carry over 22 passengers.
Class ‘b’ worker -
drivers of omnibuses licensed to carry 22 passengers and under, drivers of ambulances and drivers of lorries and vans (other 
than those by an estate and used solely for internal transport within the estate) with a licensed payload of over 20 cwt.
Class ‘C’ worker -
drivers of hiring cars and cabs, drivers of lorries and vans (other than those owned by an estate and used solely for internal 
transport within the estate) with a licensed payload of 20 cwt. and under, drivers of three wheeler and drivers of hearses.
Class ‘D’ worker -
drivers of tractors with trailers used for transport purpose, drivers of lorries with trailers (including those of the scammel 
horse type but excluding those lorries with trailers or tractors owned by an estate and used solely for internal transport within 
the estate).
Class ‘E’ worker -
drivers of the following vehicles owned by an estate and used solely within the estate namely (a) lorries and (b) tractors 
with trailers.
Class ‘f’ worker -
conductors, clerks, cashiers, ticket - clerks and booking clerks employed in omnibuses.
Class ‘G’ worker -
cleaners and porters of lorries with a licensed pay laod of over 20 cwt.
Class ‘h’ worker -
cleaners and porters of lorries with a licensed pay load of 20 cwt. and under, omnibuses, hiring cars, cabs, vans, ambulances 
and hearses.
Class ‘I’ worker -
omnibus checkers or time-keepers.
Class ‘J’ worker -
omnibus inspectors and omnibus stand supervisors.
Class ‘k’ worker -
All other workers in the motor Transport Trade excluding workers referred to in the preceding items.
In the foregoing decisions -
(a) “cleaners” means workers employed (other than in clerical capacities) in connection with the maintenance of the 
cleanliness of lorries, omnibuses, hiring cars, cabs, vans, ambulances or hearses.
