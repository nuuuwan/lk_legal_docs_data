Regulations
1. These regulations may be cited as the Drug Dependent Persons (licensing of Private Treatment Centres)
Regulations No. 01 of 2010.
2. No persons shall establish a Private Treatment Centre for the purpose of providing care and rehabilitation
for drug dependent persons, unless such person has obtained a license for such purpose in terms of these regulations.
3. Any person who intends to establish a Private Treatment Centre for the care and rehabilitation of drug
dependent persons may make an application for a license     in writing, to the National Dangerous Drug Control
Board (hereinafter referred to as the “NDDCB”) in the format set out in Form of Schedule 1 hereto, accompanied
by an application fee of rupees one thousand :
Provided that, any person who has already established a Private Treatment Centre for the care and rehabilitation
of drug dependent persons prior  to the coming into operation of this regulation may make an application within six months
from the date of operation of these regulations, to the NDDCB in the format setout in Form 1 of  Schedule 1 hereto,
accompanied by an application fee of Rupees One Thousand for a license to continue to maintain such existing Private
Treatment Centre.
4. (1) The NDDCB may after satisfying itself that the applicant has complied with the requirements set out in
the application, upon the payment of a license fee of Rupees Five Thousand, issue to such applicant, a license within
one month from the date of receipt of an application, authorizing the place specified in the application as a Private
Treatment Centre.
(2) A license issued under this regulation shall be valid for a period of two years from the date of issue of
such license. A licencee may one month before the date of expiry of a license issued to him under this regulation,
make an application in writing to the NDDCB in the format set out in Form 2 of Schedule 1 hereto, for renewal of such
license in each two years.
(3) Every application for renewal of a license issued under this regulation shall be accompanied by a fee
of Rupees Five Hundred.
(4) The NDDCB may after satisfying itself with the information furnished by a licencee in an application for
renewal of a license and upon the payment of a renewal fee of Rupees Two Thousand, may, within one month from
the date of such application  renew  such license for another period of two years.

REGULATIONS made by the President under subsection (3) of Section 7 of the Drug Dependent Persons (Treatment and
Rehabilitation) Act, No. 54 of 2007 read with paragraph (2) of Article 44 of Constitution.
MAHINDA  RAJAPAKSA,
President.
Colombo 07,
04th May, 2010.
Regulations
1. These regulations may be cited as the Treatment Centres (Assessment Panels) Regulations No. 02 of 2010.
2. (1)  The Minister shall identify and appoint not more than ten persons possessing appropriate qualifications
and experience, in the fields of law or persons having experience or knowledge in the fields of physiological or social
problems connected with drug dependence to serve as members of Assessment Panels for the purpose of these regulations.
   (2) Whenever it is necessary, the NDDCB shall constitute panels and nominate not more than three persons
to function  as an Assessment Panel from among the persons appointed by the Minister, for the purpose of these regulations.
3. (1) The functions of any Assessment Panel constituted and nominated under regulation 2, shall be—
(a) to assess the level of drug dependency of persons who seek admission to any Treatment Centre
designated   or licensed under the Drug Dependent Persons (Treatment and Rehabilitation) Act, No. 54
of 2007 (hereinafter referred to as the “Treatment Centre”) ;
(b) to assess the level of motivation for drugs of persons who seek admission to any Treatment Centre ;
(c) to assess the physical and mental health status of persons who seek admission to any Treatment Centre ;
(d) to ascertain the family background of the persons who seek admission to any Treatment Centre ;
(e) to assess the level of desire for the use of drugs, behavioral changes, improvement of relationship
with the other family members and attitude to society, of the persons who receive treatments in
Treatment Centre ;
(f) to recommend to the Director of any Treatment Centre, the admission of any person to the Treatment
Centres ;
(g) to report regularly to the Director of any Treatment Centre  of the condition of persons receiving
treatments from Treatment Centres ;
(h) to inspect the reports maintained by the Treatment Centres relating to the follow up programmes conducted
by such Treatment Centres and make recommendations to the NDDCB in order to improve the quality
of such programmes ;
(i) to recommend to the NDDCB the discharge of any inmate from any Treatment Centre, if the criteria as
formulated by the NDDCB for discharge of such inmates, have been fulfilled.

(2) Any Assessment Panel constituted and nominated by the NDDCB shall after discharging the function
assigned to it by the NDDCB, make its observations and recommendations or submit any report on such matter
to the NDDCB within three weeks from the date of constitution of such Panel or within such other period of time as
may be specified by the NDDCB at the constitution of the Panel.
4. Every member of an Assessment Panel shall have the power to :-
(a) enter any Treatment Centre at all reasonable hours of the day after prior notice ;
(b) examine any person who seeks admission to any Treatment Centre or any inmate receiving treatments in
any such Centre ;
(c) interview family members of any person who seeks admission to any Treatment Centre or any inmate
receiving treatment in any such centre and to inspect medical reports or any other document of any such
person or inmate ;
(d) inspect any report or other documents maintained by any Treatment Centre.
5. Every member appointed under regulation 2 shall unless he vacates office earlier by death, resignation or removal,
hold office for a term of three years from the date of his appointment and shall unless  he has been removed, be eligible for
reappointment  :
Provided that, no member shall be reappointed for more than two terms.
6. The Assessment Panels constituted by the NDDCB shall seize to exist when the particular function
assigned to such Panel has been discharged to the satisfaction of the NDDCB.
