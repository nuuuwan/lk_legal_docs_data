(read with   Industrial Disputes  Special Provisions  Act,  No.
37 of 1968),  has appointed  me as the Arbitrator  and referred
the following dispute for  settlement  by Arbitration.
“whether the deduction of 1% from the annual salary
increment of 802 employees of Unilever Ceylon Limited whose
names are referred to in the attached Schedule and withholding
of their service increments and reduction of their annual leave
by the Company having considered that the period from
05.05.1998 to 20.5.1998 (excluding weekends) during which
the said employees had joined the strike as members of the
Commercial and Industrial Workers Union as no pay
absenteeism is justified, and if not, to what relief the said
employees are entitled".
AW ARD
The Applicant Party of the First Part (hereinafter called
the Applicant) stated that the above reference was initially
made by the Hon. Minister to the Hon. Arbitrator Mr. A. N. D.
Balasuriya and later the Hon. Minister deemed it expedient to
revoke the said order and refer the same dispute to the present
Arbitrator to settle the dispute by arbitration.
The Applicant Union stated that the company had
introduced a new system of work to which the workers
objected and on 05.05.1998 the workers struck work and the
Union had informed the Commissioner of  Labour of the strike
action. Subsequently on 20.05.1998 they had arrived at a
settlement which was embodied in a Memorandum of
Settlement under section 12(1) of the Industrial Disputes Act
and the workers returned to work. Subsequently, a Collective
Agreement was signed on 25.09.1998.
The Union said further that the 1% annual salary increment
and the service increment due to the workers was not paid
even as at 01.01.1999 and the Company reduced the annual
leave component of the workers for the days on which they
had gone on strike considering those days as no pay leave.
The Union had made representations to the Commissioner
of Labour and the latter had advised the Company to pay the
annual leave increment and the annual leave.
The Union further submitted that what had been agreed
was that for the period of the strike no payment be made and
not to consider the days of the strike as no pay leave for the
purpose of determining the annual increment. The Union
contends that the withholding of the service increment and
the reduction of the annual leave by the Company by
considering the days of the strike period as days of no pay
leave is unreasonable and illegal.
The Union therefore prays that the Company be held to
be unjustified in considering the period of the strike from
05.05.1998 to 20.5.1998 as no pay absenteeism and order the
company to restore the 1% to the annual increment and also
restore the service increment and the annual leave.
The Respondent Party of the Second Part (hereinafter
called Unilever) stated that the workforce in the factory
resorted to strike action on 05.05.1998 and on 20.05.1998 the
strike was resolved amicably and a Memorandum of Settlement
was reached in terms of section 12(1) of the Industrial Dispute
Act. Unilever in its submissions stated that it was specifically
stipulated that the period of the strike from 05.05.1998 to
20.05.1998 will be treated as no pay. Subsequently a Collective
Agreement was signed between Unilever and the Applicant
Union on 25.09.1998.
In January 1999 when Unilever announced the Annual
Increments the Applicant Union raised a dispute on the ground
that Unilever had violated the Collective Agreement and the
terms of the Memorandum of Settlement, Since this dispute
could not be resolved this matter was referred to Arbitration
No. 2971 and since the Arbitrator was not available, it had
now been referred to the present Arbitrator.
Unilever states that the Union's argument has been that
Unilever had violated the terms and conditions laid down in
the Memorandum of Settlement signed under section 12(1)
of the Industrial Disputes Act.
Unilever further states that the Union cannot maintain
that there has been a breach of the provisions of the
Memorandum of Settlement and in any event it falls outside
the arbitration process as set out in the Industrial Disputes
Act.
Proceedings
This matter was taken up for inquiry on 28.09.2006 and
concluded on 04.11.2009 with written submissions being
tendered by both Parties.
In February 2007 Unilever filed a preliminary objection on
the basis that the reference to the arbitration is bad in law on
the ground that there is a difference in the words used in the
English version of the reference as against the Sinhala version.
Written submissions were filed by both Parties.
Unilever in filing their submissions stated that there is a
blatant error in the Sinhala and English versions of the
Reference. They argued that the Sinhala version states that
the Company had deducted 1% of the annual increment on
the basis of  'no pay leave" whereas the English version
describes it as 'no pay absenteeism".

The Arbitrator overruled this objection firstly on the
ground that according to the plain meaning in both versions
of the Reference, Unilever had deducted 1% of the annual
salary increment for the period 05.05.1998 to 20.05.1998
(excluding weekends) when the employees belonging to the
Applicant Union were away from work participating in a strike.
Secondly, the Arbitrator pointed out that Unilever had
confused itself in translating the term 'no pay leave' in the
Sinhala version of the reference into English as  'no pay leave'
as against 'no pay absenteeism' in the English version of the
Reference. It was also pointed out that Unilever refers to the
English version of the Reference as 'no pay absence' where
the connotation is very different from the meaning in 'no pay
absenteeism'.
 The arbitrator also pointed out that the simple meaning of
the word 'absenteeism' in any standard English dictionary
meant 'absenteeism' as staying away from the workplace
without permission and this would mean that such a person
would not be entitled to pay for these days. He also pointed
out that the simple meaning of  'leave' is 'being away from the
usual place of work where formal leave of absence has been
granted". Therefore 'no pay leave' will mean that a person is
away from the usual place of work on formal leave but without
pay as a specific of this leave.
Therefore the Arbitrator ruled that the Sinhala version 'no
pay leave' and English version 'no pay absenteeism' convey
the same meaning and that there is no error in the Sinhala and
English versions of the Reference. Therefore, the Reference
is not bad in law.
It was also pointed out by the Arbitrator that the Reference
does not presuppose the fact of the strike and both Parties
have admitted this. Also there is no presupposition in the
Reference that 1% had been deducted on the basis of the
strike.
It was also pointed out that the Reference is outside the
purview of the Arbitrator as he is a creation in terms of Section
4(1) of the Industrial Disputes Act and is appointed by the
Minister notwithstanding that the Parties to such dispute do
not consent to such reference. In the circumstances the
objections raised by Unilever were over ruled.
AW ARD
The main matter in dispute in the Reference made to
arbitration is the question of the period of the strike of the
employees between 05.05.1998 to 20.05.1998 (excluding
weekends). The question centered around the words 'no pay'
and 'no pay absenteeism'. In terms of clause 16 of the
Settlement under 12(1) of the Industrial Disputes Act, it was
intended that the period of the strike be treated as 'no pay'.
The question that arises is what the nature of the 'no pay' is.
Could it mean 'no pay' absence or 'no pay absenteeism'? In
both connotations, the reference is to absence during the
period of the strike. If this is so, was the period of the strike
authorized by the management as a period of absence or
not? In this situation if we refer to clause 8 of the Agreement
signed between Unilever and the Commercial and Industrial
Workers Union on 02.09.1998 which stipulates that those
who have been absent without due authority and on being
placed on no pay absence in the preceding year will only be
entitled to the annual increment of 3% as against the normal
annual increment of 4% from 1st January 1998.
It is clear from the evidence led and the submissions of
both Parties that the said period of absence was considered
as no pay amounting to no pay absence. It is clear that
these employees will be entitled only to 3% annual increment
due to their absence during the period of the strike resulting
in a 1% deduction in their annual salary increment.
One of the factors that transpired during this inquiry was
that not all the 802 employees listed in the reference were
employees who participated in the said strike. Neither Party
made any effort to clarify this position and inform the
Arbitrator of the real position. In these circumstances, it is
difficult to make an award since no details were given about
the service of these employees. Therefore Unilever is entitled
to act in terms of clause 9 of the Agreement to decide on
service increments in the case of these employees. This
could be determined in consultation with the Union and in
terms of clause 9 of the said Agreement.
Finally,  with regard to the reduction of the annual leave
of the workers who were on strike during the period
05.05.1998 to 20.05.1998, since Unilever did not consent to
or authorize the said strike they are entitled to deduct the
period of the strike from the annual leave of the striking
workmen.
In the circumstances the following awards are made:-
 1). That Unilever is entitled to the deduction of 1% from
the annual salary increment of the striking workers;
2). Unilever is within its rights to withold their service
increments and
3). Unilever is entitled to the reduction of the annual
leave of the workers who participated in the strike between
05.05.1998 and 20.05.1998.
These Awards should be implemented within one month
of the publication of these Awards in the Government Gazette
of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka.
        Dr. V . Irwin Jayasuriya, Attorney - at - law
        Arbitrator.
