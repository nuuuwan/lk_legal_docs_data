Ordinance
 1. this ordinance may be cited as the Institute of allergology and Immunology ordinance, No.02 of 2025 and shall 
come into operation with effect from18.02.2025.
 2. this ordinance shall apply to the Institute of allergology and Immunology of the university of sri Jayewardenepura 
(hereinafter referred to as the “Institute”) established by order made by the Minister under section 24a (1) of the act 
and published in Gazette Extraordinary No. 2424/58 of 2025.
 3. The Institute shall be affiliated to the University of Sri Jayewardenepura, Sri Lanka (hereinafter referred to as the 
“university”).

Part   I
Powers,  Duties   and  Functions  of  the  Institute 
 4. subject to the provisions of this ordinance, provisions of the act and any appropriate instrument made thereunder 
the Institute shall have the following powers, duties and functions:-
 (a) to initiate, facilitate and conduct basic and applied scientific research in the specialities of Allergology, 
Immunology, Molecular Medicine and biomedical engineering to better understand, treat and ultimately 
prevent allergic, immunologic and infectious diseases in sri Lanka and strengthen such research;
 (b) to admit students to the Institute;
 (c) upon the recommendations of the Institute and with the concurrence of the university and with the approval of 
the commission, to provide instructions to the students of the Institute and to conduct training and research in 
the major specialities of allergology, Immunology, Molecular Medicine and bio Medical engineering;
 (d) with the approval of the council, to conduct examinations for the purposes of ascertaining the persons who 
have acquired proficiency in the major specialities of Allergology, Immunology, Molecular Medicine and Bio 
Medical engineering;
 (e) with the approval of the council and with the concurrence of the commission, to determine bachelor’s 
Degrees, Postgraduate Degrees, Postgraduate Diplomas, Higher Diplomas, Postgraduate Certificates, and other 
academic distinctions to be awarded to the eligible students who have pursued above mentioned approved 
courses of study and who have passed the examinations of the Institute in the major specialities of allergology, 
Immunology, Molecular Medicine and biomedical engineering;
 (f) to recommend to the Council, students who having passed the examinations and having satisfied such other 
conditions as the university may, in consultation with the Institute, prescribed by by-laws, eligible for the 
award of-
 (i) bachelor’s degrees;
 (ii) Postgraduate diplomas;
 (iii) higher diplomas;
 (iv) Postgraduate certificates;
 (v) Master of Philosophy (MPhil);
 (vi) Masters with course work and a research component;
 (vii) doctor of Philosophy (Phd);
 (viii) Doctor of Medicine (MD) in Allergology and Clinical Immunology with Board Certification; and
 (ix) any other appropriate academic distinctions of the university;
 (g) to cooperate with universities or Institutions in sri Lanka or abroad having similar powers, duties and functions 
of the Institute by way of exchange of teachers, scholars, students or otherwise;
 (h) to recognize examinations passed, qualifications gained and the period of learning or study pursued by persons 
seeking admission to or by students of the higher educational Institutions, higher educational Institutes or 
any other d egree a warding I nstitutions as being equivalent to such examinations and periods of learning or 
study in the Institute or such part thereof as may be recognized by by-laws and to withdraw such recognition 
at any time;

P art  II
T he  D irector  and  O ther  O fficers  of  the  Institute
 5. The Director and the holder of any other post specified in this Ordinance as an Officer shall be the officers of the 
Institute for the purposes of this ordinance.
 6. (1) The first Director of the Institute shall be appointed by the Commission.
 (2) every subsequent director shall be appointed by the council with the concurrence of the commission from 
among the panel of three persons nominated by the board of Management of the Institute referred to in 
paragraph 10:
   Provided however, no person shall be appointed as the director for more than two consecutive terms.
 (3) Where a member of the staff of a Higher Educational Institution or a Higher Educational Institute is appointed 
as the Director, such Institution or Institute, as the case may be, shall release such member of the staff so 
appointed enabling him to accept the post of director of the Institute.
 (4) The Director shall, unless he vacates office earlier or is removed from office under subparagraph (5), hold 
office for a period of three years from the date of his appointment or until he completes his age of retirement, 
whichever event occurs earlier.
 (5) Director may be removed from office by the Commission on the recommendation of the Council on a vote of 
censure passed by not less than two-third of the total membership of the board of Management at a special 
meeting convened for such purpose. the members present at such special meeting shall elect from among 
themselves a person, other than the director, to preside at such special meeting.
 (6) the director shall be subject to the disciplinary control of the council.
 (7) A Director who resigns or is removed from office or completes his tenure may, if he was a member of the staff  
of a higher educational Institution or of a higher educational Institute immediately prior to his appointment as 
director, revert to his substantive post in such higher educational Institution or higher educational Institute, 
as the case may be, unless he has not completed his age of retirement.
 (8) Where the director by reason of leave, illness, absence from sri Lanka or any other cause is temporarily unable 
to perform his duties, the commission shall, within seven days of the occurrence of such inability, make such 
arrangements as it may think fit to appoint any other eligible person to perform the duties of the Director and 
until such arrangements are made, the registrar of the university shall perform the duties of the director.
 (9) Where a vacancy occurs in the office of Director, the Commission shall, within seven days of the occurrence of 
such vacancy, make such arrangements as it may think fit for the performance of the duties of the office of the 
director until a permanent appointment is made under paragraph 6(2) and until such arrangements are made, 
the Registrar of the University shall perform the duties of the office of the Director.

 33. In this ordinance unless the context otherwise requires-

appropriate instrument” shall have the same meaning as assigned to it in the act;
