SCHEDULE
 01.  In the said election a candidate contesting for a particular local authority may incur an amount not exceeding 50% of 
the sum of money in Sri Lankan Rupees calculated for the purpose of calculating an authorized amount for the local 
authority for which the candidate is contesting as his election campaign expenses per elector on the valid electoral 
register of the relevant local authority in which that candidate on the second list is contesting. Accordingly, the authorized 
amount that a contesting candidate in each local authority can spend is specified in Annexure 01.
 02.  In the said election a candidate on the second list contesting for a particular local authority may incur an amount not 
exceeding 20% of the sum of money in Sri Lankan Rupees calculated for the purpose of calculating an authorized 
amount for the local authority for which the candidate on the second list is contesting as his election campaign expenses 
per elector on the valid electoral register of the relevant local authority in which that candidate on the second list is 
contesting. Accordingly, the authorized amount that a contesting candidate on the second list in each local authority 
can spend is specified in Annexure 02.
 03.  In the said election a political party or independent group contesting for a particular local authority may incur an 
amount not exceeding 30% of the sum of money in Sri Lankan Rupees calculated for the purpose of calculating an 
authorized amount for the local authority for which the political party or independent group is contesting as their election 
campaign expenses per elector on the valid electoral register of the relevant local authority in which that political party 
or independent group is contesting. Accordingly, the authorized amount that a contesting political party or independent 
group in each local authority can spend is specified in Annexure 03.
 04.  The incom e and expenditure reports, including election campaign income and expenditure, shall be prepared in the 
format specified in Annexure 04 by the relevant recognized political party, independent group, and each candidate 
within twenty-one (21) days of the election result declaration and these reports must be certified by a Commissioner 
for Oaths or a Justice of the Peace and signed by the Party Secretary, the independent group leader, and the candidate, 
as applicable, and submit to the Returning Officer of the contested Local Authority.
The relevant report shall state 
 I.  the donations or contributions accepted or received on behalf of the recognized political party, independent group, 
and candidate and, if such donations or contributions were received in kind, their estimated value, whether such 
contributions were made in the form of gifts, loans, advances, or deposits, 
 II.  the name, address, and National Identity Card Number of the person or bodies of persons, whether incorporated 
or unincorporated, who made such donations or contributions, and the Taxpayer Identification Number (TIN) or 
registration number issued by the Department of Inland Revenue.
This report should include bill receipts confirming whether payments were made for: 
 (a)  expenses for advertisements, leaflets, photographs, leaflets, posters, or other advertising media; 
 (b)  costs for radio or television advertising; 
 (c)  expenditures on newspaper, periodical, social media, or any electronic media advertising; and  
 (d) transportation expenses other than on behalf of the candidate. Receipts or bills confirming payments must be 
attached. If the service was obtained without payment, the name, address, national identity card number, or tax 
registration number of the person or institution providing the service should be included.
In this case: 
(a) 
 Expenses for transporting candidates within the polling area, and 
(b)  Expenses for stationery , telephone, or other communication methods
  
do not need to be included in the report.
 05.  A  copy of such records and statements shall be issued to any person upon payment of a fee determined by the Election 
Commission. 
 06.  Any candidate, recognized political party, or independent group that contravenes the provisions of the section 4, 5, 8 
(a) and 8(b) of the Regulation of Election Expenditure Act No. 3 of 2023 commits an offence constituting an illegal 
act under the Local Authorities Elections Ordinance applicable to that election and shall be subject to the penalties 
prescribed in the Local Authorities Elections (Amendment) Ordinance (Chapter 262) No. 24 of 1977.
