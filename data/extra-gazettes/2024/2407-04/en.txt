2a
Regulations
1. these regulations may be cited as the registration of Geographical Indications regulations of 2024.
Part I
Application for Registration of Geographical Indications
2. an applicant intending to register a geographical indication under the act shall make an application for such purpose 
in the form specified in the First Schedule hereto along with the required documentation specified in the application 
and the application processing fee specified in the Second Schedule, to the Director General:
   Provided however, an applicant intending to register a geographical indication which is a homonymous 
indication to an already registered geographical indication, shall submit to the director General along with his 
application, the material factors differentiating his application from the registered geographical indications and 
particulars of protective measures adopted by the applicant to ensure the consumers of the goods relating to such 
registered geographical indication that, the already registered geographical indication is not confused in consequence 
of such registration.
3. (a)  upon receipt of an application under regulation 2, the director General shall examine the application to 
ascertain whether the applicant has fulfilled all the requirements specified in the application set out in the 
First schedule hereto.
 (b)  For the purposes of paragraph (a), the director General may -
 (i) if the Director General considers it necessary, seek advice from any expert in the field of geographical 
indications;
 (ii) make an observation visit on site in the geographical area concerning the geographical indication 
sought to be registered; or
 (iii) direct the applicant to submit any required documents from the experts in the area in support of the 
application.
 (c)  The applicant may, prior to the examination of the application by the Director General, make a request to the 
Director General to amend any particulars specified in the application or supporting documents submitted 
along with the application.
4. the director General may, in considering whether a geographical indication sought to be registered under the act 
has become generic, take into account, all the factors relating to such geographical indication, including the existing 
situation of such geographical indication in the region, place or locality from which such geographical indication 
originates, and the area of consumption of such of the goods concerning such geographical indication.
Part II
Publication of Application and Opposition to Registration
5. Where the director General is of the opinion that the geographical indication sought to be registered is admissible 
in terms of section 161A of the Act, the Director General shall upon receipt of the fee specified in the Second 
schedule hereto, publish the application in the Gazette within a period of two months from the date of receipt of such 
application by the director General.

3a
6. (a)  any person who considers that the geographical indication published in the Gazette under regulation 5 is 
inadmissible in terms of section 161a  of the act, shall within three months from the date of such publication, 
send to the director General, a notice of opposition to registration of such geographical indication in the form 
set out in the Third Schedule hereto along with the fee specified in the Second Schedule hereto.
 (b) unless the director General otherwise directs, any person giving evidence under subsection (4) of section 
161D of the Act at any hearing shall give such evidence in the form of an affidavit, and thereafter, the parties 
shall file written submissions, if any, on a date as may be determined by the Director General.
Part III
Registration of Geographical Indications
7. (a) the director General may subject to the provisions of the act and these regulations either register, register 
subject to such amendments, conditions or limitations, or for reasons to be informed to the applicant refuse 
to register such geographical indication.
 (b)  The Director General shall, upon receipt of the fee for the certificate as specified in the Second Schedule 
hereto, issue to every registered owner of a geographical indication, a certificate of registration of geographical 
indication in such form as specified in the Fourth Schedule.
8. (a)  the director General shall keep and maintain a register of Geographical Indications which may be amended, 
from time to time, for the purpose of altering any typographical error appears in such register.
 (b)  Any person may examine and obtain certified extracts of the register of geographical indications by payment 
of the fee specified in the Second Schedule hereto.
9. the director General shall proceed with the applications for registration of geographical indications in order of their 
receipt at National Intellectual Property Office, subject to fulfillment of relevant requirements.
Part IV
Alterations to Registered Geographical Indications
10. The registered owner of a geographical indication may, if he intends to amend the specifications and associated 
control plan for any reason specified in section 161L, shall make a request in writing to the Director General in the 
form specified in the Fifth Schedule hereto along with the fee specified in the Second Schedule hereto.
Part V
Renewal of Registered Geographical Indications
11. an owner of a geographical indication may, six months prior to the expiry of the registration of such geographical 
indication make an application in the form specified in the Sixth Schedule hereto along with the fee specified in the 
Second Schedule hereto, to the Director General for renewal of registration of such geographical indication:
   Provided however, the applicant may within a period of six months from the date of expiry of registration, apply 
for renewal of the registration upon payment of a surcharge specified in the Second Schedule hereto.

Interpretation
For the purposes of these regulations -
“act” means the Intellectual Property act, No.36 of 2003;
“agent” means an agent registered under section 175 of the Intellectual property act, No.36 of 2003;
“applicant” shall have the same meaning assigned to such term by section 161b of the act;
“Director General” means the Director General of the National Intellectual Property Office of Sri Lanka;
“geographical indication” shall have the same meaning as assigned to that term in the Intellectual Property  
act, No.36 of 2003;
“control plan” shall have the same meaning as assigned to that term in the Intellectual Property act, No.36 of 2003;
“goods” shall have the same meaning as assigned to that term in the Intellectual Property act, No.36 of 2003;
“Homonymous Geographical Indication” means any geographical indication which is spelled or pronounced alike, but 
identify products originating in different places, usually in different countries; and ‘‘operator” in relation to goods 
means any person involved in the process of placing such goods in the market i.e. trading, purchasing and distributing 
the goods.
