EXCISE ORDINANCE (CHAPTER 52)
Excise Notification No. 02/2024
By  virtue of the powers vested in me by Section 32 to read with Section 25 of the Excise Ordinance (Chapter 52), i, r anil 
Wickremesinghe, Minister of Finance, Economic Stabilization and National Policies do hereby declare that the following 
rules made by me shall come into effect from January 12, 2024.
The Excise Notification No. 902 published in the Extraordinary Gazette of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka 
bearing No. 1544/17 dated April 10, 2008 and the provisions in paragraph (2) of the Excise Notification No. 983 published 
in the Extraordinary Gazette of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka bearing No. 1945/17 dated December 15, 
2015 are hereby rescinded. 
 Ranil Wickremesinghe,
 Minister of Finance, Economic Stabilization 
 and National Policies.

Rules
1. These Rules may be cited as the ‘Excise Notification No. 02 /2024’.
2. In terms of the Excise Ordinance No.08 of 1912 (Chapter 52) hereinafter referred to as ‘the Ordinance’;
i. From and after the date of coming into effect of these rules, all persons expecting to obtain any liquor selling 
license which is contemporarily issued, shall forward an application to the Commissioner General of Excise 
by using the application form specified in Schedule I hereto.
ii. A request shall be made to the Commissioner General of Excise, by using the application form specified in 
the Schedule II hereto by all those persons expecting to renew an existing excise license for the sale of liquor 
for the coming year, by using the application form specified in the Schedule III hereto by an applicant for the 
co-licensure of any excise license for the sale of liquor, by using the application form specified in Schedule 
IV hereto by all persons expecting to transfer the ownership of an excise license for the sale of liquor, and by 
using the application form specified in Schedule V hereto by all persons expecting to transfer of location of 
any such licensed premises.   
iii. An amount of rs. 25,000.00 shall be paid as a non-refundable application fee in cases of applying for a new excise 
license for the sale of liquor, an extension of the validity period of such existing license, transfer of ownership 
of such license, applying for the co-licensure of such license or transfer of location of the licensed premises.
iV . However, Payment of the application fee mentioned in above paragraph iii should not be applicable to the 
Excise Licenses for the sale of liquor of the categories of Excise F.L. 14 Occasional License, Excise F.L. 15 
Supplementary License to extend Hours of Sale and Excise F.L.16 Auctioneers License.
V.  In cases of applying for a new excise license for the sale of liquor, transfer of ownership of such license, applying 
for co- licensure of such license or transfer of the location of the licensed premises, those application forms can 
be obtained from the Excise Head Office by paying the prescribed application fee. However, application forms 
for extending the validity period of such licenses can be obtained from the Excise Head Office or Offices of the 
Assistant Commissioners of Excise located island wide.
Vi. Applications obtained as per the provisions of these rules shall be completed and submitted along with relevant 
documents to the Excise Officer-in-Charge of the excise area of authority, where the proposed premises for 
the license is located in cases of new licenses, and where the existing licensed premises is located in cases of 
existing licenses.
Vii. However, the Commissioner General of Excise reserves the right to search and extract any information relating 
to any applicant from any document file available at the Excise Department or to request and obtain any 
information necessary for considering the issuance of the license applied for, including details of the income 
tax or other tax payments of the applicant.    
Viii. All applications submitted as per the above Schedule VI should be completed in all aspects and all the required 
documents should be annexed. i ncomplete applications or applications submitted without necessary documents or 
which does not conform to the guidelines and conditions set out in the Schedule Vi  to these rules will be rejected.
iX. However, the issuance of any application under the provisions of this section should not be a guarantee for 
the issuance of a new excise license for the sale of liquor, the transfer of ownership of an excise license or the 
appointment of a co- licensure to a license or the approval for the transfer of location of a licensed premises as 
applied for by such application.

3. Upon receipt of all requests under Rule 2 above, the Commissioner General of Excise shall consider those applications 
in accordance with the provisions of the Excise Ordinance and shall satisfy such applications are in accordance with the 
guidelines and conditions specified in Schedule VI hereto.
4. Relevant license fee specified for the particular type of license for the particular period of time should be paid by the 
relevant licensee in cases of granting a new excise license to sell liquor or extending the period of validity of such license 
already been granted under these rules.
5.  All excise licenses for the sale of liquor are issued for a period of one calendar year (January 01 to December 31), and the 
relevant license fee for the particular year should be paid at least seven working days before the date of commencement 
of the relevant fiscal year. However, in case of not receiving the said license fee by the relevant Divisional Secretary 
within seven working days from the commencement of the relevant financial year, permission should be granted to pay 
the said license fee subjected to a fine of one percent of the license fee within a period of four weeks. Afterwards that time 
period of four weeks the said fine should be three percent of the license fee.
6. Those licenses issued at some point in the middle of the year, will be so issued only for the rest of the year. in respect of 
those licenses issued in the middle of the year, license fees should be charged proportionately for the rest of that year.
7. Further, every person who transfer the right to any existing excise license for the sale of liquor, become a co-licensee of 
such license or transfers the location of the said license, shall for the time being pay the following fees in respect thereof.
 Fee for the transfer of location of a license   - rs.      250,000/-
 Fee for the transfer of rights of an Excise F.L.4 license         
in case of a legal heir  - rs.      250,000/-  
  in case of a non- legal heir  - rs. 15,000,000/-
 Fee for the transfer of rights of an excise license other than Excise F.L.4 license     
in case of a legal heir  - rs.      250,000/-    
in case of a non- legal heir  - rs.   2,500,000/-
8. Generally, these guidelines and conditions shall be applicable until they are amended or repealed regarding the issuance 
of excise licenses for the sale of liquor in respect of a calendar year beginning on the 1st day of January of a particular 
year and ending on the 31st day of december of that year, as well as for any period beginning from a date after the initial 
date of a particular year and ending on or prior to december 31 of that year.  
9. Every person to whom a liquor license is been granted should write and sign a counterpart agreement covering the 
validity period of their license as prescribed by the Excise Ordinance. Further, the security deposit applicable for the 
time being in respect of the type of excise license for the sale of liquor applied for shall be deposited as a security for the 
proper execution of his agreement.
10. If the Commissioner General of Excise refuses granting a liquor license to the applicant, he should inform the applicant 
those reasons for holding such opinion by registered mail within 30 days of the receipt of the relevant application, 
and also should inform the applicant to submit if he has reasons to justify granting of a license for him. Accordingly, 
those reasons against the reasons given by the Commissioner General of Excise should be forwarded to him in writing 
within two weeks of the receipt of the notice of the Commissioner General of Excise by registered post, along with any 
documentary to prove the facts presented.
11. Thereafter and within a period not later than 30 days from the date of receipt of a reply from the applicant, after considering 
all the facts and if appears to be required and appropriate the Commissioner General of Excise shall hold an inquiry at his

12. In order to be eligible for a license to sell liquor, the following requirements shall be fulfilled with respect to the location 
and premises of the proposed place:
(a) Licensed premises or proposed premises for the licenses for the retail sale of liquor not for consumption on 
the premises shall have a floor area of   at least 250 square feet, and licensed premises or proposed premises 
for the licenses for the retail sale of liquor for consumption on the premises shall have a floor area of   at least 
500 square feet.
(b) Licensed premises or proposed premises for the sale of Beer, Ale, Stout and Wine only, by retail not for 
consumption on the premises shall have a floor area of   at least 100 square feet, and Licensed premises or 
proposed premises for the sale of Beer, Ale, Stout and Wine only, by retail for consumption on the premises 
shall have a floor area of   at least 250 square feet. 
 (c) In case of licensed premises or proposed premises for those licenses for the retail sale of liquor not for 
consumption on the premises issued for the Super Markets/ Grocery Stores of which the annual turnover of 
their  companies/ businesses or parent companies exceeds Rs. 1000 million shall have a minimum floor area 
of 3,000 square feet that is dedicated for providing consumer services as prescribed by the Commissioner 
General of Excise. 
(d) Liquor wholesale licenses shall have a minimum floor area of 1000 square feet.
(e) Location and establishment of Licensed premises or proposed premises for the licenses of Excise F.L. 07 
Hotel License, Excise F.L. 08 Hotel Bar License, Excise F.L. 11 Restaurant License and Excise F.L. 12 Rest 
House License for the retail sale of liquor for consumption on the premises that do not require the approval 
of the Sri Lanka Tourism Development Authority should be done in accordance with the criteria prescribed 
in Schedule Vii hereto. Such hotel licenses would be contrasted from restaurant licenses only if those hotels 
do possess at least five (05) rooms for the accommodation of guests. 
(f) Licensed premises or proposed premises for the licenses for the retail sale of liquor not for consumption 
on the premises should be located by maintaining the straight distance that exceeding one hundred (100) 
meters from their nearest boundary to the closest boundary of the nearest government registered schools and 
government registered public religious places of worship. Further, licensed premises or proposed premises 
for the licenses for the retail sale of liquor for consumption on the premises should be located maintaining 
the straight distance that exceeding five hundred (500) meters from their nearest boundary to the nearest 
boundary of the closest government registered schools and government registered public religious places of 
worship.
(g) If any complaint is received on violation of prescribed distance requirements to the nearest government 
registered schools and government registered public religious places of worship, the actual distances should 
be determined by way of a survey report obtained from the Government Surveyor subject to the payment 
of the relevant survey fees by the applicant of the license. As per the survey report, if any violation of the 
distance regulations is found, then the relevant license should be cancelled. 
(h) Licensed premises or proposed premises for the retail sale of liquor for consumption on the premises that 
are registered with the Sri Lanka Tourism Development Authority, Urban Development Authority, National 
Film Corporation, or relevant local government institute under the Licensing of Clubs Law bearing no. 17 
of 1975 should be located by maintaining the straight distance that exceeding two hundred and fifty (250)

13. Any objection or protest received by the Commissioner General of Excise from a member of an organization of the 
public either before or after the issuance of a license, on the ground that there has been a violation of the provisions 
of the Excise Ordinance or violation of the rules, guidelines and conditions set forth herein, will be notified by the 
Commissioner General of Excise to the applicant or the licensee as the case may be and will thereafter be inquired into 
by the Commissioner General of Excise or any other excise officer duly empowered to do so and a decision would be 
made on the basis of the findings. 
14. In such an inquiry held in accordance with the provisions of rule 11 above, if it is found that the establishment or 
continuance of the license under concern may threat or likely threat to the maintenance of law and order in the area, 
Commissioner General of Excise may decide to relocate the licensed premises or the proposed premises to a suitable 
place.  
15. Further, the decisions made by the Commissioner General of Excise subsequently to the inquiries conducted in 
accordance with the provisions of Rules 11 and 12 above, should be final.

(2) Extension of the validity of existing Excise Licenses for the sale of liquor
i. Current holders of excise licenses are not entitled to automatic renewal thereof, and upon the expiry of the validity 
period of the said licenses, if the relevant licensee intends to extend further the validity period of the said license, 
he shall submit his application within 30 days in advance of each year according to these guidelines and conditions. 
ii. The application that submitted as prescribed above will be considered by the Commissioner General of Excise 
in accordance with these guidelines and conditions and in accordance with any other provisions laid down in the 
Excise Ordinance.
iii. Applications for a license in respect of a licensed premises already in operation should have been fully paid and 
settled for all claims due in respect of the license already in operation or the license previously in operation or any 
other license in the name of the applicant.
iV . If there is an inquiry still pending in respect of such licenses already in operation, to execute a license on the same 
premises or to execute another license in the name of the applicant will be considered upon a security deposit 
equivalent to a compounding fee that may be imposed by the Commissioner General of Excise in accordance with 
the Excise (Amendment) Act No. 37 of 1990 and subject to the results of such inquiry.
(3) Transfer the ownership of an existing Excise License for the sale of liquor:
i. A request for transfer the ownership of an excise license to another person will be considered only after paying the 
relevant application fee and obtaining an application form in the name of the proposed transferee, duly completed 
and submitted along with the relevant documents, and only if the proposed transferee fulfills all the requirements 
mentioned in these rules to be completed in order to obtain the license.
ii. if the proposed transferee is not a legal heir of the excise license holder concerned, the proposed transferee should 
be liable to pay the fee prescribed in the present for transferring the right of the license.
(4) Applying for the co-licensure of an existing Excise License for the sale of liquor 
i. A request to grant the co-licensure to another person or persons in addition to the current license holder or license 
holders related to any excise license will be considered only if the relevant application fee is paid and obtaining an 
application form or application forms in the name of the proposed co-licensee or co-licensees and duly completing 
the relevant documents and only if the proposed co-licensee or co-licensees meet all the requirements of these rules 
to be completed to obtain the licensure.
ii. The proposed co-licensee should pay the relevant fee prescribed in the present for the appointment as a co-licensee. 
(5) Transferring the location of  an existing Excise License for the sale of liquor:
i. Transfer of premises on which an excise license has been granted for the sale of liquor is not normally permitted. 
However, in cases of a natural disaster or due to a Government development activity or any other reason considered 
reasonable by the Commissioner General of Excise such as proximity to other licenses and uneven distribution of 
licensed premises etc., it should be permissible to transfer the location to which an excise license is issued within 
the same district in which it was in operation. 
ii. However, the rule contained in the section I above, shall not apply for the wholesale licenses for the sale of liquor.
iii. Transfer of the location of a premises granted with an excise license would be permitted only if the proposed  
premises conforms to the rules, guidelines and conditions set out in this notification.

Schedule VII
Criteria for the approval of restaurants and hotels related to the location and establishment of the licensed 
premises or proposed premises to sell liquor by retail for onsite consumption that does not require the 
approval of the Sri Lanka Tourism Development Authority.
1. Criteria for Location:
(a) The location and the surrounding environment should be suitable for the establishment and operating of a 
restaurant/ Hotel
(b) The appearance and construction of the building shall be in good condition.
2. Criteria for the extent of floor area:
(a) The minimum floor area should be 500 square feet
(b) There should be sufficient space to arrange tables and chairs to provide seating accommodation for at least 25 
persons at a time.
3. Criteria for Building:
(a) The whole household including all ancillaries thereof (that is the building, furniture, furnishings, equipment, 
shelves, fixtures, etc.) should be of high standard and properly maintained.  
(b) Should have a sufficient number of electric fans and air conditioning facility. 
(c) Tables and chairs as well as furniture, amenities and equipment should be of appropriate high standard. 
(d) Proper ventilation should be provided with.
(e) Facilities for listening to live or recorded music should be available.
(f) Household and space thereof should be free from creatures, insects and rodents.
4. The kitchen:
(a) The kitchen should be separated from the serving area and tidily maintained
(b) Cookery, cutlery and glassware should be of good quality and without damage.
(c) Table linen should be of quality material and clean.
(d) The kitchen and pantry should be well designed and equipped to ensure efficient and hygienic preparation of 
food and should be well maintained, clean and in hygienic condition. 
(e) The kitchen should be ready with suitable wood, gas ovens, and electrical stoves/cookers.
(f) Kitchen floors and walls should be cleaned and of impervious material.
(g) Kitchen utensils should be adequate of good quality and be cleaned.
(h) Kitchen tables and shelves should be built of good quality material and should be of impervious finish.
(i) There should be adequate cold storage facilities.
(j) There should be separate facilities for purpose of washing of crockery, cutlery, tableware, cooking utensil, 
equipment, pots and pans etc.,
(k) Separate facilities should be available for cleaning hands of staff.
(l) Kitchen drains and outlets from sinks should be in good condition and unexposed within the building.

5. Foods:

(b) Food required for serving must always be available when Restaurant /Hotel is kept opened. 
(c) Drinking water should be boiled and filtered or otherwise suitably treated.
6. Criteria for Toilet Facilities:
(a) There should be toilets with modern sanitary facilities, with separate toilets for females and males.
(b) Toilets should be maintained in excellent condition and all fittings and equipment should be in good working 
order.
(c) All toilets to be provided with water on tap, soap, towels, etc.
(d) There should be separate toilets with modern sanitary facilities for the use of the staff.
7. Criteria for Liquor Store and Liquor Bar:
(a) A safely lockable place with suitable and sufficient space shall be reserved for the liquor store
(b) If a liquor bar is also proposed to be established in the said premises, the liquor bar should be located as close 
as possible to the liquor store.
(c) The liquor bar should be constructed in such a way that it can be locked and securely closed as required.
(d) There should be sufficient number of glassware, folks, knives and serviettes etc. in the liquor bar, and there 
should be sufficient water and cooling facilities to wash and keep them clean.
8. General:
(a) The premises should have an atmosphere of elegant and attraction. 
(b) There should be at least five rooms to qualify for an Excise F.L.7 license in granting excise license for the sale 
of liquor.
