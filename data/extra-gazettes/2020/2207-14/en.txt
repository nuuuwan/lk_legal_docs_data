Government  Notifications
1A- G 33368 — 25 (12/2020)
(SALE   IN   AuCTION   Of   RIGhT   Of   REAPING   hARvEST   Of   CINNAmON   PLANTATIONS   Of 
STATE   LANDS)   SALE   IN   AuCTION   Of   RIGhT   Of   REAPING   hARvEST   Of   STATE   LANDS 
SITuATED   ThE   vILLAGES   Of   uLLALA   AND   KIRINDA   mAGuN   PAhALA   IN   mATARA 
DISTRICT 
1. It is hereby notified that the right of reaping cinnamon harvest of the state land which is fully described in the 
following Schedule for a period of 10 years from 01 st January 2020 to 31 st December 2030 (subject to relevant 
conditions) will be sold in action by Divisional Secretary of Kamburupitiya (hereinafter called and referred to as 
the Divisional Secretary and that term includes any other officer duly authorized by him) in auction in the land 
concerned at 10.30 a.m. on 30th December 2020.
2. The initial bid shall be Rs. 281,500.00. Demands at the initial bid or less is not accepted.
3. Annual rental will be decided on the bid confirmed at the auction and after five years annual rental for the period 
of next 05 years will be amended based on the valuation made by the Government Valuer. In case of any change 
in between valuation of the Government Valuer and annual amount of rental paid by the bidder, higher value from 
the two valuations shall be deemed as the annual rental. (Except occasions in which there will be higher decrease 
of price of cinnamon at the market or the plantation has become adversely affected and damaged by natural 
disaster and weeds or insects).
4. Before any bid is accepted, Divisional Secretary should be satisfied of the honesty of the bidder and his capability 
to accept all conditions of the auction. If Divisional Secretary is not satisfied, he is able to continue the auction by 
refusing that accepting of the bid.

5. Every bidder is expected to be present himself at the auction. If it is unable to be present a representative could 
be sent and the said representative should submit the letter of authorization to Divisional Secretary to be checked 
prior to the auction.
6. The accepted bidder should pay half of the bid to the Divisional Secretary of Kamburupitiya at that occasion 
itself. The balance has to be paid within two weeks from the date of the auction. The possession of the land will be 
delivered up to the selected bidder only after the full amount of the bid is paid. In the event of failure to pay half 
of the bid soon after it was accepted or the balance within two weeks from the date of the auction, the Divisional 
Secretary reserves the powers to cancel the auction and confiscate any money if paid.
7. The selected bidder is also expected to deposit a bond if so required by Divisional Secretary. The amount so 
required should not more than ten percent (10%) of his bid.
8. The bidder is entitled to receive a permit with following conditions after making payments of the bid made and 
amount of bond.
 
a. The said permit could be terminated by Divisional Secretary by giving a one month prior notice to the permit 
holder. That written notice should be personally handed over or sent by registered post to the address of the 
residence at which he resided last.
b. All assessments and tax, valuation and expenses related to the land should also be paid by the permit holder.
c. The permit holder should not make any permanent or temporary buildings or construction in the land.
d. The permit holder is expected to maintain all bounder stones of the land in the good condition.
e. The permit holder should not fell trees and remove them from the land or damage them in any manner.
f. Soil, sand, gravel or any type of mineral should not brought out from the land without a permission of Divisional 
Secretary of Kamburupitiya.
g. The permit holder should not transfer, sublet or mortgage the land without obtaining the approval from the 
Land Commissioner.
h. Divisional Secretary or any other officer authorized by him reserves the right to enter and inspect the land at 
any occasion.
i.  If it is required to acquire the land or a portion thereof to the State prior to the expiration of the permit and when 
one month period is given, the permit holder should return the said land or portion thereof to the Divisional 
Secretary. In such an event it is possible to withdraw a certain amount of taxes paid and deduct the tax amount 
to be paid for the remaining period of the permit.
j.  In case of violating conditions by the permit holder, Divisional Secretary reserves the rights to regain the 
possession of the land paying no compensation and confiscate the full amount of the bond or part thereof.
k. The permit holder is not entitled for any compensation for improvements made by the permit holder.
l.  The permit holder is also expected to properly maintain the plantation by controlling weeds and insects, using 
fertilizer in standard quantities, pruning bushes, following soil conservation methods, maintaining a maximum 
number of bushes of the plantation etc. and the plantation should always be maintained under instructions and 
guidance given by Export Agriculture Extension Officer.
