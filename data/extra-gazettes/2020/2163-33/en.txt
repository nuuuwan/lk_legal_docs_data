 3. (1) The driver of every vehicle using the User Fee National Highways, shall pay a user fee in respect of the 
use of such User Fee National Highway by such driver, at such rates as specified in Part A of the Schedule to these 
regulations.        
(2) The user fee shall be charged in respect of the whole distance, or part thereof, of a User Fee National Highway.
(3) Different user fees shall apply for different categories of vehicles.
(4) The user fee shall be paid-
                 
                   (a) manually, by cash; or  
                  
     (b) electronically, through a device (hereinafter referred to as “E-tag”) attached to the vehicle.
 
 4. (1) Every driver other than an E-tag user entering a User Fee National Highway from an interchange other than 
from a designated location prior to being permitted to proceed along User Fee National Highway, shall, obtain a ticket 
from the teller on duty, at such interchange.
  (2) Every driver who has obtained a ticket under regulation 4 (1) shall retain such ticket securely in his 
possession until he reaches the paying station of the interchange or the designated location through which he intends to 
exit such User Fee National Highway.
  (3) A vehicle, when arriving at a paying station of an interchange or a designated location for the purpose 
of exiting such User Fee National Highway, the driver of that vehicle shall-
              (a) surrender the ticket obtained at the interchange through which he entered the User Fee National 
Highway, to the Teller; or
                        (b) where he entered from a designated location, inform the Teller the designated location through 
which he entered the User Fee National Highway.
 (4) The teller shall inform the driver of a vehicle using manual user fee collection lane, the amount of 
the fee payable and the driver shall pay such amount, prior to being permitted to pass through such interchange or 
designated location, and the teller shall issue a receipt in respect of such payment.
 (5) Where the driver -
(a)  is unable to surrender the ticket; or

  5. (1) An owner or driver of a vehicle who is desirous of making payments electronically (hereinafter referred 
to as “E-tag user”) shall register with the Road Development Authority, established under the Road Development 
Authority Act, No. 73 of 1981, (hereinafter referred to as “the Authority”) and shall be required to open an Electronic 
Toll Collection System User Fee Account (hereinafter referred to as the “ETCS Account”) at the Customer Service 
Centre of the Authority.
               (2)  Every application for registration as an E-tag user, shall be made to the Customer Service Centre of the 
Authority. 
                (3) Upon receipt of an application for registration the Authority may, on being satisfied with the information 
provided in the application including the information relating to the ETCS account, register such person as an E-tag 
user and issue him an E-tag for the purposes of these regulations.
   
            (4) Every E-tag user shall enter into an Electronic Toll Collection System User Fee customer agreement 
(hereinafter referred to as the “ETCS customer agreement”) subject to the terms and conditions specified in such 
agreement. 
(5) Every E-tag issued shall be fitted on to the windscreen of the vehicle.
(6) Every E-tag user shall maintain a sufficient credit balance in the ETCS Account at all times when using 
an electronic user fee collection lane (hereinafter referred to as the “ETC lane”).
 
(7) In case of insufficient credit, the E-tag user may top-up the ETCS account and the processing time of 
such top-up shall be not less than one hour before the E-tag user may be able to use ETC lane. 
(8) Every E-tag user shall ensure that the E-tag is in proper working order and shall forthwith inform the 
Authority of any damage or loss to such E-tag.
  6.    (1) Where an E-tag user with sufficient credit to pay the user fee, enters the User Fee National Highway from 
an interchange other than from a designated location, such E-tag user shall be permitted to proceed along the ETC lane 
of such interchange.
    
                  (2) Where a vehicle fitted with an E-tag is being used on an ETC lane, the user fee shall be debited against 
the ETCS Account automatically and the E-tag user may obtain a statement in relation to the usage of such account 
from the Customer Care Centre of the Authority.
               (3) Where an E-tag user, does not have sufficient credit to pay the user fee, such E-tag user shall divert his 
vehicle from the ETC lane to the manual user fee collection lane. 
 7.   (1) The Authority shall ensure that, for the purpose of implementation of the provisions of these regulations, 
sufficient number of gates are positioned at the required places along the User Fee National Highways. 
     (2) The gates so positioned along the User Fee National Highway shall be owned, operated and maintained 
by the Authority, and each gate shall be manned by a Teller. 
    (3) The teller shall for the collect the user fee at every gate referred to in paragraph (2), at such rates as 
specified in Part A of the Schedule from every driver using such User Fee National Highway:

               Provided that, the provisions of these regulations shall not apply to a driver who is driving a vehicle of a

 8. The Teller shall at such times as are specified, hand over the day’s collection of the fees to the officer authorized 
in that behalf by the Authority and obtain a receipt in respect of such amount.
 9.  The money so collected as user fee shall be credited to the Fund of the Authority and be used for the purpose 
of developing and maintaining the User Fee National Highways to which these regulations apply.
 10. The driver of a vehicle who contravenes the provisions of these regulations commits an offence and shall on 
conviction after summary trial by a Magistrate be liable in the case of-      
   
  (a) a first offence, to a fine not exceeding five thousand rupees; and 
  (b) a second or subsequent offence, to a fine not exceeding fifty thousand rupees.
 11. For the purpose of these regulations-
“Designated location” means  an authorized access point, where a driver of a vehicle is permitted to enter 
the User Fee National Highway without obtaining a ticket and includes New Kelaniya Interchange, 
Peliyagoda interchange, Katunayake interchange and Katunayake (25.80Km). 
“E-tag” means an electronic device that is permanently installed on the windscreen of the vehicle for the 
purpose of payment of user fee electronically when passing through an electronic user fee lane;
“Teller” means an officer who is manning the gates at an interchange or a designated location;
“User Fee National Highways” means the Southern User Fee National Highway from Kottawa (0.000 
km) to Mattala (200.451 km), Andarawewa – Hambantota User Fee National Highway from 
Andarawewa (0.000 km) to Hambantota (16.558 km), Outer Circular User Fee National Highway 
from Kottawa (0.000 km) to Kerawalapitiya (28.867 km) and Colombo – Katunayake User Fee 
National Highway from Colombo (0.000km) to Katunayake (25.800 km). 
 12. The National Thoroughfares (User Fee) regulations published in the 
Gazette Extraordinary No. 2148/47 of 
November, 08, 2019 and Gazette Extraordinary No. 2148/48 of November, 08, 2019 are hereby rescinded 
without prejudice to anything done thereunder.
