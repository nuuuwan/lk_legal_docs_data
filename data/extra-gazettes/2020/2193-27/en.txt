THE    PILGRIMAGES    ORDINANCE    (CHAPTER   175)
The Esala Perahera Festival of Historic Sankhapala Raja Maha Viharaya - 2020
ATTENTION of the General Public is drawn to the regulations relating to pilgrimages in terms of the Gazette Extra Ordinary
No. 2153/15 of 12.12.2019 published under the Pilgrimages Ordinance Chapter 175. I hereby notify the following particulars
relating to those regulations.
1.  Duration of conducting the Procession
• The kap planting ceremony for the Esala Perahera 2020 will be held at the
auspicious time on 18.09.2020
• All night Pirith Chanting on 26.09.2020 and alms giving on the following day.
• Kumbal Perahera on 27.09.2020,
• Salu Perahera on 28.09.2020,
• Randoli Perahera on 29.09.2020,
• Ginisilu Perahera Festival on 30.09.2020,
• Maha Perahera on 01.10.2020,
• Alms giving to the god at dawn on 03.10.2020
and the Esala Perahera will be concluded.
2. The Camp Area:-
• The camp area where the Esala Festival to be conducted shall be the area bounded to the North by the
Sankhapala peak, to the South by the ‘Rakwana’ river, to the East by Perahera Mawatha and to the West by
Perahera Mawatha.

3. Vehicles
3.1 No vehicle, including motor vehicles, bicycles, and carts , without my permission or a permission given by an
authorized officer, shall park or enter any place or festival premises except the area reserved for parking.
3.2 Entering vehicles to the sacred area through the gateway erected facing to Ratnapura - Embilipitiya main road
is strictly prohibited.
4. Construction of Buildings
4.1 No person shall construct or build any shelter or structure whether temporary or permanent or add a new part
to such building which is already exist within the camp area during the period of time from the 26th September
to the 03 rd October 2020, without my written permission or such permission obtained from an authorized
officer.
4.2 Whereas, it shall be permitted to enter any land or place situated in the camp area in order to implement any
regulation, among the regulations imposed by me or any authorized officer or to erect a pillar or to conduct a
constrcution or a building on any land or place within the camp area in order to display any notice or
signboard.
5. Health
5.1 No person who is suffering from any contagious or infectious disease shall enter the camp area. Every person
shall thoroughly heed to follow the health instructions currently operative in the country.
5.2 The participation of the devotees is limited to the date of the Maha Perahera and the crowd should be
provided with the required health facilities as per the health instructions.
5.3 Other days on which the Perahera to be conducted shall be without devotees and shall limited to the rituals
only.
5.4 Dancing troupes to be participated shall be limit and keeping records on the artists, doing testings before
starting the Perahera and providing accommodation and following health instructions shall be adhered.
5.5 The food provided to the participants of the procession shall be supervised by the Medical Officer of Health.
5.6 Providing health services before and during the Perahera shall be entrusted to the Office of the Medical
Officer of Health- Udawalawa, District Hospital- Embilipitiya, Base Hospital - Udawalawa and the Provincial
Directorate of Health Services.
6. Trade
6.1 No person shall, within the camp area, sell lotteries, flags or collect contributions for any other purpose
without my permission or the permission given by an authorized officer and sale and consumption of tobacco
products and every kind of drugs are strictly prohibited in the premises and within the Devalaya boundary,
during the period of conducting the Perahera.
6.2 Actions shall be taken to keep closed the boutiques which are currently selling flowers and other sacred items
in the camp area from 5.00 p.m. until the Perahera ends.

7. Beggars
• No person shall beg or solicit alms within the camp area except such places as may be appointed for the
purpose outside the sacred area.
8. Power Supply Facilities
• Ceylon Electricity Board and Pradeshiya Sabha- Embilipitiya Shall be responsible for the installation of street
lamps and extra lamps on the temple premises and the Perahera route.
9. Water
• Installation of additional water tanks providing water for bowsers and providing drinking water facilities to
the devotees is entrusted to the National Water supply and Drainage Board,Udawalawa, Pradeshiya Sabha,
Embilipitiya, and to the Divisional Secretariat Embilipitiya.
10. Security
• All security arrangements and handling traffic on the road without obstructing the devotees shall be assigned
to the security forces and to the police.
11. Accommodations
• Kolambageara Maha Vidyalaya and Sankhapala Gunaratana Vidyalaya are nominated for accommodation for
the dancing troupes participating in the Perahera and carrying out such activities as per the health instructions
shall be entrusted to the Zonal Education Office and the principals of the aforesaid schools.
Orders 1 to 11 above shall be valid until the end of the Perahera Festival.
