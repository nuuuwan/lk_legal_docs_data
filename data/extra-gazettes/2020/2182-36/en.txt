04. General role of the service
Orientation of Teachers in relation to syllabuses and textbooks acting as specialists in the relevant subject in the
Education Zone / Division under the purview of the Line and Provincial Education Ministries in conformity to the
National Education Policy, implementation of tasks necessary to train Teachers identifying the teacher  training
needs of the zone, active contribution in the process of national research and evaluation work and facilitation
towards the enhancement of the education standards as regards their subjects in the Division / Zone.

11. Appointment to the posts
The Secretary of the Ministry of Education, in accordance with the number of posts approved for each province, will release
the officers of the Teacher Advisors Service to the posts in the Provincial Public Service with the concurrence of the
Provincial Education Authorities. The placement of officers in the Provincial Public Service and making transfers are handled
by the Provincial Education Authority.
12. Conditions governing in the service
12.1. The apprentice officers recruited to the service following the completion of the induction training conducted in
an institution decided by the Secretary of the Ministry of Education should obtain the certificate concerned.
Those officers who  fail to complete the induction training will be reverted to their former posts or their service
will be terminated.
12.2. Every officer recruited following an acting period of one year will be confirmed in service subject to the provisions
in 12.1 above, the provisions of the Establishments Code and the Procedural Rules of the Public Service
Commission.

14.1. Absorption to the Grade System
This Service Minute is applicable only to those who are in service as at the effective date thereof. All officers
drawing salaries under the Salary Scale of GE-01 or GE-02 on the due date as per the Public Administration
Circular No. 02/2006 and Circulars incidental thereto are absorbed subject to the provisions in Section 04 of
Chapter VII of the Establishments Code as follows.
The period of service is computed based on the date of appointment to the post of Teacher Advisor. However,
there should be no change in the date of salary increment of the officer concerned due to the reason of
absorption and the said date should be in force unchanged as the date of salary increment that existed prior to
the absorption. Furthermore, the officer concerned should not be placed in the next higher salary step in terms
of Section 4.4 of Chapter VII of the Establishments Code on his last drawing salary step being in correspondence
with the new salary step in the conversion.
(i) GE-03 – Grade I
(a) Teachers with an active and satisfactory period of service not less than twenty (20) years in the Sri Lanka
Teacher Service and those who have been duly appointed to attend to the duties of the post of Teacher
Advisor by the Provincial Director of Education or Secretary of the Provincial Ministry of Education or
Provincial Public Service Commission having not less than ten (10) years of service in the said post will be
absorbed in to the Grade I of the Teacher Advisors Service.
(b) The period of service with experience in the post of Teacher Advisor will be reckoned as a period of service
in Grade II from the date on which the officers absorbed into Grade I of the Teacher Advisors Service
complete an active and satisfactory period of service of twenty (20) years in the Sri Lanka Teachers Service
or the date on which they complete the experience in service of ten (10) years on being duly appointed to
attend to the duties relating to the post of Teacher Advisor, whichever comes later.
(ii) GE-03 – Grade II
(a) Teachers who have not completed the qualification in Sub Sections above having not less than ten (10)
years of active and satisfactory period of service in the Sri Lanka Teachers Service and who have been duly
appointed by the Provincial Director of Education or secretary of Provincial Ministry of Education or
Provincial Public Service Commission will be absorbed into Grade II of the Teacher Advisors Service.
(b) The period of active and satisfactory service with experience in the post of Teacher Advisor will be
reckoned as a period of service in Grade II from the date on which the officers absorbed into Grade II of the
Teacher Advisors Service have been duly appointed to attend to the duties relating to the post of Teacher
Advisors or the date on which they complete an active and satisfactory period of service of ten (10) years
in the Sri Lanka Teachers Service, whichever comes later.
14.2. Conditions of absorption
All officers who are absorbed into the Service should complete the first Efficiency Bar Examination stated in 8.1
and the requirement of Link Language stated in 9.1 within a period of five (05) years from the effective date of
the Service Minute.
14.3. The teachers who have been duly appointed to attend to duties relating to the post of Teacher Advisor by the
Provincial Director of Education or the Secretary of the Provincial Ministry of Education or Chief Provincial
Secretary or the Provincial Public Service Commission should express their willingness for absorption subject
to the conditions set out in the Service Minute of Teacher Advisors service within a period of six (06) months
from the effective date of this Service Minute. The secretary of the Ministry of Education will issue a notice with
application to express such willingness subsequent to publishing this Service Minute.

Particulars on the competitive examination for the recruitment of officers to the Class 2 of the Teacher Advisors Service
01. Written Examination.– Written examination will consist of following question papers:
Subject Duration Marks
01. Practical Education Psychology, Education Management, 2 Hours 100
A case study on modern education practices
02. Proficiency in related area of subject 2 Hours 100
02. Syllabus
02.1. Practical Education Psychology, Education Management, A case study on modern education practices
(Duration - 2 hours)
Answers to questions synchronous to short questions in line with following case study areas should be
furnished in the accompaniment of collateral concomitance of education curricular alongside the candidate’s
creative skills based on his practical experiences as a Teacher Advisor, not merely on his theoretical knowledge:
I. Distinguishing of Management Concepts and Five pronged roles of Education Management,
II. Processes of Learning, Teaching and Evaluation and Supervision, role of teacher in above process and
the importance of education for everybody,
III. Students with special needs and resolution of practical class room issues,
Eg :- Class room management, ways of achieving optimum productivity from minimum of resources,
IV . Organization and implementation of activities associated with subjects,
V. Methods of teacher skill evaluation,
VI. Principles of supervision, ways of supervision, problems confronted and solutions thereto,
VII. Nature of school curriculum, The manner in which the school can be affiliated by the curriculum and
how to bring about professional trend,
VIII. Approaches to varied education researches, data collection methods and analyses.
