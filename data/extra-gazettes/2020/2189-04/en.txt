IMPORTS AND EXPORTS (CONTROL) ACT, No. 1 OF 1969
In terms of the powers vested in me by Section 20 read together with Sub-Section 4(1), Section 6 and Section 14 of the Imports
and Exports (Control) Act, No. 1 of 1969 as amended by Act No. 48 of 1985 and Act No. 28 of 1987, I, Mahinda Rajapaksa,
the Minister of Finance, promulgate following Regulations.
M
AHINDA RAJAPAKSA,
Minister of Finance.
Ministry of Finance,
Colombo 01,
17th August, 2020.
REGULATIONS
1. The Following Regulations are issued as an interim measure for strengthening and reviving the national economy
from the unprecedented consequences of the COVID-19 pandemic and these regulations may be cited as the
Imports and Exports (Control) Regulations No. 05 of 2020.
2. Regulation Nos. 19, 20, 21 and 23 of the Imports and Exports (Control) Regulations No. 02 of 2020, published in the
Gazette Extraordinary No. 2176/19 dated 22nd May 2020 and Regulation Nos. 4, 9, 10, 11, 12, 14 and 15 of the
Imports and Exports (Control) Regulations No. 03 of 2020, published in the Gazette Extraordinary No. 2182/10

dated 09 November 2017 as Regulation No. 4
“4. Banks shall not process any payment relating to importation of any items which are subject to Import
Control License, as specified in the Schedule I of the Gazette Extraordinary No. 2044/40 dated 09 Novem-
ber 2017 as amended, without a valid Import Control License”.
5. Final decision on any issues arising in relation to implementation of these Regulations shall be made by the
Controller General of Imports and Exports in consultation with the Minister of Finance considering the revival of the
national economy from consequences of the COVID-19 pandemic, public health, environment and national security.
