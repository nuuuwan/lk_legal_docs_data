(5) “By insertion of the following new Heading and “01. Subjects and Functions, 02. Special Priorities and
03. Related Institutional and Legal Framework” under thereof after the Heading No. “10.2 State Minister of
Indigenous Medicine Promotion, Rural and Ayurvedic Hospitals Development and Community Health” in that
notification;
10.3  State Minister of Primary Health Care, Epidemics and COVID Disease Control
01. Duties and Functions
Assisting in the formulation of policies in relation to the subject of Primary Health Care, Epidemics and
COVID Disease Control for the creation of “Healthy Community” under the direction and guidance of
the Minister of Health in conformity with the prescribed Laws, Acts and Ordinances and implementing,
projects under the National Budget, State Investment and National Development Programme, and
implementing, monitoring and evaluating subjects and functions of the below-mentioned State
Corporations and Statutory Organizations.
02. Special Priorities
1. Implementing a joint programme with all offices of Provincial and Regional Director of Health
Services and Medical Officer of Health Offices in coordination with the Ministry of Health for the
control of COVID-19 and other communicable diseases.
2. Prevention of the spread of epidemics and strengthening the maternity and infant health care
services to keep the maternal and infant mortality rate as low as possible.
3. Expansion of Medical, nursing and other allied support facilities in all regional and primary hospitals
so that the rural people can easily access health services.
4. Implementing relevant programs to improve the immunity of children.
5. Taking necessary steps to improve primary health care and basic infrastructure.
6. Implementing health promotion and health education programmes to enhance public health
education.
7. Expansion of adult health care facilities targeting low income earners in rural, backward and urban
areas.
8. Strengthening the health services of low income and rural people by coordinating the dispensaries
and maternity clinics owned by Local Government Institutions, the Department of Health Services,
Family Health Care Services, Provincial Ministries and Departments of Health.
