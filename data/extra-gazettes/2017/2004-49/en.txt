 PUBLIC  SERVICE   COMMISSION
Delegation of Powers by the Public Service Commission in terms of Article 57(1) of the Constitution
01. The Public Service Commission in terms of the provisions in Article 57(1) of the Constitution of the Democratic
Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka hereby delegates to the Public Officers stated in Column II of the Schedule "A"
hereto, the powers set out in Column I thereof.
02. However, where there exists an uncertainty concerning the scope of any delegation of powers made by the
Public Service Commission or the discharge of any delegation of powers so made by the said Commission, the
decision of the Public Service Commission shall be final and conclusive.
03. Moreover, in the event that a Public Officer referred to in Column II of the Schedule hereto, is either the
complainant or a witness or a potential witness at any disciplinary inquiry in respect of an officer belonging to
a category set out in the corresponding item in Column I of the Schedule hereto, the powers delegated in
respect of such officer shall be deemed to have been rescinded and the Public Service Commission shall
exercise the powers so delegated in respect of such officer, as if no such delegation has been made in respect
of such officer.
04. The provisions relating to the subject of Health concerning the delegation of powers set out in the Schedule "A"
published in Gazette Extraordinary No.1990/24 dated 27.10.2016 that delegated powers in terms of Article
57(1) of the Constitution of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka are hereby rescinded, without
prejudice to any action taken or purported to be taken under the Delegation of Powers published in the aforesaid
Gazette .

Service Commission as stated in the Schedule "B" hereto, the powers set out in Column II in respect of the

Service Commission or the discharge of any delegation of powers so made by the said Commission, the decision
of the Public Service Commission shall be final and conclusive.

published in Gazette Extraordinary No.1990/24 dated 27.10.2016 that delegated powers in terms of Article
57(1) of the Constitution of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka are hereby rescinded, without
prejudice to any action taken or purported to be taken under the Delegation of Powers published in the aforesaid
Gazette .
