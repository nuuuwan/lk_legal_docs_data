 Revocation of Order under Section 4(1)
WHEREAS by Order made under Section 4(1) of the Industrial Disputes Act, Chapter 131 of the Legislative Enactments of Ceylon (Revised
Edition 1956) as amended by Acts, No. 14 and 62 of 1957, 14 of 1962 and 39 of  1968 read with Industrial Disputes (Special Provisions)
Act No. 37 of 1968 dated 21.01.2016 and published in the Gazette of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka Extraordinary No.
1951/8 dated 25.01.2016 the Industrial Dispute in respect of the matter specified in the statement of the Commissioner of Labou r dated
21.12.2015.
Mr. C. J. Ranjith, No. 16/3, Terrance Avenue, Via St. Anthony's Road, Mount Lavinia.   of the one part
and
 Bank of Ceylon, Head Office, No. 04, Bank of Ceylon Mawatha, Colombo 01.   of the other part
Was referred to Mr. T. E. Santharajan for settlement by arbitration.
AND whereas it is now deemed expedient that the said Order be revoked, I, W. D.J. Seneviratne, Minister of Labour, Trade Union
Relations and Sabaragamu Development do hereby revoke the said Order and further make order that no proceedings be taken upon the said
Order dated 21.01.2016.
W.D.J. SENEVIRATNE,
Minister of Labour, Trade Union Relations and
 Sabaragamu Development.

 Bank of Ceylon, Head Office, No. 04, Bank of Ceylon Mawatha, Colombo 01.  of the other part
STATEMENT OF MATTER IN DISPUTE
The matter in dispute between the aforesaid parties is –
Whether injustice had been caused to Mr. C. J. Ranjith who was an Employee of the Bank of Ceylon,
01. By being terminated from the said bank on 08.09.1987 on the ground of vacation of post,
02. Being reinstated in his post on 19.06.2003 unconditionally but disputed by the said Bank, and
03. Non granting of arrears of salary, Promotions and other dues upon reinstatement from 08.09.1987 to 19.06.2003
and if so, to what reliefs he is entitled.
Dated at the office of the Commissioner General of Labour.
Colombo this 22nd day of July 2017.
A. W
IMALAWEERA,
