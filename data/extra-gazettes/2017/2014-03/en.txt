 PUBLIC  SERVICE  COMMISION
Delegation of Powers by the Public Service Commission in terms of Article 57 (1) of the Constitution
01. THE Public Service Commission in terms of the provisions in Article 57(1) of the Constitution of the  Democratic
Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka hereby delegates to the Public Officers  stated in Column  II of the Schedule “A”  hereto,
the powers set out in Column  III in  respect of the Category of officers stated Column I thereof.
02. However, where there exists an uncertainty concerning the scope of any delegation of powers made by the Public Service
Commission or the discharge of any delegation of powers so made by the said Commission, the decision of the Public
Service Commission shall be final and conclusive.
03. Moreover, in the event that a Public Officer referred to in Column II of the Schedule hereto, is either the complainant or
a witness or a potential witness at any disciplinary inquiry in respect of an officer belonging to a category set out in the
corresponding item in Column I of the Schedule hereto, the powers delegated in respect of such officer shall be deemed
to have been rescinded and the Public Service Commission  shall exercise the powers so delegated in respect of such
officer, as if no such delegation has been made in respect of such officer.
04. The provisions relating to the subject of Education concerning the delegation of powers set out in the Schedule “ A ”
published in Gazette Extraordinary No.1989/29 dated 19.10.2016 that delegated powers in terms of Article 57(1) of the
Constitution of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka are hereby rescinded, without prejudice to any action
taken or purported to have been taken under the Delegation of Powers published in the aforesaid Gazette.

Delegation of Powers by the Public Service Commission in terms of Article 56 (1) of the
Constitution
01. The Public Service Commission in terms of the provisions in Article 56 (1) of the Constitution of the Democratic Socialist
Republic of Sri Lanka hereby delegates to the Education Service Committee appointed by the Public Service Commission,
the powers set out in Column II of the Schedule “B” hereto in respect of the category of officers st ated in Column I
thereof.
02. However, where there exists an uncertainty concerning the scope of any delegation of powers made by the Public Service
Commission or the discharge of any delegation of powers so made by the said Commission, the decision of the Public
Service Commission shall be final and conclusive.
03. The provisions relating to the subject of Education concerning the delegation of powers set out in the Schedule “B”
published in Gazettes Extraordinary No.1989/29 dated 19.10.2016 and No.1992/7 dated 07.11.2016 that delegated powers
in terms of Article 56(1) of the Constitution of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka are hereby rescinded,
without prejudice to any action taken or purported to have been taken under the Delegation of Powers published in the
aforesaid Gazettes.
