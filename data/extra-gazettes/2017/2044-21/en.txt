THE  FINANCE  ACT,  No.  11  OF  2006
Notification
BY virtue of the powers vested in me by Section 6(2) read with Section 8 of the Finance Act, No. 11 of 2006, I, Mangala
Samaraweera, Minister of Finance and Mass Media, do by this Order declare the following regulations. These regulations
shall come into force with effect from 08
th November, 2017.
MANGALA  S AMARAWEERA ,
Minister of Finance and Mass Media.
Ministry of Finance and Mass Media,
Colombo 01,
07
th November, 2017.
REGULATIONS
1. These regulations may be cited as the Finance (Teledrama, Film and Commercials Levy) Regulations, No. 1 of
2017.
2. The Orders made under Section 8 of the Finance Act, No. 11 of 2006 and published in Extraordinary Gazette
Notification No. 1451/16 of 29.06.2006, the Extraordinary Gazette  Notification No. 1455/6 of 24.07.2006 and the
Extraordinary Gazette Notification No. 1465/21 of 05.10.2006 are hereby rescinded.
3. The Teledrama, Film and Commercials Levy (hereinafter referred to as “the Levy”), chargeable in terms of
Part II of the Finance Act, No. 11 of 2006 (hereinafter referred to as “the Act”), shall be charged by “The Secretary”, from
and after the date of the coming into operation of these regulations, in relation to –
(a) every teledrama or film at such rates as corresponds to the duration of the telecast of such tele-drama or film as
is specified in Part (i) ; and

4. The Levy charged as aforesaid shall be remitted to the Consolidated Fund within fifteen days from the date of
collection.
5. No teledrama, film or commercial shall be telecast by any licensed institution unless such institution has obtained
a Certificate of Clearance issued by the Secretary in accordance with the provisions of regulation 13 of these regulations.
Where the Secretary is of the opinion that telecasting of any teledrama, film or commercial is likely to be
obnoxious to the Sri Lankan values, Sri Lankan identity or Sri Lankan ethics, he may request the Panel referred to in
Regulation 11 of these regulations for a determination as regards the suitability or otherwise of telecasting such teledrama,
film or commercial. Where the Panel determines that such teledrama, film or commercial is so obnoxious, the Secretary
shall refuse to issue such Certificate of Clearance and shall, in writing inform such institution accordingly.
Provided that where the Panel requires such institution to comply with such conditions as may be deemed by the
Panel to be appropriate, the Secretary shall, if such institution agrees to comply with such requirement, issue such Certificate
of Clearance specifying such conditions as an alternative to such refusal and it shall be the duty of such institution to
comply with such requirement.
6. The Secretary shall, for the purposes of the Act, in consultation with the Secretaries of the relevant line
Ministries, appoint a Group of Resource Persons consisting of not more than thirty persons. These persons shall be
chosen from among persons who have gained eminence in fields such as theatre, television, literature, languages, science,
history, psychology, law and Sri Lankan Culture.
7. (1) Every licensed institution seeking to telecast a teledrama or film, shall forthwith pay the Levy in terms of
Part (i) of Schedule I to these regulations, where –
(a) the licensed institution on its own motion determines that it is liable to pay the Levey ; or
(b) the Certificate issued under regulation 12, requires the licensed institution to pay the Levy referred
to therein, as such teledrama or film is not eligible for exemption in terms of regulation 8.
(2) ( a) Every licensed institution shall, before telecasting any commercial, pay the Levy at the appropriate rate
specified in Part (ii) of the First Schedule to these regulations.
(b) The Levy paid by any licensed institution – (the first mentioned licensed institution) - shall also be
deemed to have been paid by any other licensed institution nominated for the purpose by the first-
mentioned licensed institution - (the nominated licensed institution) - and accordingly such nominated
licensed institution may proceed in accordance with Regulation 13 of these regulations ; the Secretary
shall, as regards the fact of having paid the Levy by such nominated licensed institution, accept the
statement furnished by such nominated licensed institution and referred to in that regulation.
8. Every licensed institution seeking to telecast a teledrama or film shall in terms of the proviso to subsection
(1) of section 6 of the Act be eligible to claim exemptions if it is of the opinion that the Levy is not chargeable in respect
of such teledrama or film as it falls into any one or more of the following categories, namely :
(a) documentary ; or
(b) educational ; or
(c) solely for the purpose of providing entertainment for children ; or

(d) prescribed categories :–
(i) religious ;
(ii) any international or regional event ;
(iii) any teledrama or film originally produced in Sinhala, Tamil or English, and not dubbed ;
(iv) any teledrama or film of which the main cast and the majority of the production crew are Sri Lankan
citizens.
“For the purposes of this regulation,
i. “main cast” means the actors and actresses, who play the main roles including the main actor, main
actress, supporting actor and supporting actress.
ii. the “majority of the production crew of any teledrama or film shall be deemed to consist of Sri
Lankan citizens, if more than one-half of the production crew including the director, the assistant
director, the director of music, the main cameraman, the script writer, the main editor, the main
background male singer and the main background female singer consists of citizens of Sri Lanka”.
9. Every licensed institution seeking to telecast any teledrama or film shall, where such institution seeks to
claim an exemption in terms of regulation 8 of these regulations make an application in that behalf to the Secretary
substantially in the form specified in Schedule II to these regulations.
10. The Secretary shall, on receipt of such application refer the same to a Panel constituted in the manner set out
in regulation 11 of these regulations to make a determination as to whether or not such teledrama or film is eligible for an
exemption as set out in regulation 8 of these regulations.
11. Panels comprising not more than three persons from among the members of the Group of Resource Persons
appointed under regulation 6 shall be nominated by the Secretary to consider the applications submitted under regulation
9 and make a determination thereon. One of the members of the Panel shall be nominated to be the Chairman. The
Secretary shall in selecting the Panels be guided by the subject expertise of such persons.
12. The panel shall be required to make a determination as to whether or not the teledrama or film referred to the
Panel is eligible for an exemption as set out in regulation 8 of these regulations. Upon arriving at a determination on the
matter referred to them, the Panel shall notify the Secretary of its decision by forwarding to him of a Certificate in the
form set out in Schedule III to these regulations. The decision of a Panel shall be final.
12A. Any licensed institution aggrieved by the determination of a Panel under Regulation 5 or Regulation
12 may, within fourteen days from the date on which such determination is conveyed to such institution,
appeal against such determination to the Board of Appeal. The appeal shall, be in writing and shall state
the grounds of appeal. The Board of Appeal shall on receipt of any appeal hear and determine the
appeal within fifteen days of its receipt.
For the purposes of this regulation “Board of Appeal” means a Board, the members of which shall be
the Secretary and four persons chosen by the Secretary from the Group of Resource Persons referred
to in Regulation 5, so however that no Resource Person who was a member of the Panel which made
the determination appealed against, shall be a member of the Board of Appeal, The Secretary shall be
the Chairman of the Board of Appeal. The decision of the Board of Appeal which shall be conveyed to
such institution by the Secretary, shall be final and conclusive for the purpose of these regulation.
13. Every licensed institution which has paid the Levy in respect of any teledrama, film or commercial as provided for
in regulation 7 of these regulations, shall furnish to the Secretary a written Statement in the form set out in Schedule IV to
these regulations, no later than two weeks prior to the intended date for the telecasting of such teledrama or film or any part

14. The Secretary shall from time to time issue guidelines setting out the criteria to be taken into consideration

15. For the purposes of these regulations, –
“licensed institution” means in institution licensed under the Sri Lanka Rupavahini Corporations Act, No. 6 of

“the Secretary” means the Secretary to the Ministry of the Minister to whom the subject of Mass Media is
assigned ;
“teledrama, film or commercial” means any teledrama, film or commercial filmed or made outside Sri Lanka and
brought into, or imported into Sri Lanka for the purpose of being telecast.
