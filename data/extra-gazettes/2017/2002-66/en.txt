PART IV (A) - GAZETTE   EXTRAORDINARY   OF   THE   DEMOCRATIC   SOCIALIST   REPUBLIC   OF   SRI   LANKA - 20.01.2017
1. This Statute may be cited as the Sabaragamuwa Provincial Appropriation Statute No. 04 of
2016 and shall come into operation of the date of receiving the assent of the Governor of the Sabaragamuwa
Province.
2.(1) Without prejudice to any other law authorizing any expenditure of the Sabaragamuwa
Provincial Council which estimate will be Rupees Twenty Five Billion Seven Hundred and Nine Million
Two Hundred Thousand (Rs. 25,709,200,000) for the service of the period beginning on 01st January
2017 and ending on 31st December 2017, in this Statute referred to as the “Financial year - 2017” shall
be met.
(a) For payments which are hereby authorized to be made out of the Sabaragamuwa Provincial
Council fund or any other fund or monies of or at the disposal of the Sabaragamuwa
Provincial Council and,
(b) For the proceeds of loans which are hereby authorized to be raised, within the province of
Sabaragamuwa for and on behalf of the Sabaragmauwa Provincial Council. The sum of
Rupees Twenty Five Billion Seven Hundred and Nine Million Two Hundred Thousand
(Rs. 25,709,200,000) here in before referred to may be duly expended as specified in the
first Schedule to this Statute.
(2) The provision of subsection (1) of this section shall have effect witout prejudice to the
provisions of any other written law authorizing the raising of loans for and on behalf of the Sabaragamuwa
Provincial Council.
3. (1) The receipts of the Sabaragamuwa Provincial Council during the financial year, 2017 from
each activity specified in Column (iv) of the Second  Schedule to this statute shall be credited to the
account of such activity, the aggregate of receipts so credited shall be not less than the minimum limit
specified in the corresponding entry in Column (vi) of that Schedule the net surplus, if any, of such
activity shall be paid to the Sabaragamuwa Provincial Council fund before the expire of six months after
the close of the financial year 2017.
(2) For the purpose of determining the net surplus under Subsection (1) of the following
charges shall be set off against the revenue of each activity.
(a) The working, establishment and other expenses of the activity whether paid or accrued
properly chargeable to the revenue of the activity and,
(b) Provision to cover the depreciation of the movable and immovable property of the activity.

4. The expenditure incurred by the Sabaragamuwa Provincial Council, during the financial year
2017 each activity specified in Column (iv) of the second schedule to this statute shall be paid out of the
receipt of the Sabaragamuwa Provincial Council from such expenditure shall not exceed the maximum
limit specified in the corresponding entry in Column (v) of that Schedule.
5. The debit balance, outstanding at the end of the financial year 2017 of any activity specified
in Column (iv) of the second schedule to this statute shall not exceed the maximum limit specified in the
corresponding entry in Column (vii) of that Schedule, and the total liabilities of that activity at the end of
that financial year shall not exceed the maximum limt specified in the corresponding entry column (viii)
of that Schedule.
6. Whenever at any time durng the financial year 2017 the receipts of that Sabaragamuwa
Provincial Council from any activity specified in Column (iv) of the second schedule to this statute are
insufficient to meet the expenditure incurred by the Sabaragamuwa Provincial Council on such activity,
the minister may, from time to time, by order direct that such sums may deem necessary to meet such
expenditure shall be payable by way of advances out of Sabaragamuwa Provincial Council fund or any
other fund or monies of or at the disposal of, the Sabaragamuwa Provincial Council, so however that the
aggregate of the sums so advances does not exceed the maximum limit of entry in column (v) of that
schedule. Any sum so advanced in respect of such activity shall be refunded to the Sabaragamuwa Provincial
Council fund in such manner as the Minister may by order direct.
7. (1) Any money which, by virture of the provissions of the first schedule to this statute,
have been allocated to recurrent expenditure under any programme appearing undr any head specified in
that schedule but have not been expended or may likely to be unexpended, may be transferred to the
allocatoin of recurrent expenditure or capital expenditure under any other programme within that head
by order of the Chief Secretary to the Sabaragamuwa Provincial Council or any other officer authorized
by him.
(2) Any money allocated to capital expenditure under  any programme appearing under any
head specified in the first schedule to this statute shall be transferred out of that programme or project
only by order of the Chief Secretary of Sabaragamuwa Provincial Council.
8. (1) That the receipts from taxes and other sources wil be less than the amount anticipated to
finance authorized expenditure, or ;
(2) That amount originally appropriated for a paticular purpose or purposes are no logner

part, any amounts previously  released for expenditure under the authority of a warrant issued by him
from the Sabaragamuwa Provincial Council fund or  from any other fund or monies of or at the disposal
of the Sabaragamuwa Provincial Council.
9. The Minister with the approval of the Sabaragamuwa Provincial Council may, on or before
31st May 2017 by order, vary or alter.
(a) Any of the maximum limits specified in Column (v), Column (vii) and Column (viii) of
the second schedule to this Statute.
(b) The maximum limits specified in Column (vi) of the Second Schedule to this Statute.
No order made  under this Section shall have effect unless it has been approved by the
Sabaragamuwa Provincial Council by resolution.
Any such order shall, if so expressed therein be deemed to have had effect from such date prior
