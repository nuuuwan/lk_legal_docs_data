FISHERIES AND AQUATIC RESOURCES  ACT No. 2 OF 1996
REGULATIONS made by the Minister of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources Development and State Minister of Mahaweli
Development under Section 61(1)(u) of the Fisheries and Aquatic Resources Act, No. 2 of 1996.
MAHINDA A AMARAWEERA,
Minister of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources Development,
and State Minister of Mahaweli Development.
Colombo,
14th June, 2017.
Regulations
1. These regulations may be cited as the Fish and Fishery Products, Export, Import and Re-export Management
Regulations 2017.
2. Subject to the provisions of the Food Act, No. 26 of 1980 and any regulation made thereunder relating to fish
species or fishery product imported for local consumption, any person engaged in, exporting, importing, and re-exporting
of any fish species or fishery product for food, for human consumption or to be used as a bait in fresh, frozen or canned
form or any type of processed or live form shall be subject to the terms and conditions of these regulations.
3. Export, import or re-export of fish and fishery products shall be subject to the terms and conditions imposed
by the Director-General in accordance with international obligations and to the provisions of the Customs Ordinance
(Chapter 235) and the Imports and Exports (Control) Act, No. 1 of 1969.
4. Exporter, importer or re-exporter of any Fish or Fishery product shall fulfill the requirements stipulated in
accordance with the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and  Flora (CITES).

PART  I
REGISTRATION FOR EXPORT, IMPORT AND RE-EXPORT OF ANY FISH OR FISHERY PRODUCT
5. Any person engaged in Export, import and re-export of any Fish or Fishery products shall register  with the
Department of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources for that purpose.
6. Any person who intends to register an establishment to export, import and re-export of fish or fishery products
shall submit an application in Form I, Form 2 and Form 3 respectively specified in Schedule I of these regulations to the
Director General subject to terms and conditions issued by him.
7. Annual registration fee specified in Schedule 2 hereto shall be paid by the applicant together with the
application for registration of any establishment.
8. Any authorized officer shall conduct an on site inspection at the relevant establishment or company where
applicable according to the guidelines on export, import and re-export of any Fish or Fishery product.
PART II
REQUIREMENTS FOR EXPORT, IMPORT AND RE-EXPORT OF FISH AND FISHERY PRODUCTS
9. These regulations shall be applicable to any fish or fishery product entering Sri Lanka with a valid bill of lading
or airway bill.
10. Any fish or fishery product entering Sri Lanka without a valid bill of laden or airway bill or engaged in
transshipment shall be subject to the Port State Measures to prevent, deter and eliminate illegal, unreported and
unregulated Fishing Regulations 2015.
11. Any person carrying out on-port transactions of fish or fishery products shall furnish a bill of lading and other
traceability documents as specified by the Director-General.
12. Prior to coming into operation of these regulations no person shall engage in export or re-export of fishery
products in an establishment unless such establishment is registered with the Department of Fisheries and Aquatic
Resources under Fish Processing Establishment Regulations No. 1 of 2003.
13. Any person who intends to export, import and re-export of any fish or fishery product shall declare the
species, type and form of product according to the applicable HS codes which are in accordance with the international
commodity coding system.
14. No person shall export, import and re-export of any fish or fishery products prohibited under Fisheries and
Aquatic Resources Act, No. 02 of 1996.
15. No person shall export, import and re-export any fish or fishery product categorized or declared as
originated from illegal, unreported or unregulated fishing by any Regional Fisheries Management Organization (RFMO).
16. No person shall export fish or fishery products which are not clearly traceable in the accompanying certified
log sheet it applicable.
17. No person shall export, import or re-export any fish or fishery products without a valid catch certificate
where appliable or a validating document as legally caught fish or fishery product made out of such fish in terms of the
conditions specified in the Fish Catch Date Collection Regulation 2014.
18. Details of all fish species shall be provided under the statistical document programmes of relevant Regional
Fisheries Management Organization as required by the Director-General.

19. Statistical documents mentioned in regulation 18 shall be validated by an authorized government official of
the flag state of the vessel that harvested the fish, or if the vessel is operating under a charter arragement, by an authorize d
officer of the exporting state.
20. The re-export certificate of such statistical document shall be validated by an officer authorized by the
Director-General in writing.
21. The Director-General with the approval of the Minister assigned the subjects of Fisheries and Aquatic
Resources Development may impose precautionary management measures such as closure periods, limitations for sizes,
volume, quantities, types, forms and duration for export or re-export for conservation purpose.
PART III
INSPECTION OF IMPORT AND FISH PROCESSING ESTABLISHMENT FOR EXPORT OR RE-EXPORT OF FISH AND FISHERY PRODUCTS
22. Every importer, owner or license of the fish or fishery product processing establishment shall permit and
facilitate any officer authorized by the Director-General to enter, inspect, collect samples and data and documents at the
said establishment or at the point of import without prior notice.
23. Such officer authorized by the Director-General shall provide a details inspection report within seven days
of the inspection specified in regulation 22 carried out in terms of the said regulation.
PART IV
Suspension or cancellation of the Registration and Licenses
24. Where any person contravenes any provisions of these regulations, the Director-General shall suspend or
cancel the registration for a period not exceeding six months, taking into consideration the magnitude of such
contravention.
25. An applicant or any person who is aggrieved by the decision of the Director-General refusing to grant a
certificate or the renewal of a certificate issued, as the case may be, shall be, entitled to appeal against such decision to
the Secretary of the Ministry of the Minister assigned the subject of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources Development,
within thirty days of such decision.
26. The Secretary may upon receipt of such appeal, make such inquiries as he may consider necessary or
approprirate and thereafter.
(a) allow the appeal and direct of the Director-General to issue a certificate or renew a certificate issued, as
the case may be ; or
(b) disallow the appeal and confirm the decision made by the Director-General.
27. In these regulations :
“Air freight” means import, export or re-export by air ;
“Authorized Officer” means any officer authorized under subsection (1) of Section 46 of the Fisheries
and Aquatic Resources Act, No. 02 of 1996 ;
“Baits” means Food placed on a fishing gear to entice fish or other animals ;

“Catch Certificate” means a certification given by an authority of the flag country of a fishing vesseel to
prove the particular type of fish caught by the same vessel has not been involved in any activity
relating to illegal, unregulated and unreported fishing.
“CITES” means obligations declared under convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of
Wild Fauna and Flora ;
“Closed Period” means a period of the year in which the catching of a particular type of fish is not
allowed ;
“Director General” means the Director General of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources appointed under
Section 2 of the Fisheries and Aquatic Resources Act, No. 2 of 1996 ;
“Fish or Fishery product” means any type of Fish or Fishery product for food, for human consumption or
to be used as bait in fresh, frozen or canned form and any type of processed form or live form.
“HS code” means Harmonized Commodity Description and Coding Systems, also known as the
Harmonized System (HS) of tariff nomenclature is an internationally standardized system of names
and numbers to classify traded products.
“Illegal, Unregulated and Unreported” refers to the activities set out in paragraph I of the resolution 17103
adopted by Indian Ocean Tuna Commission specified in Section 14G of the Fisheries and Aquatic
Resources Act, No. 2 of 1996 ;
“Process” means any type of value addtion to the fish or fishery product subject to export, import and re-
export purposes.
“Re-export” means the process of exporting products or goods that were previously imported into a
country ;
“Regional Fisheries Management Bodies” means a mechanism through which States or organizations that
are parties to an international fishery agreement or arrangement work together towards the
conservation, management and development of fisheries.
“Third party exporter” means a person or party who re-export imported fish or fishery products processed
through an approved establishment owned by other person or party.
“Vessel” means any vessel ship or boat used for or intended to be used for fishing ;
“World Trade Organization” means the global international organization dealing with the rules of trade
between nations ;
