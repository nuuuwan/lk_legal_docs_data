MINUTE   OF   THE   CENTRAL   PROVINCIAL   PUBLIC   MANAGEMENT   ASSISTANTS’   SERVICE
Amendment   No. 01
Hon. Governor on Central Province has approved to amend the Minute of the Central Provincial Public Management
Assistants’ Service published in the Gazette Extraordinary  No. 1869/4 dated 30 th June 2014 of the Democratic
Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka, in the following manner.
1. The Section 7.3.2.3 is removed and substituted by the following Section.
7.3.2.3 Experience:
Shall have completed active and satisfactory service of at least 05 years in a permanent post in the Central
Provincial Public service as at the prescribed date, which fact shall be certified by the Head of the Department
(The clarification under this Section will remain unchanged.)
2.  The following Section is included as serial number (iv) at the end of the Sub sections under the Section
7.3.2.5
7.3.2.5 Other
(iv) Shall have completed a satisfactory service period of five (05) years immediately preceding the prescribed
date.
3. The Section 8.3 is removed and substituted by the following Section.
8.3 Authority for conducting Examination: The Commissioner General of Examinations or an institution authorized
by the Central Provincial Public Service Commission
4. The Section (a) (iii) of the para 10.3.1.1 is removed and substituted by the following Section.
(iii) Shall be an officer who has obtained a degree from a University recognized by the University Grants
Commission and has completed an active and satisfactory service period of five years in the service.
5. The para 10.3.1.2 is removed and substituted by the following para.
10.3.1.2 Method of Promotion
Appointments equivalent to 50% of the total vacancies in Supra Grade shall be made on the results of a limited
competitive examination. Candidates should obtain at least 40 marks for each subject relevant to the examina-
tion for passing the limited competitive examination. The candidates who have satisfied the qualifications shall

6. The para 10.3.2.2 is removed and substituted by the following para.
10.3.2.2 Method of Promotion
Appointments equivalent to 50% of the total vacancies in Supra Grade shall be made on the basis of merit. For
this purpose, candidates should pass an Aptitude Test conducted by the Commissioner General of Examinations
on behalf of the Central Provincial Public Service Commission, obtaining at least 40 marks for each subject.
Priority shall be determined on the results of the said examination and on the order of the aggregate of marks
allocated on the basis of seniority and experience by the board appointed by the Commission. Candidates shall
appear for an interview held by a board appointed by the Commission for verification of qualifications. No marks
shall be allocated at that interview. (Annex 10)
7. The ‘Note’ under the Annex 04 is replaced by the following part.
Note 01
Officers who have satisfied the following requirements of the Computer Test relevant to the Efficiency Bar
Examination of Grade III of the Central Provincial Public Management Assistants’ Service shall be exempted.
01. Officers who have followed computer courses offered by the following institutions, of which the syllabus
is similar to the computer course indicated in the Service Minute.
a. Sri Lanka Institute of Development Administration (SLIDA)
b. National Apprentice and Industrial Training Authority
c. V ocational Training Authority
d. Courses conducted by Technical Colleges and Technological Colleges which function under
the Department of Technical Education and Training.
e. Universities or other institutions recognized by the University Grants Commission
f. National Youth Council
02. Officers who have obtained a degree in Computer Science from a recognized university registered under
the University Grants Commission or officers who have followed Computer Science as a main subject
for the degree.
Note 02
Officers who satisfy the qualifications indicated under the Note in the Annex 04 of the Minute of the Central
Provincial Public Management Assistants’ Service No. 1869/4 dated 30.06.2014 shall also be exempted from the
Computer Test for a period of two years from the date on which the gazette containing these revisions is
published.
