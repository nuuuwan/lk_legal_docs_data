( 1956 REVISED EDITION)
Mr. S. A. D. Siripala, No. 282/1, Makola North, Makola of the one part
and
Sri Lanka Transport Board, No. 200, Kirula Road, Colombo 05, of the other part
STATEMENT OF MATTER IN DISPUTE
The matter in dispute between the aforesaid parties is
Whether the claim made by Mr. S. A. D. Siripala who worked as the Building Administrator of the Sri Lanka Transport Board to be paid the
unpaid salary for 08 months and 9 days (the arrears salary for 08 months and 02 days plus 07 days fine) from 23.08.2011 to 25.04.2012, the
period during which he had been interdicted, setting aside the appeal order dated 22.11.2012 following the disciplinary inquiry held on 03.11.2011
and the disciplinary order dated 24.04.2012 is just and equitable and if so, to what reliefs he is entitled.
M. D. C. AMARATUNGA,
Commissioner of Labour.
Dated at the Office of the Commissioner of Labour,
Colombo, this 08th day of February, 2017.

STATEMENT OF MA TTER IN DISPUTE
The matter in dispute between the aforesaid parties is
Whether the claim made by the five employees recruited to the Thurusaviya Fund in 2002 on contract basis; namely G . K. Sudath Kumara,
S. V . Thushari Priyangika, Nayana Darshani Rajapakse, Sandakelum Prabha de Silva and K. W. M. Chandima Priyadarshani, who had been
placed on salary scales considering that they had earned the salary increments from their date of appointment to the contract basis post and /
or from the date of fulfilling the required educational qualifications and whose confirmations had been backdated, have thereafter been caused
injustice by the reduction of the increased salary stating that the salary revisions were incorrect and the payment of a lesser salary is just and
if so, to what reliefs each of them is entitled.
