AMENDMENT    OF    IMMIGRANTS    AND    EMIGRANTS    REGULATIONS  1956  RELATED  TO
THE    IMMIGRANTS   AND   EMIGRANTS   ACT,  No. 20  OF  1948
BY virtue of powers vested in me under section 52 that should be read in conjunction with the section 23 of the Immigrants
and Emigrants Act pertaining to the Ministerial  subject assigned to me, I Wajira Abeywardana as the Minister of Internal
& Home Affairs and Provincial Councils & Local Government and as per the Cabinet Memorandum No. 8/IA/2019 dated
31.01.2019 submitted by me on   “Amendments to be made to the Immigrants and Emigrants Regulations for existing visa
categories and to revise relevant fee in the visa Issuing system of the Department of Immigration and Emigration” , the
revised Regulations pertaining to the Immigrants and Emigrants Act, No. 20 of 1948 (Chapter 351) attached to the aforesaid
Cabinet Memorandum are submitted herewith and I do hereby order to publish the amended regulations  in the Government
Gazette and subsequently to table  the same in the Parliament for approval.
W
AJIRA ABEYWARDANA,
Minister of Internal & Home Affairs and Provincial
Councils and Local Government.
Ministry of Internal & Home Affairs and
Provincial Councils & Local Government,
“Suhurupaya”,
Sri Subhuthipura Road,
Battaramulla.
20th May, 2019.

Regulations
The Immigrants and Emigrants Regulations 1956, published in the Supplement to the Ceylon Government Gazette
No. 10,896 of February 24, 1956 are hereby amended as follows :-
1. in regulation 8 -
(1) in paragraph (1) by the substitution for the words ‘‘ furnish such Number of copies of the photograph or
of the finger- prints or of both,” of the words ‘‘ furnish such Number of copies of the photograph or of
the bio metrics or of both,’’;
(2) in paragraph (2), by the substitution for the words, “or of the finger-prints  furnished” of the words, “ or
of the bio metrics furnished”.
2.   by the insertion immediately after regulation 10, of the following new regulation :-
“10A. (1) Any person who contravenes the condition which specifies the period of stay in any visa
granted or issued to that person, by overstaying in Sri Lanka, shall be required to pay a penelty of United
States Dollars 500, in addition to the applicable visa fee for the perdiod of over stay.
(2) Notwithstanding the provisions of paragraph (1), the prescribed Authority may grant a grace period -
(a) of two days at the port of depature to a person referred to in paragraph (1) ; or
(b) seven days when applying for an extension of the period of stay specifed in the visa from the
Department of Immigration and Emigration.
3. by the substitution, for regulation 13 thereof, of the following regulation :-
“13. (1) The fees payable in respect of visas shall be as set out in the table below :-
