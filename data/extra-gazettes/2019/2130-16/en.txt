IMPLEMENTATION  OF   ALL   THE   POWERS   IN   THE   DISTRIBUTION   OF   FILMS   BY   NATIONAL
FILM   CORPORATION   OF  SRI   LANKA, WHICH   HA VE   BEEN   VESTED   IN   NATIONAL   FILM
CORPORATION   OF   SRI   LANKA   BY   NATIONAL   FILM   CORPORATION   OF   SRI   LANKA   ACT,
No. 47  OF  1971  AMENDED BY  ACT,  No.  45  OF 1980
NOTIFICATION published by the Chairman of National Film Corporation of Sri Lanka by virtue of the powers vested in
the Chairman under Section 57 of National Film Corporation of Sri Lanka Act, No. 47 of 1971 amended by Act, No. 45 of
1980
In relation to the case No. DSP 99/2018 filed by private film distribution circuits against National Film Corporation,
the order of suspension, which has been issued by Court suspending the notification made by the Chairman of National
Film Corporation of Sri Lanka to film distribution circuits on 19.06.2018 making the agreement/understanding which has
been made between Corporation and distribution circuits entrusting film distribution to private circuits, null and void, has
been dissolved and further interim injunction requested from the Court against National Film Corporation by private
distribution circuits has also been rejected.
By virtue of the powers vested in me as the Chairman in charge of the subject under Section 57 of National Film
Corporation of Sri Lanka Act, No. 47 of 1971 amended by Act, No. 45 of 1980, I do hereby notify that the powers
implemented so far by private film distribution circuits in the distribution of local/foreign films are cancelled from 18
June 2019 based on the above litigation in order to formalize the distribution of local/foreign flim within Sri Lanka, and
further it is hereby declared that all tasks pertaining to distribution of flims including the scheduling of films for Cinema
Halls, releasing films for screening and collection of income on distribution will be performed, from that date, totally by
National Film Corporation of Sri Lanka.
A
NUSHA GOKULA FERNANDO,
Chairperson,
The National Film Corporation of Sri Lanka.18th June, 2019.

Methodology for distribution and screening of films
National Film Corporation of Sri Lanka
1. Introduction
By virtue of the powers vested in the Chairman under Section 57 of National Film Corporation of Sri Lanka Act No 47 of
1971 amended by Act No 45 of 1980, powers which have been entertained so far by private distribution circuits, have been
cancelled from 18 June 2019 and from that date National Film Corporation of Sri Lanka performs all the functions
including scheduling films for cinema halls, releasing films for screening and collecting income from the distribution of
films totally as per the powers of the aforesaid acts.
2. Methodology
Distribution of films within Sri Lanka shall be performed by National Film Corporation of Sri Lanka (Hereinafter referred
to as “ Corporation”).
By virtue of the powers vested in the National Film Corporation of Sri Lanka under National Film Corporation of
Sri Lanka Act No 47 of 1971 amended by Act No 45 of 1980, following methodologies should be in the distribution and
screening films.
2.1 By virtue of the powers vested in the Corporation under the provisions of the Act these regulations are
hereby stipulated and all relevant parties should be bound to adhere to all these regulations.
2.2 Method for registration of films
2.2.1 Registration of local films
Every local film, which is expected to be screened at cinema halls, should be registered properly at
the Corporation.
The document containing the method for registration of local films is attached herewith. (Annex 01)
2.2.2 Importation of films
Registered Importers should obtain the written approval of the Chairman of the Corporation
(Hereinafter referred to as “Chairman”) fulfilling all conditions as per the importation policy issued
by the Corporation to be followed before importation of films.
2.3 Local films registered in the proper manner or films imported will be scheduled by the Corporation for the
purpose of screening.
2.4 Following requirements should be satisfied for the purpose of categorisation and scheduling films.

2.4.1 Local films
2.4.1.1 Local films, which are expected to be screened, should be submitted for categorising to a local
films evaluation board (Hereinafter referred to as “ Evaluation Board”) appointed properly by
the Board of Directors of the National Film Corporation.
The document containing the composition of the Evaluation Board is attached herewith.
(Annex 02)
2.4.1.2 The aforesaid Evaluation Board will categorise films in the following manner after watching
the films submitted to the board.
* Films with classical - attractive characteristics
* Films with commercial-attractive characteristics
* Films with the combination of  classical - attractive characteristics and commercial-attractive
characteristics
The document containing the criteria prepared by the aforesaid Evaluation Board and applicable
to the categorisation of films is attached herewith. ( Annex 03)
Films which are not included in the above categorization will not be scheduled for screening at
Cinema Halls. A Board of Appeal for the evaluation of local films (Hereinafter referred to as
“Board of Appeal”) has been appointed giving opportunity to producers for making an appeal.
The document containing the composition of the aforesaid Board of Appeal is attached herewith
(Annex 04)
2.4.1.3 Local films, which are categorised by the aforesaid Evaluation Board, should be submitted to
the Public Performance Board by the producer of film.
2.4.1.4 Producer should hand over the films, to which the certificate of Public Performance Board is
issued, to the Corporation for scheduling and distribution.
2.4.1.5 The films handed over by the producer (Hereinafter referred to as “Film Reserve”) will be
included in the Films Reserve to be screened as per the order of dates in the certificate issued
by Public Performance Board.
2.4.1.6 For the purpose of obtaining recommendations for releasing films to Cinema Halls a Scheduling
Committee for local films will be appointed (Hereinafter referred to as “Scheduling Committee”).
The document containing the composition of the Scheduling Committee is attached herewith
(Annex 05)
2.4.2 Imported films
2.4.2.1 Films, which are imported in terms of the importation policy of National Film Corporation,
should be handed over to the National Film Corporation by the respective importer.
2.4.2.2 The films, which are handed over in the above manner, will be submitted by Corporation for the
approval of the Public Performance Board.
2.4.2.3 The films, to which the certificate of the Public Performance Board is issued, will be distributed
by the Corporation.

10. The films, which are scheduled for screening through films reserve, will be maintained under each such channel
and action should be taken to schedule at least 05 films under one channel. (Accordingly a waiting list of scheduled
15 films should be maintained under three channels at every time.)  This section will be implemented subject to
the provisions in Para 04.
11. If it is required to release, at a specific occasion, a film with limited number of copies to a certain channel for a
short period, such film can be released in a manner not to cause hindrance to the main release of the respective
channel on the written approval of the Chairman based on the recommendations of scheduling committee and
evaluation board.
12. The schedule made for screening under channel system will not be revised except on reasonable grounds and
written approval of the Chairman of the Corporation should be obtained, if such revision is to be made. (The list
containing such reasonable grounds is attached herewith- Annex - 09 )
13. However, in case where the appointed screening opportunity is not obtained in respect of a particular film without
above mentioned reasonable grounds, the film should be included in the last place of the waiting list mentioned
under 10. Further the chance given to not obtain such screening opportunity should be limited to two occasions.
14. A new film will be scheduled in each channel from the film reserve within two weeks from the release of one film
out of the films scheduled under each channel. This order will be applied continually.
15. Action will be taken to screen the trailer of films scheduled under each channel at least with two films screened
previously and no payment should be made to a cinema hall or any other party for screening trailers.
All publicity campaigns related to a film can be carried out on the discretion of the producer of the film. However
if it is expected to entrust pre- publicity campaign to the Corporation, action should be taken in the following
manner. In order to send following items to Cinema Halls, they should be handed over to Corporation in the
following manner.

17. Producer/ importer of the film should enter in to a formal agreement for distribution with Corporation before the

18. Each cinema hall should, along with the duly perfected daily reports, remit the share to be remitted to the Corporation
from the daily collection received from the screening during the following day and action will be taken to suspend
the release of next film to cinema halls, which do not follow this process.
19. The share of the producer out of the remittance made by cinema halls to the Corporation relevant to each film will
be paid within 45 days from screening.
20. When the films released under channels are removed from screening the holdover figure of the released film will
further be applied.
21. Charges and distribution structure for cinema halls implemented under Strategic Development Projects will be
determined according to the Memorandums of Understanding signed with the Corporation.
22. Even though this methodology is implemented with a view to maintain film industry without interruption, this
methodology will be revised from time to time considering the recommendations and observations made in
scientific and research aspects by the Investigation and Research Division of the Corporation on practical issues
arisen in Distribution, Marketing, Production, Research and Investigation divisions.
23. However the Corporation always  considers the suggestions and views submitted logically, scientifically and with
common agreement by all parties relevant to the field including  producers, importers, directors, cinema hall
owners, managers, artists and any other individual or organization , who wishes progress and enhancement of the
cinema industry.
24. The Chairman of the Corporation retains all powers for revision, cancellation and determination of matters including
circulars/guidelines issued for the   purpose of implementation of the film screening and distribution methodology.
25. Any decision taken by the Chairman of the Corporation regarding any matter in relation to the methodology issued
to ensure proper distribution of films should be the final.
26. In case of any inconsistency observed among Sinhala, Tamil and English texts, Sinhala text should prevail.
In this methodology, unless the context otherwise requires,
“Act” means the National Film Corporation of Sri Lanka Act No 47 of 1971 amended by Act No 45 of 1980.

Annex - 01
NFC/102/01/01
Registration of Local Films
1. Every film should be registered at National Film Corporation of Sri Lanka before commencing its production. Fee
for registration is Rs.5,000/=.
2. Relevant application and instructions can be obtained from the Production Division of National Film Corporation
of Sri Lanka. Further it can be downloaded from web site- www.nfc.gov.lk
3. Following documents, which are duly perfected, should be submitted for registration of the film.
3.1 Application for registration (Annex 01 -I)
3.2 Certified photocopy of the registration certificate of sole proprietorship/partnership/company
3.3 Photocopy of the National Identity card of the producer
3.4 In case partnership business, a letter by which a partner has been nominated and authorized by other partners
to deal with National Film Corporation of Sri Lanka
3.5 Copies of agreements entered in to with main actor/actress, Director, Cinematographer, Editor join with the
production.
3.6 Synopsis of the film
3.7 An affidavit to the effect that the synopsis submitted is a new creation
4. Production works should not be commenced without registering the film at Film Corporation and films, which are
produced without registering, will not be distributed by the Corporation.
5. Relevant certificate should be obtained from Public Performance Board for screening.
6.  Action will be taken to schedule the film in terms of the methodologies approved by National Film Corporation of
Sri Lanka.
7. A copy of the film approved by Public Performance Board for screening should be handed over to the film
conservation unit of the National Film Corporation for conserving at the repository of the Department of National
Archives.
8. It is strictly prohibited to show the film in Television or any other media and to produce DVD copies until the lapse
of one year from the commencement of screening.
9. National Film Corporation of Sri Lanka should be informed regarding any change made in the contact numbers
(Fixed line and mobiles) and permanent address of the producer/production company registered at the Corporation
for the production of film.
10. National Film Corporation should be informed without delay regarding any change made in the ownership of the
film (Producer/ Production Company) and further certified copies of relevant documents / letters should also be
submitted.
11. Rs. 3,000/= should be paid to National Film Corporation in the revision of the title of the film.
12. At such occasions, where foreign artists and technicians are engaged in the production of a local film, following
payments should be made to the film development fund.
For main actor/actress, mai n technician - Rs. 250,000.00
For Supporting actor/actress, Assistant T echnician - Rs. 100,000.00
13. It is required to be bound by the decisions of the Film Categorizing Committee.
14. National Film Corporation is not bound necessarily to screen every film registered at the Corporation.
Chairman,
National Film Corporation of Sri Lanka.

BOARD   OF   APPEAL   ON   EV ALUATION   OF   LOCAL   FILMS
Member of the Evaluation Board of Local Films watch films and then select films under the categories such as films with
classical - attractive characteristics ,films with commercial-attractive characteristics and films with the combination of

entertain these appeals.

* One member from Evaluation Board of Local Films, who has not participated to watch relevant film,
* Two members from Scheduling Committee ( Out of the members appointed to the relevant committee, a
representative from Cinema Hall owners and one member from three independent members appointed by the
Chairman of the Corporation on the approval of the Board of Directors should be included.)

SPECIFIC  FILMS
Films, which contain one or several of the following features, are treated as specific films.
1. The films recommended by Evaluation Board of Local Films and Scheduling Committee of Local Films as
having capacity for attracting larger audience.
2. Films, for which requests are made by majority of Cinema Halls for scheduling.
3. Films with timely themes.
4. Films, which won awards recognized at national or international level
\
    * “Specific” film - This should be a film certified by Public Performance Board for unlimited release.
