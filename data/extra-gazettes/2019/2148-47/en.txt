Regulations
(1)  These Regulations may be cited as the National Thoroughfares (User Fee National Highway) Regulations  
(2)   These regulations shall apply to –
(a) the “Southern User Fee National Highway” declared by order made under Section 11 of the National 
Thoroughfares act, No. 40 of 2008 published in the Gazette Extraordinary No. 1731/2 of November, 8, 
2011;
(b) the “Galle - matara User Fee National Highway” declared by order made under Section 11 of the National 
Thoroughfares act, No. 40 of 2008 published in the Gazette Extraordinary No. 1850/33 of February, 19, 
2014;

c) the “Godagama – Kasagala section of Southern expressway User Fee National Highway” declared by 
order made under section 11 of the National Thoroughfares act, No. 40 of 2008 published in the Gazette 
Extraordinary No. (Gazette No. to be inserted once Godagama – Kasagala section of Southern 
Expressway has been declared as a National Highway)
d) the “Colombo – Katunayake User Fee National Highway” declared by order made under section 11 of 
the National Thoroughfares act, No. 40 of 2008 published in the Gazette Extraordinary No.1831/31 of 
october, 12, 2013; and
e) the “outer Circular User Fee National Highway”  declared by order made under Section 11 of the National 
Thoroughfares act, No. 40 of 2008 published in the Gazette Extraordinary No. (Gazette No. to be inserted 
once OCH has been declared as a National Highway)
  
(3) (1) There shall be levied from a driver of every vehicle being used on the aforesaid User Fee National Highways, 
a fee in respect of the use of the User Fee National Highway by such driver, at such rates specified in Part A of the Schedule 
to these regulations.        
(2) The fee shall be charged in respect of the whole distance, or part thereof, of the User Fee National Highway.
(3)  Different fees shall be applied for different categories of vehicles specified in Part A of the schedule to these 
regulations.
(4) (1) a driver of every vehicle who enters a User Fee National Highway from an interchange and desirous of making 
payments   manually by cash or electronically through a device (hereinafter referred as “e – Tag”) attached to the vehicle 
shall, prior to being permitted to proceed along such highway, obtain a ticket from the Teller on duty, at such interchange or 
electronically.
(2) The driver of a vehicle which has been proceeding along the User Fee National Highway, when arriving at 
the gate of an interchange for the purpose of exiting such User Fee National Highway shall surrender to the Teller the ticket 
obtained at the interchange through which he entered.
(3)  The Teller having identified the category of vehicle shall inform the driver of the amount of the fee payable 
and accordingly the driver shall pay such amount, prior to being permitted to pass through such interchange, and the teller 
shall issue a receipt in respect of such payment.
(5) (1) a driver of every vehicle shall keep the ticket referred to in regulation 4 (1), securely in his possession until he 
reaches the toll gate of the interchange through which he intends to exit such User Fee National Highway.
(2) Where the driver is unable to surrender the ticket to the Teller, and the Teller fails to identify the entry location, 
the driver shall be deemed to have travelled the entire distance of the User Fee National Highway from the furthest possible 
point of entry to the actual point of exit and shall accordingly be charged a fee at the rates specified in Part A of the schedule 
to these regulations.
(6) (1) The Road development authority, established under the Road development authority act, No. 73 of 1981, 
(hereinafter referred to as “the authority”) shall ensure that, for the purpose of implementation of the provisions of these 
regulations, sufficient number of gates by taking in to account the average Traffic volume at exit and entry points are positioned 
at the required places along the User Fee National Highways.

(2) The gates so positioned along the User Fee National Highway shall be owned, operated and maintained by the 
authority, and each gate shall be manned by a Teller. 
(3) The Teller shall be responsible for the collection of the user fee at every gate referred to in  paragraph (2), in such 
sum as is specified in Part A of the Schedule to these regulations from every driver using such User Fee National Highways :
Provided that the provisions of these regulations shall not apply to a driver who is driving a vehicle of a 
category specified in Part B of the Schedule to these regulations, which is exempt from such payment. 
(7) The Teller shall at such times as are specified, hand over the day’s collection of the fees to the officer authorized 
in that behalf by the authority and obtain a receipt in respect of such amount.
(8)  The money so collected as user fee shall be credited to the Fund of the authority and be used for the purpose of 
developing and maintaining the User Fee National Highways to which these regulations apply.
(9) For the purpose of these regulations-
“Designated location” means an authorized access point, where a driver of a vehicle is permitted   to enter the 
User Fee National Highway without obtaining a ticket and includes New Kelani Interchange, Peliyagoda 
Interchange, Katunayake Interchange, Katunayake (25.8km) and Kerawalapitiya Interchange.
“e- Tag” means an electronic device that is permanently installed on the windscreen or head lamp of the 
vehicle for the purpose of payment of user fee electronically when passing through an electronic User 
Fee Lane.
“Teller” means an officer who is manning the gates at an interchange;
“User Fee National Highways” includes the Southern User Fee National Highway from Kottawa (0.000 km) 
to Pinnaduwa (95.275 km), Galle – matara User Fee National Highway from Pinnaduwa (95.275km) 
to Godagama (124.806 km), Godagama – Kasagala Section of Southern expressway User Fee National 
Highway from Godagama (124.806 km) to Kasagala (163.456 km),  Colombo – Katunayake User Fee 
National Highway from Colombo (0.000km) to Katunayake (25.800km) and outer Circular User fee 
National Highway from Kottawa (0.000 km) to Kerawalapitiya (28.867km) 
(10)  The driver of a vehicle who contravenes the provisions of these regulations shall commit an offence and shall 
on conviction after summary trial by a magistrate be liable in the case of-      
   
(a) a first offence; to a fine not exceeding five thousand rupees; and 
(b) a second or subsequent offence; to a fine not exceeding fifty thousand rupees.
  
11. The National Thoroughfares (User Fee) Regulations No. 2 of 2015 published in the Gazette extraordinary                 
No. 1924/8 of July, 20, 2015, Gazette extraordinary No. 1924/52 of July, 25, 2015 and the National Thoroughfares (User 
Fee) Regulations No. 1 of 2015 published in the Gazette extraordinary No. 1912/2 of april, 27, 2015 are hereby rescinded 
without prejudice to anything done thereunder.
12. The Regulations relevant to electronic Toll Collection System (eTC) is included in the Annex 1.

Annex 1
(1)     (1)  an owner or driver of a vehicle who is desirous of making payments electronically (hereinafter referred as “e-tag 
user”) shall register with the Road development authority, established under the Road development authority 
act, No. 73 of 1981, (hereinafter referred to as “the authority”) and shall be required to open an electronic Toll 
Collection System User Fee account (hereinafter referred to as the “eTCS account”) at the Customer Service 
Centre of the authority.
 (2)  every application for registration as an e- tag user shall be made to the Customer Service Centre of the authority.
 (3)  Upon receipt of an application for registration the Authority may, on being satisfied with the information provided 
in the application including the information relating to the eTCS account, register such person as an e-tag user 
and issue him an e-tag for the purposes of these regulations.
 (4)  every e-tag user shall enter into an electronic Toll Collection System User Fee customer agreement (hereinafter   
referred to as the “ETCS customer agreement”) subject to the terms and conditions specified in such agreement.
 (5)  Every E-tag issued shall be fitted on to the windscreen or head lamp of the vehicle.
 (6)  It shall be the duty of every E-tag user to maintain a sufficient credit balance in the ETCS Account at all times 
when using an electronic user fee collection lane   (hereinafter referred to as the “eTC lane”) enabling such 
person to make the payment of such user fee.
 (7)  In   case of insufficient credit, the E- tag user may top-up the ETCS account and the processing time of such 
top-up shall be not less than one hour before the e-Tag user may be able to use eTC lane.
 (8)  every e-tag user shall ensure that the e- tag is in proper working order and shall inform the authority of any 
damage or loss to such e- tag.
(2)  (1)  where an E-tag user with sufficient credit to pay the user fee, enters the User Fee National Highway from an 
interchange other than from a designated location, such e-tag user shall be permitted to proceed along the eTC 
lane of such interchange.
 (2)  Where a vehicle fitted with an E- tag is being used on an ETC lane, the user fee shall be debited against the ETCS 
account automatically and the e-tag user may obtain a statement in relation to the usage of such account from 
the customer care Centre of the authority.
 (3)  Where an E-tag user, does not have sufficient credit to pay the user fee, such E-tag user, shall divert his vehicle 
from the eTC lane to the manual Toll fee collection lane (hereinafter referred to as the “mTC lane”). The provisions 
of regulation 3 shall apply in relation to the payment of such fees.
(3) (1)   Where a driver of a vehicle which is  not fitted with an E - tag enters the User Fee National Highway from an 
interchange other than from a designated location prior to being permitted to proceed along mTC lane, shall, 
obtain a ticket from the Teller on duty, manning the paying station at the gates of such interchange.
 (2)  The driver of a vehicle referred to in paragraph (1) of this regulation, when arriving at a paying station of an 
interchange or a designated location for the purpose of exiting such User Fee National Highway shall -
