PRINTED AT THE DEPARTMENT OF GOVERNMENT PRINTING , SRI LANKA.
PROVINCIAL  COUNCIL  OF  THE  UV A  PROVINCE  OF  THE  DEMOCRATIC  SOCIALIST
REPUBLIC  OF  SRI  LANKA
Co-operative Societies (Amendment) Statue No. 1 of 2019 of the Provincial Council of Uva Province
This is a statute impose to amend the Co-operative Societies Statute No. 03 of 2011 of the Provincial
Council of Uva Province
Imposed as such by the Provincial Council of Uva Province in accordance with the powers vested to the
provincial councils by the 13th Amendment to the constitution of the Democratic Socialist Republic of
Sri Lanka.
01. This statute is identified as the Co-operative Societies (Amendment) statute No. 1/2019 of Uva
Provincial Council and except as otherwise stated, it shall be deemed to have been effective for all purposes
from the date it was approved by the Governor of Uva Province or from the date on which it was decided.
02. (Hereinafter referred to as the Main Statute). It is hereby amended by removing the subsection
(5) of Section 5 and Paragraph (a) and (b) of subsection (5) of the Co-operative Societies Statute No. 3 of
2011 of the Provincial Council of Uva Province and incorporating the paragraphs shown below.
(a) All the powers or some power out of them of the registrar, under any regulation made under
this statute or considered to be made, should be able to transferred to any Deputy Registrar,
Assistant Registrar, Deputy Commissioner, Assistant Commissioner by the Registrar through
a document.
(b) The authorized Deputy Registrar, Assistant Registrar, Deputy Commissioner, Assistant
Commissioner should able to exercise any power vested on the above paragraph (a).
03. It is hereby amended by removing the Subseciton (4) of the Section (69) of the main statute and
incorporating the new subsection shown below:
When a certificate is issued to a magistrate under the Paragraph ( c) of the subsection (4)(1), the
magistrate should summon the defaulter to give reasons why further actions shouldn’t be taken against him
to recover the money even though he could not be punished by imprisonment, if he did not give adequate
reasons that amount should be considered as the fine imposed in the penal order by the magistrate regarding
the defaulter for a offense only punishable with a fine. Then the provisions of section 291 of the code of
criminal procedure act No. 15 of 1979 (other than the paragraphs ( a) and (d) of that section) should be
applicable, when impose the penal order, the magistrate can made any command, that can be made by him
according to the provisions of that section. Any offender who has been penalized for the period of
imprisonment in default of the fine imposed under section 291 of the code of criminal procedure act No. 15
of 1979, must not be released for paying any amount mentioned in the specified certificate referred to
paragraph (c) of sub section stated above.
04. If there is any inconsistency between the Sinhala and English text of this statute, the Sinhala text
has to be enforced.
