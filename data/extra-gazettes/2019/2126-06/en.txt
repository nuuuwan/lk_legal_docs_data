Regulations
1. These regulations may be cited as the Issuance of Certificates of Competence for Skippers (Local Fishing
Board) Regulations, 2019.
2. These regulations shall be applicable to persons who apply to serve as skippers of multiday local fishing boats
having a length of not less than ten decimal three meters (10.3m).
3. No person shall serve as a skipper in a multiday local fishing boat having aforementioned specifications
without obtaining a Certificate of Competence issued by the Director-General of the Department of Fisheries and Aquatic
Resources (hereinafter referred to as the “Director-General”) or a coxswain license issued by a recognized maritime
training institute for the purpose.

4. Any person applying for a Cetificate of Comptence referred to in regulation 3 shall-
(a) not be less than eighteen (18) years of age as at the date on which the relevant written or oral test as the
case may be, is to be held ;
(b) have, not less than two (2) years experience on a multiday local fishing boat referred to above, at the sea,
either as a skipper or as a crew member ; and
(c) have completed a fishing vessel skipper training programme of not less than one (1) week duration, in any
recognized institute approved by the Director - General.
5. The application for a Certificate of Competence shall be accompanied by the following documents :-
(a) a fitness certificate issued by a recognized medical practioner or a recognized medical institute including
the hearing and visual state of the applicant ;
(b) a certificate of competence issued by any one of the following institutions, namely -
(i) Ocean University of Sri Lanka, established under the Ocean University of Sri Lanka Act, No. 31 of
2014 ;
(ii) Colombo International Nautical and Engineering College recognized as a degree awarding institute
under the Universities Act, No. 16 of 1978 ;
(iii) Mahapola Academy established under the Sri Lanka Ports Authority Act, No. 51 of 1979 ; or
(iv) any other institute recognized for the purpose by the Director-General.
certifying that the applicant has undergone training successfully as a skipper in a fishing vessel for a
period of not less than one (1) week ; and
(c) a document or documents to prove that the applicant has undergone sufficient training including methods of
fishing, sailing, radio communication, personal safety and life saving, fire prevention, fire extinguishing,
emergency first aid at the sea, international obligations of high seas fishing and prevention of marine pollution.
6. Every application for a Certificate of Competence under these regulations shall be sent, addressed to the
Director-General, and shall be in the form specifed in Schedule I hereto.
7. Every eligible applicant shall be informed of the venue and the date of the oral or written test as the case may
be, in writing by the Director-General at least one week before such date.
8. The Director-General may, from time to time made amendments to the syllabus applicable to the oral or
written test as the case may be, to be conducted under these regulations in order to maintain high standards of such test.
9. The Director-General shall, upon being satisfied that the candidate has achieved the required standards at the
oral or written test as the case may be, issue a certificate of competence to the successful candidate within a period of two
weeks from such test.
10. The Director-General shall issue a skipper license in the form specified in Schedule II hereto, to any applicant
who obtained the certificate of competence referred to in regulation 9 upon payment of the license fee referred to in
Schedule III hereto.

11. Any candidate who is unable to achieve the required standards at the oral or written test may be granted a
further period of time by the Director-General to undergo training at any of the institutions referred to in regulation 5(b)
and such candidate shall be given only two opportunities to sit for such oral or written test.
12. In these regulations -
“Skipper” means the person having command of a fishing vessel and provides instructions on the route, estimate
of the catch and management of the boat workers and the crew and includes a Master or Captain who
serves in the same capacity  in a fishing vessel.

Terms & Conditions
* The skipper of the vessel shall comply with the following rules & regulations under the Fisheries and
Aquatic Resources Act, No. 02 of 1996.
* Registration number and Radio Call Sign should be marked on the vessel.
* Fishing gear in the vessel should be marked.
* The valid boat registration certificate and the operation licence or certified copies and log book
should be onboard.
* The gill nets in the vessel shall not exceed 2.5 km in length.
 The Skipper should
* Maintian the log book properly and submit to the authorized officer at the harbour after every fishing
trip.
* Not fish in the EEZ’s of other coastal states.
* Not catch, keep onboard, land and carry out transhipment of Thresher Shark species, Ocean whitetip
shark, Whale shark and Marine Mammals.
* Assist the local or foreign authorized officers for inspection at sea or port.
(The skipper licence is issued only in respect of training courses approved by the Fisheries Department)
