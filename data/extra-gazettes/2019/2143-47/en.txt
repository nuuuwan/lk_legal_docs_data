ORDERS ISSUED   BY   THE ELECTION   COMMISSION   IN   ADDITION   TO   THOSE   PREVIOUSLY
ISSUED BY   THE   ELECTION   COMMISSION   BY   GAZETTE   EXTRAORDINARY   No. 2141/52   DATED
19TH   SEPTEMBER   2019
WHEN the Election Commission decides that it is necessary to inquire further into the information or complaints received
by it regarding the violation of an order or orders issued by the Election Commission to prevent the misuse of government
property in connection with the election and orders to submit relevant print or electronic documents, discussion notes,
reports including those relating to the recruitment or appointment, to the Election Commission or of an officer authorized
by the Election Commission, public officers in charge of such documents should take action to provide all relevant
information. In such instances when the Election Commission or an officer authorized by the Election Commission
orders to submit any oral or written statement for further inquiries into an incident where any order or orders issued by
the Election Commission have been violated, relevant public officers should be responsible for providing all-inclusive
statements. They are bound to do so.
When a public official is summoned by the Election Commission to inquire into election malpractices related to
violation of provisions regarding the misuse of government property in relation to elections, the public officer who is
summoned should act in such a manner as to come, explain all necessary matters and submit the necessary documents.
That officer is bound to do so.
In all the places where the government is referred to in these orders, it refers to all government institutions
including ministries, departments, provincial councils, local authorities, state corporations, statutory boards, commissions,
state banks, state universities, state-owned companies and institutions morethan 50% of whose shares, are owned by
government or state-owned corporations.
