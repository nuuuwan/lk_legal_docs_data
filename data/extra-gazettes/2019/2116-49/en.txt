RULES made by the Construction Industry Development Authority under section 57 of the Construction Industry
Development Act, No.33 of 2014 read with section 44 of the aforesaid Act.
Chairman,
(for and on behalf of the )
Construction Industry Development Authority.
Colombo,
18th  February, 2019.
Rules
1. These rules may be cited as the Construction Industry Development (Registration of Heavy Construction
Machinery and Equipment) Rules of 2019.
2. An owner of a heavy construction machinery and equipment specified in the First Schedule hereto, shall not
use such heavy construction machinery and equipment unless such heavy construction machinery and equipment are
registered with the Construction Industry Development Authority (hereinafter referred to as the “Authority”).
3. (1) Every application for a Certificate of Registration under rule 2 shall be made to the Director-General of
the Authority in a form as may be determined by the Authority accompanied by a fee specified in the Second Schedule
hereto.

(2) Every application under sub-rule (1) of rule 3 shall be accompanied with the following documents :-
(a) the Certificate of Registration issued by the Commissioner General of Motor Traffic and the
copy of the valid Revenue License for the road running machinery ;
(b) an affidavit from the owner specifying the ownership of the machinery or equipment and any other
documents to prove the ownership ; and
(c) status report of the machinery and equipment relating to the serviceability issued within a period
of six months from the date of application by a Mechanical Engineer who is graduated from a
university recognized by the University Grants Commission of Sri Lanka in the field of Mechanical
Engineering.
(3) An application under this rule shall contain the details of the heavy construction machinery and equipment.
The Director-General may, where necessary require an applicant to furnish further information.
4. Upon the receipt of an application under rule 3, the Director-General shall, on the consideration of the matters
contained in the application and documents, either to register such machinery or equipment and issue a Certificate of
Registration or for the reasons to be recorded by him refuse to register such machinery or equipment. The Director-
General shall in writing inform the applicant of his decision and in a case of a refusal to register such machinery or
equipment, he shall state his reasons therefor.
5. Every Certificate of Registration shall be in a form as may be determined by the Authority and such Certificate
of Registration shall, unless revoked earlier, be valid for a period specified therein and shall be subject to such terms and
conditions specified therein.
6. The Director-General may revoke a Certificate of Registration issued under rule 4, if he is satisfied that the
owner of machinery or equipment to whom the Certificate of Registration has been issued has violated any provisions of
the Construction Industry Development Act, No. 33 of 2014 or regulations or rules made thereunder or any of the terms
and conditions of the Certificate of Registration.
7. (1) A Certificate of Registration issued under  rule 4 shall be renewable on application being made in that
behalf to the Director-General not less than thirty days before the expiry of the Certificate of Registration and be
accompanied by a fee specified in the Second Schedule hereto.
(2) A Certificate of Registration shall be renewed by the Director-General, only if he is satisfied that  the
owner of machinery or equipment to whom the Certificate of Registration has been issued has complied with the provisions
of the Construction Industry Development Act, No. 33 of 2014 or regulations or rules made thereunder or any of the
terms and conditions of the Certificate of Registration.
8. Every person who is aggrieved by the decision of the Director-General may make an appeal to the Appeals
Board established under the provisions of section 53 of the Construction Industry Development Act, No. 33 of 2014.
9. The Director-General shall cause -
(a) to maintain a register in which every heavy construction machinery and equipment registered with the
Authority under these rules shall be entered in a format set out in the  Third Schedule hereto ; and
(b) to update regularly and be accessible to the general public.
