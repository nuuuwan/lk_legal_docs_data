Head of State, Head of the Executive and of the Government and Commander-in-Chief of the Services.

Air Force of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka and others.

Service Medal.

petals in shape, with a diameter of 3.7 cm bearing on the observe in the centre a design comprising

REGULATIONS made by the President of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka, to grant, forfeit, restore and

Meritorious Service Medal
1. The Medal shall be awarded to the Minister of Defence, the State Minister of Defence, the Secretary of Defence
of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka for the valuable contribution made since 19.05.2009 and all  Ranks of
the Regular and V olunteer Force of the Army, Navy and Air Force who have been in active service on Nineteen day of May
Two Thousand Nine or thereafter, and who have completed an aggregate period of Six Years excluding the basic training
period with a good conduct and the permanent civil staff members with Six years of aggregate service in Armed Services.
2. Those who have retired, cashiered, discharged or resigned before the completion of six years from the Nineteenth
day of May Two Thousand Nine are not eligible for the Medal.
3. The award may be conferred posthumously to the lawful next-of-kin of the recipient or to a person nominated
by the next-of-kin.
4. A Board of not less than three Senior Officers nominated by the respective Service Commanders will examine
the recommendations made for the award of the Meritorious Service Medal. The recommendation of the Board along with
the recommendation of the respective Service Commanders will be transmitted to His Excellency the President through
the Secretary, Defence for  His Excellency's approval. The recommendations of the Minister of Defence, the State Minister
of Defence and the Secretary of Defence of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka should be sent directly by the
Secretary of Defence for His Excellency's approval.
5. Every recommendation shall include the Number, Rank and Name in full, of the person recommended.
6. The names of persons upon whom the Medal is conferred upon, shall be published in the respective Service
Orders and register of all such recipients shall be maintained under the direction of the respective Service Commander.
7. When a person who is a recipient of the Medal suffers death by sentence of a Court Martial, or any other Court,
he shall forfeit the Medal conferred to him unless His Excellency the President shall direct otherwise. A register of the
names of the Minister of Defence, the State Minister of Defence, and the Secretary of Defence of the Democratic Socialist
Republic of Sri Lanka shall be maintained under the Ministry of Defence.
8. Any Officer of the Sri Lanka Army, Navy or Air Force who is cashiered, dismissed or has been called upon to
resign or retire his Commission, shall forfeit the Medal conferred on him unless His Excellency the President shall direct
otherwise.
9. Every soldier, seaman or airman who has been discharged from the Army, Navy and Air Force with disgrace or
with ignominy, shall forfeit the Medal unless His Excellency the President shall direct otherwise.

10. When a person who is a recipient of the Medal is convicted of an offence of the following nature, that is to say
treason, sedition, mutiny, cowardice, desertion or disgraceful conduct of an unnatural kind or is dismissed or  discharged or
who is called upon to resign his Commission by His Excellency the President for misconduct or who whilst subject to
Military, Naval and Air Force Law, is convicted by the civil power, shall forfeit the Medal conferred to him unless His
Excellency the President shall direct otherwise.
11. A Medal forfeited by any person may be restored by His Excellency the President at his discretion.
12. A notice of forfeiture or restoration shall in every case be published in the respective Service Orders.
13. When a Medal has been lost, and it is desirous to replace it, such a loss should be reported to the Police. A
declaration testified by a Justice of the Peace stating the circumstances under which the loss occurred should be submitted
with a complaint made to the Police. This declaration shall be forwarded to the Commanding Officer who will transmit it to
the respective Service Commander through the usual channel of communication. The Medal lost therewith will be replaced
on payment if the explanation as to the loss is considered satisfactory.
14. In those Regulations -
(a) “Service” means - The Sri Lanka Army,
The Sri Lanka Navy and
The Sri Lanka Air Force
(b) “Service Commanders” means - The Commander of the Army,
The Commander of the Navy and
The Commander of the Air Force.
(c) “Military Personnel” means - Army, Navy and Air Force personnel
(d) “Member of the Civil Staff” means - Civilians Working in the Army, Navy and Air Force
(e) “Army” means - The Sri Lanka Army
(f) “Navy” means - The Sri Lanka Navy
(g) “Air Force” means - The Sri Lanka Air Force
(h) “Soldier” means - Male/Female Soldier
(i) “Seaman” - Also includes a Woman Sailor
(j) “Airman” - Also includes an Air Woman
