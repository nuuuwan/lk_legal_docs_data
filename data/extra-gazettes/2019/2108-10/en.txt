TRUSTS   ORDINANCE  (CHAPTER 87)
REGULATIONS made by the Minister of Justice and Prison Reforms,  under Section 115A read with Section 6A and
section 19A of the Trusts Ordinance (Chapter 87).
THALA THA ATHUKORALA ,
Minister of Justice and Prison Reforms,
Colombo,
January 24, 2019.
Regulations
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Trusts (Information Relating to Express Trusts) Regulations 2019.
2. (1) Upon creation of an express trust under Section 6 of the Trusts Ordinance in relation to any immovable or
movable property, the trustee of such trust shall, for the purpose of subsection (1) of Section 19A of that Ordinance, keep
records of information specified in Schedule I.
(2) The information kept under Sub-regulation (1) shall be verified and updated every three months to the greatest
extent possible.
3. The Registrar General shall, for the purpose of subsection (1) of Section 6A of the Trusts Ordinance, prepare
and maintain a register containing the information specified in Schedule II in respect of every express trust.
4. (1) The trustee of any immovable property or movable property in respect of an express trust shall, for the
purpose of subsection (2) of Section 6A, forward the information specified in Scheduel III –

(2) The trustee of any immovable or movable property in respect of an express trust that has been created prior to
the date of coming into operation of these Regulations shall comply with the provisions of sub-regulation (1) within six
months from the date of coming into operation of these Regulations.
(3) Where there is any update in the information in respect of any express trust forwarded to the Registrar
General and the relevant Registrar of Lands under sub-regulation (1) or (2), the trustee of such express trust shall
immediately forward such updated information as specified in Schedule III, along with the prior registration details of the
instrument of trust, to the Registrar General and the relevant Registrar of Lands.
5. (1) The Registrar General shall require every Registrar of Lands to maintain a registrar in the form specified
in Schedule IV (Hereinafter referred to as the “Day Book”) to enter the information on express trusts received
under Regulation 4.
(2) The original of every instrument of trust submitted to any Registrar of Lands under Regulation 4 shall,
upon registration, be returned to the trustee of the relevant express trust.
6. For the purpose of these regulations -
“valid document of identity” means -
(a) in the case of a person who is citizen of Sri Lanka-
(i) a National Identity Card issued by the Commissioner General for the Registration of Persons ;
(ii) a valid passport ;
(iii) a valid driving licence ; or
(iv)  in the absence of any of the documents referred to in (i), (ii) or (iii) above, a photograph certified by
a  Notary Public or a Justice of the Peace ; and
(b)  in the case of a person who is not a citizen of Sri Lanka, a valid passport and a valid Visa.
7. The Trusts (Information Relating to Express Trusts) Regulations 2018 published in the Gazette Extraordinary
No. 2099/20 of November 28, 2018 is hereby rescinded.
