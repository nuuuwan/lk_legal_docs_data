Revocation of Order under Section 4(1)
WHEREAS by Order made under Section 4(1) of the Industrial Disputes Act, Chapter 131 of the Legislative Enactments of
Ceylon (Revised Edition 1956), as amended by Acts, Nos. 14 and 62 of 1957, 14 of 1962 and 39 of 1968 read with Industrial
Disputes (Special Provisions) Act, No. 37 of 1968 dated 04.01.2018 and published in the Gazette of the Democratic Socialist
Republic of Sri Lanka Extraordinary No. 2053/25 dated 10.01.2018 of the Industrial Dispute in respect of the matter specified in

AND

was referred to Mr. P . Nawaratne for settlement by arbitration.
AND whereas it is now deemed expedient that the said Order be revoked, I, Ravindra Samaraweera, Minister of Labour and

Order dated 04.01.2018.

Order under Section 4(1)

AND
Distilleries Company of Sri Lanka PLC, No. 110, Norris Canal Road, Colombo 10.
NOW THEREFORE I, Ravindra Samaraweera, Minister of Labour and Trade Union Relations do by virtue of the powers

Edition), as amended by Acts, Nos. 14 of 1957, 4 of 1962 and 39 of 1968 (read with Industrial Disputes - Special Provisions) Act,

Mr. K. Nawaratnerajah, No. 27/16, Tangalle Road, Samagipura, Hambantota.
AND
Distilleries Company of Sri Lanka PLC, No. 110, Norris Canal Road, Colombo 10.
STATEMENT OF MATTER IN DISPUTE
The matter in dispute between the aforesaid parties is–
Whether Mr. K. Nawaratne Rajah who worked as the Security Guard of Distilleries Company of Sri Lanka PLC – whole sale
outlet, Ambalantota had been caused injustice by the non-payment of overtime from 2004 to 2010 and the non-payment from
2004 to 2010, the allowance promised for the tranport of rice powder purchased from different places by the lorries of the
Company according to the order of the Head of the Institution to the Ambewela Animal Farm, and if such injustice had been
caused, to what relief he is entitled.
A. W
IMALAWEERA,
Commissioner General of Labour.
Dated at the Office of the Commissioner of Labour,
Colombo this 23rd day of January, 2019.
