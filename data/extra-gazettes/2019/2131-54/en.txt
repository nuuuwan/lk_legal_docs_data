EXTRAORDINARY 
The Code of Regulations on Co-operative Elections (Amendment) formulated on procedures in electing 
Committee Members of Regions at Multipurpose Co-operative Societies in North-Western Province and matters 
connected or consequential thereto where authority has been vested in the Commissioner of Co-operative 
Development and the Registrar of Co-operative Societies to formulate regulations for the purpose of electing 
Regional Committees in terms of Registered By-laws. 
 
BY virtue of powers vested in me through the by -law 23 of the registered by -laws of the Multipurpose Co -operative 
Societies in North -Western Province or any other specific by -law or/and the by -law No. 24 of the by -law of the 
Udubaddawa Multipurpose Co -operative Society Ltd and by section 50.2 of the Code of Regulations for electing 
Committee Members of Regions at Multipurpose Co -operative Societies in North -Western Province enacted by the 
Extraordinary Gazette Notification No.1981/2 and dated 2016.08.22 of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka I, 
Dissanayake Mudiyanselage Kulamina Chandana Dissanayake, the Commissioner of Co -operative Development and 
Registrar of North -Western Province, do hereby amend and stipulate the following regulations of the Code of 
Regulations enacted by me under mentioned for the purpose of electing Regional Committee Members of the 
Multipurpose  Co-operative Societies in North-Western Province. 
 
                                                                                                        D.M.K.C. DISSANAYAKE, 
  Commissioner of Co-operative Development & 
 Registrar Co-operative Societies (North-Western) 
 
 
Kurunegala, 
On 11th July, 2019.

  4.3 section 15.4 of the Code of Electoral Regulations is hereby amended by the addition therefor 
of the following paragraph. 
  
The nomination paper should be prepared in 02 copies. Such nomination paper should 
conform to the form “a” of the Schedule four of this Code of Electoral Regulations and 
should have been duly completed and placed signature. 
  
  5.1 section 16.1of the Code of Electoral Regulations is hereby amended by the addition therefor 
of the following paragraph. 
  
Accepting nominations for a Region should be commenced at 9.00 a.m. and concluded at 
10.00 a.m. on the due Polling Center on the date specified for accepting nominations and the 
nomination paper prepared in conformity with electoral regulations 15.1, 15.2, 15.3 and 15.4 
should be submitted to the Polling Officer in charge of the Center / Assistant Polling Officer 
by one of the three persons viz candidate or proposer or seconder referred to at that 
nomination paper only during the period specified above and the identity of those handing 
over the nomination should be confirmed by producing one of the following valid identity 
cards. Nevertheless, a person who is producing an identity card in such an unclear condition 
that its photograph cannot be identified and/or the contents printed thereon are illegible has 
no right to handover the nomination paper. 
  
    (a) National Identity Card 
  
    (b) Valid Driving License 
  
    (c) Valid Passport 
  
    (d) Pensioners’ Identity Card issued by the Director of Pensions 
  
    (e) Elders’ Identity Card issued by the Divisional Secretary 
  
    (f) A valid Identity Card issued to the monks / priests by Ministry of Religious Affairs or a 
State Department in charge of Religious Affairs. 
  
  5.2 section 16.2of the Code of Electoral Regulations is hereby amended by the addition therefor 
of the following paragraph. 
  
After examining a nomination paper handed over, it should be rejected only during the 
period from 10.00 a.m. to 10.15 a.m. only at the occasions under mentioned. 
  
  
  5.3 By the addition of the following new section as 16.2 (III) after 16.2 (II). 
  
    (III) At an instance where one of three persons of candidate or proposer or seconder has not  
handed over the nomination paper.

5.4 section 16.3of the Code of Electoral Regulations is hereby amended by the addition therefor of 
the following paragraph. 
  
The Polling Officer in charge of the Center / Assistant Polling Officer should make arrangement 
to stick and publicly exhibit a copy (01) of a nomination paper not rejected by the Polling 
Officer in charge of the Center / Assistant Polling Officer, at a suitable location of the Polling 
Center as per the electoral regulation 16.2 and the remaining copy of the nomination paper 
should be kept securely in his custody.Work on such sticking of copies of unrejected nomination 
paper at a suitable place in the Polling Center for exhibition should be completed between 10.15 
a.m. to10.30 a.m. 
  
  5.5 section 16.4 of the Code of Electoral Regulations is hereby amended by the addition therefor of 
the following paragraph. 
  
The nomination papers to be exhibited as per electoral regulations 16.3 should be exhibited 
during the period from 10.30 a.m. to 11.30 a.m. The Polling Officer in charge of the Center / 
Assistant Polling Officer should take action to remove the nomination papers so displayed after 
11.30 a.m. 
  
  5.6 section 16.5 of the Code of Electoral Regulations is hereby amended by the addition therefor of 
the following paragraph. 
  
Nevertheless, no candidate reserves the right to submit another nomination paper when his 
nomination paper has been accepted by the Polling Officer in charge of the Center / Assistant 
Polling Officer. At such circumstance, the Polling Officer in charge of the Center / Assistant 
Polling Officer should take action to reject all other nomination papers submitted by such 
candidate except his first nomination paper already accepted.
