 (for and on behalf of the).

RULES
 1. These Rules may be cited as the Construction Industry Development (Directory of Importers, Manufacturers and 
Suppliers) Rules 2023.
 2. Every importer, manufacturer or supplier of construction materials and components in Sri Lanka shall make 
an application along with particulars as may be required  by the Construction Industry Development Authority 
(hereinafter referred to as the “Authority”) to register such importer, manufacturer or supplier, as the case may be, 
with the Authority for the purpose of maintaining a directory of importers, manufacturers and suppliers.

 6. Every importer, manufacturer or supplier, as the case may be, registered with the Authority under these rules shall 
be graded by the Authority on the performance of such importer, manufacturer or supplier as specified in the First 
schedule hereto.
First Schedule
Criteria for Registration  of Importers, Manufacturers and Suppliers of Construction 
Materials and Components
 1. A propective Importer, Manufacturer and Supplier (IMS) of construction materials and components shall - 
 (a) be eligible to register with the Authority, be registered either with the Registrar of Companies under the 
Companies Act, No. 7 of 2007 or with the Divisional Secretariats under Business Names Ordinance or 
Provincial Business Names Statutes;
 (b) not be included in any list of blacklisted or suspended operations within the last three years by the Government 
of Sri Lanka;
 (c) not be legally insolvent or declared bankrupt at time of application.
 2. (1) IMS who engage in the following basic activity areas shall be registered under one or more of the three categories 
given below:-
 (a) Importer (IM) - Importers of Construction materials and components. 
 (b) Manufacturer (M) - Manufacturers of construction materials and components in their own establishments.
 (c) Supplier (S) - Suppliers of construction materials and components.
  (2) Any IMS who wishes to engage in more than one activity area shall pay applicable registration fee for each such 
category and obtain registration with the authority, separately.
 3. The procedure for registration shall be as follows:-
 (a) It is necessary to submit the duly perfected application with documents pertaining to business registration 
and relevant documents to support the key areas of evaluation as specified in sub paragraph 4.2 of this 
Schedule along with the processing fees, to the Information Centre of the Authority.
 (b) If all the documents are in order, the evaluated application will be sent to the Registration Panel (within two 
weeks from the date of receipt of application), for scrutinizing and authorizing.

 (c) The evaluation process for grading will be carried out in accordance with the scheme given in paragraph 4.
 (d) After the processing of the application and establishing the grade, the applicant will be assigned with a 
registration number, and a certificate of registration will be issued after paying the registration fee as specified 
in Schedule 2.
 (e) Such registration shall be valid for the period as specified in the certificate, and renewed per year basis, 
subject to an annual review of quality/test reports, audited statements and payment of annual registration fee.
 (f) The registration and grading shall be renewed one month prior to the expiry of the validity period after 
carrying out performance assessment by a surveillance team of Authority.
 (g) IMS applying for registration in multiple imports, manufactures and supply categories as listed in the booklets 
available with the Authority shall submit separate applications along with relevant documents. However, if 
a respective import, manufacture or supply given in an application includes different brands, all such brands 
should be stated in the application and the registration would be in respect of such brands only.
 (h) In the event the registration is lapsed, re-registration could be obtained by submitting a new application. 
Registration will not be revalidated with retrospective effect.
 4. Grading
 
 4.1 Grading Criteria
IMS will be graded in three basic levels for specified imports, manufactures and supplies. Performance appraisals of the IMS 
by the IMS Registration System (IMSRS) are taken into account for such grading. The level of performance is measured on 
the following “Performance Indicators” :- 
 (a) Quality;
 (b) Business Capability;
 (c) Financial Capacity;
Marks for Grading will be allocated for the performance indicators as given  in Table (c) to Table (e) specified under the 
heading “Total Points and Key areas of Evaluation” according to Weightings given in Table (b). Total minimum marks will 
be as per the Point Table given in Table (a) of the same heading.
