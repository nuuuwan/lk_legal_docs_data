   20.2.2 Promotion to Grade ii from Grade iii
 (a) Section 20.2.2 of the Gazette Extraordinary No. 2164/13 dated 25.02.2020 is applicable.
	 (b)	 Following	provisions	are	applicable	to	the	officers	who	had	satisfied	the	qualifications	required	to	
be promoted to Grade ii on or before 05.07.2021 in terms of the provisions of the  previous Service 
minute but have not been promoted to Grade ii before the said date.
	 The	active	and	satistactory	period	of	service	up	to	05.07.2021	from	the	date	on	which	the	officer	
completed	the	qualifications	required	to	be	promoted	to	Grade	II	in	terms	of	the	provisions	of	the	
previous Service minute is considered as an active and satisfactory period of service in Grade ii 
when promoting to Grade i.
   20.2.4 Promotion to Supra Grade from Grade i
	 (a)	 Officers	who	have	been	promoted	to	Grade	I	before	05.07.2021,	the	effective	date	of	09th	amendment	
to the Service minute are promoted to Supra Grade with effect from the date of completion of an 
active	and	satisfactory	service	period	of	07	years	in	Grade	I	considering	the	qualifications	given	
under	10.3.1	except	the	qualification	(i)	given	there	under.
	 (b)	 Officers	who	have	completed	07	years	in	Grade	II	before	05.07.2021,	the	effective	date	of	09th	
amendment to the Service minute are promoted to Supra Grade with effect from the date on 
which he/ she completes an active and satisfactory cumulative service of 22 years in the Nursing 
Service including the active and satisfactory service period of 07 years in Grade i considering the 
qualifications	given	under	10.3.1	except	the	qualifications	(i)	given	there	under.
	 (c)	 The	active	and	satisfactory	Period	of	service	of	more	than	07	years	in	Grade	I	of	the	officers	who	
were promoted to the Supra Grade class on or before 05.07.2021, the effective date of the 09th 
amendment to the Service minute, shall be treated as the period of service in the the Supra class 
subject to a maximum period of 03 years. however, there will be no change in the date of promotion 
to the Supra class in terms of these provisions.
