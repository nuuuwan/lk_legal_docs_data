Part II
Sub sections 7.2 a 2 and 7.2 a 5 are amended as follows.
7.2 a 2 Educational and Professional Qualifications and Experience for Recruitment.
All medical graduates with valid full registration of Sri Lanka Medical Council for practicing as a Medical Officer who 
have obtained a medical degree awarded by a faculty of medicine of a State University or any Local medical college or a 
foreign university approved and recognized by the Sri Lanka medical council and successfully completed one year period 
of compulsory internship at a hospital recognized by the Sri Lanka medical council, are eligible for recruitment.
Note 1:
Sri Lankan citizens who have obtained foreign medical degrees after the date 01.11.1988 should have passed the special 
examination for Sri Lankan citizens in terms of the sections 29.1 (b) (II) (cc) and 29.2 (b) (III) of the Medical Ordinance 
and successfully completed the compulsory internship for a period of one year at a hospital recognized by the Sri Lanka 
medical council.
7.2 a 5 method of recruitment, calling applications and selection.
Applications will be called from eligible candidates with the above qualifications by a departmental circular and a public 
notice published in the Government Gazette and on the website of the ministry of Health. accordingly, the priority for 
appointments will be given as follows,
 1. medical Graduates of State Universities.
 2. medical Graduates of any other Local medical college approved by the Sri Lanka medical council.
 3. Medical Graduates of foreign universities who have qualified with the special examination under the Sections 29.1 
(b) (ii) (cc) and 29.2 (b) (iii) of the medical Ordinance.
Selections are made in the order of merit of the graduates of state universities, order of merit of graduates of General Sir John 
Kotelawala defence University, graduates of other local medical colleges provided by the University Grants commission 
and in case of graduates of foreign universities, in the order of merit provided by the Sri Lanka medical council. Selected 
graduates will be recruited to the Preliminary Grade as Medical Officers with the approval of the Public Service Commission.
Above amendments shall come into force with effect from 31.07.2019.
