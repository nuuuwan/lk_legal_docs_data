Regulations
1. These regulations may be cited as Recreational Fishing Management Regulations 2023.
2. These regulations shall apply in respect of any person engaging himself in recreational fishing activities.
3. A person shall not engage in any recreational fishing activitiy expect under the authority of license issued in that 
behalf under these regulations.

4. A person shall not alter, tamper or use frudulently any recreational fishing license issued under these regulations.
5. All license and certificates issued under these regulations shall be produced before any authorized officer when 
required to do so.
6. Any endangered, threatened and protected fish species caught under any recreational fishing activity shall be 
released alive.
7. Any person engaging himself in any recreational fishing activity shall – 
(1) Release live fish as soon as possible
(2) Use a hook of appropriate size and pattern for fishing 
(3) Avoid removing fish from the water to unhook
(4) Use a wet rag or wet hands to handle fish
(5) Treat fish gently to reduce stress and injury
8. a person shall not -  
(1) Purchase, sell or export the catch of any recreational fishing activity;
(2) Transship the fish catch of any recreational fishing activity;
(3) Use stainless hooks for recreational fishing;
(4) Catch any fish species prohibited under the fisheries and Aquatic Resources Act, No. 2 of 1996 and the 
Faura and Flora Protection ordirance (Chapter 469);
(5) Engage in any fishing operation prohibited under the Fisheries and Aquatic Resources Act, No. 2 of 1996 
or any other fishing operation not referred to in schedule A of these regulations as a prescribed fishing 
operation for recreational fishing;
(6) Engage in any recreational fishing activity during the closed season declared by the Department of 
Fisheries and Aquatic Resources or in any area declared as spawning or breeding areas of fish or as 
a Marine Protected area by the department of Fisheries and aquatic Resources or the department of 
Wildlife Conservation respectively;
(7) Leave fishing gear unattended except when using techniques that are designated for fishing activities 
carried out passively;
(8) Engage himself in recreational fishing unless a de-hooker and line cutter are kept on board when required 
to release fish alive;
(9) dump any waste materials, pollutants such as fuel, oil or any chemical into marine, brackish water, fresh 
water and shore based ecosystems.
9. every application for a license under these regulations shall be substantially in ‘’Form  a” referred to in Schedule 
E of these regulations and shall be sent to the Director – General of the Department of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources 
accompanied by the relevant license fee specified under Schedule B of these regulations.
10. Upon receipt of the license fee specified in Schedule B hereto, the Director-General shall issue license substantially 
in “Form B” referred to in Schedule “e” of these regulations and shall be in force for a period of one year from the date of 
issue of the license unless cancelled earlier.

PaRT I
RegistRation of RecReational fishing vessels
12. A person shall not operate any vessel for recreational fishing without having on board a valid certificate of 
registration issued under these regulations.
13. Any person engaging himself in recreational fishing Shall register every vessel with the Department of Fisheries 
and aquatic Resources prior to obtaining a license under these regulations.
14. every such application for registration shall be substantially in “Form C” of Schedule e of these regulations 
and shall be submitted to the director General of the department of Fisheries and aquatic Resources accompanied by the 
relevant registration fee specified in Schedule C of these regulations together with the following documents :-
(1) a valid skipper license issued by the director General of the department of Fisheries and aquatic Resources 
with proof of a training course followed by the Skipper as specified in Schedule D of these regulations.
(2) A certificate of sea worthiness obtained from the Department of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources or any 
other international registered organization having authority to issue such certificates relating to the vessel.
(3) Proof that the vessel is equipped with all essential life saving and safety equipment for both crew and 
passengers.
(4) a comprehensive insurance cover for the vessel including its crew members and passengers of such vessel, 
obtained from any insurance company approved by the Insurance Regulatory Commission of Sri Lanka.
15. Upon reeceipt of the Registration fee Specified in Schedule C hereto, the Director General shall issue certificate 
of registration substantially in “ Form D” of schedule E these regulation, to any recreational fishing vesseld under these 
regualations.
16. The period of validity of such certificate of registration shall be one year from the date of issue of such certificate.
17. Any application for the renewal of any such registration shall be submitted one month prior to the date of expiry 
of such certificate of registration.
PaRT II
Data RepoRting of RecReational fishing
18. Every owner of any licensed vessel shall submit details on catch species on a quarterly basis in the Forms specified 
in Schedule e hereto, to the director General of the department of Fisheries and aquatic Resources.
19. every owner of any licensed vessel shall provide details separately on release of all catch species and on landing 
of all catch species as per the template provided in Schedule e of these regulations for data reporting.
20. In these regulations:
“Authorized Officer” means any officer authorized by the Director General of the Department of Fisheries 
and Aquatic Resources under Section 46(1) of the Act and includes any officer of the 
Army, Air Force or Police not below the rank of Sergeant and any officer of the Navy 
not below the rank of Petty Officer;
