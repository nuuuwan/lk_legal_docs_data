Regulations
1. These regulations may be cited as departure and arrival of Mechanized  Fishing Boats Regulations, No. 1 of 2023.
2. These regulations shall apply to any mechanized fishing boat registered under the Registration of Fishing Boats 
Regulations, 1980, published in Gazette Extraordinary No. 109 of October 03, 1980 engaged in fishing operations in               
Sri Lanka Waters or in the High Seas.

3. unless under the authority of a valid permit issed by the director-General for departure and arrival of a mechanized 
fishing boat specified in these regulations a person or an organization shall not operate any mechanized fishing boat for fishing

4. (1)  Every owner of a mechanized fishing boat who seeks to obtain a permit for departure and arrival of  such 
boat in respect of fishing in Sri Lanka Waters or in the High Seas shall submit an application to the Director-General or any 
other officer authorized by him, substantially in the form set out in Schedule I hereto.
 (2) The director-General may, subject to the provisions of the Fisheries and aquatic Resources act, No. 2 of 
1996 and the regulations made thereunder and upon being satisfied of the fulfillment by the applicant of the requirements 
specified in these regulations, issue to the applicant a departure permit substantially in the form set out in Schedule II hereto 
and an arrival permit substantially in the form set out in Schedule III hereto.
 (3) The permits issued under sub-regulation (2) of this regulation shall be obtained for each departure and arrival 
of the relevant mechanized fishing boat.
 (4) Submission of the application under sub-regulation (1) of this regulation and the issue of the permits under 
sub-regulation (2) of this regulation can be made by electronic means through the official website of the Department of 
Fisheries and aquatic Resources.
 (5) The director-General may suspend, cancel or revoke the issued under these regulations if it is proved to the 
satisfaction of the Director-General that the requirements specified in these regulations have not been complied with. 
 (6) The permits issued under these regulations shall not be transferable and every permit holder shall notify the 
director-General and obtain his approval in writing for the following:-
 (a) change of the permit holder;
 (b) change of skipper or any crew member of the relevant boat;
 (c) change of any information provided by the permit holder; or
 (d) change of any facility, equipment or procedure in relation to the boat, which has been approved by the 
director-General.
 (7) every permit holder shall abide by the terms and conditions set out in Schedule IV hereto pertaining to the 
crew members and use of identification devices.
 (8) The requirements specified in regulation 4 or any privilege granted to the permit holder under such permit 
may be amended, from time to time, if the director-General considers it expedient in the interest of tne safety, regularity or 
efficiency of service provided by the permit holder or in the interest of national security.
5. For the purpose of these regelations –
“director-General” means the director-General of Fisheries and aquatic Resources appointed under section 
2 of the Fisheries and aquatic Resources act, No. 2 of 1996;
“electronic” shall have the same meaning assigned to it by the electronic Transactions act, No. 19 of 2006;
“High Seas” shall have the same meaning assigned to it by the Fisheries and aquatic Resources act, No. 2 
of 1996;
“official website” of the Department of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources means the website “www.fisheriesdept.
gov.lk”; and
“Sri Lanke Waters” shall have the same meaning assighed to it by the Fisheries and aquatic Resources act, 
No. 2 of 1996.

SCHeduLe IV
[Regulation 4(7)]
TERmS AND CONDITIONS fOR CREw mEmBERS
 1. Every permit holder shall ensure that all crew members specified in item 8 of the departure permit set out in 
Schedule II hereto have a valid life insurance coverage issued by an insurance company recommended by the 
Department of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources before each departure of the relevant mechanized fishing boat.
 2. Every such crew member shall be on board at the time of departure and arrival of the relevant mechanized fishing 
boat.
 3. Crew members shall not be exchanged from or transferred to a local or foreign fishing boat after the departure 
of the relevant mechanized fishing boat unless in a situation where any crew member needs a medical or other 
assistance or in case of any similar emergency and in such a situation the crew member can be transferred to 
another fishing boat by prior notice to the Department of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources via Single Side Band 
(SSB) radio communication.
TERmS AND CONDITIONS ON ThE uSE Of IDENTIfICATION DEVICES
 1. (1) Every permit holder shall ensure that the relevant mechanized fishing boat is equipped with an Automatic 
Identification System (AIS) configured with Maritime Mobile Service Identity (MMSI) number for the buoys 
to mark the fishing gear and such devices are configured in compliance with the proviaions of the Act and any 
regulation made thereunder and in accordance with the directions given by the department of Fisheries and 
aquatic Resources and the Telecommunication and Regulatory Commission established under the Sri Lanka 
Telecommunications act, No. 25 of 1991.
  (2) unless the aforesaid procedure is adhered to the permit holder shall not be permitted to depart the relevant 
mechanized fishing boat for fishing either in Sri Lanka Waters or in the High Seas.
