NATIONAL POLICE COmmISSION
The delegation of Powers by The National Police Commission in terms of article 155J of the 21st amendment to the 
Constitution of the democratic socialist republic of sri lanka.
   1 .  By virtue of the Powers vested in the National Police Commission under and in terms of article 155G of the 
Constitution of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka, from among the Officers employed in the Sri Lanka 
Police Service, in respect of the Officers specified in column II of the schedule hereto, the Powers specifically mentioned 
in Column III of the said schedule are hereby delegated to the Officers specified in Column I of the schedule hereto by 
the National Police Commission.
   Provided that, in the event of any question arising in relation to any delegation of Powers or as to the exercise of 
Power so delegated, the decision of the National Police Commission shall be final and conclusive.
   Provided further that, if any Police Officer specified in Column I, is a complainant or a witness or a potential witness 
at any disciplinary inquiry against a Police Officer specified in Column II, the Powers of appointment, promotion, 
transfer, disciplinary control and dismissal, as are specified in Column III of the schedule hereto, shall be exercised as 
if no delegation in respect thereof had been made to the relevant Officer specified in Column I.
2. The National Police Commission hereby specify that in the exercise of the Powers hereby delegated by the 
National Police Commission to the Inspector General of Police and to any other Police Officers, to whom such Powers are
