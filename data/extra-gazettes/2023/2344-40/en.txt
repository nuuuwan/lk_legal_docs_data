 01. these regulations may be cited as the “Levy of fees for the Licenses for Public Performances, No. 01 of 2023”

 (a-i)  the license shall be issued within 5 working days upon the submission of the duly completed application to

 (i) t o obtain the license within 24 hours from the submission of an application, an extra  payment of 50

 (ii) t o obtain the license on the same day of the application, an extra payment of 50 percent of the prescribed 
fee. (such applications should be submitted before 9.30 a.m. on any of the working days.) ;
 (iii) Where the screening facilities are not available in the Board theatre, the applicant shall bear the 
responsibility to have arranged the screening facilities on behalf of the Board within the time period in

 (iv) the applicant may change the date of screening by informing the Board 48 hours in advance.

             (b)         Stage Dramas

 (B-i)  the scripts of stage dramas should be submitted to the Board 21 days prior to the date of the public exhibition.

 (C-i)     applications for musical and performing shows should be submitted to the Board 10 days prior to the date of 
the public exhibition. an extra payment of 25 percent of the prescribed fee shall be charged on the applications 
submitted thereafter.
 03. fees for any performing shows which has a social, educational, cultural or religious importance may be waived off 
from the payment of fees by the Board at its discretion. any request for exemption of fees thereon should be made 
in writing 14 days prior to the date of the public exhibition.
 04. if a new license is required to be obtained in lieu of any misplaced license, a fee of rs. 1,500.00 therefor is payable.
