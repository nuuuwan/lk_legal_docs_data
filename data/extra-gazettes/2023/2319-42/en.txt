2. (1) any liquid food which is in a ready to drink form and which contains sugar shall be labeled in the manner as 
specified in these regulations.
    (2) Where-
 (a) any liquid food has to be prepared or reconstituted without changing its liquid form before consumption 
in accordance with the directions specified in the label of the container of such food ; or 
 (b) any solid or semi solid food has to be prepared or reconstituted and brought to liquid form before 
consumption in accordance with the directions specified in the label of the container of such food;
and such liquid or solid or semi solid food upon being so prepared or reconstituted contains sugar, the container of such food 
shall be labeled in the manner as specified in these regulations.
(3) Where any food refers to in this regulation is a pre-packaged imported product, a supplementary label may be 
affixed on the container or the package of such food and such label shall contain the logos as specified in these regulations.
3. (1) Where any food referred to in these regulations contains any amount of sugar specified in Column I of the 
schedule hereto, the label of such container or the package which contains such food shall have the corresponding logo 
(hereinafter referred to as “the logo”) specified in Column III of the Schedule hereto in the corresponding colour specified 
in Column II of the schedule hereto.
(2) The logo specified in sub-regulation (1) of this regulation shall be prepared,  designed and displayed in 
accordance with the following :-
 (a) the height of the logo shall not be less than 2cm and the width of the logo shall not be less than 1cm ;
 (b) (i) a description of the “sugar” content in such food shall be as specifed in Column III of the schedule 
hereto and shall be displayed within the logo in sinhala, tamil and english languages respectively 
in bold white and shall be of minimum 2 mm font size ;
  (ii) a numeric description of the sugar content in such food shall be within a white box as shown in 
Column III of the schedule hereto and shall be of minimum 1.5 mm font size in bold black.
4. the logo shall be displayed in the main panel of the label of the container or the package which contains such food.
5. Where any food referred to in these regulations contains in glass bottles, which are in circulation at the time of 
coming into operation of these regulations, the logo shall be displayed on the crown or closure of such glass bottle until  
May 1, 2024.
6. the provisions of these regulations shall not apply to :-
 (a) any food referred to in these regulations which contains inherent sugar or added sugar in an amount less 
than 0.5g/100 ml;
 (b) bottled or packaged drinking water or natural mineral water ;
 (c) any standardized milk, semi skimmed milk, low fat milk, skimmed milk, non fat milk or toned milk which 
has been subjected to pasteurization or sterilization or Ultra Heat Treatment (UHT) ;
 (d) spices and spice mixes, food additives, flavouring substances, black tea, green tea, groud coffee, single 
ingredient herbal infusion, Bee honey, treacle and 100% glucose ;
 (e) infant formulae (starter), follow - up formulae ;

7. Food (Colour Coding for sugar levels) Regulations 2016 published in Gazette Extraordinary No. 1965/18 of  
May 3, 2016 are hereby rescinded without prejudice to anything lawfully done thereunder.
8. In these regulations, unless the context otherwise requires - 
“amber” means R255, G195 and B9 in Red, Green, Blue colour system (RGB) or equivalent ;
“green” means G195 in Red, Green, Blue colour system (RGB) or equivalent ;
“main panel” means  the part of the label which is most likely to be displayed, presented, shown or examined 
under customary conditions of display for retail sale ;
“red” means R230 in Red, Green, Blue colour system (RGB) or equivalent ; and
“sugar” means total sugar contained in food, in the form of monosaccharides and disaccharides.
