THE  LICENSING  OF  SHIPPING  AGENTS FREIGHT  FORWARDERS, NON-VESSEL OPERATING  COMMON
CARRIERS AND CONTAINER  OPERATORS   ACT,  No. 10 OF  1972
REGULATIONS  made by the Minister of Highways, Ports and Shipping under Section 10 read with Section 3 of the Licensing
of Shipping Agents Freights Forwarders, Non Vessel Operating Common Carriers and Container Operators Act, No. 10 of 1972.
MAHINDA RAJAPAKSHA,
Minister of Highways, Ports and Shipping.
Colombo,
28th  August, 2014.
Regulations
1. These regulations may be cited as the Freight Forwarders and Non Vessel Operating Common Carriers (Licensing)
Regulations, No. ............. 2014  and shall come into effect from the date hereof.
2. Every company desirous of carrying on or carrying on the business of a Freight Forwarder or a Non V essel
Operating Common Carrier shall make and application for a licence in that behalf, to the Director General of
Merchant Shipping (hereinafter referred to as “ the Director-General”) substantially in the Form set out in the
Schedule hereto.
3. Every application for a licence shall contain all such information as the Director-General may require for the
purpose of processing the application and shall be accompanied by the relevant fee.
4. Every application for a licence shall be accompanied by a written statement stating that the applicant company
shall without delay furnish information relating to any change in the Board of Directors of the company, to the
Director-General.
5. No company shall be eligible for a License to carry on the business of a Freight Forwarder or a Non V essel
Operating Common Carrier unless it is a limited liability, public or private company duly formed and registered or
re-registered under the Companies Act, No. 7 of 2007, in which the majority of Directors are citizens of Sri Lanka
and not less than sixty per centum of the issued shares carrying voting rights are held by citizens of Sri Lanka

6. Notwithstanding the provisions in the regulation 5 above, a company is eligible, for a licence to carry on the
businesses referred to therein if-
(a) Such company has already received the approval of the Board of Investment of Sri Lanka in terms of the
Exchange Control notice published in Gazette Extraordinary No. 1232/14 of April 19, 2002 for a higher
percentage of foreign investment and only up to that percentage of issued share capital is held by non Sri
Lankan citizens.
(b) Such company has been approved by the Board of Investment in terms of Finance Act/Commercial Hub
Regulation No. 1 of 2013 made under part IV of the Finance Act, No. 12 of 2012 is amended by Finance Act,
No. 12 of 2013.
(c) Such company receiving approval in terms of the Exchange Control notice published in Gazette Extraordinary
No. 1232/14 of April 19, 2002 for a higher percentage of foreign investment from the Board of Investment of
Sri Lanka, after the publication of this regulation, has brought in a minimum investment of US$ 10 million
toward the share capital of the company by way of an inward remittance and have a minimum of 20% issued
shares carrying voting rights of the company are held by citizen of Sri Lanka.
7. A citizen of Sri Lanka holding shares in any company referred to Section 5 and 6  shall be deemed not to be holder
of such shares or part hereof it he holds such shares as the nominee or the trustee or the agent of a non-citizen of
Sri Lanka.
Provided further that such company shall, if required to do so by the Director General, furnish proof is that behalf
by way of affidavit from such shareholders.
8. An applicant which is a Freight Forwarder, or Non Vessel Operating Common Carrier which furnished a document
of carriage or a Forwarders Cargo Receipt or provides all other connected services with respect of international
carriage of goods, including total logistical services, shall be issued a “Class A” category licence, subject to the
following conditions :-
(a) that a bank guarantee to the value of Rupees Five Hundred Thousand (Rs. 500,000)  shall be provided in
favour on the Director-General ;
(b) that a staff officer who has a minimum of five years experience in the field of international transportation
services shall be employed on the staff ;
(c) that the applicant is registered as a Freight Forwarder with the Exchange Control Department of the Central
Bank of Sri Lanka ;
(d) that the applicant possess as a forwarders “all risk liability” insurance cover for a minimum of US $ One
Hundred Thousand (US $ 100,000) and minimum cover of US $ Fifty Thousand (US $ 50,000) for errors and
omissions, from an insurance company recognized by the Director-General ;
(e) that there shall be in its permanent staff a minimum of two persons who have completed professional
training and are holding valid certification in cargo handling, by air or sea, including handling of dangerous
cargo from a recognized Institute ;
(f) that the “Standard Trading Conditions” as accepted by Insurance Companies in Sri Lanka for the issuance
of Liability Insurance Covers or the issuarance of International Federation of Freight Forwarders Association
Bills of Lading, specified by the Director-General shall be complied with.
9. An applicant which is engaged in carrying on Freight and Transport related services excluding the issuance of
Documents of Carriage for international freight trasnporation such as Bill of Lading, House Bill of Lading Seaway

10. (1) Every licence issued in terms of these regulations shall, unless earlier cancelled or suspended be valid upto
the  Thirty First day of December of the year in respect of which such license was issued.
(2) Every Freight Forwarder or a Non Vessel Operating Common Carrier shall pay an annual license fee of
Rupees Ten Thousand (Rs. 10,000) .
11. (1) Every application for renewal of a license shall be made substantially in the Form set out in the Schedule
hereto.
(2) Every application for the renewal of a licence for a any year shall be made on or before the thirtieth day of
November of the preceding year.
12. Every license carrying on the business of a Freight Forwarder or a Non Vessel Operating Common Carrier shall
comply with such guidelines as may be issued, from time to time, by the Director-General, in the interest of the
general public.
13. Where any information or particulars, contained in any Declaration or Affidavit submitted as required under these
regulations, is found to be incorrect or false, the Director-General may forthwith take steps to cancel the licence
issued to such company or withdraw or suspend the same or withdraw or suspend the use of any privileges
granted to such company.
14. For the purpose of this regulation company shall means a limited liability, public or private company duly formed
and registered or re-registered under the Companies Act, No. 7 of 2007.
15. The Regulations made under Section 10 read with Section 3 of the Licensing of Shipping Agents Freight Forwarders,
Non-V essel Operating Common Carriers and Container Operations Act, No. 10 of 1972 and published in the
Gazette Extraordinary No. 1847/53 of January 31, 2014 cited as Freight Forwarders, Non V essel Operating Common
Carriers (Licensing) 2014 is hereby rescinded .
