Provincial Environmental Statute No. 12 of 1990
ORDER  UNDER  SECTION  39
BY VIRTUE of the powers vested in me by Section 39 of the Provincial Environmental Statute No. 12 of 1990, I, Dharmasiri
Dassanayake, Provincial Minister of Environment, do by this Order-
1. prohibit -
(i) the manufacture of polythene or any polythene product of twenty (20) microns or below in thickness for
in country use ; or
(ii) the sale, offer for sale, offer free of charge, exhibition or use of polythene or any polythene product
which is twenty (20) microns or below in thickness within the North Western Province :
Provided that polythene or any polythene product of twenty (20) microns or below in thickness may be
permitted to be used with the prior written approval of the Authority for the purposes specified in the
Schedule hereto.
2. Declare that the Order published in Gazette Extraordinary  No. 1567/4 of September 15, 2008 is hereby
rescinded without prejudice to anything done there under.
For the purposes of this Order-
“Authority” means the Provincial Environmental Authority established under the Provincial
Environmental Statute No. 12 of 1990 ;

PERMITTED  USES
1. Use of the following material for the purpose of laminating :-

(e) Polyvinyl chloride (PVC);
(f) Polyethylene Terephthalate Glycol (PETG).

SCHEDULE
Activities :-
(i) the manufacture of food wrappers from polythene as a raw material for in country use; and
(ii) the sale, offer for sale, offer free of charge, exhibition or use of food wrappers manufactured from
polythene as a raw material within the North Western Province.
For the purposes of this Order-
‘‘food wrappers’’ means lunch sheets.
‘‘Polythene” includes high density polyethylene, low density polyethylene and polypropylene.

REGULATIONS
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Provincial Environmental (Prohibition of open burning of refuse and
other combustible matters inclusive of plastics) Regulations No. 1 of 2018.
2. No person shall burn openly or cause to, allow or permit the open burning of refuse or other combustible
matters inclusive of plastics.
3. Any person who fails to comply with the regulations above shall be liable to an offence and punishable under
Section 60 of the Provincial Environmental Statute, No12 of 1990.
