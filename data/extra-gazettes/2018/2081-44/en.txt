THE  MOTOR  TRAFFIC  ACT  (CHAPTER  203)
REGULATIONS made by the Minister of Transport and Civil Aviation under Section 237 of the Motor Traffic Act (Chapter
203) read with Section 128A of the aforesaid Act.
NIMAL SIRIPALA DE SILV A,
Minister of  Transport and Civil Aviation.
Colombo,
24th July, 2018.
Regulations
1. These regulations may be cited as the Motor Traffic (Public Service Motor Tricycle) Regulations.
2. These regulations shall be applicable to every person who obtains a driving license valid for motor tricycle
after these regulations come into operation and uses or adapts to be used or keeps such motor tricycle for the carriage of
passengers for a fee or reward (hereinafter referred to as “person to whom these regulations apply”).
3. No person to whom these regulations apply shall drive a public service motor tricycle, except under special
endorsement on the license endorsed in that behalf by the Commissioner-General of Motor Traffic (hereinafter referred
to as the “Commissioner-General”).
4. Every person who desires to apply for an endoresement under regulation 3 shall -
(a) be not less than the age of thirty-five years and not more than the age of seventy years ; and
(b) have at least a period of two years' driving experience after obtaining the driving license for the motor
tricycle

5. Every application for an endorsement under regulation No. 03 shall be made to the Commissioner-General
substantially in the form set out in the Schedule hereto and shall be accompanied by-
(a) a medical certificate issued by the National Transport Medical Institute or any other institution
authorized by the Commissioner-General ;
(b) a certificate of a training course designed, approved and conducted by the Commissioner-General or
such training course conducted by an institution authorized by the Commissioner-General ;
(c) A police report, confirming that the applicant is not an Island Registered Criminal ; and
(d) a fee of rupees two thousand
6. A person to whom these regulations apply shall be disqualified from being a driver of a public service motor
tricycle if he has been registered as an Island Registered Criminal under the Prevention of Crimes Ordinance (Chapter
22).
7. In this regulaiton-
“motor tricyle” shall have the same meaning as specified in the Motor Traffic Act (Chapter 203) ; and
“public service motor tricycle” means any motor tricycle used or adapted to be used or kept for the carriage of
passengers for a fee or reward.
