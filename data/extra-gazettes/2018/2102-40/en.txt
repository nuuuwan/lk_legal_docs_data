BY virtue of the powers vested in the Institute of Chartered Accountants of Sri Lanka (hereinafter referred to as the

sized Entities with effect from 27
th September 2018, published herewith for the purpose of the Sri Lanka Accounting and
Auditing Standards, Act, No. 15 of 1995. This amendments shall be effective from Twenty Seventh of September Two
Thousand Eighteen.Earlier application is permitted.

Amendments to the Sri Lanka Accounting Standard (SLFRS) for Small and Medium-sized Entities
Paragraph 1.1A is added and paragraph 1.1 is amended. New text is underlined and deleted text is struck through.
Section 1
Small and Medium-sized Entities
Intended scope of this Standard
1.1 The SLFRS for SMEs is intended for use by small and medium-sized entities (SMEs). The section describes
the characteristics of SMEs.
Following companies categorized under the Section 5 of the “Sri Lanka Accounting and Auditing Standards
Act, No.15 of 1995” will not fall under the definition of SME as per this standard.
…
- Companies listed in a stock exchange licensed under the Securities and Exchange Commission Act,
No.36 of 1987, 
other than the companies listed on the Empower Board subject to the provisions
stated in paragraph 1.1A of this standard.
1.1A The Companies listed on the Empower Board under the Securities and Exchange Commission Act, No.36
of 1987, may apply SLFRS for SMEs to prepare financial statements for three consecutive financial periods
after the date of listing on such a board with the following additional disclosures;
o Disclosures on impact to the Statement of Comprehensive Income for the period, if SLFRS 15
Revenue from Contracts with Customers had been applied from the financial year commencing on
or after 01st January 2018. If that determination of impact is impracticable, that fact shall be disclosed.
o Disclosures required as per SLFRS 9 Financial Instruments,  if the company has complex financial
instruments.
o Disclosures required as per SLFRS 13 Fair Value Measur ement, if the company is following fair
value measurements in valuing their assets and liabilities as per SLFRS 13.
