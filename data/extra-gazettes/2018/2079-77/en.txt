EXTRADITION   LAW,  No.  8    OF   1977
Order under Section 3
BY virtue of  the powers vested in me by Section 3 (1) of the Extradition Law, No. 8 of  1977, I, Maithreepala Sirisena,
President do by this Order declare that, since a Treaty was signed at Ankara on the 02nd day of  December 2008 between
the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka and the Republic of Turkey, a foreign State, in order to establish a bilateral
legal mechanism governing the transfer of persons subject to prosecution and sentenced persons and to improve the
efficiency of justice which would develop Co-operation between the State Parties to the Treaty in the prevention of
offences and inflict of punishment in respect of such offences and that the parties to the Treaty undertake to extradite to
each other, upon request and subject to the provisions of the Treaty, the persons who are found within the territories of the
respective State Parties for prosecution or execution of sentences ; and that the provisions of such Treaty is in conformity
with the provisions of Section 4 of the Extradition Law, No. 8 of 1977; the provisions of the said Law No. 8 of 1977 shall
apply in respect of such foreign State, subject to such modification, limitation or condition as specified in the Treaty.
M
AITHREEPALA S IRISENA,
President.
Colombo,
20th June, 2018.
