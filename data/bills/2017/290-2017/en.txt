STATEMENT OF LEGAL EFFECT
Clause 2 :  This clause amends the Fisheries (Regulation of Foreign Fishing Boats) Act
(hereinafter referred to as the “principal enactment”) by making a general amendment and
the legal effect of the section as amended is to transfer the functions of the Secretary in
relation to the subject to the Director General.
Clause 3 :  This clause amends section 3 of the principal enactment and the legal effect
of the section as amended is to include Director General of Coast Guard Department and
any other person designated by name or by office by the Minister in writing as persons
who are authorized to implement and enforce the provisions of the Act.
Clause 4 : This clause amends section 13 of the principal enactment and the legal effect
of the section as amended is–
(1) to enable any authorized officer to–
(i) bring any abandoned foreign fishing boat to the nearest port;
(ii) produce any foreign fishing boats seized or detained as the case may be
under this Act with the crew where such boat is not abandoned, before
the Magistrate instead of High-Court in order to overcome practical
difficulties; and
(2) to transfer the authority of the Director or the Government agent to the Director
General.
Clause 5 :  This clause amends section 13 of the principal enactment by introducing
new section 13
A and the legal effect of the section as amended is to provide for the
submission of a notice to the consular officer of the country to which the abandoned or
seized foreign fishing boat belongs with details specified therein and for recovery of cost
and incidental expenses accrued, to the Government of Sri Lanka in respect of the detention.
Clause 6 : This clause amends section 15 of the principal enactment and the legal effect
of the section as amended is to impose enhanced fines based on the length of the fishing
boat for the offences committed contravening section 4 and section 5 of the Act in the
territorial waters and the Exclusive Economic Zone of Sri Lanka.
Clause 7 :  This clause amends section 16 of the principal enactment and the legal effect
of the section as amended is to enable the Magistrates to exercise power in respect of
offences committed under this Act, and to enhance the fine in respect of violation of
conditions of a permit by a foreign fishing boat.
Clause 8 :  This clause amends section 17 of the principal enactment and the legal effect
of the section as amended is to enable Magistrates to exercise power in respect of enhanced
fines for the offence of obstructing authorized officers, taking into consideration the
length of the fishing boat.
Clause 9 :  This clause inserts new section 17
A to the principal enactment and the legal
effect of the section as amended is to make all offences committed under this Act cognizable
and non-bailable.

<!-- page 3 -->

Clause 10 :  This clause amends section 18 of the principal enactment and is consequential
to the amendment made by clause 8.
Clause 11 :  This clause amends section 19 of the principal enactment and the legal
effect of the section as amended is to enable High Court to hear the original appeals giving
an opportunity to be heard again by Court of Appeal and to change the designation of
Director as the Director General.
Clause 12 :  This clause inserts new sections 19
A, 19B and 19 C to the principal enactment
and the legal effect of the section as amended is to vest the ownership of the abandoned
foreign fishing boat in the State until legal entitlement is proved by a relevant party within
the prescribed period to the Magistrate.
Clause 13 : This clause amends section 20 of the principal enactment and the legal
effect of the section as amended is to provide for the imposition of a penalty by the
Director General for offences committed for the first time in the Exclusive Economic
Zone together with procedure to be adhered to.
Clause 14 :  This clause amends section 20 of the principal enactment by the insertion
of new section 20
A to the principal enactment and the legal effect of the section as
amended is to provide for the appointment of a panel of experts.
Clause 15 :  This clause amends section 21 of the principal enactment and the legal
effect of the section as amended is to provide for the release of any boat on the provision
of a satisfactory bond or security until the conclusion of any prosecution and to enable the
Magistrate to exercise powers in respect of offences committed under this Act.
Clause 16 :  This clause amends section 23 of the principal enactment and the legal
effect of the section as amended is to make provision for the presumption, on violation of
the provisions of the Act where a foreign fishing boat is found in Sri Lanka Waters.
Clause 17 :  This clause amends section 24 of the principal enactment and the legal
effect of the section as amended is to enable the Magistrate to exercise powers in respect of
offences committed under this Act.
Clause 18 :  This clause amends section 26 of the principal enactment and the legal
effect of the section as amended is to widen the regulation making power for the
implementation of standards stipulated in International conventions and agreements.
Clause 19 :  This clause amends section 26 of the principal enactment by the insertion
of new section 26
A and the legal effect of the section as amended is to impose a fine in
respect of violation of regulations.
Clause 20 :  This clause amends section 27 of the principal enactment by the insertion
of new section 27 A and the legal effect of that section as amended is to establish a fund
called and known as Fisheries Reward Fund.
Clause 21 : This clause amends section 28 of the principal enactment and the legal
effect of the section as amended is to include new definitions to the expressions “abandoned
foreign fishing boat” and “Director General” and to substitute definitions for the expressions
“fish”, “fishing”, “local fishing boat”, “related activities”, “Secretary” and “Sri Lanka
Waters”.
