STATEMENT OF LEGAL EFFECT
 Clause 2: This clause amends section 7 of the National Audit Act, No. 19 of 2018 (hereinafter 
referred to as the “principal enactment”) and the legal effect of the section as amended is to make 
necessary provisions to complaint to a law enforcement authority for legal action in respect of any 
fraud, corruption or misappropriation found by the Auditor General in carrying out an audit of an 
auditee entity. 
  
 Clause 3: This clause amends section 9 of the principal enactment and the legal effect of the 
section as amended is to extend the application of paragraph (a) of that section to the Surcharge 
Review Committee appointed under section 21 of the principal enactment. 
  
 Clause 4: This clause amends section 11 of the principal enactment and the legal effect of 
the section as amended is to extend the application of the provisions of that section to the Chief 
Accounting Officer and the Accounting Officer of each audited entity and extend the period within 
which the Annual Detailed Management Audit Report shall be presented, from five months to six 
months.
  
 Clause 5: This clause amends section 18 of the principal enactment and the legal effect of the 
section as amended is to enable the Auditor-General to deduct a sum equal to  fifteen per centum 
of the audit fees charged under that section and to credit such sum to the Audit Fund established 
under section 37.
  
 Clause 6: This clause amends section 19 of the principal enactment and the legal effect of the 
section as amended is-
(a)   to empower the Auditor-General to make recommendation to impose a surcharge 
on the amount of any deficiency or loss in any transaction of an auditee entity to 
the Surcharge Review Committee;
(b)  to empower the Surcharge Review Committee to consider the recommendations 
made by the Auditor-General and to decide the amount to be imposed as a 
surcharge; and
(c)  to specify the procedure to be adopted by the Surcharge Review Committee in 
determining the amount of surcharge and the recovery of the surcharge. 
  
 Clause 7: This clause repeals section 20 of the principal enactment and is consequential to the 
amendment made to the principal enactment by clause 6 and clause 8.
  
 Clause 8: This clause amends section 21 of the principal enactment and the legal effect of 
the section as amended is to incorporate the provisions in the principle enactment to appoint the 
Surcharge Review Committee and to specify the composition thereof. 
  
 Clause 9: This clause inserts new sections 21 A, 21B, 21C, 21D, 21E and 21 F in the principal 
enactment and the legal effect of the new sections is to  provide for the provisions relating to the

such members, vacation of office of such members, meetings and quorum of such Committee,

of the Committee.
  
 Clause 10: This clause amends section 22 of the principal enactment and is consequential to the

 Clause 11: This clause amends section 23 of the principal enactment and is consequential to the

 Clause 12: This clause amends section 38 of the principal enactment and is consequential to the

 Clause 13: This clause amends section 42 of the principal enactment and the legal effect of 
the section as amended is to enhance the punishment to be imposed on a person who commits an 
offence under that section.
  
 Clause 14: This clause amends section 43 of the principal enactment and the legal effect of 
the section as amended is to enhance the punishment to be imposed on a person who commits an 
offence under that section.
  
 Clause 15: This clause amends section 55 of the principal enactment and the legal effect of 
the section as amended is to make provisions to incorporate new definitions for the expressions, 
“Committee on Public Accounts”, “Committee on Public Enterprises” and “Institute of Chartered 
Accountants of Sri Lanka”.
