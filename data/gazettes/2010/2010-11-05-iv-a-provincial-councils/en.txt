INDIVIDUAL and services eligible to submit bids.– Sealed bids are
invited from Sri Lankans who are permanently residing in the Central
Provincial or Sri Lankan firms, Co-operative Societies those which
there registered offices are situated in the Central Province for the
supply of cooked meals for the institutions stipulated in Column I
of the Schedules 1, 2, 3 annexed herewith during the period of 01st
January, 2011 to 31st December, 2011 (Including these two days.)
by Provincial Director of Health Services, Central Province.
02. Bid documents.– Relevant Bid document could be obtained
from the Provincial Director's office on payment of Non-refundable
tender document fee is Rs. 500 and refundable deposit mentioned in
Column 3 of Sub Schedule.
03. The following conditions 3.2 to 3.4 should be fulfilled by the
below mentioned approved societies at the time of requesting their
tender forms.
3.1 Approved societies :
(a) Sammurdhi Balakayas
(b) Multi purpose Co-operative Societies
(c) Rural Development Societies
(d) Registered farmer organizations.
3.2 ( a) The hospital should be situated within the area of
authority of the societies and a letter from the
Divisional Secretaries should be submitted to prove
this.
(b) (i) Rural Development Societies and Sammurdhi
Balakayas should submit a certificate of financial
position and certificate of eligibility of supplying
cooked provision, to hospitals, which was obtained
from the Divisional Secretaries.
(ii) Registered farmer organizations should submit the
certificate of registration obtained from the
Assistant Commissioner of Agrarian Services.
3.3 Multi-purpose Co-operative Societies can only submit
tenders for supply foods to the hospitals situated within
their area. They should submit a letter issue from the
relevant bank, regarding the financial status (The account
name should be the society's name)
3.4 All approved societies should submit their registration
certificate along with the Bid documents.
3.5 Above mentioned societies can get special benefit if the
hospital is situated in the relevant area. If not they will be
considered as a normal applicants.
04. These approved societies will be allowed a preference up to
5% over the lowest Bids.
05. Issuing for Bid forms .– The Bids document for hospitals
stipulated under Schedule 1, 2 and 3 will be issued on 01st December,
2010 until 2.30 p. m. at Provincial Director of Health Services Office,
Kandy.
06. Declaration of assets. – All Bids when applying for Bids
forms must submit acceptable of Assets issued by relevant Divisional
Secretary after 01.09.2010 in the form of General 170. The value of
the certificate of Assets should be as follows :

09. Bid Bond.– A bid bond for all hospitals as mentioned for the
amount in 3rd column of Schedule 1, 2, 3 refundable deposit should
be paid and receipt should be obtained from the Provincial Director's
office. Bid bonds are not necessary to submit by approved societies
mentioned in 3.1 and 3.2(a). Bid Bonds are necessary to submit by
societies mentioned in 3.5.
10.(a) Quantities.– Approximate quantities of supplies required
are given in 6th column of the Schedule 1, 2, 3 and 5th column of the
Schedule 4, 5, 6 annexed herewith. (Price and rate should be submitted
for one full diet for patient. One full diet means breakfast, lunch and
dinner).
(b) Bidders should forward price and rate for all type of diets
fresh fish, dry fish, chicken, eggs, Lanka dhal with vegetable. As an
economy measure the Department may decide to substitute one of
the diets in place of another Viz. In lieu of an egg diet chicken diet or
fresh fish or dry fish diet may be ordered if this is cheaper than that.
However a vegetable diet cannot be ordered in lieu of an egg, chicken,
fresh fish or dry fish diet. Contractor should note well that an egg or
chicken or fish should be supplied for lunch and vegetable with
Lanka dhal for dinner and Bidders should be prepared to supply
accordingly. Meanwhile for breakfast every day in week Red rice
with dhal (uncooked rice 75g. dhal 15g), milk rice with lunumiris
(rice 75g). Munata with lunumiris (unata 90g) Bread 120g with
1 plantain, Kadala with lunumiris (kadala 90g), contractors should
prepared to supply any other nutrient meal without in low rates.
(c) One diet means supplying of breakfast, lunch and dinner for
patients (3 meals per day).
(d) Price and rates should be submitted according to Schedule
No. 04 and 05 for patients Digana Rehabilitation Hospital.
(e) For Tuberculosis patients at General Hospital, Matale should
be provided foods according to Schedule No. 06.
11. Forwarding Bids.– All tenders should be forwarded in two
copies each copy should be enclosed in separate sealed envelopes
and send both the copies in one cover to the Provincial Director of
Health Services, Office of the Provincial Director of Health Services,
P. O. Box 163, Sangaraja Mawatha, Kandy. The cover enclosing the
tender should bare at the top left corner the nature of the services of
diet contract "Tender to supply cooked provisions for the .............
Hospital in .......... District" at the bottom left corner the name and
the address of the tender should be written clearly. The cover
enclosing the sealed Tender may be sent by registered post or
deposited in the Bids box at office of the Provincial Director of
Health Services Kandy. Any responsibility will not be taken regarding
postal delays.
12. Accepting of Bids. – All Bids with the date stamp from the
office of Provincial Director of Health Services would be accepted
on 02nd December, 2010 until 10.30 a. m. Bid applications will be
opened at 11 a. m. on the same date.
13. Bidders can be presented on the time of opening of the tender.
14. Decision of Bidders.– The total cost of the services arrived at
on the rates quoted for item in Schedule "B" diets in the Bids form
will be the basis for a decision on the Bids received. The tender
should however quote for items in Schedule "C" extras in the tender
form. But the prices to be paid shall be rates quoted in the tender or
prevailing market rate or rates submitted by the Divisional Secretary.
The lowest price of those will be selected Bids procedure of supply
of cooked provision will be subjected to amend regulation tax and
various statuses which are issued by the Government from time to
time. Final Bids decision will be taken by the Secretary of the
Ministry of Health Central Province, Provincial Director of Health
Services, Central Province and the Board of Procurement of the
relevant Deputy Directors of Health Services. 5% withholding tax
will be charged by bills which are exceed Rs. 50,000. This tax will be
remitted to the Commissioner of Inland Revenue.
15. L. P . Gas should be used for cooking meal at Nawalapitiya
District General Hospital and Digana Rehabilitation Hospital in
Kandy District, Matale District General Hospital in Matale District,
District Base Hospital Dickoya and Divisional Hospital Maskeliya
in Nuwara Eliya District. Therefore using gas for cooking should be
taken into consideration when submitting rates for a diet.
16. Validity of Bid Rates.– The rates quoted by the Bids should be
valid for the period of contract mentioned in paragraph 1 above and
under no circumstances will the rates be permitted to be varied
during such period.
17. Security Deposits.– The selected tenders will be required to
sign agreement. In addition to that a performance bond (issued by a
reputed bank which is accepted by Central Bank) should be submitted
for 10% of full tender amount. Contractors who did not work under
the relevant agreement in first month will not be pay any payment
and would like to inform that their names will be entered in the black
list.
18. Storage Accommodation.– Successful Bidders will not be
provided the facilities in the nature of store room, or in the institution
or within its premises.
19. Bids Conditions.– Prospective tenders should study the Bids
conditions on the reverse of the Bids forms and comply with
requirements therein. Bidders should also note the conditions of the
agreement in the Bids form.
20. Supply of type and quantity of rice. – The rates quoted by
Bidders should be for the supply of two rice meals of 100 grams for
each patient. The permitted red rice should be supplied for meals.
21. Bid forms will not be issued for the black listed Bidders or
Societies. The decision of the Procurement Board will be the final
decision.

IMPORTANT  NOTICE  REGARDING  PUBLICATION   OF  GAZETTE
THE Weekly issue of the Gazette of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka  is normally published on
Fridays. If a Friday happens to be a Public Holiday the Gazette is published on the working day immediately preceding
the Friday. Thus the last date specified for the receipt of notices for publication in the Gazette also varies depending
on the incidence of public holidays in the week concerned.
The Schedule below shows the date of publication and the latest time by which notices should be received
for publication in the respective weekly Gazette. All notices received out of times specified below will not be
published. Such notices will be returned to the sender by post for necessary amendment and return if publication
is desired in a subsequent issue of the Gazette. It will be in the interest of all concerned if those desirous of ensuring
the timely publication of notices in the Gazette  make it a point to see that sufficient time is allowed for postal
transmission of  notices to the Government Press.
The Government Printer does not accept payments of subscription for the Government Gazette.
Payments should be made direct to the 
Superintendent, Government Publications Bureau, No. 132, Maya Avenue,
Kirulapone, Colombo 05.
Note.—Payments for inserting Notices in the Gazette of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka
will be received by the Government Printer and not by the Superintendent, Government Publications Bureau.
