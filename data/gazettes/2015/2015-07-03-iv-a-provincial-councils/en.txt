ATTENTION is drawn to the Notification appearing in the 1st week of every month, regarding the latest dates and times of acceptance of Notices for
publication in the weekly Gazettes, at the end of every weekly Gazette of Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka.
All Notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes shall close at 12.00 noon of each Friday, two weeks before the date of publication. All
Government Departments, Corporations, Boards, etc. are hereby advised that Notifications fixing closing dates and times of applications in respect
of Post-Vacancies, Examinations, Tender Notices and dates and times of Auction Sales, etc. should be prepared by giving adequate time both from
the date of despatch of notices to Govt. Press and from the date of publication, thereby enabling those interested in the contents of such notices to
actively and positively participate please note that inquiries and complaints made in respect of corrections pertaining to noti fication will not be
entertained after three months from the date of publication.
All notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes should reach Government Press two weeks before the date of publication i.e. notices
for publication in the weekly Gazette of 24th July, 2015 should reach Government Press on or before 12.00 noon on 10th July, 2015.

OPEN COMPETITIVE EXAMINATION FOR THE
RECRUITMENT TO THE POST OF SMALL INDUSTRY
INSTRUCTOR (GRADE III) OF THE NORTH WESTERN
PROVINCIAL DEPARTMENT OF SMALL INDUSTRIES -
2015
APPLICATIONS are hereby invited from the permanent residents
(male/female) of the North Western Province who have fulfilled the
qualifications mentioned in this notification, for recruitment to the post
of Small Industry Instructor (Grade III) of the North Western Provincial
Department of Small Industries.
The number of vacancies expected to be filled from the open
competitive examination :
Carpentry Instructor 05
Coir Industry Instructor 05
Pottery Instructor 01
1. It is hereby notified that this examination will be conducted in an
Examination Centre set up in Kurunegala by the North Western
Provincial Council Public Service Commission. The North Western
Provincial Council Public Service Commission reserves the right to
cancel or to postpone the examination.
2. Salary Scale :—
2.1 Salary Scale applicable for this post as per the Public
Administration Circular  06/2006 (iv) - MN 1/2006A  is  - Rs.
13,210 – 10x145 – 11x170 –10x320 – Rs. 22,040
Note i :
(a) This Salary Scale is subject to the revisions effected
from time to time, Officers who are recruited to the
Grade III of this post are required to pass the 1st
Efficiency Bar Examination within 03 years from the
date of appointment as per the scheme of recruitment.
The appointees should pass the 2nd Efficiency Bar
Examination within 03 years from the date of promotion
to the Grade II and they should pass the 3rd Efficiency
Bar Examination within 05 years from the date of
promotion to the Grade I.
(b) In terms of the Public Administration Circular 01/2014
and consequential circulars, official language
competency should be obtained within 05 years from
the date of appointment.
2.2 Age Limit .— Applicants should be above 18 years and not
more than 35 years. The upper age limit is not applicable for
those who are already serving in the Public Service/
Provincial Public Service.
2.3 Conditions of Service :
(i) This post is permanent and pensionable. Selected
candidates should contribute to the Widow/Widowers and
Orphans Pension Scheme.
(ii) Provisions of sub-sections 10 and 12 of the Chapter II of
the Establishment Code are applicable to this post.
(iii) Selected appointees should be committed to serve in any
area of the territory of the North Western Provincial
Council and they are subjected to the transfers.
(iv) The appointees should abide by the regulations of the
Establishment Code of the Democratic Socialist Republic
of Sri Lanka, Financial Regulations, Departmental Orders,
North Western Provincial Disciplinary Procedure Code,
Provincial Financial Regulations, Scheme of Recruitment
to the post of North Western Provincial Instructors,
Regulations of the Government and the North Western
Provincial Council already issued and will be issued from
time to time.
3. Educational and Professional Qualifications  :—
3.1 Educational Qualifications :
(a) Should have passed Six (06) subjects in not more than
two sittings of the General Certificate of Education
(Ordinary Level) Examination with Credit passes for
Sinhala/Tamil/English, Mathematics and two (02) other
subjects.
and
(b) Should have passed at least one subject (excluding the
Common General Test) of the General Certificate of
Education (Advanced Level) Examination.
3.2 Professional Qualifications
(i) Carpentry - Should have obtained a two year Certificate
in Carpentry from a Government Institute or an
Institute recognized by the Government.
(ii) Coir & Pottery -Should have undergone a training not
less than 06 months at a Government Institute or an
Institute recognized by the Government.
P.S. : All the candidates must fulfill all the above
educational, professional and other qualifications on
or before 03.08.2015.
4. Other Qualifications :—
4.1 Should be a Sri Lankan Citizen.
Examinations, Results of Examinations, & c.,

4.2 Applicants who are sitting for the competitive examination
should be permanent residents of the territory of the North
Western Provincial Council for a period of 03 years to the
closing date of applications. (residency should be proved at
the interview by a certificate issued by the Grama Niladhari
and counter signed by the Divisional Secretary)
4.3 Applicants should have an excellent moral character.
4.4 Applicants should have completed all the above qualifications
on or before the date mentioned in the application notification/
Gazette Notification.
5. Method of Recruitment .— Recruitment to the Post of Small
Industries Advisor (Grade III) will be made on merit on the results of
an examination consisting of General Knowledge and Intelligence
Quotient Test of 02  hours, Theory Paper of the relevant profession
of 02 hours and a Practical Test of 03 hours, conducted by the North
Western Provincial Public Service Commission, after conducting an
interview to ascertain the qualifications. (testing the theory knowledge
and the practical knowledge for Carpentry will not be required as the
certificate is awarded at national level after conducting examinations,
and they will be required only to sit for the general knowledge and
intelligence quotient test) 50% of the vacancies of the Small Industries
Instructors will be filled through the open competitive examination,
and 50% of the vacancies will be filled through the limited examination
and through the primary grade recruitments of the public service. If
there are no enough internal applicants, vacancies will be filled by
the external applicants.
6.  Method of Examination .— Examination will be held in Sinhala,
Tamil and English Medium and the applicants are not allowed to
change the Medium applied.
Subjects
I. General Knowledge and Intelligence quotient -
(Duration - 02 hours)
This is a paper prepared to identify the general intelligence of
the applicant including the knowledge on social , economic,
religious and cultural sectors of Sri Lanka. (General Knowledge
- 50 marks, Intelligence quotient - 50 marks, Total - 100 marks)
For Coir and Pottery Instructors
II. Theory test :
A written paper of 02 hours prepared in accordance with the
approved syllabus, to test the theoretical knowledge of the
coir and pottery trainees.
Theoretical Knowledge on Coir Industry
* Introduction to the Coconut husks
* Preparation of coir, identification of types of coir
* Preparation of coir ropes
* Identification of various products (brooms, door mats, floor
mats, furniture)
* Introductions to varieties of dye, methods of dyeing
* Methods of weaving
* Introduction to the types of door mats
* Designing
* Introduction to the decorating methods
Theoretical Knowledge on Pottery
* Introduction to clay
* Chemical analysis of clay
* Introduction to the mixes of clay and ceramics
* Introduction to the equipment
* Introduction to the production methods
* Production of various clay products
* Decorating
* Drawing of designs
III. Practical Test :
Applicants mentioned above (ii) should submit a creation/
creations according to their respective fields.
Practical Knowledge on Coir Industry
Preparing estimates in the Coir Sector, designing , preparation
of coir and production of items.
Practical Knowledge on Pottery
Identification of equipment and machinery of the pottery
industry, drawing of designs, construction, burning and
designing.
7.  A sum of Rs. 500 being the examination fee should be paid to
the  Divisional Secretariat of the area of permanent residence or the
workplace to be credited to Revenue Head 2003-02-13 of Secretary,
North Western Provincial Council Public Service Commission and
the receipt WPSM/2 obtained from the Divisional Secretariat should
be affixed to the application. Receipts other than the receipts obtained
from the Divisional Secretariat or Money Orders will not be accepted
and it should be noted that the examination fee is non-refundable
under any circumstance.
8. Method of Application :
8.1 Applications should be prepared in A4 size (21 cm x 29cm)
paper using the both sides as per the specimen application
given at the end of this notification including the paragraphs
from No.01 to No. 05 to the first page Paragraph No. 06
and the other Paragraphs to the rest of pages. Duly filled
applications should be sent under registered cover to reach
“Secretary, North Western Provincial Council Public Service
Commission, Provincial Council Complex, Kurunegala” on
or before 03.08.2015. The words “Recruitment to the Post
of Small Industries Instructor Grade III - 2015” should be
clearly indicated on the top left hand corner of the envelope
enclosing the application. (Officers who are already serving
in the Public or Provincial Public Service should forward
their applications through their Heads of the Department).

IMPORTANT  NOTICE  REGARDING  PUBLICATION   OF  GAZETTE
THE Weekly issue of the Gazette of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka  is normally published on
Fridays. If a Friday happens to be a Public Holiday the Gazette is published on the working day immediately preceding
the Friday. Thus the last date specified for the receipt of notices for publication in the Gazette also varies depending
on the incidence of public holidays in the week concerned.
The Schedule below shows the date of publication and the latest time by which notices should be received
for publication in the respective weekly Gazette. All notices received out of times specified below will not be
published. Such notices will be returned to the sender by post for necessary amendment and return if publication
is desired in a subsequent issue of the Gazette. It will be in the interest of all concerned if those desirous of ensuring
the timely publication of notices in the Gazette  make it a point to see that sufficient time is allowed for postal
transmission of  notices to the Government Press.
The Government Printer does not accept payments of subscription for the Government Gazette.
Note.—Payments for inserting Notices in the Gazette of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka
will be received by the Government Printer.
