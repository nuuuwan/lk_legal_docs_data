IMPORTANT  NOTICE  REGARDING  ACCEPTANCE  OF  NOTICES
FOR  PUBLICATION  IN  THE  WEEKLY  “GAZETTE”
ATTENTION is drawn to the Notification appearing in the 1st week of every month, regarding the latest dates and times of accept ance
of Notices for publication in the weekly Gazettes, at the end of every weekly Gazette of Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka.
All Notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes  shall close at 12.00 noon of each Friday, two weeks before the date of
publication. All Government Departments, Corporations, Boards, etc. are hereby advised that Notifications fixing closing dates and times
of applications in respect of Post-Vacancies, Examinations, Tender Notices and dates and times of Auction Sales, etc. should be  prepared
by giving adequate time both from the date of despatch of notices to Govt. Press and from the date of publication, thereby enab ling those
interested in the contents of such notices to actively and positively participate please note that inquiries and complaints mad e in respect
of corrections pertaining to notification will not be entertained after three months from the date of publication.
All notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes should reach Government Press two weeks before the date of publication i.e.
notices for publication in the weekly Gazette of  13th December, 2013 should reach Government Press on or before 12.00 noon on 29th
November,  2013.

shall be published in the Gazette, then such requirement shall be deemed to have been satisfied if such rule, regulation, order , by-law,
notification or other matter is published in an electronic form of the Gazette.”

By-Laws
BALANGODA  PRADESHIYA  SABHA
By-laws
IN terms of the powers vested by me under section 123 of
Pradeshiya Sabha Act, as the Minister in charge of the subject of
Local Government, I hereby declare that I have approved the
following by-law imposed by the Balangoda Pradeshiya Sabha of
the Administrative jurisdiction of Sabaragamuwa Provincial Council
by virtue  of the powers vested in it under section 122 and 126 of
Pradeshiya Sabha Act, No. 15 of 1987.
M
AHEEPALA HERATH,
Minister in-charge of the subject of
Local Government.
Sabaragamuwa Province.
On this 11th of June, 2012.
BALANGODA  PRADESHIYA  SABHA
Draft by-laws pertaining to the Damahana Crematorium operated
by L/P gas.
01. These by-laws may be cited as by-laws pertaining to the
Damahana Cermatorium operated by L. P. gass and belonging
to Balangoda Pradeshiya Sabha.
02. These By-laws are enacted to regulate the affairs related to
cremation to levy charges and to assure effective
management of the Damahana Crematorium beloning to
Balangoda Pradeshiya Sabha.
03. No human body shall be cremated in the Damahana
Crematorium without a legally valid premit issued by the
Chairman of the Balangoda Pradeshiya Sabha or by an officer
duly authorised by him on that behalf.
04. Any person who wishes to obtain a permit as stated in
Paragraph 3 above “herein after called the applicant shall
prepare and submit an application to the Chairman
substantially in form a appended here to with following
documents.
I. a certificate stating the relationship between the
deceased person and the applicant issued by the
Grama Niladhari of the Grama Niladhari Division in
which the deceased person had lived.
II. a photocopy of the National Identity Card of the
Applicant.
III. if an inquest has been head on the death of the deceased
person by an inquired in to sudden deaths or by a
magistrate under the provisions of the criminal
Procedure Code, a certificate under Section 41(5) of
the registration of births and deaths ordinance to the
effect that the dead body may be cremated. Such
certificate shall state in the 7th column that the body
may be cremated in the Damahana Crematorium.
IV . If the dead body is that of a still birth, a certificate
under Section 43(1) of the births and deaths registration
ordinance shall be attached to the application.
05. Completed applications after having been forwarded to the
Chairman shall be registered in the order of receipt.
06. If the dead body is accepted for cremation the permit shall be
issued to the applicant after receiving the fixed charges and a
deposit of Rupees One Thousand. The time of handing over
the dead body shall be stipulated in the permit
07. If the dead body is not accepted for cremation the applicant
should be informed in writing the reasons for such non
acceptance.
08. Dead human bodies shall be accepted for cremation daily
between 6.30 a. m. and 7.00 p.m.
09. The applicant shall hand over the dead body to the caretaker
of the crematorium during the time or before such time as
specified in the permit. The Pradeshiya Sabha shall not be
liable for any inconvenience caused on handing over the dead
body on late hours.
10. In respect of each cremation the caretaker of the crematorium
shall record in writing the names of applicants, their addresses,
the name of the deceased person and the relationship between
the applicant and the deceased person as the case may be.
11. No dead body of any person shall be cremated unless a
certificate is issued to that effect by a Registrar of Births and
Deaths under the provisions of Act, No. 41 of 1975 or unless
a certificate is issued by a Grama Niladhari to that effect.
12. At the time the crematorium is reserved the applicant or a
person who is closely related to the deceased shall supply to
the caretaker of the crematorium a pot or a suitable utensil to
deposit the ashes of the cremated dead body indicating the
name and address of the deceased person affixed to that pot
or utensil.
13. The caretaker of the crematorium shall exercise due care in
accordance of Section 12 to carefully deposit the ashes in the
pot or utensil supplied to him and deposit the same in the
space or land reserved for that purpose.
14. In case the applicant of the deceased person or the relatives
fail to remove the ashes deposited in the pot or the 14 days of
the remove of such ashes from the crematorium or fail to
supply a pot or utensil as specified in Section 12, The
Pradeshiya Sabha has the right to bury such ashes in the
burial grounds reserved for that purpose.

15. The ashes removed from the crematorium shall not be handed
over to any person other than the applicant or the close
relatives of the deceased person.
16. In consultation with the Chairman the Sabaragamuwa
Provincial Council shall appoint a Caretaker and an Assistant
for the Crematorium.
17. The Caretaker shall maintain the crematorium and its
surroundings cleanly and beautifully.
18. The Caretaker of the crematorium shall be bound by a list of
duties which would be assigned to him either by the Chairman
or the Secretary.
19. To enter the crematorium premises without a permit obtained
either from the Chairman or the caretaker shall be regarded
an offence. Never theless it would be presumed that
permission has been granted to a person and other who
accompany him  for the cremation of the dead body.
20. No person shall enter the crematorium premises during the
times specified in Section 8 without a valid permit and no
person shall loiter about or spend time in the crematorium
premises without any valid cause after the cremation
activities are finalised.
21. The caretaker shall have authority to evict any person causing
disturbance to the operations incidental to cremation.
22. No person coming to pay last respects to any dead person
shall behave in such a manner as to cause damage to the
monuments, the buildings of crematorium and shall not behave
in such a manner as to  bring disrespect to the deceased
person.
23. Under the provisions of cemeteries and burial grounds
ordinance action will be taken against any person who violates
the provisions stipulated in Section 22 above.
24. It will be an offence to cause any loss or damage to the
crematorium or the crematorium premises or to cause any
disturbance or threat to the caretaker of the crematorium or
to any of his assistants or servants on duty or display any
decoration in the crematorium premises.
25. Persons reserving the crematorium or their assistants shall
be liable for any damage caused to the crematorium or any
property thereof or any instrument there of in circumstances
stated in Section 27 hereof and they shall be determined by
the chairman.
26. It shall be an offence to violate the provisions of Sections 20,
24, 25 of these by laws and any person who violates any of
them shall on conviction by court be liable to a fine specified
in Section 122(2) of the Pradeshiya Sabha Act, No. 15 of
1987.
27. The chairman shall have the authority to bury any dead
body handed over to the crematorium for cremation in such
event the cremation is not possible during cremation or before
it due to any technical defect or electricity failure. The
decision of the Chairman is final and conclusive on such
problematic situation.
28. The fees chargeable for the cremation of bodies shall be
formed annually by the Pradeshiya Sabha by resolution
adopted to that effect. The Pradeshiya Sabha may amend
and review such fees on any occasion as it deems fit to do so.
29. In these by-laws, unless the context otherwise requires
“Sabha” shall mean and include the Balangoda Pradeshiya
Sabha.
“Chairman” shall mean the Chairman of the Balangoda
Pradeshiya Sabha.
“Secretary” shall mean the Secretary of the Balangoda
Pradeshiya Sabha.
“Caretaker of the Crematorium” shall mean the person
appointed to takecare of Damahana Crematorium.
“Dead Body” shall mean the human dead body.
30. In the event of any inconsistency between Tamil and English
texts of these by-laws, the sinhala text shall prevail.
