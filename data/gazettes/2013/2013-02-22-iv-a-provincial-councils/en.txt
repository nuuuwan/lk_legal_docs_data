Eastern Province Road Passengers Transport Authority
Statute No. 02 of 2009
IT is hereby published the regulations under clause 11(8) of Eastern
Province Road Passengers Transport Authority Statute No. 02 of
2009 in the Eastern Province.
This regulations shall come into operation with effect from 22nd of
the Month of February, 2013.

IMALAWEERA DISANAY AKE,
Minister of Education, Cultural Affairs, Land,
Land Development and Transport,
Eastern Province ,
Trincomalee.
At the Ministry of Education, Cultural Affairs,
Land, Land Development and Transport,
Eastern Province Provincial Council,
Trincomalee ,
on the 31st January, 2013.
This is the Public  Gazette  Notification of the Eastern Road
Passenger Transport Authority Statute 02 of 2009 of the Eastern
Province, regulations of Three Wheels Act under 11(8).
Regulations
1. The Regulations shall be cited as Three-wheeler Passengers
Transport Service Regulations of Eastern Province Road
Passenger Transport Authority Act, No. 01 of 2013 of the
Eastern Provincial Council and shall come into operations
on ........
2. With effect from .........no one should operate a Three-wheeler
for transport of passengers by way of charging a fee
individually or on hiring basis without a permit issued by
Eastern Province Road Passenger Transport Authority or
without registration with the Authority.
3. A Permit should not be issued to a person if he is not the
registered owner of the three-wheeler.
4. The priority should be given to the residents of the particular
Local Government Authority area when issuing a permit.
5. The Authority has the right to grant a permit, to renew, to
transfer a permit from one three-wheel stand to another, or
to turn down such requests.
5.1 When exercising the discretion the Authority should
give due consideration to following facts:
(a) To what extent the passenger transport needs have
fulfilled by three-wheelers operating in relevant stand
at the time.
(b) It is not appropriate to issue a permit if passenger
transport needs are fulfilled by three-wheelers operating
in relevant stand at the time.
(c) The all three wheels should be consider which are
providing service without the stands in the particular
area.
6. In order to obtain, renew, transfer or amend a permit, an
application should be submitted according to the prescribed
forms (Schedule 01) within a month by the registered owner
or in case of a company by its authorized officer. Along with
the said application, the following documents and fees shall
be submitted.
6.1 The Registration Certificate of the three-wheeler or a
Certificate issued by the Commissioner of Motor Traffic
including the particulars of its registration and a
photostat copy of that certificate.
6.2 The certificate of full Insurance covering the Three-
wheel and third parties and an unlimited Insurance
covering on all the Passengers traveling in the three-
wheeler.
6.3 Annual Revenue License and its photostat copy.
6.4 Medical certificate of the Driver and its photostat copy.
6.5 In case where the application is for the renewal of a
permit, the Permit Book.
6.6  The fee prescribed by the Authority.
6.7 If it is a three-wheeler operated in another Provincial
Council area its letter of release.
6.8 Membership certificate issued by Three-wheel
Operator’s Society/Company recognized by the
Authority.
7. The permit shall be in the form as given in Schedule two
herein.
8. The permit label (Windscreen Label) shall be in the form
given in Schedule three herein.
9. The fees prescribed by the authority shall be as in the
Schedule four herein.
10. Every application for a passenger transport permit should
be made not less than one month before the date on which
the applicant requires the permit to take effect.

11. The permit shall contain following terms and conditions:
11.1 The permit is not transferable.
11.2 Unless otherwise stated in the permit, the permit is
valid for a period of 12 months from the date of issue.
11.3 The diver of the Three-wheeler should carry the original
of the permit and label in the three-wheel.
11.4 The permit label issued by the authority should be
exhibited on the left side of the windscreen of the three-
wheel.
11.5 The permit holder should pay the service charges
prescribed by the Authority before the beginning of the
every month.
11.6 The permit holder and his employees should maintain
the Three-wheeler used for the relevant service in a
good mechanical condition and clean and in a good
running condition.
12. The things to be exhibited and things to be carried in the Three-
wheeler:
12.1 Things to be carried in the Three-wheeler by the driver:
(a) The original of the permit and the label.
(b) The medical certificate of the driver.
(c) The fare-table approved by the Authority.
(d) The things to be carried under the motor traffic Act:
I. Revenue License,
II. Certificate of Insurance,
III. Driving License.
12.2 The things to be exhibited :
(a) The photostat copy of the Passenger Service Permits
Label.
(b) “Smoking is prohibited in the Three-wheeler” notice.
(c) Notice showing the fare prescribed by the Authority.
(d) The Permit No. issued by the Authority and Registered
No. of the three wheeler.
13. The Permit-Holder’s responsibilities:
13.1 The permit holder should operate the Three-wheel
according to the rules adopted by the Three-wheel
Operator’s Society that have the approval of the
Authority.
13.2 In case of any accident with regards to the three-wheel,
the matter should be brought to the notice of the Authority
through the society within seven days.
13.3 In case of a serious accident or fatal accident the three
wheeler should not be allowed to run unless a new fitness
certificate is obtained.
13.4 A driver who is responsible for a serious or fatal accident
should not be taken back to service until such time he is
recommended to be medically fit. (Serious accident means
an accident as a result of which an injured person had to
undergo treatment in a hospital for more than 7 days of
an accident were damage to the property is more than
Rs. 10,000 . The accidents where all the three wheels had
moved out of the road are also considered as serious
accidents. A fatal accident means an accident where a
person who has met with the accident dies, as a result of
that same accident.).
13.5 Operators should install fare meters with effect from the
date determine by the Authority.
14. The responsibilities of the diver of the Three-wheeler:
14.1 He should drive the Three-wheeler ensuring the safety
and comfort of all the passengers travelling in the Three-
wheeler.
14.2 Should not carry more than the prescribed number of
passengers.
14.3 Should be of good conduct and behaviors and should be
courteous and disciplined towards all the passengers,
public and other officers.
14.4 Should refrain from taking Alcohol or dangerous drugs
while engaged in driving or during the duty hours.
14.5 Should not drive the vehicle recklessly, negligently or
dangerously.
14.6 The driver should be a driver registered with the society
approved by the Authority.
14.7 He should park the vehicle at the place prescribed by
the society and refrain from parking obstructing the
activities of the stand.
14.8 The driver should wear the uniform whenever he is on
duty.
14.9 In the instance of a failure to take the passenger to the
agreed upon destination due to any unavoidable
circumstances such as an accident or mechanical failure
the fee should be charged only up to that point.
14.10 He should not charge from passengers more or less than
the fee precribed.
14.11 The driver should not allow anybody to smoke or to
consume alcohol inside the three-wheeler.
15. Responsibilities of the passengers :
15.1 Should refrain from smoking; consume alcohol or dangers
drugs inside Three-wheeler.
15.2 Should be of decent behavior whilst inside the Three-
wheeler.

16. Regulations regarding Three-wheeler operators Organizations:
16.1 There should be an organization of all three-wheel
operators registered under Company Act or under the
Cooperative Society Act.
16.2 It is the responsibility of such organizations to ensure
that all operators perform according to regulations made
herein.
16.3 The organizations should incorporate necessary
provisions to their constitution to give effect to mention
in above sentence.
17. Regulations regarding three-wheeler stands :
17.1 Authority should established number of Three-Wheel
stand with the concurrence of relevant local government
Authorities.
17.2 The Three-wheel operator Society/Company should
coordinate/organise each stand according to the rules
prescribe by the Authority.
17.3 The Three-wheel operator ’s Society/Company should
pay the due fees for the stands to the Local Government
bodies.
18. The breach of regulations by the drivers will be treated as a
breach of regulations by the permit holder.
19. The permit holders should reset their fare meters according
to the fare table revised by the authority.
20. When an application is made for revalidation, Authority may
extent the validity period of a permit for period of one year
or for a period less than that if the Authority is satisfied that:
I. The applicant has performed according to the provisions of
the Eastern Province Road Passenger Transport Authority
Statute and the rules made under it.
II. He has not been punished for violation of any provision of
the statute or ruled made under it.
III. The applicant had paid all the due fees to the Authority and
to the three-wheeler operators Society and fulfilled all the
other conditions enforced by them.
21. Any person acting in breach of, in default of or in negligence
of these regulations will be guilty of an offence.
22. Where at an inquiry made by the officers authorized by the
Authority or by an inspection team, the parties who are in
breach of the regulations admit that there is a breach of the
regulations given under schedule 5(A) and (B), the Authority
is empowered to take the following steps:
(I) To obtain a statement to the effect that the breach of regulation
revealed at the inspection will not be repeated and be rectified.
(II) To charge a payment of Rs. 200 for the breach of regulation
under schedule 5(A) and Rs. 400 for the breach of regulations
under schedule 5(B) as administrative costs and other costs.
(III) If the permit holder agrees to act under (i) and (ii) the Authority
had the power not to act under section 11(4) of the Eastern
Province Road Passenger Transport Authority statute No. 2
of 2009 or not to file, or maintain any legal action under
section 44, against the permit holder.
23. A permit holder should not transfer the ownership of his
permit to another person at his will. If such a transfer made
it becomes invalid. Nevertheless, in the event of such a
transfer is required to fulfill the passenger demands of the
area the Authority may transfer the ownership of permit.
24. The Authority may issue instructions to a recognized
Society/Company of three-wheel operators on procedures
to be followed by them on the management of three-wheeler
operations and on reports that they should maintain and the
reports they should submit to the Authority.
