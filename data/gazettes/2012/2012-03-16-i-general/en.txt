IMPORTANT  NOTICE  REGARDING  ACCEPTANCE  OF  NOTICES
FOR  PUBLICATION  IN  THE  WEEKLY  “GAZETTE”
ATTENTION is drawn to the Notification appearing in the 1st week of every month, regarding the latest dates and times of accept ance
of Notices for publication in the weekly Gazettes, at the end of every weekly Gazette of Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka.
All notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes  shall close at 12.00 noon of each Friday, two weeks before the date of
publication. All Government Departments, Corporations, Boards, etc. are hereby advised that Notifications fixing closing dates and times
of applications in respect of Post-Vacancies, Examinations, Tender Notices and dates and times of Auction Sales, etc. should be  prepared
by giving adequate time both from the date of despatch of notices to Govt. Press and from the date of publication, thereby enab ling those
interested in the contents of such notices to actively and positively participate please note that inquiries and complaints mad e in respect
of corrections pertaining to notification will not be entertained after three months  from the date of publication.
All notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes should reach Government Press two weeks before the date of publication i.e.
notices for publication in the weekly Gazette  of 23rd March, 2012 should reach Government Press on or before 12.00 noon on
09th March, 2012.
LAKSHMAN  GOONEWARDENA,
Government Printer.
Department of Govt. Printing,
Colombo 08,
January 01, 2012.

THE   PROTECTION   OF   THE   RIGHTS   OF  ELDERS
ACT,  NO. 9  OF  2000
REGULATIONS  made by the Minister of Social Services under
Section 34 of the Protection of the Rights of the Elders Act, No. 9
of 2000 read with Section 17 and 18 of the Act.
Minister of Social Services.
        2012,
Colombo.
REGULATIONS
1. These regulations may be cited as the Protection of the Rights of
Elders Regulations, 2012.
2. (1) Every person or organisation engaged in the establishment
and maintenance of any Institution intended for providing
residential care for elders shall, if such institution has more
than five elders residing therein, forthwith make an
application for registration of such Institution providing
residential care for elders.
(2) Every application for registration shall be made in terms
of Section 16 and Section 18 of the Act, and shall be
forwarded to the Secretary of the Council. Such application
shall substantially be in the form set out in Schedule I to
these regulations.
3. Where the applicant is an organisation -
(1) the following documents shall accompany the
application -
(a) a copy of the Constitution of the organisation;
(b) a copy of the Minutes of the last annual general
meeting of the organisation;
(c) a copy of the statement of accounts of the organisation
of the year preceeding;
(d) a report on the services and assistance provided by
the organisation; and
(e) a report from the Director of Health Services of the
Administrative District within whose Jurisdiction
the Institution sought to be registered is situated.

(2) Every application shall be duly completed and shall be
signed by the Secretary of the organisation.
4. Where the applicant is a person -
(1) the following documents shall accompany the
application -
(a) a report on the services and assistance provided by
that person;
(b) two letters of recommendation issued by two eminent
persons;
(c) a report on the income, assets and liabilities of that
person; and
(d) a report from the Director of Health Services of the
Administrative District within whose jurisdiction
the Institution sought to be registered is situated.
(2) Every application shall be duly completed and signed by
the person in charge of the Institution.
5. Every organisation or person who is registered in terms of this
Act, shall be issued a Certificate of Registration required by section
18 of the Act which shall be in the form as set out in Schedule II to
these regulations. A Certificate of Registration shall not be transferable.
6. (1) Every registered person and registered organisation shall
maintain the following documents in accordance with the
directions given by the Council in connection with the
Institution maintained by them which provided residential
care for elders -
(i) cash book with bank accounts;
(ii) petty cash book;
(iii) main ledger;
(iv) main journal;
(v) register of members;
(vi) membership fees register;
(vii) payment vouchers;
(viii) debtors and creditors ledger;
(ix) receipt books;
(x) assets register;
(xi) books containing minutes of committee meetings
and general meetings;
(xii) reg isters of resident elders who receive residential
care in the Institution;
(xiii) register of staff details;
(xiv) file containing relevant Acts, regulations and rules.
(2) The provisions of sub-paragraphs (v), (vi), and (xi) of
paragraph (1) shall apply only in relation to a registered
organisation.
7. (1) Every registered person or registered organisation shall, as
the case may be -
(a) Act in conformity with special or general directions
issued from time to time by the Council;
(b) maintain proper accounts of the income and expenditure,
assets and liabilities and all other transactions of such
person or organisation;
(c) conduct the general meetings and committee meetings
regularly;
(d) indentify projects and programmes which would
highlight the objectives of the Act taking into
consideration the funds available to such person or
organisation, and submit them for approval of the
Council;
(e) submit where relevant an audited statement of accounts
and an annual progress report to the Council along with
the Minutes of the annual general meeting, within one
month of the date of the holding of the annual general
meeting.
(2) The provisions of sub-paragraph (c) of paragraph (1) shall
apply only in relation to an approval organisation.
8. Every approved person and approved organisation shall furnish
to the Council, not later than January, 31st of every year subsequent
to the registration of the Institution, the following information
regarding resident elders -
(1) ( a) a certified copy of the register of resident elders in
the Institution at the time of applying for registration;
(b) a certified copy of the register of resident elders in
the Institution on January, 01st of every year;
(c) a certified copy of the register of deceased resident
elders for the year ending on 31st December of the
year immediately preceding that year; and
(2) a report referring to the qualifications of the staff in
employment on January, 01st of every year.
9. Every person and organisation, engaged in the establishment and
maintenance of any Institution intended for providing residential
care for elders for a fee shall submit to the Council on or before
January, 31st of every year, a statement indicating -
(a) the monthly fee to be charged during that year; and
(b) services intended to be provided for the residents therein
during that year.
10. No resident elder who has been an inmate in an Institution
providing residential care for elders for a period of more than one year
shall be removed from the Institution:
Provided that a resident elder may be removed from an Institution
for the purposes of transferring such elder to another home, only
with the written consent of the elder. Such consent must be certified
by a registered medical practitioner.
11. The Provincial Social Services Director, the Divisional Secretary,
a Social Sevices Officer, a Social Development Assistant, an Elders
Rights Promotion Assistant or any person authorized in writing by
the Secretary of the Council may without prior notice, at any
reasonable time, enter into and inspect any institution providing or
purported to be providing, residential care for elders.

on that date shall make an application for a new registration,
if such person or organisation is liable to register an
Institution providing residential care for elders under the
provisions of the Act.
16. In these regulations -
“Act” means Protection of the Rights of Elders Act, No. 9 of
2000, as amended by Act, No. 5 of 2011;
“Approved person” or approved organisation” means a person
or an organisation registered under Section 18 of the Act, as the case
may be;
“Council” means the National Council for Elders established
under Section 2 of the Act.
