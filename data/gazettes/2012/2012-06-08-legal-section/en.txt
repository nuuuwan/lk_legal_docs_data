IMPORTANT  NOTICE  REGARDING  ACCEPTANCE  OF  NOTICES
FOR  PUBLICATION  IN  THE  WEEKLY  “GAZETTE”
ATTENTION is drawn to the Notification appearing in the 1st week of every month, regarding the latest dates and times of accept ance
of Notices for publication in the weekly Gazettes, at the end of every weekly Gazette of Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka.
All notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes  shall close at 12.00 noon of each Friday, two weeks before the date of
publication. All Government Departments, Corporations, Boards, etc. are hereby advised that Notifications fixing closing dates and
times of applications in respect of Post-Vacancies, Examinations, Tender Notices and dates and times of Auction Sales, etc. sho uld be
prepared by giving adequate time both from the date of despatch of notices to Govt. Press and from the date of publication, the reby
enabling those interested in the contents of such notices to actively and positively participate please note that inquiries and  complaints
made in respect of corrections pertaining to notification will not be entertained after three months  from the date of publication.
All notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes should reach Government Press two weeks before the date of publication i.e.
Notices for publication in the weekly Gazette of 15th  June, 2012 should reach Government Press on or before 12.00 noon on 01st June,
2012.
LAKSHMAN  GOONEW ARDENA,
Government Printer.
Department of Govt. Printing,
Colombo 08,

AN  ACT   TO  INCORPORATE  THE  BAREERAH
MUSLIM LADIES  ARABIC COLLEGE
I, M. S. Thowfeek do hereby give notice under standing Order
No.48 (1) of the Parliament of the Democratic Socialist Republic of
Sri Lanka that I Propose to introduce in Parliament a Bill entitled
“An Act to incorporate the Bareerah Muslim Ladies Arabic College”
The aim of the Bill is to incorporate the said College which has
the following general objects :-
1. to Produce Islamic Theologists to lead the Community under
the Islamic Law of "Shariaa".
2. To Protect Islam.
M. S. T
HOWFEEK,
Member of Parliament.
Trincomalee District.
06-310
AN ACT TO INCORPORATE THE WAREHOUSE
PROJECT
I, Hon. Victor Anthony Perera do hereby give notice under Standing
Order No. 48 of the Standing order of Parliament of the Democratic
Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka, that I propose to introduce in
Parliament a Bill entitled "An Act to incorporate the Warehouse
Project" which has the following general objects :
(i) To provide social services to the less privileged persons in
all parts of the country by helping them to meet their basic
needs, train and develop individual skills, help find
employment and improve their standard of living.
(ii) To provide assistance to low income families in Urban and
Rural communities to meet their basic needs and uplift living
standards.
(iii) To use creative arts as a platform to help children and youth
of all walks of life and different cultural and religious
backgrounds, to meet and interact with each other to foster
unity.
etc.
H
ON. VICTOR ANTHONY PERERA,
Member of Parliament,
Puttalam District.
06-337
AN ACT TO INCORPORATE THE WHITE ROSE
FOUNDATION
I, Hon. Victor Anthony Perera, MP do hereby give notice under
Standing Order No. 48 of the Standing Order of the Parliament of
the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka that I propose to
introduce in Parliament a Bill entitled "An Act to incorporate the
White Rose Foundation"  which has the following general objects :
(i) To provide social services to the less privileged persons in
all parts of the country by helping them to meet their basic
needs, train and develop individual skills, help find
employment and improve their standard of living.
(ii) To impact the lives of children and youth by providing them
with opportunities to learn creative arts to bring about a
positive change in their mental and physical wellbeing.
(iii) To provide opportunities and financial assistance to
individuals with varied talents to grow to their full potential.
(iv) To provide opportunities for youth to learn new skills and
receive on the job training and assist them to set up small
scale businesses within their communities.
H
ON. VICTOR ANTHONY PERERA,
Member of Parliament,
Puttalam District.
