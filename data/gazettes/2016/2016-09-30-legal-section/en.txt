IMPORTANT  NOTICE  REGARDING  ACCEPTANCE  OF  NOTICES
FOR  PUBLICATION  IN  THE  WEEKLY  “GAZETTE”
ATTENTION is drawn to the Notification appearing in the 1st week of every month, regarding the latest dates and times of accept ance
of Notices for publication in the weekly Gazettes, at the end of every weekly Gazette of Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka.
All notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes  shall close at 12.00 noon of each Friday, two weeks before the date of
publication. All Government Departments, corporations, Boards, etc. are hereby advised that Notifications fixing closing dates and times
of applications in respect of Post-V acancies, Examinations, Tender Notices and dates and times of Auction Sales, etc. should be  prepared
by giving adequate time both from the date of despatch of notices to Govt. Press and from the date of publication, thereby enab ling those
interested in the contents of such notices to actively and positively participate please note that inquiries and complaints mad e in respect
of corrections pertaining to notification will not be entertained after three months  from the date of publication.
All notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes should reach Government Press two weeks before the date of publication i.e.
Notices for publication in the weekly Gazette of 21st October, 2016, should reach Government Press on or before 12.00 noon on 07th
October 2016.
Electronic Transactions Act, No. 19 of 2006 - Section 9
“Where any Act or Enactment provides that any proclamation, rule, regulation, order , by-law, notification or other matter
shall be published in the Gazette, then such requirement shall be deemed to have been satisfied if such rule, regulation, order , by-law,
notification or other matter is published in an electronic form of the Gazette .”

PART II – GAZETTE OF THE DEMOCRATIC SOCIALIST REPUBLIC OF SRI LANKA – 30.09.2016
To the Respondents above - named :
Take Notice that the above named Petitioner, Dampahalage Don Somaweera Chandrasiri has Presented on 11th September 2015
the Election Petition bearing No. CA/EP/01/2015 to the Court of Appeal under Parliamentary Elections, Act No.1 of 1981,
Canvasing the election of Members of Parliament from the United Peoples Freedom Alliance, on the basis of the total number
of votes polled at the General Election Holden on the 17th August, 2015 under Article 99A of the Constitution ;
And whereas when this matter was mentioned before the Court on 11th December 2015, His Lordship, A. H. M. D. Nawaz J
(sitting as the appointed Election Judge) directed to give notice by Publication the Government Gazette, to the Respondents
indicating the time and place of the trial of the said Election Petition will be fixed by the Court of Appeal in terms of Rule 17 of
the Fourth Schedule thereof (Read with Section 94 of said Act) ;
And wherefore Notice is hereby give that this Election Petition is fixed for trial in terms of Rule 17 (Read with Section 94) of
Fourth Schedule to the Parliamentary Elections Act No. 1 of 1981 for 20th October 2016 at 10.00 a.m. before the Court of Appeal
of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka.
You are also hereby given notice that any Respondent to an Election Petition is entitled to act and take steps in terms of Rule
7 of Fourth Schedule to the Parliamentary Elections Act No. 01 of 1981 the Respondents ; and Rule 7 is hereby reproduced
herein below ;
Rule 7 of the Parliamentary Elections Act No. 1 of 1981
zzlist of objections in recriminatory case"
“7. The Respondent in a petition complaining of an undue return and claiming the seat for some person may lead
evidence to prove that the election of such person was undue, and in such case such respondent shall, six days before
the day appointed for trial, deliver to the Registrar, and also at the address, if any, given by the Petitioner, a list of the
objections to the election upon which he intends to rely, and the Registrar shall allow inspection of office of such lists
to all parties concerned ; and no evidence shall be  given by a Respondent of any objection to the election not
specified in the list except by leave of the Court, upon such terms as to amendments of the list, postponement of the
inquiry, and payment of costs, as may be orderedZZ
You are further given notice of the other rules of the Fourth Schedule to the Parliamentary Elections Act No.1 of 1981 including
Rule 6 which deals with “List of votes objected to where seat claimed by unsuccessful candidate” and Rule 9 which deals with
“Appointment of agent by Respondent.”
