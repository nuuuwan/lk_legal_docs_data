IMPORTANT  NOTICE  REGARDING  ACCEPTANCE  OF  NOTICES
FOR  PUBLICATION  IN  THE  WEEKLY  “GAZETTE”
ATTENTION is drawn to the Notification appearing in the 1st week of every month, regarding the latest dates and times of accept ance
of Notices for publication in the weekly Gazettes, at the end of every weekly Gazette of Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka.
All notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes  shall close at 12.00 noon of each Friday, two weeks before the date of
publication. All Government Departments, Corporations, Boards, etc. are hereby advised that Notifications fixing closing dates and times
of applications in respect of Post-Vacancies, Examinations, Tender Notices and dates and times of Auction Sales, etc. should be  prepared
by giving adequate time both from the date of despatch of notices to Govt. Press and from the date of publication, thereby enab ling those
interested in the contents of such notices to actively and positively participate please note that inquiries and complaints mad e in respect
of corrections pertaining to notification will not be entertained after three months  from the date of publication.
All notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes should reach Government Press two weeks before the date of publication i.e.
notices for publication in the weekly Gazette of 26th September, 2014 should reach Government Press on or before 12.00 noon on 12th
September, 2014.
Electronic Transactions Act, No. 19 of 2006 - Section 9
“Where any Act or Enactment provides that any proclamation, rule, regulation, order, by-law, notification or other matter shall
be published in the Gazette, then such requirement shall be deemed to have been satisfied if such rule, regulation, order, by-l aw,
notification or other matter is published in an electronic form of the Gazette.”

ACCOUNTING  POLICIES
SUMMARY OF SIGNIFICANT POLICIES
1. General Policies
1.1 The Balance Sheet, Statement of Income and Expenditure, changes in Net Assets and Cash flow together with Accounting Policies
and Notes as at 31st December 2012, for the year then ended, comply in all material aspects with the Sri Lanka Public Sector
Accounting Standard.
1.2 Comparative Information :
Where necessary comparative figures have been modified to conform with changes in presentation in the current year.
1.3 Valuation of Assets :
1.3.1 Inventories :
Inventories are valued at actual cost using First In First out (FIFO) method.
1.3.2 Cash Flow Statement :
The cash flow has been prepared by using the “Indirect method”.
1.3.3 Property, Plant and Equipment :
(a) Cost and Valuation –
All Property, Plant and Equipment are recorded at cost and are stated at historical cost less depreciation. However
valuation of some Land and building is pending.
(b) Depreciation :
Provision for depreciation is calculated on all Property, Plant and Equipment, other than free hold land, in order to write
off the cost and/or valuation over the estimated economic life of such assets. Although, the depreciation, till end of 2011
was provided either for the half year or full year, beginning from 01st of January, 2012 it is provided from the date of
purchase.
The annual rates of depreciation are as follows :
Building 5%
Furniture 10%
Equipment and Vehicle 20%
Books 20%
02. Liabilities and Provisions :
2.1 All known liabilities have been accounted for in preparing the Financial Statements.
2.2 Retirement benefit plan-Gratuity :
Provision for gratuity was made in accordance with gratuity Act, No. 12 of 1983 and SLAS 16. The Gratuity liability is not
externally funded nor actually valued. The Gratuity fund Account is shown under Non-current liabilities in the balance sheet, based
on half month salary as of the last month of the financial year of all employees for each completed year of service, commencing from
the first year of service.
2.3 Income Statement :
2.3.1 Revenue Recognition :
The Revenue generated through internal sources, Government grant received and the expenditure were recorded on accrual
basis. The Government grant released by the Public Enterprises Division was taken into account.

the timely publication of notices in the Gazette  make it a point to see that sufficient time is allowed for postal
transmission of  notices to the Government Press.
