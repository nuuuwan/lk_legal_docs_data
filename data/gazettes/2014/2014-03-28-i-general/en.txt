IMPORTANT  NOTICE  REGARDING  ACCEPTANCE  OF  NOTICES
FOR  PUBLICATION  IN  THE  WEEKLY  “GAZETTE”
ATTENTION is drawn to the Notification appearing in the 1st week of every month, regarding the latest dates and times of accept ance
of Notices for publication in the weekly Gazettes, at the end of every weekly Gazette of Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka.
All notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes  shall close at 12.00 noon of each Friday, two weeks before the date of
publication. All Government Departments, Corporations, Boards, etc. are hereby advised that Notifications fixing closing dates and times
of applications in respect of Post-Vacancies, Examinations, Tender Notices and dates and times of Auction Sales, etc. should be  prepared
by giving adequate time both from the date of despatch of notices to Govt. Press and from the date of publication, thereby enab ling those
interested in the contents of such notices to actively and positively participate please note that inquiries and complaints mad e in respect
of corrections pertaining to notification will not be entertained after three months  from the date of publication.
All notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes should reach Government Press two weeks before the date of publication i.e.
notices for publication in the weekly Gazette of 17th April, 2014 should reach Government Press on or before 12.00 noon on 04th April,
2014.
Electronic Transactions Act, No. 19 of 2006 - Section 9
“Where any Act or Enactment provides that any proclamation, rule, regulation, order, by-law, notification or other matter shall
be published in the Gazette, then such requirement shall be deemed to have been satisfied if such rule, regulation, order, by-l aw,
notification or other matter is published in an electronic form of the Gazette.”

INSTRUCTIONS  FOR  ISSUING  OF  MEDIA  IDENTITY
CARDS  BY  THE  DEPARTMENT  OF  GOVERNMENT
INFORMATION
DEPARTMENT of Government Information issues media identity
cards with the purpose of facilitating duties and protecting the
professional dignity of Sri Lankan journalists. Therefore, all heads
of media institutions are kindly requested to provide this media
identity card for journalists whom it is essential.
02. Additional details on issuing of Media Identity Cards, which
are not included in this procedure are stated in attachment
1. Please pay your kind attention in that regard.
03. Media Identity Cards will be issued in the following manner
in order to affirm their safety and to prevent them being
achieved by irrelevant persons.
(i) Local Journalists:
Journalists attached to a recognized media institution on
permanent or on contractual basis or journalists who have
served for more than six months.
(ii) Photographic Journalists:
Photographic Journalists, who are serving in a recognized
media institution on permanent or on contractual basis, for
more than six months.
(iii) Website Journalists:
Identity cards will be issued subject to a maximum of 5
Identity Cards for a website. (Website should be daily
updated, allocate more space for news and current affairs,
should have continuously maintained for six months and
should be registered at the Ministry of Mass Media and
Information.)
(iv) Freelance Journalists:
Journalists who have continuously, within six months,
presented more than 12 programmes/articles for a recognized
print or media institution (published news items should be
provided).
(v) Media Administrators:
Media administrators who are directly engaged in media
administration in a recognized media institution.
For media institutions, which 100-200 journalists are employed
- 2 identity cards
For media institutions, which 200-300 journalists are employed
- 4 identity cards
For media institutions, which more than 300 journalists are
employed - 5 identity cards
(vi) Regional Journalists:
Journalists who are appointed as Regional Journalists and
who can prove to have continuously reported to a national level
print and electronic media institution for six months.
