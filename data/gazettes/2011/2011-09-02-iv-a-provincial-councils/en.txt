BY-LAWS ON REGULARISATION  OF  CONTROLLING  THE DISPLAY  OF PROPAGANDA
ADVERTISEMENTS – KURUWITA  PRADESHIYA  SABHA
1. These By-laws are known as the by-laws of regularisation of controlling the display of propaganda advertisements within the
authority area of Kuruwita Pradeshiya Sabha.
2. Henceforth no one shall display or cause to display any propaganda advertisement seemingly to any street, road, canal or air
without a permit obtained from the Chairman.
3. Every application which is submitted under these by-laws should consists the following things :–
(a) A written application addressed to the secretary ;
(b) A ground plan drawn to the scale not less than 1:1000 ;
(c) A sketch of the creation drawn to the scale not less than 1:1000 ;
(d) Any amendments made to that plan, sketch or specification in order to consistent with these by-laws ;
(e) A copy of that advertisement to be displayed which is drawn to a smaller scale with all the colours ;
(f) A ground plan of the propaganda advertisement with adjacent streets and land marks ;
(g) For the purpose of giving permission to display a propaganda advertisement/board, following documents are required
by the Pradeshiya Sabha :–
(i) A letter of consent obtained from the owner of the land where propaganda advertisement or board is fixed ;
(ii) If it is a road reservation, a letter of permission obtained from the Road Development Authority ;
(iii) If it is an expressway, a letter of permission obtained from the Environmental and Landscape Division of the
Urban Development Authority.
4. All the applications to get a permit under the 2nd By-law of the above, should be submitted to the Secretary of the Pradeshiya
Sabha at least seven days prior to the display of advertisement.
5. No one shall cause to erect any board to display a propaganda advertisement, wall board, notice board or any other structure
except according to the plans, diagrams and specifications as written approval of the Pradeshiya Sabha.
6. Where an application is approved by the Pradeshiya Sabha, the applicant shall not be issued a permit until he pay the taxes as
mentioned in the 1st Schedule hereto.
7. Any person who receives a permit, shall not display the advertisements other than the specified places in the 2nd Schedule
hereto.
8. Where a permit is issued to any person, the Pradeshiya Sabha may order him to put the name of the permit holder and reference
number of the permit on every advertisement to be displayed.

9. The charges menioned in the 1st Schedule below are not applicable to the below “a” to “e” items and to the aerial name boards: –
(a) A notice belongs to any concert which the net income received uses for charity ;
(b) A display of a propaganda advertisement regarding to any concert where such concert is held ;

(e) A name board for professional activities which is not exceeding 0.093m. ;
(f) A propaganda advertisement on a vehicle, with the name, address and phone or telex Nos., which is used for a trade or

10. The owner, lessee or advertiser shall take a permit from the Pradeshiya Sabha by paying the required charges if the wall board,
notice board or any other structure of a business premises under these by-laws includes a trade name of any goods or services, brands and
diagrams other than the business name, address, registered number, telephone number or telex number.
11. (i) No person shall display a propaganda advertisement over or so as to project towards a road or foot path or a street.
(ii) Display of a propaganda advertisement on a board at a height exceeding 12 meters from the ground.
(iii) Fix or hang a propaganda advertisement which is longer than the fafade of an adjoining building which faces a street or
a road in the fafade of that building or in the name board of a trade stall.
(iv) A propaganda advertisement by nature of aerial name board shall not be erected without the prior approval of the
Pradeshiya Sabha.
12. No person shall display a propaganda advertisement in a private or public building, a public sacred place, public park, bridge
or passenger bridge over a road or on a telephone or electricity post, side wall, dam, weir, culvert, rock or on a tree.
13. No one shall display any propaganda advertisement on any notice board or name board which is erected or constructed in
accordance with the manner approved by the Pradeshiya Sabha within the specified area by a resolution of the Pradeshiya
Sabha and publish it in the gazette.
14. On any road or street within the authority area of the Kuruwita Pradeshiya Sabha, no one shall :–
(i) carry or cause to carry any propaganda advertisement displayed on any board, frame, stand ;
(ii) use a radio, a gramophone, musical instrument, bell or any other instrument for the purpose of introducing or displaying
propaganda advertisement.
15. (i) The Pradeshiya Sabha may order by written to stop the display of a propaganda advertisement when such advertisement
is discolored, unpleasant or if it is displaying something contrary to the moral and public peace, if it is made of plastic
or polythene which are not environmental friendly.
(ii) Any one who has been rendered a notice under the Paragraph One of these by-laws, shall act according to the way stated
there within a specified period of time.
16. Owner or lessee of any board that is used to display a propaganda advertisement :–
(i) shall maintain in good repair ensuring the safety of property of the public ;
(ii) shall write clearly and legibly advertiser’s/lessee’s/owner’s name, address and the telephone number on the front side of
the board.
17. No one shall distort or cause to distort any symbol, mark, letter, word or number which is placed on a propaganda
advertisement by the Pradeshiya Sabha to show that the relevant levies have been paid.
18. (i) Within the authority area of the Kuruwita Pradeshiya Sabha, if any one is started, implementing, restarted or completed
a task to display a propaganda advertisement without a permit issued by the Chairman or in contravention of the
conditions in the permit, other than the remedies in these by-laws ; the owner or lessee shall be ordered by a written
notice to :–
(a) stop the display of the propaganda advertisement ; or
(b) destroy, erase or alter the propaganda advertisement within less than four days of the starting or implementing or
completing of the task.

IMPORTANT  NOTICE  REGARDING  PUBLICATION   OF  GAZETTE
THE Weekly issue of the Gazette of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka  is normally published on
Fridays. If a Friday happens to be a Public Holiday the Gazette is published on the working day immediately preceding
the Friday. Thus the last date specified for the receipt of notices for publication in the Gazette also varies depending
on the incidence of public holidays in the week concerned.
The Schedule below shows the date of publication and the latest time by which notices should be received
for publication in the respective weekly Gazette. All notices received out of times specified below will not be
published. Such notices will be returned to the sender by post for necessary amendment and return if publication
is desired in a subsequent issue of the Gazette. It will be in the interest of all concerned if those desirous of ensuring
the timely publication of notices in the Gazette  make it a point to see that sufficient time is allowed for postal
transmission of  notices to the Government Press.
The Government Printer does not accept payments of subscription for the Government Gazette.
Payments should be made direct to the 
Superintendent, Government Publications Bureau, No. 132, Maya Avenue,
Kirulapone, Colombo 05.
Note.—Payments for inserting Notices in the Gazette of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka
will be received by the Government Printer and not by the Superintendent, Government Publications Bureau.
