IMPORTANT  NOTICE  REGARDING  ACCEPTANCE  OF  NOTICES
FOR  PUBLICATION  IN  THE  WEEKLY  “GAZETTE”
ATTENTION is drawn to the Notification appearing in the 1st week of every month, regarding the latest dates and times of accept ance
of Notices for publication in the weekly Gazettes, at the end of every weekly Gazette of Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka.
All notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes  shall close at 12.00 noon of each Friday, two weeks before the date of
publication. All Government Departments, Corporations, Boards, etc. are hereby advised that Notifications fixing closing dates and times
of applications in respect of Post-Vacancies, Examinations, Tender Notices and dates and times of Auction Sales, etc. should be  prepared
by giving adequate time both from the date of despatch of notices to Govt. Press and from the date of publication, thereby enab ling those
interested in the contents of such notices to actively and positively participate please note that inquiries and complaints mad e in respect
of corrections pertaining to notification will not be entertained after three months  from the date of publication.
All notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes should reach Government Press two weeks before the date of publication i.e.
Notices for publication in the weekly Gazette  of 26th August, 2011 should reach Government Press on or before 12.00 noon on 12th
August, 2011.
LAKSHMAN  GOONEW ARDENA,
Government Printer.
Department of Govt. Printing,
Colombo 08,
January 01, 2011.

An Act to Incorporate the Sri Lanka Korean Acupuncture
Medical Service Association
I, Shantha Bandara, Member of Parliament do hereby give notice
under standing Order No. 48 (1) of the Parliament of the Democratic
Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka that I propose to introduce in
Parliment a Bill entitled “an act to incorporate the Sri Lanka Koraen
Acupuncture Medical Service Association”.
The aim of the Bill is to incorporate the said Association which has
the following obejects :-
1. To unite all members of the Sri Lanka Government
Ayurvedic Medical Officers who have studied korean
Acupuncture Medical System into a single vital body
capable of playing an important role in the promulgation
of professional and ethical standards for the practice of
Korean Acupuncture Medical System :
2. To foster cooperation and friendliness amongst the
members of the Association and also different allied
professions and organizations for the best interest of the
Association and enchancement of Public health and social
Welfare :
3. To provide up to - date and relevant information to medical
practitioners and potential medical students to help them
make the best informed and considered choice with regard
to korean Acupuncutre Medical System; etc.
