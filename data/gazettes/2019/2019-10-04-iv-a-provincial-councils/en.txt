BY LAW ON ELIMINATION OF ANNOYANCE CAUSED TOWARDS PUBLIC BY PIG FARMING AND ENSURE 
PUBLIC HEALTH
 1. This by law is enacted for making provisions for the elimination of annoyance caused towards public by maintenance 
of pig farming for obtaining pork, for breeding of pigs, and for the preparation of food for pigs, maintaining places 
for processing pork, for disposal of waste and transporting pigs along public roads within the area of authority of 
Pradeshiya Sabha Bingiriya and to ensure public health.
 2. This by law is enacted under Sections 126 (iX) and 126 (Xi)) to be read with Section 122 (1) of Pradeshiya Sabha 
Act, No. 15 of 1987.
 3.  This by law is cited as the by law on elimination of annoyance caused towards public by pig farming and ensuring 
public health within the area of authority of Pradeshiya Sabha Bingiriya.
 4. Every person who maintains a place within the area of authority of Pradeshiya Sabha Bingiriya for rearing pigs for 
obtaining pork, breeding of pigs, preparation of food for pigs and processing pork shall obtain a license speciﬁ ed in 
schedule No. I of this by law from the Chairman of the Pradeshiya Sabha.
 5. Every person who applies for a license under the above Section 4 shall apply through an application substantially 
prepared in accordance with the Schedule II of this by law.
 6. Fees levied for a license and an application for obtaining a license under this by law shall be decided upon an adop-
tion of resolution published by a gazette notiﬁ cation by the Pradeshiya Sabha from time to time.
 7. There shall be a committee consisting the following of ﬁ cers to provide instructions and recommendations with 
regard to the implementation of the provisions under this by law.
 (a) Secretary - Chairman to the Sabha 
 (b) Medical Of ﬁ cer of Health or his agent - Committee Member
 (c) Veterinary or his agent or his agents - Committee Member

8. Every person who applies for a license under the above Section 4 shall ful ﬁ ll the following conditions.
 (a)  Every construction made at the relevant premises for which the license is applied shall be constructed in conformity 
with a prescribed plan approved by the Chairman.
 (b)  The maximum number of pigs to be reared in any building shall be decided by the Chairman, considering the age 
limits of the animals and the minimum area to be allotted for each grown animal shall be considered as 10 sq.ft.
 (c)  Every premise shall be covered with an adequate height and the height to be covered shall be decided by the 
Chairman considering the recommendation of the committed set out under Section 7.
 (d)  Every construction relevant to the business of the premises under the license shall beconstructed at the minimum 
distance of 15 meters from the poultry houses and if a public premises or a neighbouring land adjacent to the said 
premises is located it shall be covered with a parapet wall.
 (e)  The minimum distance from a pig farm to the boundary of a land in which the farm is located relevant to the 
license shall be as follows.
 I.  In case the domestic level pig farms the minimum distance from the pig farm to the boundary of the land 
shall be 10 meters.
 II. In the case of minor scale pig farms the minimum distance from the pig farm to the boundary of the land 
shall be 15 meters.
 III. In the case of medium scale pig farms the minimum distance from the pig  farm to the boundary of the 
land shall be 20 meters.
 IV . In the case of major scale pig farms the minimum distance from the pig farm to the boundary of the land 
shall be 50 meters.
 V . The minimum distance between two pig farms within a premise shall be 2 meters.
 (f) It shall be lawful for the Chairman to take necessary action considering the recommendations of the committee 
in respect of business places which are not complied with the distances speciﬁ ed under Section I, II, III, IV , V 
of this by law, from the effective date of this by law. In case dissatisﬁ ed about the said decision an appeal could 
be submitted the Provincial Commissioner and his decisions shall be ﬁ nal and conclusive.
 (g)  Every construction relevant to the license applied shall be located at the minimum distance of 15 meters from 
a water source, well or a water course.
 (h) In case the premises for which the license is applied is a food and meat processing center, appropriate measures 
shall be taken to control the sound generated by the machinery without causing annoyance towards the public.
 (i) The waste generated at the premises shall be disposed to a septic pit and provided that, in case a medium scale 
pig farm waste shall be directed to a bio gas unit and shall be reﬁ ned and in case of a major scale pig farm waste 
shall be directed to a bio gas unit or a waste treatment system and shall be reﬁ ned.
 (j)  The constructions made at the premises under the license applied shall be made to prevent from leaking or mixing 
water from the rain or any other way and a covered drainage system shall be constructed for drainage of wasted 
water within the premises.

 10. Every licensed premises shall,
 (a) have adequate facilities for rearing animals,
 (b) methodically dispose the waste generated at the premise and take appropriate measures to control the bad 
odor generated at the premise.
 (c) maintain the drains daily and cause to construct drains so as to ﬂ ow water easily.
 (d)  Any waste material removed from the premise shall be used for manure and it should be buried in a pit                 
speciﬁ cally prepared for the said purpose and in any case shall not be heaped at the open space of the premises. 
Waste disposed under this Sub Section shall not be whatever manner buried unless the minimum distance 
from a water source is 30 meters.
 (e) It shall be lawful to take action to dispose the waste generated at the pig farm premises as per the supervision 
of the committee. Further, if the waste generated at the pig farm premises is dumped to another land the 
procedure to be followed by the licensee may be ordered by the Chairman under the recommendations of 
the committee set out under Section 7.
 (f) Every employee shall wear sanitary tools and the licensee shall responsible for ensuring to that effect.
 (g) Toilet facilities and lavatory facilities shall be adequately provided for the workers employed at the premises 
and those toilets and lavatories shall be maintained in proper sanitation.
 (h)  In case of gas emission is taken place on account of a bio gas, such emission of gas should be released                    
incompliance with the detoxiﬁ cation standards.
 (i)  A person who is suffering from a communicable or infectious disease or has been attending to any person 
suffering from a communicable or infectious disease during the incubation period shall not be employed.
 (j) If adequate facilities are not provided for storing left-over or parts of animals which are brought for the 
purpose feed the pigs such left-over or parts of animals shall not be kept more than one day and when such 
left-over and parts of animals are transported they shall be transported in covered receptacles.
 (k) A suitable place shall be properly maintained for cooking food and for consuming food for the employees.
 (l) In case the premises is a place where meat is processed the equipment and tools used for processing meat 
shall be maintained with proper hygiene and standard facilities shall be provided for storing and packing 
meat.

maintenance of paultry farming for obtaining Chicken and eggs for breeding of chicks, for the preparation of food 
for fowls, maintaining places for processing chicken’, for disposal of waste and transporting fowls along public
