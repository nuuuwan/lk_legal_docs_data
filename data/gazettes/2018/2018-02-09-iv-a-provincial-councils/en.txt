IMPORTANT  NOTICE  REGARDING  ACCEPTANCE  OF  NOTICES 
FOR  PUBLICATION  IN  THE  WEEKLY  “GAZETTE”
ATTENTION is drawn to the Notiﬁ cation appearing in the 1st week of every month, regarding the latest dates and times of acceptance of Notices 
for publication in the weekly Gazettes, at the end of every weekly Gazette of Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka.
 All Notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes shall close at 12.00 noon of each Friday, two weeks before the date of publication. 
All Government Departments, Corporations, Boards, etc. are hereby advised that Noti ﬁ cations ﬁ xing closing dates and times of applications in 
respect of Post-Vacancies, Examinations, Tender Notices and dates and times of Auction Sales, etc. should be prepared by giving  adequate time 
both from the date of despatch of notices to Govt. Press and from the date of publication, thereby enabling those interested in the contents of such 
notices to actively and positively participate please note that inquiries and complaints made in respect of corrections pertaining to notiﬁ cation will 
not be entertained after three months from the date of publication.
 All notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes should reach Government Press two weeks before the date of publication i.e. notices 
for publication in the weekly Gazette of  02nd March, 2018 should reach Government Press on or before 12.00 noon on 16th February, 2018.
 Electronic Transactions Act, No. 19 of 2006 - Section 9
 “Where any Act or enactment provides that any Proclamation, rule, regulation, order, by-law, noti ﬁ cation or other matter shall be 
published in the Gazette, then such requirement shall be deemed to have been satisﬁ ed if such rule, regulation, order, by-law, notiﬁ cation or other 
matter is published in an electronic form of the Gazette.”

03. Educational and other Qualiﬁ cations :
 3.1 Educational Qualiﬁ cations :
Should have passed 06 subjects in not more than two sittings with Credit passes for Sinhala/Tamil, Mathematics,

 and

 3.2 Other Qualiﬁ cations :
I. Applicant should be a Citizen of Sri Lanka.
II. Applicants who are sitting for the competitve examination should be permanent residents within the territory 
of the North Western Provincial Council for a period at least 03 years preceding to the closing date of
applications. (Residency should be established at the interview by producing the Electoral Register or a
Certiﬁ cate issued by the Divisional Secretary based on the Electoral Register).
III. Should have excellent moral character and should be in good health condition.
IV . Every applicant should be physically and mentally ﬁ t to serve in any area of the North Western Province
and to perform the duties of the post.
V . Age should be not less than 18 years and not more than 30 years as at closing date of applications. (The
upper age limit will not be applicable to the applicants who are serving in permanent posts of the Public
Service or Provincial Public Service ).

04.  Conditions of Service :
  4.1 This post is permanent. The pension scheme applicable to this post will be subjected to a policy decision taken 
by the Government in future.
  4.2 The appointees to this post will be subjected to a probation period of 03 years.
  4.3 The appointees should abide by the regulations of the Establishment Code of the Democratic Socialist Republic 
of Sri Lanka, Financial Regulations, Departmental Orders, Disciplinary Procedure Code of the North Western 
Provincial Council, Financial Regulations Code of the North Western Provincial Council, Scheme of Recruitment 
and Promotion of the North Western  Provincial Revenue Inspector Service and other regulations and orders of 
the Government and the North Western Provincial Council already issued and will be issued from time to time.
05.  Method of Recruitment :
   70% of the total vacancies will be ﬁ lled on the results of the open competitive examination. In order to be eligible 
for the appointment, candidates will have to obtain at least 40% of the marks allocated for each and every 
subject and 50% of the total marks allocated for the  examination. A number of candidates equal to the number 
of vacancies will be recruited in order of merit according to the marks obtained out of the marks allocated for 
the examination and through an interview which will be conducted to check the basic quali ﬁ cations required 
for the recruitment and to test the physical ﬁ tness.
06.  Examination Procedure :
   The examination which will test the quali ﬁ cations and skills of the candidates for the ofﬁ cial duties will comprise 
of two question papers. This examination will be conducted in Sinhala, Tamil and English mediums and the 
candidates will not be allowed to change the applied Language medium at a later stage.
 I.  Intelligence Quotient Test - Duration : One Hour (100 Marks)
  This is a question paper which tests the general intelligence, logical ability, expression of ideas, numerical 
Skills and comprehension skills of the applicants.
 II. Language Skills - Duration : One Hour (100 marks)
  This is a question paper which tests the skills of the applicants on spelling, grammar usage, clear and 
explicit expression of ideas, understanding on language phrases, summerizing and essay skills of the 
applicants.
07.  Examination Fees :
   A sum of Rs. 500 being the examination fee should be paid to the Divisional Secretariat of the area of permanent 
residence of the North Western Province to be credited to Revenue Head 2003-02-13 of Secretary, North 
Western Provincial Council Public Service Commission, and the receipt WPSM 02 obtained from the Divisional 
Secretariat should be afﬁ xed in the appropriate place of the application and submitted. Receipts other than the 
receipts obtained from the Divisional Secretariat, Money orders or stamp Fees will not be accepted. It should be 
noted that the examination fee is non-refundable under any circumstance and it will not be allowed to transfer 
the examination fees paid to any other examination.
