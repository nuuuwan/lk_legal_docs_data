ATTENTION is drawn to the Notification appearing in the 1st week of every month, regarding the latest dates and times of acceptance of Notices 
for publication in the weekly Gazettes, at the end of every weekly Gazette of Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka.
 All Notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes shall close at 12.00 noon of each Friday, two weeks before the date of publication. 
All Government Departments, Corporations, Boards, etc. are hereby advised that Notifications fixing closing dates and times of applications in 
respect of Post-Vacancies, Examinations, Tender Notices and dates and times of Auction Sales, etc. should be prepared by giving adequate time 
both from the date of despatch of notices to Govt. Press and from the date of publication, thereby enabling those interested in the contents of such 
notices to actively and positively participate please note that inquiries and complaints made in respect of corrections pertaining to notification will 
not be entertained after three months from the date of publication.
 All notices to be published in the weekly Gazettes should reach Government Press two weeks before the date of publication i.e. notices 
for publication in the weekly Gazette 25th October, 2024 should reach Government Press on or before 12.00 noon on 11th October,  2024.

REGISTRATION Of RECOGNIzED MOTOR GARAGES fOR ThE ISSuANCE Of fITNESS CERTIfICATES - 
YeAR 2025
APPLICATIONS are invited from the Garage owners who can produce evidences that they are registered their business and 
pay business turn over tax, and from the depots of Sri Lanka Transport Board, to issue fitness certificates for commercial 
motor vehicles registered within the Central Province. Applications are invited only from the garages located within the 
Central Province.
1.  Applicants shall have fulfilled following requirements :
 I. facilities 
 1.1 20  feet long, 2 1/2 feet wide and 4 1/2 feet deep vehicle inspection pit or an inspection platform or a lift 
providing similar facilities.
 1.2 Brake testing equipment
 1.3 headlamp testing equipment
 1.4 Mechanical tools such as wrenches, spanners, hammers etc., are used to check the undercarriage of the 
vehicle, steering joints, brakes, cables etc., and worn parts.
 II. Staff
  The Certifying Officers with the following qualifications should be employed in the garage
 II.1 Shall have possessed a Special Proficiency in Motor Vehicle engineering or National Technological 
Diploma in Mechanical engineering or Junior Technical Officers’ Certificate in Mechanical engineering 
with five (05) years of experience in the field of motor vehicle engineering.
 II.2 Shall have passed the four (04) year course in motor mechanics at Moratuwa Ceylon German Technical 
Training Institute or any other equivalent qualifications with eight (08) years of experience in the field of 
motor vehicle engineering.
2.   following documents must be furnished with the applications
 2.1 Attested copies of the certificates confirming the qualifications of the certifying officers.
 2.2 A bank receipt for a sum of Rs. 2,000 credited to the Bank of Ceylon, Account no. 33965 in the name of 
“Commissioner of Transport, Central Province”, as a non-refundable deposit.
 2.3 A rough sketch showing the exact locaiton of the garage and the road access from the center of the city 
as accessible to the inspecting officer (Brief details on road map)
3. having a smart phone is necessary as details and photos of the tests to be uploaded in applicaiton software as to issue 
vehicle revenue licenses under eRL. 2.0 program launched to issue vehicle revenue licenses. Therefore, having a smart 
phone will be considered a mandatory part of a garage for regisration of motor garages to issue fitness certificates in the 
year 2025.

THe Weekly issue of the Gazette of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka is normally published on 
fridays. If a friday happens to be a Public holiday the Gazette is published on the working day immediately 
preceding the Friday. Thus the last date specified for the receipt of notices for publication in the Gazette also 
varies depending on the incidence of public holidays in the week concerned.
The Schedule below shows the date of publication and the latest time by which notices should be re-
ceived for publication in the respective weekly Gazette. All notices received out of times specified below will 
not be published. Such notices will be returned to the sender by post for necessary amendment and return if pub-
lication is desired in a subsequent issue of the Gazette. It will be in the interest of all concerned if those desirous 
of ensuring the timely publication of notices in the Gazette  make it a point to see that sufficient time is allowed 
for postal  transmission of  notices to the Government Press.
The Government Printer accept payments of subscription for the Government Gazette.                
Note.—Payments for inserting Notices in the Gazette of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka 
will be received by the Government Printer.
