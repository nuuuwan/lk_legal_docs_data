open competitive examination for the Recruitment of Graduates on District basis for the class 3 Grade 
I (A) of Sri Lanka Teachers’ Service for the existing Vacancies in the Provincial council Schools of the 
north Western Province - 2023
APPLICATIONS are hereby invited from the eligible graduates for recruitment on District basis to the post of Class 3 Grade 
I (A) of the Sri Lanka Teachers’ Service as per the Service Minute of Sri Lanka Teachers’ Service in North Western Provincial 
Council Public service to fill the existing vacancies in the North Western Provincial Council Schools.
1. Graduates will be recruited on District basis for Sinhala, Tamil and English medium subjects and one applicant can apply 
for maximum 3 subjects mentioned in the Schedule. Priority will be given to the permanent residents of the respective 
Districts.
2. However, the residents of Kurunegala District should apply for the vacancies existed in Kurunegala District and the 
residents of the Puttalam District should apply for the vacancies existed in Puttalam District. In the event of there are no 
adequate number of qualified applicants from a District to fill the vacancies of that particular District, those vacancies 
will be filled from the qualified applicants of the other District.
3. The available number of vacancies for Sinhala, Tamil and English medium in each District is given in the Schedule 
which is prepared subject to the number of vacancies approved for recruitment to the North Western Province.
4. Permanent residents of other provinces can also apply for 03 vacancies in any subject mentioned in the Schedule and 
preference will be given to permanent residents who have 03-year residency in the respective District in selection. In the 
event of absence of qualified candidates for each subject, the applicants who are permanent residents in North Western 
Province but do not have 03-year residency in North Western Province will be considered. If vacancies are still existed 
such vacancies will be filled with the eligible applicants from other provinces who have applied for respective subjects.
5. Salary scale :
 Salary scale applicable to this post is Rs. 31,490 - 6x445 - 7x525 - 2x600 - Rs. 39,035/- monthly combined salary scale 
in Segment GE 1-2016-A of the Public Administration Circular No. 03/2016 (IV) dated 05.01.2022. Salaries will be 
paid placing on the salary step of Rs. 37,835/-, the fourteenth step of this salary scale.
 
6. Educational and Other Qualifications :
 6.1 Educational Qualifications :
 I. Should have obtained a degree related to the subjects  mentioned in the Schedule, and belonging to 
specialized subject areas recognized for teaching purposes, from a university or Degree Awarding Institute 
recognized by the University Grants Commission. This degree should be effective before the closing date 
of applications ; 
And
 II.    should have passed Sinhala or Tamil Language and Mathematics in G.C.E. Ordinary Level Examination 
as a subject.
  Very Important :- (Should be considered when filling the applications)
 • Should be mindful of the subjects which can be applied according to the  subject of the degree owned by 
the applicant.
 • Should apply for vacancies only according to the major subject / subjects of the degree.

 • Maximum number of subjects that can be applied one person is 3 and shall be mentioned according to the 
order of preference.
 • Applicants will not be allowed to change the applied subjects at a later stage under any circumstances
 6.2 Physical and Mental Fitness:
  Every applicant should be physically and mentally fit to perform duties as a teacher.
 6.3 Age:
  Age should be not less than 18 years and not more than 35 years as at closing date of applications.
 6.4 Other Qualifications:
 I. Applicant should be a citizen of Sri Lanka.
 II. Should have an excellent moral character and physical fitness. 
 III. Should be able to serve in any area of North  Western Province.
 IV .  Residency should be proved at the interview by a Residential Certificate issued by the Grama Niladhari, to 
be a permanent resident in North Western Province for a period not less than three (03) years preceding to 
the closing date of applications.
 
 V . In case the applicants do not have permanent residency in the North- Western Province by the closing date 
of applications, if their spouses have permanent residency in North-Western Province such applicants will 
also be considered as per Section 04 (Spouse's residential certificate and marriage certificate should be 
produced at the time of interview)
 VI. Applicants from other provinces who have applied for each subject should submit a residential certificate 
issued by the Grama Niladhari of the Divisional Secretary’s area where they reside, at the interview.
7. Conditions of Service:-
 I. Selected graduate applicants will be appointed to the post of Class 3 Grade I (A) of the Sri Lanka Teachers’ 
Service subject to the general conditions which govern the appointments to the public / provincial public service, 
conditions stipulated by the Sri Lanka Teachers’ Service Minute in North Western Provincial Public Service and 
to the amendments to same which have been made already or will be made in future.
 II.   This post is permanent. Appointees will be subjected to a policy decision taken by the  Government with regard 
to the pension scheme entitled for this post.
 III. Appointees will be subjected to a probation period of 03 years.
 IV .  Promotions will be made in terms of  the  Service  Minute  of  Sri  Lanka Teachers’  Service in North Western 
Provincial Public Service.
 V .  The Second Language competency level applicable to the post according to the Public Administration circular 
No. 18/2020 dated 16.10.2020 should be obtained within 03 years from the date of appointment.
 VI. Appointees should serve at least 05  years  in  the  Educational  Zone  of the school to which the first appointment 
is given and cannot request for transfers during those 5 years.
 VII.  In the event of the school of first appointment is not in a difficult or very difficult school, such appointees are 
bound to serve in a difficult or very difficult school for a period of at least 5 years at any time during the period 
of service.

8. Recruitment :
 8.1 Method of Recruitment:-
  Recruitments are made under the provisions of the Service Minute of Sri Lanka Teachers’ Service in North 
Western Provincial Public Service.
 8.2 Written Examination – This Examination will have two question papers.
 
Subject No. Subject Maximum Marks Marks required to pass the 
examination

Syllabus :
Subject No. 01  -  Aptitude Test
 This question paper will be prepared to test the skills and logical intelligence of the applicants. This paper will comprise 
of 50 multiple choice and short answer questions. (02 marks for each question and total marks is 100)
Subject No. 02 – General Knowledge
 This paper will comprise of 50 multiple choice questions to test  the general knowledge in social, economical, political 
areas. (02 marks for each question and total marks is 100)
8.3 Examination Scheme:
 I Applicants are allowed to apply for this Examination in Sinhala / Tamil   / English medium.
 II Every applicant should sit for both question papers.
 III  Every applicant should sit for all question  papers  in  one  language medium. This language medium should 
be the language expected for the appointment
 IV  Applicants who apply for English Language subject should sit for the examination only in English medium.
 V Applicants are not allowed to change the language medium mentioned  in the application, at a later stage.
8.4 Interview:-
 This interview will be conducted to test the qualifications mentioned in this gazette  notification, physical and mental 
fitness and to give marks for the class of the degree and additional qualifications. Three times the number of vacancies 
will be called for the respective interview based on the merit order of marks in the written examination. The detailed 
degree certificate should be produced at the interview.

preceding the Friday. Thus the last date specified for the receipt of notices for publication in the Gazette also 
varies depending on the incidence of public holidays in the week concerned.
The Schedule below shows the date of publication and the latest time by which notices should be re-
ceived for publication in the respective weekly Gazette. All notices received out of times specified below will 
not be published. Such notices will be returned to the sender by post for necessary amendment and return if pub-
lication is desired in a subsequent issue of the Gazette. It will be in the interest of all concerned if those desirous 
of ensuring the timely publication of notices in the Gazette  make it a point to see that sufficient time is allowed 
for postal  transmission of  notices to the Government Press.
