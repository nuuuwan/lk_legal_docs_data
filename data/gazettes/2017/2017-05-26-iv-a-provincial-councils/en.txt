RECRUITMENT OF GRADUATES ON DISTRICT BASIS FOR THE V ACANCIES OF CLASS 3 GRADE I (A) 
OF THE SRI LANKA TEACHER’S SERVICE EXISTING IN THE 
PROVINCIAL COUNCIL SCHOOLS OF NORTH WESTERN PROVINCE – 2017
(SINHALA/TAMIL/ENGLISH MEDIUM)
APPLICATIONS are called from the Graduates who are permanent residents of the North Western Province and who are 
qualiﬁ ed as mentioned in this notiﬁ cation, to be recruited on District basis according to the Service Minute of the Sri Lanka 
Teacher’s Service of the North Western Provincial Public Service, for the vacancies of Class 3 Grade I (A) of the Sri Lanka 
Teacher’s Service which exist in the Provincial Council Schools of the North Western Province.  
01. Recruitment will be done on District basis giving preference to the residents of the respective district and therefore, 
residents of Kurunegala district should apply for the vacancies existing in the Kurunegala District and the residents of 
Puttlam District should apply for the vacancies existing in the Puttlam District. Action will be taken to ﬁ ll vacancies for 
speciﬁ c subjects from the qualiﬁ ed applicants from the other District, if adequate number of qualiﬁ ed applicants for that 
subject is not available from the respective District. 
02. The number of vacancies is 1000. The number of vacancies for each subject is given in the Schedule 01. However, 
the number of applicants who will be recruited may be changed according to the available provisions and service                            
requirements. 
03. Salary : -
The Salary Scale GE 1-2016: Rs. 27,740 – 6 x 300 – 7 x 380 – 2 x 445 – Rs. 33,090/-, which is mentioned in the Schedule 
I of the Public Administration Circular 03/2016 dated 25.02.2016 will be applicable for this post. However, the salary 
will be paid according to the provisions stipulated in the Schedule II of the above circular, until 01.01.2020. 
 
 04. Educational and Other Qualiﬁ cations:
 4.1. Educational Quali ﬁ cations:
i. Should have obtained a Degree in a ﬁ eld mentioned in the Schedule 01 from a University or a Degree Awarding 
Institute recognized by the University Grants Commission, of which the subject ﬁ elds are recognized and speciﬁ ed 
for the appointment of teachers. This degree should be effective before the last date of calling of applications,                        
and   
ii. Sho uld have passed the subjects of Sinhala Language or Tamil Language and Mathematics at the General                      
Certiﬁ cate of Education (Ordinary Level) Examination. 
Very Important :-
 Applicants should pay attention to the eligible subjects according to their respective degrees.  
 Applicants should apply only according to the major subject / subjects of the degree. 
 Maximum number of subjects which can be applied is 03.
 Applicants are not allowed to change the subject / subjects applied, at a later stage.  
     4.2  Physical and Mental Aptitude :-
Every applicant should be physically and mentally ﬁ t to perform duties as a Teacher.

 4.3  Age:
            Minimum age limit as at the last date of calling applications is 21 years. The upper age limit is 42.  
 4.4  Other Qualiﬁ cations:
i. Applicant should be a Citizen of Sri Lanka.
ii. Applicant should possess an excellent moral character.
iii. Applicant should be willing to serve in any area of the North Western Province.
iv. Applicants should be permanent residents of the territory of the North Western Provincial Council for a period 
of not less than 03 years preceding to the last date of calling applications. (Residency should be proved at the 
interview from the Register of V oters) 
05.   Service Conditions  :
 i. Quali ﬁ ed applicants will be appointed to the Class 3 Grade I (A) of the Sri Lanka Teacher’s Service subject 
to the general conditions governing the appointments of the Public / Provincial Public Service, conditions 
stipulated in the Service Minute of the Sri Lanka Teacher’s Service of the North Western Provincial Council 
Public Service, amendments done to the Service Minute and to the amendments which will be done hereafter. 
 
 ii. This post is permanent. Appointees are subjected to the policy decision taken by the Government on Pension. 
 iii. Appointees are subjected to a probation period of 03 years. 
 iv. Promotions will be given in terms of the provisions of the Service Minute of the Teacher’s Service of the 
North Western Provincial Council Public Service. 
 v. Appointees should obtain the Second Language competency within 05 years from the date of appointment in 
terms of the provisions of the Public Administration Circular No. 01/2014 dated 21.01.2014 and amendments 
done time to time for that circular. 
 vi. Appointees should serve a minimum period of 10 years in the District appointed initially, a minimum period 
of 06 years in the Divisional Secretary Division, and a minimum period of 05 years in the School appointed 
initially subject to service requirements. Whenever the initially appointed school is not a remote / very remote 
school, the applicants are abide by the condition of serving a minimum period of 05 years in a very remote 
/ remote school at anytime during the period of service. 
06.   
      6.1  Method of Recruitment  :
 i. Applicants residing in the Kurunegala District will be recruited to the vacancies existing in the Kurunegala 
District and the applicants residing in the Puttlam District will be recruited to the vacancies existing in the 
Puttlam District, on District basis. 
 ii. A written competitive examination or a practical test will not be held if the number of applicants in each 
District is similar or less than the number of vacancies for respective subjects. In such case an interview to 
check the qualiﬁ cation will only be held prior to the recruitment.

08. Method of Selection:-
(A) A written competitive examination or a practical test will not be held if the number of applicants in each District 
is similar or less than the number of vacancies for respective subjects. In such case marks will not be given at 
the interview. Accordingly, the applicants will be recruited only after checking their basic quali ﬁ cations and 
the physical and mental ﬁ tness to serve as a teacher. 
(B) If the number of applicants applied for each subject in each District are more than the number of vacancies:
  (i) A number of applicants equal to triple the number of vacancies in each District for each subject, who have 
passed both subjects at the written examination and permanent residents of each District, will be called for 
the interview in order of merit. 
  (ii)     Applicants who are quali ﬁ ed from the interview will be placed in order of merit according to the total 
marks obtained at the written examination and the interview.  A number of applicants equal to the double 
the number of vacancies for each subject will be called for the practical test.  
(iii) The order of merit of the applicants will be decided according to the total marks obtained at the written 
examination, interview and the practical test and according to the subject applied and the District of per-
manent residency. The most qualiﬁ ed applicants will be recruited according to the number of vacancies. 
  
  (C) Even if the selection is over from a respective District, applicants from the other District will be considered in 
order of merit if the vacancies are still available. 
(D) Whenever an applicant has been selected for two or three subjects, the subject of appointment will be decided 
by the North Western Provincial Council Public Service Commission. 
 
09. Examination Fees :
A sum of Rs. 500 being the examination fee should be paid to the Divisional Secretariat of the area of permanent residence 
of the North Western Province to be credited to Revenue Head 2003-02-13 of Secretary, North Western Provincial Council 
Public Service Commission and the cash receipt in the NWPC uq/2 format should be pasted at the appropriate place in the 
application. The receipts obtained by other institutions after paying examination fees, money or money orders will not be 
accepted. It should be noted that the examination fee is non-refundable under any circumstance and it will not be allowed to 
transfer the examination fees paid to any other examination.  
10.  Application Procedure: 
10.1.  Applications should be prepared using A4 size (21 cm x 29cm) paper by including the; 
 i. paragraphs from no.01 to no. 06 to the ﬁ rst page,
 ii. paragraphs from paragraph no. 07 to the rest of pages.
 iii. Applications that do not comply with the specimen application form, which are incomplete and the appli-
cations of the candidates who have not fulﬁ lled the required qualiﬁ cations will be rejected without notice. 
Applicants should check whether the application form complies with the specimen application form. It 
would be useful to keep a photocopy of the completed application with the applicant. 
 iv. The subject / subjects applied cannot be changed and one applicant is allowed to submit only one applica-
tion. 
 v. A copy of the detailed Degree Certi ﬁ cate should be attached to the application.
 vi. Certifying the Signature of the Applicant: 
  The signature of the applicant in the application form should be attested by a Principal of a Government 
School / a Justice of Peace / Commissioner of Oaths / Attorney-at-Law / Commissioned Of ﬁ cer in the 
Armed Forces / an ofﬁ cer holding a gazetted post in the Police Service or an ofﬁ cer holding a permanent 
post in the Public Service / Provincial Public Service whose annual combined salary is above Rs. 240,360/-.
