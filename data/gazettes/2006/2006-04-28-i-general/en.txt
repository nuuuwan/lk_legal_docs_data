PART   I :  SEC .  (I) – GAZETTE OF THE DEMOCRATIC SOCIALIST REPUBLIC OF SRI LANKA – 28.04.2006
IMPORTANT NOTICE REGARDING ACCEPTANCE OF NOTICES
 FOR PUBLICATION IN THE WEEKLY "GAZETTE"
ATTENTION is drawn to each  of the Notices appearing in the 1st week of every month, regarding the latest dates and time of acceptance
of notices for publication in the weekly Gazette  at the end of each Part of the Gazette of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka.
All notices to be published in every Part of the Gazette shall close at 12 noon of each Friday, a fortnight before the date of
publication. All Departments, Corporations, Boards, etc.,  are advised that any notification fixing specific dates for closing times of
applications  in regard to vacancies, tender notices and the dates and times of auction sales, etc.,   should be prepared with due regard
to this change, i.e. by  giving adequate time both from the time of despatch of notice  to the Government Press and from the date of
publication thus enabling those interested in the contents of the notices to actively and positively participate.
All notices to be published in the weekly Gazette  should reach this Department positively by 12 noon of the Friday, two
weeks prior to date of publication e.g., Notices for publication in the weekly Gazette of 05th May, 2006 should reach the Government
Press on or before 12  noon on 21st April, 2006.
L AKSHMAN   GOONEWARDENA ,
Government Printer.
Department of Govt. Printing,
Colombo 08,
January 01, 2006.

1:2 Scope of Audit :
Audit opinion, comments and findings in this report are based on a review of the financial statements presented to audit and
substantive tests of samples of transactions. The scope and the extent of such review and tests were such as to enable as
wide an audit coverage as possible within the limitations of staff, other resources and time available to me. The audit was
carried out in accordance with Sri Lanka Auditing Standards, Methods and Practices to obtain reasonable assurance as to
whether thefinancial statements are free of material misstatements. The audit included examination of evidence supporting
the amounts and disclosures in financial statements and assessment of accounting principles and significant estimates and
judgements made in the preparation of the financial statements,evaluation of the overall presentation and determining whether
accounting policies adopted were appropriate, consistently applied and adequately disclosed. Sub-sections (3) and (4) of
Section 13 of the Finance Act, No. 38 of 1971 give discretionary powers to the Auditor - General to determine the scope
and extent of the audit.
2. Accounts
2:1 Audit Opinion :
Except for the effects on the financial statements of the matters referred to in Paragraph 2:2 of my report, I am of opinion
so far as appears from my examination and to the best of my information and according to the explanations given to me that
the financial statements have been prepared in accordance with Sri Lanka Accounting Standard and the stated accounting
policies and give true and fair view of the financial position of the University as at 31st December, 2003 and the financial
results and cash flow for the year then ended.
2:2 Comments on Accounts
2:2:1 Accounting Deficiencies :
Following accounting deficiencies were observed :-
(a) Fixed assets had been shown in the balance sheet at book value and not at physically verified value ;
(b) Deposits aggregating Rs. 43,279 with the District Secretariat, Jaffna made in 1975, 1979 and 1990 had not been
disclosed in the accounts ;
(c) Three months salary recoverable from those who had not given three calendar months notice of their desire to leave
the service of the University in terms of the agreement entered in to with the University had  been ascertained and
brought to account ;
(d) Damages caused to the buildings and assets of the Faculty of Agriculture due to military operations had been included
in the value of assets shown in the accounts ;
(e) Advances amounting to Rs. 4,890,674 paid for subscriptions for renewal of magazines (foreign and local) for the
year 2004 had been charged to capital expenditure (books and periodicals) instead of being charged to supplies
advance for books and periodicals ;
(f) 3,428 books received as donations during the year under review had not been valued and brought to account ;
(g) Shortages in the supplies of consumable items and equipment to the value of Rs. 349,820 and Rs. 1,395,548
respectively had been treated as import advances in the accounts instead of being accounted under shortages of
supplies recoverable account ;
(h) Although livestocks valued at Rs. 104,200 shown in the accounts were not alive,  no provision had been made in
the accounts in respect of these losses ;
(i) Digital printing machine valued at Rs. 500,000 (approximately) donated in 2001 by the Ministry of Education,
Teachers Education and Teachers Development Project for Teachers Education Development had not been brought
to account.
2:2:3Lack of Evidence for Audit :
49 items in the accounts aggregating Rs. 837,264,453 could not be satisfactorily vouched or accepted in audit due to
the absence of valuation reports, verification reports, estimates, bills of quantities, agreements, measurement sheets,
age analysis, quotations, bills, work completion certificates, approvals, etc.
